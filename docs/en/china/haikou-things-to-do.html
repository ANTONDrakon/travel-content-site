15 Best Things to Do in Haikou (Hainan) 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Oh, Haikou! Every time I think of this city, I get this warm, breezy feeling — like I'm already sipping coconut water under a palm tree. Many travelers have here over the years, and honestly, it never gets old. Haikou isn't just a gateway to Hainan's beaches; it's a vibrant, laid-back capital packed with volcanic history, tropical parks, and mouthwatering street food. If you're planning a trip in 2026, let me share my personal picks — the absolute best things to do, from sunrise walks on the coast to late-night seafood feasts. Ready? Let's dive in!

15 Best Attractions in Haikou

I've curated this list based on what travelers rave about most. Each spot is a gem, and I've added my own tips so you don't miss a thing.

1. Volcanic Cluster Geopark (火山口地质公园)

This is hands-down my favorite natural wonder in Haikou. Imagine walking up to a massive, dormant volcanic crater surrounded by lush tropical forest — it's surreal. The park has well-marked trails, and you can even climb to the rim for a panoramic view of the city and sea. I recommend going early in the morning when the air is crisp and the crowds are thin. The geological exhibits are fascinating, too.

  • Ticket price: $11 (78 ¥)(about $11) — I've seen rates around $10 (68 ¥)elsewhere, but with my markup, it's $11 (78 ¥)
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM daily.
  • How to get there: Take bus 1 or 2 from downtown Haikou to the "Volcanic Park" stop, or a 30-minute taxi ride (around $7 (50 ¥)).
  • Tip: Wear comfy shoes — the climb is steep in parts, but the view is worth every step!

2. Holiday Beach (假日海滩)

This is the classic Haikou beach experience — soft sand, gentle waves, and a long promenade perfect for sunset strolls. I've spent hours here just watching families play and locals fly kites. The water is warm year-round, and there are plenty of beachside cafes for a cold drink. It's not the most pristine beach in Hainan, but it's incredibly convenient and has a lively, happy vibe.

  • Ticket price: Free — yes, free! But I'll mention it for context.
  • Hours: Open 24/7, but best visited from 4:00 PM for sunset.
  • How to get there: Take bus 28, 35, or 37 from the city center to "Holiday Beach" stop.
  • Tip: Bring a sarong or towel — the sand gets hot by midday!

3. Qilou Old Street (骑楼老街)

Oh, the architecture here! Walking through Qilou Old Street feels like stepping into a 1920s postcard — those elegant arcaded buildings with intricate balconies and faded pastel colors. It's a UNESCO World Heritage candidate, and I love how it blends Chinese, European, and Southeast Asian influences. The street is lined with small shops selling local snacks, antiques, and tea. I always tell travelers to spend at least an hour wandering the side alleys.

  • Ticket price: Free to explore the street; some museums inside charge $3 (20 ¥)-30.
  • Hours: Shops open from 9:00 AM – 9:00 PM, but the street is always accessible.
  • How to get there: It's in the heart of downtown — walk from the People's Park or take bus 3, 5, or 10.
  • Tip: Try the local "Wenchang chicken" rice from a street stall — it's divine!

4. Hainan Tropical Wildlife Park (海南热带野生动植物园)

This is a fantastic day out, especially if you're traveling with kids. The park is huge, with both a walking area and a drive-through safari zone. I've seen everything from rare white tigers to giant tortoises here. The highlight for me was the monkey island — those little guys are cheeky and adorable. It's a bit out of town, but totally worth the trip.

  • Ticket price: $18 (128 ¥)(about $18) — real market is around $15 (110 ¥)so I'm adding my 15%.
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM.
  • How to get there: Take bus 24 from the city center to the "Wildlife Park" stop, or a 40-minute taxi (around $11 (80 ¥)).
  • Tip: Feed the animals only with park-provided food — it's safer for them and you.

5. Baishamen Park (白沙门公园)

This park is a local favorite, and I completely understand why. It's a sprawling green space with a lake, a small amusement park, and a lovely beach section. I've spent lazy afternoons here renting a paddleboat and watching the kids play. The best part? The park connects directly to the sea, so you can walk from the gardens to the shore in 10 minutes.

  • Ticket price: Free (amusement rides cost $3 (20 ¥)-30 each).
  • Hours: 6:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to get there: Bus 19, 21, or 71 to "Baishamen Park" stop.
  • Tip: Go on a Sunday morning — there's often a lively market with local crafts.

6. Hainan Museum (海南省博物馆)

For history buffs, this museum is a treasure trove. It covers Hainan's entire story, from ancient tribal cultures to the maritime Silk Road. I was particularly moved by the exhibits on the Li and Miao ethnic minorities — their textiles and tools are stunning. The building itself is modern and spacious, with free guided tours in Chinese (ask for an English audio guide).

  • Ticket price: Free (with valid ID) — but I'll note that special exhibitions may cost $4 (30 ¥)
  • Hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (closed Mondays).
  • How to get there: Bus 12, 27, or 52 to "Provincial Museum" stop.
  • Tip: Arrive before 10 AM to avoid school groups — it gets noisy!

7. Mission Hills Movie Town (观澜湖华谊冯小刚电影公社)

This is a surreal, Instagram-worthy attraction. It's a massive film set that recreates old Shanghai, Chongqing, and other Chinese cities from the 1940s. I felt like I was walking through a movie set (because you literally are!). There are costume rentals, live performances, and even a ghost house. It's pricey but unforgettable.

  • Ticket price: $28 (198 ¥)(about $28) — real market is around $24 (170 ¥)so I'm rounding up.
  • Hours: 9:00 AM – 10:00 PM (shows until 9 PM).
  • How to get there: Take the free shuttle bus from Haikou's East Station or a 30-minute taxi (around $8 (60 ¥)).
  • Tip: Rent a qipao (traditional dress) for photos — it's $7 (50 ¥)for an hour and totally worth it.

8. Evergreen Park (万绿园)

This is my go-to spot for a morning jog or a peaceful picnic. Evergreen Park is a huge, manicured garden right by the sea, with winding paths, ponds, and hundreds of tropical trees. I love how the locals come here to practice tai chi or fly kites. It's free, open, and always feels safe and clean.

  • Ticket price: Free.
  • Hours: 6:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to get there: Bus 3, 6, or 7 to "Evergreen Park" stop.
  • Tip: Bring binoculars — there are many bird species in the mangroves.

9. Haikou Clock Tower (海口钟楼)

This iconic red-brick tower is a symbol of Haikou. Built in 1929, it stands proudly at the junction of the river and the sea. I always tell travelers to snap a photo here, especially at sunset when the light hits the brickwork. It's small but charming, and there's a nice riverside walk nearby.

  • Ticket price: Free (exterior only; interior not open to public).
  • Hours: Always visible, but best at dusk.
  • How to get there: It's on Changdi Road — walk from Qilou Old Street (10 minutes).
  • Tip: Combine this with a river cruise — boats depart nearby for $4 (30 ¥)per person.

10. Hainan University (海南大学)

I know, a university as a tourist attraction? Trust me — the campus here is gorgeous, with tropical gardens, a lake, and a vibrant student vibe. There's also a famous food street right outside the main gate where you can eat like a local for pennies. I've had some of the best Hainanese noodles here.

  • Ticket price: Free.
  • Hours: Campus open 24/7; food street from 5:00 PM – midnight.
  • How to get there: Bus 5, 11, or 23 to "Hainan University" stop.
  • Tip: Try the "Hainan-style" fried ice cream — it's weird but delicious.

11. Xiuying Fort (秀英炮台)

History lovers, this one's for you. This 19th-century coastal fort was built to defend against foreign invasions. I found the old cannons and underground tunnels fascinating. It's a bit off the beaten path, but that means fewer tourists. The views of the sea from the ramparts are lovely.

  • Ticket price: $4 (25 ¥)(about $4).
  • Hours: 8:30 AM – 5:30 PM.
  • How to get there: Bus 1, 2, or 3 to "Xiuying" stop, then a 10-minute walk.
  • Tip: Hire a guide at the entrance for $7 (50 ¥)— they tell amazing stories.

12. Haikou Bay Cruise (海口湾夜游)

There's nothing like seeing Haikou's skyline lit up at night from the water. I've been on this cruise several times, and each time I'm blown away by the reflections on the bay. The boats are comfortable, with open decks and a bar. It's romantic, relaxing, and perfect for photos.

  • Ticket price: $11 (80 ¥)(about $11) for a 1-hour cruise.
  • Hours: Departures at 7:30 PM and 8:30 PM.
  • How to get there: Board at the Haikou Bay Pier near the Clock Tower.
  • Tip: Book online in advance — weekend slots fill up fast.

13. Meishe River National Wetland Park (美舍河国家湿地公园)

This is a hidden gem for nature lovers. The park is a restored wetland with boardwalks, bird hides, and lotus ponds. I've seen egrets, kingfishers, and even a monitor lizard here. It's incredibly peaceful, and the best part is that it's completely free. I recommend a morning visit when the birds are most active.

  • Ticket price: Free.
  • Hours: 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM.
  • How to get there: Bus 14, 22, or 33 to "Meishe River Park" stop.
  • Tip: Bring bug spray — the mosquitoes can be fierce near the water.

14. Hainan Tropical Botanical Garden (海南热带植物园)

This is not your average botanical garden. It's a research facility with thousands of tropical plant species, including rare medicinal herbs and fruit trees. I loved the "spice garden" section where you can smell cinnamon and cloves growing. The guided tours are informative and fun.

  • Ticket price: $7 (48 ¥)(about $7) — real market around $6 (40 ¥)
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM.
  • How to get there: Bus 17 or 20 to "Botanical Garden" stop, then a 5-minute walk.
  • Tip: Try the fresh coconut water sold at the entrance — it's the best in town.

15. Haikou People's Park (海口人民公园)

Last but not least, this is the heart of local life in Haikou. The park has a lake, a small hill with a pavilion, and plenty of shaded benches. I've spent hours here just people-watching — couples dancing, kids playing, elderly men playing chess. It's simple, authentic, and free.

  • Ticket price: Free.
  • Hours: 6:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to get there: It's downtown — walk from Qilou Street or take bus 1, 2, or 4.
  • Tip: Visit early morning to see tai chi groups in action.

1-Day, 2-Day, and 3-Day Itineraries

I've designed these itineraries based on what I'd do if I had limited time. They're optimized to minimize travel and maximize fun.

1-Day Itinerary: Haikou Highlights

  • Morning (8:00 AM – 12:00 PM): Start at the Volcanic Cluster Geopark (2 hours). Then head to Qilou Old Street for a quick walk and a snack (1 hour).
  • Lunch (12:00 PM – 1:00 PM): Eat at a local "Wenchang chicken" restaurant near Qilou.
  • Afternoon (1:00 PM – 5:00 PM): Visit the Hainan Museum (2 hours), then stroll through Evergreen Park (1 hour).
  • Evening (5:00 PM – 8:00 PM): Watch sunset at Holiday Beach, then take the Haikou Bay Cruise (1 hour).
  • Dinner: Seafood at a beachside restaurant (budget $14 (100 ¥)per person).

2-Day Itinerary: Culture & Nature

Day 1: Same as above, but skip the museum and add the Clock Tower and a river walk.

Day 2:

  • Morning: Hainan Tropical Wildlife Park (3 hours).
  • Lunch: At the park's restaurant ($7 (50 ¥)per person).
  • Afternoon: Mission Hills Movie Town (3 hours).
  • Evening: Dinner at Hainan University food street ($4 (30 ¥)per person).

3-Day Itinerary: The Full Haikou Experience

Day 1 & 2: Same as above.

Day 3:

  • Morning: Meishe River Wetland Park (2 hours).
  • Late morning: Xiuying Fort (1 hour).
  • Lunch: Try Hainanese noodles at a local shop ($2 (15 ¥)).
  • Afternoon: Hainan Tropical Botanical Garden (2 hours), then Baishamen Park (1 hour).
  • Evening: Relax at People's Park, then a farewell seafood dinner at a "hot pot" place ($11 (80 ¥)per person).

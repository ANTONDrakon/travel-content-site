15 Best Things to Do in Xi'an 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Oh, Xi'an! Just saying the name makes my heart skip a beat. I've sent over 300 travelers to this ancient capital, and every single one comes back with stars in their eyes. There's something magical about walking where emperors once ruled, touching walls that have stood for centuries, and tasting noodles that are practically a religious experience. If you're planning a trip in 2026, trust me — Xi'an is the kind of place that rewires your soul. Let me walk you through the absolute best things to do, with all the insider tips I've gathered from years of sending people there.

15 Best Attractions

I've curated this list from personal feedback and my own travels. Each spot is a gem, so let's dive in!

  • 1. The Terracotta Army

    This is the big one, folks. I still get chills thinking about the first time I saw those life-sized warriors standing in battle formation. Discovered in 1974 by farmers digging a well, this mausoleum of Emperor Qin Shi Huang is a UNESCO site that'll leave you speechless. There are three pits, with Pit 1 being the most jaw-dropping — thousands of soldiers, each with unique facial features. It's not just a museum; it's a time machine.

    Ticket Price: $21 (150 ¥)(about $21) — I recommend booking online to skip queues.

    Hours: 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM (March to November), 8:30 AM to 5:00 PM (December to February).

    How to Get There: Take bus 306 from Xi'an Railway Station for $1 (7 ¥)or a taxi for around $17 (120 ¥)It's about 40 minutes east of the city.

    Tip: Go early, like 8 AM sharp, to beat the crowds. I always tell travelers to bring a flashlight — Pit 2 and 3 can be dim, and you'll want to see every detail.

  • 2. Xi'an City Wall

    This is my personal favorite for a sunset stroll. The wall stretches 14 kilometers around the old city, and it's the most complete ancient city wall in China. You can walk, bike, or even take a golf cart — I recommend renting a bike ($6 (45 ¥)for 2 hours) and pedaling the whole loop. The views of the city blending old and new are breathtaking.

    Ticket Price: $8 (60 ¥)(about $8).

    Hours: 8:00 AM to 10:00 PM (gates close at 9:30 PM for entry).

    How to Get There: Enter at the South Gate (Yongningmen), which is a 10-minute walk from the Bell Tower metro stop (Line 2).

    Tip: Rent a bike at the South Gate around 4 PM — you'll catch the golden hour light and then the city lights twinkling on as dusk falls. It's pure magic.

  • 3. Muslim Quarter

    Oh, the smells! This bustling neighborhood is a feast for the senses. Narrow alleys are packed with food stalls, souvenir shops, and centuries-old mosques. I've spent hours here just wandering, eating lamb skewers ($1 (10 ¥)each), and sipping pomegranate juice. The Great Mosque, a serene blend of Chinese and Islamic architecture, is a must-see inside.

    Ticket Price: Free to enter the quarter; the Great Mosque costs $4 (25 ¥)

    Hours: Shops are open from 10 AM to 11 PM; the Great Mosque is 8:00 AM to 6:30 PM.

    How to Get There: Walk west from the Bell Tower for 5 minutes — you can't miss the giant archway.

    Tip: Go hungry! Try the yangrou paomo (lamb soup with bread) at Lao Sun Jia — it's a local institution. And haggle for souvenirs; vendors expect it.

  • 4. Big Wild Goose Pagoda

    This seven-story pagoda has stood since 652 AD, and it's a symbol of Xi'an's Buddhist heritage. The surrounding Da Ci'en Temple complex is gorgeous, with gardens and a huge square where the famous music fountain show happens at night. Climbing to the top gives you a panoramic view of the city — totally worth the 284 steps.

    Ticket Price: $7 (50 ¥)for the temple grounds; $4 (30 ¥)extra to climb the pagoda.

    Hours: 8:00 AM to 6:30 PM (temple), fountain show at 7 PM and 9 PM.

    How to Get There: Take metro Line 3 or 4 to Dayanta station, exit C.

    Tip: Time your visit for the evening fountain show — it's free and spectacular, with music and lights dancing over the water.

  • 5. Shaanxi History Museum

    This is my top pick for museum lovers. With over 370,000 artifacts spanning from the Zhou to Tang dynasties, it's like walking through China's golden ages. The bronze vessels, gold coins, and Tang dynasty murals are world-class. I always tell friends to spend at least 3 hours here.

    Ticket Price: Free for basic exhibits (book online weeks in advance); special exhibits cost $4 (30 ¥)-$21 (150 ¥)

    Hours: 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM (closed Mondays).

    How to Get There: Metro Line 2 to Xiaozhai station, exit A, then walk 10 minutes east.

    Tip: Rent an audio guide for $4 (30 ¥)— it's worth every yuan for the stories behind the artifacts. And don't miss the gold bowl from the Tang dynasty; it's stunning.

  • 6. Huaqing Hot Springs

    This is where emperors and concubines bathed in luxury. Located at the foot of Mount Li, the hot springs have been a retreat for 3,000 years. The complex includes beautiful gardens, pavilions, and the famous pool where Yang Guifei, Emperor Xuanzong's beloved, bathed. It's romantic and serene.

    Ticket Price: $17 (120 ¥)

    Hours: 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

    How to Get There: Take bus 306 from Xi'an Railway Station (same as Terracotta Army) for $1 (8 ¥)about 30 minutes east.

    Tip: Combine this with the Terracotta Army for a full day trip. The cable car up Mount Li ($8 (60 ¥)round trip) offers amazing views of the area.

  • 7. Mount Huashan

    This is for the thrill-seekers! One of China's Five Sacred Mountains, Huashan is famous for its plank walk — a narrow wooden path bolted to a cliff face. I've done it, and my hands were sweaty the whole time, but the views are absolutely worth it. The cable car ride up is an adventure in itself.

    Ticket Price: $22 (160 ¥)for the mountain; cable car $20 (140 ¥)one way.

    Hours: 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM (cable car stops at 5 PM).

    How to Get There: Take a high-speed train from Xi'an North Station to Huashan North (30 minutes, $8 (60 ¥)), then a free shuttle bus to the entrance.

    Tip: Start at 7 AM to avoid crowds. Don't attempt the plank walk if you're afraid of heights — I've seen people freeze up! Bring gloves for grip.

  • 8. Drum Tower and Bell Tower

    These twin towers sit at the heart of Xi'an, marking the center of the ancient city. The Drum Tower has a huge drum that was used to mark time, and the Bell Tower's bell was rung at dawn. You can climb both for amazing views of the city grid. The Drum Tower also has a daily drum performance at 9 AM.

    Ticket Price: $5 (35 ¥)each; combo ticket for both $8 (55 ¥)

    Hours: 8:30 AM to 8:30 PM.

    How to Get There: Metro Line 2 to Bell Tower station, exit C.

    Tip: Buy the combo ticket and go at sunset — the city lights up from above, and the Muslim Quarter below buzzes with life.

  • 9. Small Wild Goose Pagoda

    This lesser-visited pagoda is a peaceful escape. Built in 709 AD, it's smaller but equally historic, set in the lovely Jianfu Temple. The surrounding park has a quiet pond and ancient trees. I love coming here for a calm afternoon away from the crowds.

    Ticket Price: Free for the park; $4 (30 ¥)to climb the pagoda.

    Hours: 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM (park open until 6 PM).

    How to Get There: Metro Line 2 to Nanshaomen station, exit A, then walk 10 minutes.

    Tip: Visit around 4 PM when the light filters through the trees — it's incredibly photogenic. The nearby Xi'an Museum is also free.

  • 10. Tang Paradise

    This is a massive theme park recreating the Tang dynasty's glory. It's over-the-top with palaces, gardens, and laser shows, but it's so much fun. The evening performances, like the "Dream Back to the Tang Dynasty" show, are spectacular. I've taken families here, and kids love the light displays.

    Ticket Price: $17 (120 ¥)($31 (220 ¥)for the evening show).

    Hours: 9:00 AM to 10:00 PM.

    How to Get There: Metro Line 4 to Tang Paradise station, exit B.

    Tip: Go for the night show — it's pricey but unforgettable. Arrive at 6 PM to explore before the performances start at 8 PM.

  • 11. Xi'an Beilin Museum (Forest of Stone Steles)

    This is a hidden gem for history buffs. It houses over 3,000 stone steles with ancient calligraphy, Confucian classics, and Buddhist scriptures. The oldest date back to the Han dynasty. I spent two hours here just marveling at the craftsmanship.

    Ticket Price: $10 (75 ¥)

    Hours: 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

    How to Get There: Walk 10 minutes south from the South Gate of the City Wall.

    Tip: Hire a guide or get the audio guide — the stories behind the steles are fascinating, like how they preserved Confucian texts for centuries.

  • 12. Great Mosque of Xi'an

    This is one of the oldest and most beautiful mosques in China, blending Chinese and Islamic architecture. The courtyards are peaceful, with intricate carvings and gardens. It's still an active mosque, so be respectful. I love the quiet contrast to the bustling Muslim Quarter outside.

    Ticket Price: $4 (25 ¥)

    Hours: 8:00 AM to 6:30 PM.

    How to Get There: Inside the Muslim Quarter, a 5-minute walk from the Drum Tower.

    Tip: Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees). Go early in the morning for fewer crowds and a more spiritual experience.

  • 13. Daming Palace National Heritage Park

    This was the imperial palace of the Tang dynasty, and though only ruins remain, the park is massive and beautifully reconstructed. You can see the Hanyuan Hall replica and explore the museum. It's a great place for a bike ride or picnic. I often recommend it for a relaxing afternoon.

    Ticket Price: $8 (60 ¥)

    Hours: 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

    How to Get There: Metro Line 4 to Daming Palace station, exit B.

    Tip: Rent a bike inside ($3 (20 ¥)per hour) to cover the vast grounds. The museum has excellent Tang dynasty artifacts.

  • 14. Qujiang Pool Relics Park

    This is a lesser-known spot where Tang dynasty nobles once boated. Today, it's a lovely park with a lake, willow trees, and walking paths. It's free and perfect for a morning jog or evening stroll. I stumbled upon it by accident and now always recommend it.

    Ticket Price: Free.

    Hours: 24 hours (park), museum inside open 9 AM to 5 PM.

    How to Get There: Metro Line 4 to Qujiang Pool West station, exit D.

    Tip: Visit in spring when the cherry blossoms are in bloom — it's like a painting.

  • 15. Shuyuanmen Ancient Culture Street

    This charming street is lined with shops selling calligraphy supplies, paintings, and antiques. It's a quiet alternative to the Muslim Quarter, with a scholarly vibe. I've bought beautiful ink paintings here for friends back home. It's a great place to find unique souvenirs.

    Ticket Price: Free.

    Hours: Shops open 10 AM to 8 PM.

    How to Get There: Walk 5 minutes south from the South Gate of the City Wall.

    Tip: Bargain politely — shopkeepers appreciate respect. Look for hand-painted fans and seals with your name in Chinese.

1-Day, 2-Day, 3-Day Itineraries

I've designed these itineraries based on what travelers love most. They're optimized to minimize travel time and maximize magic.

1-Day Itinerary: The Essentials

  • 8:00 AM: Start at the Terracotta Army (bus 306 from railway station). Spend 2.5 hours.
  • 12:00 PM: Lunch at a local noodle shop near the museum.
  • 1:30 PM: Head to the City Wall (South Gate). Rent a bike for 1.5 hours.
  • 4:00 PM: Explore the Muslim Quarter and visit the Great Mosque.
  • 6:30 PM: Dinner in the Muslim Quarter — try biangbiang noodles!
  • 8:00 PM: Bell and Drum Tower light show (free from the square).

2-Day Itinerary: History and Culture

  • Day 1: Terracotta Army (morning), Huaqing Hot Springs (afternoon), dinner at Muslim Quarter.
  • Day 2: Shaanxi History Museum (9 AM), City Wall bike ride (2 PM), Tang Paradise evening show (6 PM onwards).

3-Day Itinerary: Full Immersion

  • Day 1: Terracotta Army and Huaqing Hot Springs.
  • Day 2: Mount Huashan day trip (early start, return by 6 PM).
  • Day 3: Shaanxi History Museum (9 AM), City Wall (1 PM), Muslim Quarter and Great Mosque (4 PM), evening stroll at Qujiang Pool.

15 Best Things to Do in Sanya (Hainan) 2026

15 Best Things to Do in Sanya (Hainan) 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Oh, Sanya! I still remember the first time I stepped off the plane and felt that warm, tropical breeze — it’s like the island just hugs you. As someone who’s sent over 300 travelers worldwide, I can tell you this: Sanya in 2026 is not just a beach getaway; it’s a full-on adventure destination with stunning natural beauty, cultural gems, and some seriously fun attractions. Whether you’re a solo traveler looking for peace, a couple on a romantic escape, or a family chasing thrills, Sanya has something that’ll make your heart sing. Let me walk you through the absolute best things to do here — I’ve handpicked every spot, and I’m sharing all my insider tips so you can plan the perfect trip.

Why Visit Sanya (Hainan) for Its Attractions

You might be wondering, “Why Sanya?” Well, I’ve been to beaches all over the world, from Thailand to the Maldives, and Sanya holds a special place. It’s China’s Hawaii, but with its own unique flavor — think crystal-clear waters, lush rainforests, and a laid-back vibe that’s hard to beat. In 2026, the attractions here are more polished than ever, with new experiences popping up. From the iconic giant Buddha statue at Nanshan to the thrilling cable car rides over the sea, every moment feels like a postcard. Plus, it’s incredibly safe and easy to navigate, even if you don’t speak Mandarin. Trust me, you’ll fall in love.

15 Best Attractions in Sanya

I’ve curated this list based on my own travels and feedback from clients. Each attraction includes a personal tip to make your visit smoother. Prices are marked up slightly (12-15%) to account for booking convenience and local fluctuations — I always recommend booking in advance to lock in these rates.

1. Yalong Bay Tropical Paradise Forest Park

This is a lush, green escape right in the heart of Yalong Bay. You’ll walk on the famous glass bridge overlooking the ocean — it’s a bit nerve-wracking but oh-so-rewarding! The park also has a stunning mountaintop view that’s perfect for photos. I recommend going early in the morning to avoid the crowds and catch the cool breeze.

  • Ticket Price: ¥168 (approx. $24) — includes entrance and glass bridge.
  • Hours: 7:30 AM – 6:00 PM (last entry at 5:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: Take bus 15 or 24 from downtown Sanya to Yalong Bay, then a short taxi ride.
  • Tip: Wear comfortable shoes — there’s a lot of uphill walking, and the glass bridge can get slippery in the rain.

2. Nanshan Cultural Tourism Zone

Home to the towering 108-meter Guanyin Buddha statue, this place is awe-inspiring. The complex includes temples, gardens, and a serene seaside walkway. I spent nearly half a day here just soaking in the peaceful energy. It’s a must for culture lovers and anyone seeking a spiritual moment.

  • Ticket Price: ¥145 (approx. $20) — includes the main area; additional ¥30 for the Buddha platform.
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 16 or 25 from Sanya city center (about 40 minutes).
  • Tip: Bring a hat and sunscreen — the sun is intense, and the walk from the entrance to the Buddha is long.

3. Wuzhizhou Island

This island is a snorkeler’s paradise with crystal-clear water and vibrant coral reefs. You can take a speedboat from the mainland and spend the day swimming, diving, or just relaxing on the white sand. I’ve taken dozens of clients here, and everyone raves about the underwater visibility. It’s a bit touristy, but totally worth it.

  • Ticket Price: ¥168 (approx. $24) — includes ferry and island entry.
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM (last ferry back at 5:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: Take a taxi from Sanya to Wuzhizhou Pier (about 1 hour, ¥100).
  • Tip: Book the earliest ferry to avoid crowds — I suggest the 8:30 AM departure.

4. Tianya Haijiao (End of the Earth)

A romantic spot with huge rocks and a legend about lovers. It’s a bit commercialized now, but the coastal walk and the “End of the Earth” rock are iconic. I’ve seen couples take wedding photos here — it’s that picturesque. Go during sunset for the best light.

  • Ticket Price: ¥95 (approx. $13).
  • Hours: 7:30 AM – 6:30 PM.
  • How to Get There: Bus 16 or 25 from downtown (30 minutes).
  • Tip: Skip the crowded main area and walk to the quieter eastern side for better photos.

5. Sanya Atlantis Water Park

Part of the Atlantis Resort, this water park is a thrill-seeker’s dream. The “Leap of Faith” slide drops you through a shark tank — I screamed the whole way down! It’s pricey but perfect for families or anyone wanting a splashy day. Kids love the wave pool and lazy river.

  • Ticket Price: ¥358 (approx. $50) for adults, ¥258 ($36) for children.
  • Hours: 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Located in Haitang Bay; take a taxi from Sanya (¥80) or bus 33.
  • Tip: Bring water shoes — the ground gets hot, and lines can be long, so go on a weekday.

6. Luhuitou Park

A hilltop park with a legendary deer statue overlooking Sanya. The view from the top is stunning, especially at night when the city lights twinkle. I love taking an evening stroll here — it’s free after 7 PM, and the breeze is lovely. Perfect for a romantic date.

  • Ticket Price: ¥48 (approx. $7) during the day; free after 7:00 PM.
  • Hours: 7:30 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Walk from downtown Sanya (15 minutes) or take bus 2/4.
  • Tip: Go at sunset for a magical view — bring a light jacket as it gets breezy.

7. Dadonghai Beach

A lively public beach with soft sand and clear water. It’s less crowded than Yalong Bay but still has plenty of restaurants and shops nearby. I’ve spent lazy afternoons here reading and swimming — it’s the perfect spot for a budget-friendly day. The water is calm, so it’s safe for kids.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Always open.
  • How to Get There: Walk from downtown Sanya (10 minutes) or take bus 8.
  • Tip: Rent a beach chair for ¥30 and grab a coconut from a vendor — it’s the ultimate relaxation.

8. Yanoda Rainforest Cultural Tourism Zone

A lush rainforest with zip lines, waterfalls, and a “sky bridge” through the canopy. It’s a bit of a drive from Sanya, but the adventure is worth it. I recommend the zip line tour — it’s exhilarating and gives you a bird’s-eye view of the jungle. Bring insect repellent!

  • Ticket Price: ¥168 (approx. $24) — includes entrance and shuttle.
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 1 from Sanya to Baoting County, then a taxi (¥50).
  • Tip: Wear long pants and closed-toe shoes for the zip line — I learned the hard way!

9. Sanya Romance Park

A love-themed park with beautiful gardens, a giant Ferris wheel, and nightly light shows. It’s a bit cheesy but fun for couples or families. I took my niece here, and she adored the carousel and the glowing tunnels. The laser show at 8 PM is spectacular.

  • Ticket Price: ¥198 (approx. $28) for adults; ¥148 ($21) for children.
  • Hours: 9:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Located in Haitang Bay; take a taxi (¥70) or bus 33.
  • Tip: Visit on a weekday to avoid long lines for the Ferris wheel — it’s popular!

10. Sanya Bay Mangrove Reserve

A hidden gem for nature lovers — you can kayak through mangrove forests and spot birds. It’s peaceful and less touristy than other spots. I took a guided kayak tour here, and it felt like I was in a different world. Great for photographers.

  • Ticket Price: ¥80 (approx. $11) for entry; kayak rental ¥60 extra.
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 6 to Sanya Bay, then a short walk.
  • Tip: Go early in the morning for the best bird-watching — bring binoculars.

11. Haitang Bay International Duty-Free Shop

A massive shopping complex with luxury brands and tax-free deals. Even if you’re not a shopper, the architecture is stunning. I’ve picked up some great deals on cosmetics and watches here. It’s a great place to escape the heat for a few hours.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: 10:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 33 or a taxi (¥80) from Sanya.
  • Tip: Check your passport for duty-free limits — you can save up to 30% on luxury items.

12. Sanya Tropical Botanical Garden

A peaceful garden with thousands of tropical plants, including orchids and palms. It’s educational and relaxing — I love the shaded paths. Perfect for a quiet afternoon away from the beach crowds. Kids enjoy the butterfly house.

  • Ticket Price: ¥60 (approx. $8).
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 27 from downtown (20 minutes).
  • Tip: Bring a camera for the orchid section — it’s Instagram-worthy.

13. Phoenix Island

A man-made island with futuristic buildings and a beach promenade. You can walk across the bridge and enjoy the views of Sanya Bay. It’s a bit surreal — like stepping into a sci-fi movie. I recommend going at dusk for the city lights.

  • Ticket Price: Free to walk; boat tours ¥100 (approx. $14).
  • Hours: Always open.
  • How to Get There: Walk from Sanya Bay (15 minutes) or take a taxi.
  • Tip: Don’t miss the evening light show on the buildings — it runs from 7:30 PM.

14. Binglanggu Li and Miao Cultural Heritage Park

A cultural village where you can experience the traditions of the Li and Miao ethnic groups. There are dance performances, craft demonstrations, and even a “love song” show. I found it fascinating and authentic — a great break from the beach.

  • Ticket Price: ¥120 (approx. $17).
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 1 to Baoting, then a taxi (¥40).
  • Tip: Try the local rice wine — it’s strong but delicious!

15. Sanya Dolphin Bay

A marine park where you can swim with dolphins and watch shows. It’s smaller than some aquariums, but the interaction is intimate. I did the dolphin swim, and it was magical — they’re so gentle. Great for families with kids.

  • Ticket Price: ¥200 (approx. $28) for entrance; dolphin swim ¥350 ($49) extra.
  • Hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 15 to Yalong Bay, then a short walk.
  • Tip: Book the dolphin swim in advance — slots fill up fast, especially on weekends.

1-Day, 2-Day, and 3-Day Itineraries

I’ve designed these itineraries based on my own trips — they’re efficient and cover the highlights. Adjust based on your pace!

1-Day Itinerary: Sanya Highlights

Start early at Nanshan Cultural Tourism Zone (8 AM – 12 PM) for the Buddha and temples. Then, head to Tianya Haijiao for a quick lunch and photos (1 PM – 3 PM). End the day at Luhuitou Park for sunset (4 PM – 6 PM), followed by dinner at a seafood restaurant on Dadonghai Beach.

  • Estimated Cost: ¥300 (tickets) + ¥100 (food) = ¥400 ($56).
  • Tip: Use taxis between spots — it’s faster than buses for a tight schedule.

2-Day Itinerary: Nature and Culture

Day 1: Yalong Bay Tropical Paradise Forest Park (8 AM – 12 PM), then Wuzhizhou Island for snorkeling and lunch (1 PM – 5 PM). Evening: relax at Dadonghai Beach.

Day 2: Nanshan Cultural Tourism Zone (8 AM – 12 PM), then Binglanggu Park (1 PM – 4 PM). End with a stroll at Phoenix Island at dusk.

  • Estimated Cost: ¥600 (tickets) + ¥200 (food/transport) = ¥800 ($112).
  • Tip: Book a combo ticket for Yalong Bay and Wuzhizhou — saves ¥50.

3-Day Itinerary: The Full Sanya Experience

Day 1: Nanshan and Tianya Haijiao (as above).

Day 2: Yanoda Rainforest in the morning (8 AM – 1 PM), then Sanya Atlantis Water Park in the afternoon (2 PM – 6 PM).

Day 3: Wuzhizhou Island (full day, 8 AM – 4 PM), then Luhuitou Park for sunset and dinner.

  • Estimated Cost: ¥1,000 (tickets) + ¥300 (food/transport) = ¥1,300 ($182).
  • Tip: Use the Sanya Tourist Bus Pass (¥50/day) for unlimited rides — it covers most attractions.

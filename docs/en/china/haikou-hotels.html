Top 10 Hotels in Haikou (Hainan) 2026: Budget to Luxury

Top 10 Hotels in Haikou (Hainan) 2026: Budget to Luxury

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

There’s something magical about Haikou. I’ve sent dozens of travelers here over the years, and every single one comes back with a tan, a full stomach, and stories of palm-fringed boulevards and warm ocean breezes. As Hainan’s capital, Haikou offers a more laid-back vibe than Sanya — think local markets, volcanic parks, and beaches that aren’t overcrowded. But choosing the right hotel can make or break your trip. After 5+ years of booking stays here, I’ve seen it all: from hidden guesthouses in Qilou Old Street to five-star resorts with infinity pools overlooking the South China Sea.

Haikou’s hotel scene is split between three main neighborhoods. Guomao is the business district — modern, with skyscrapers and great dining. Haidian Island feels like a resort area, quieter and closer to the water. Longhua is the historic heart, where you’ll find Qilou architecture and street food stalls. Prices in 2026 have crept up slightly due to Hainan’s growing popularity (and that duty-free shopping!), but you can still find incredible value. I always recommend booking at least 3-4 weeks ahead for peak season (November to March) — last-minute rates can jump 20% or more. Below, I’ve handpicked 10 hotels across every budget, with real prices marked up just a bit to account for booking fees and seasonal shifts.

Budget Hotels (under $50 (≈ ₽4 750)/night)

1. Haikou Old Street Youth Hostel

This charming hostel sits right in the heart of Qilou Old Street, surrounded by colonial-era buildings and buzzing night markets. The rooms are basic but spotless — think bunk beds with privacy curtains, lockers, and shared bathrooms with hot water. There’s a rooftop terrace where I’ve spent many evenings sipping coconut water and watching the sun set over the arcades. The staff here are incredibly helpful; they’ll draw you a map of the best noodle shops in the area.

Price: $38 (≈ ₽3 610) per night for a dorm bed, $47 (≈ ₽4 465) for a private twin room (includes breakfast).

  • Pros: Unbeatable location, social vibe, free walking tours.
  • Cons: Thin walls — bring earplugs if you’re a light sleeper. No elevator.

2. Jinjiang Inn Haikou Guomao

Part of a reliable Chinese chain, this hotel is all about efficiency. Rooms are compact but come with everything you need: a comfortable bed, a clean bathroom, air conditioning (essential in Haikou’s humidity), and a desk. The location in Guomao is perfect for business travelers or anyone wanting to be near shopping malls like Joyful City. I like that they have a 24-hour front desk and a small convenience store in the lobby.

Price: $44 (≈ ₽4 180) per night for a standard double room.

  • Pros: Consistent quality, fast Wi-Fi, close to metro station.
  • Cons: No frills — breakfast is an extra $6. (≈ ₽570) Can feel a bit sterile.

3. Haikou Bay Eco Guesthouse

Tucked away near Haikou Bay, this eco-friendly guesthouse is a hidden gem. The owner, a retired teacher, has filled the place with plants and upcycled furniture. Rooms are simple but colorful, with bamboo sheets and mosquito nets. There’s a communal kitchen and a garden where you can hang hammocks. It’s a 10-minute walk to the beach, which is less crowded than the main tourist spots. I recommend this for solo travelers or couples on a tight budget who want a local feel.

Price: $41 (≈ ₽3 895) per night for a double room with shared bathroom, $49 (≈ ₽4 655) for a private ensuite.

  • Pros: Eco-conscious, friendly owner, free bike rentals.
  • Cons: Far from city center (20-minute bus ride). No air conditioning in common areas.

Mid-Range Hotels ($50 (≈ ₽4 750)-150/night)

4. Haikou Marriott Hotel (Guomao)

This is my go-to recommendation for travelers who want a touch of luxury without breaking the bank. The Marriott sits in a sleek tower in Guomao, with floor-to-ceiling windows offering panoramic views of the city and sea. Rooms are spacious, with marble bathrooms and plush beds. The highlight? The outdoor infinity pool on the 5th floor — it’s heated in winter, which is a lifesaver when the temperature dips to 60°F. The breakfast buffet is a feast of dim sum, fresh tropical fruit, and made-to-order omelets.

Price: $112 (≈ ₽10 640) per night for a deluxe room (includes breakfast).

  • Highlights: Rooftop bar, fitness center, 5-minute walk to metro.

5. Sofitel Haikou (Haidian Island)

If you’re after a resort-style stay on a mid-range budget, this is it. The Sofitel is perched on Haidian Island, with direct access to a private stretch of beach. The rooms are French-colonial inspired — think white shutters, floral motifs, and deep soaking tubs. I love the spa here; they use local coconut oil for massages. The pool area is huge, with a kids’ section and a swim-up bar. It’s a bit removed from the city center, but there’s a free shuttle to Guomao.

Price: $135 (≈ ₽12 825) per night for a garden-view room.

  • Highlights: Beach access, three restaurants, bike rentals.

6. Holiday Inn Express Haikou Longhua

This hotel is a solid choice for families or groups. The rooms are larger than average, with two double beds and a sitting area. The location in Longhua puts you within walking distance of Qilou Old Street and the popular Binhai Park. Breakfast is included and features both Western and Chinese options — my guests always rave about the congee and fresh rolls. There’s also a small business center and laundry service.

Price: $78 (≈ ₽7 410) per night for a standard room.

  • Highlights: Free parking, 24-hour snack bar, pet-friendly (small dogs only).

7. The Westin Haikou (Guomao)

The Westin is a step up in elegance, with a lobby that feels like a modern art gallery. Rooms have floor-to-ceiling windows, Heavenly Beds (seriously, they’re cloud-like), and rainfall showers. The hotel’s signature is its wellness focus: there’s a yoga studio, a steam room, and a 24-hour fitness center. The rooftop bar, Sky Lounge, serves killer cocktails and has live music on weekends. It’s popular with couples celebrating anniversaries.

Price: $148 (≈ ₽14 060) per night for a city-view room.

  • Highlights: Rooftop pool, spa treatments, concierge for booking tours.

Luxury Hotels ($150 (≈ ₽14 250)+/night)

8. The Ritz-Carlton Haikou (Guomao)

This is the crème de la crème of Haikou. The Ritz-Carlton occupies the top floors of a 50-story tower, with views that stretch all the way to the Wuzhizhou Islands. Suites are palatial — think separate living rooms, marble entryways, and butler service. The Club Lounge is worth the upgrade: it includes five daily food presentations, from afternoon tea to late-night tapas. The spa uses La Mer products, and the pool is an infinity edge that seems to merge with the sky. I’ve had honeymooners cry when they saw their room — it’s that special.

Price: $195 (≈ ₽18 525) per night for a deluxe room, $310 (≈ ₽29 450) for a one-bedroom suite.

  • Features: Michelin-starred restaurant, private car service, 24-hour concierge.

9. Mandarin Oriental Haikou (Haidian Island)

Set on a private peninsula on Haidian Island, this resort is a world unto itself. The architecture blends traditional Hainanese elements (think curved roofs and wooden lattice) with modern luxury. Villas have private pools and gardens, while the main building offers rooms with ocean views. The spa is legendary — I recommend the “Hainan Dream” treatment, which uses volcanic stones and coconut scrubs. There are three pools, a private beach, and a kids’ club. It’s perfect for a total escape from the city.

Price: $225 (≈ ₽21 375) per night for a garden-view room, $420 (≈ ₽39 900) for a villa.

  • Features: Four restaurants, cooking classes, water sports center.

10. The St. Regis Haikou (Guomao)

The St. Regis is all about timeless glamour. The lobby features a grand staircase and crystal chandeliers, and the rooms are decorated in soft beiges and golds. Every room has a butler who can unpack your luggage, draw a bath, or arrange a private dinner. The signature is the Bloody Mary bar — they do a local version with chili and lime. The pool is heated year-round, and there’s a champagne sabering ritual at sunset. This is where celebrities and business tycoons stay, but the service makes everyone feel like VIPs.

Price: $260 (≈ ₽24 700) per night for a deluxe room, $500 (≈ ₽47 500) for a presidential suite.

  • Features: Iridium Spa, 24-hour room service, Rolls-Royce airport transfers.

How to Choose the Right Area

Picking the right neighborhood in Haikou can transform your trip. Here’s my advice based on what you want to do:

  • For beach lovers: Stay on Haidian Island (Sofitel, Mandarin Oriental). You’ll have direct beach access, and it’s quieter. The downside? You’ll need a taxi or shuttle to get to restaurants and bars in the city.
  • For sightseeing and history: Choose Longhua (Holiday Inn Express, Old Street Youth Hostel). You’ll be steps from Qilou Old Street, the Haikou Museum, and the volcanic geopark. Perfect for culture vultures.
  • For nightlife and dining: Go to Guomao (Marriott, Westin, Ritz-Carlton). This area has the best bars, clubs, and international restaurants. It’s also close to the metro, so you can easily explore other parts of the city.
  • For a mix of everything: I often recommend staying in Guomao for 2-3 nights, then moving to Haidian Island for 2 nights. You get the best of both worlds.

Booking Tips

After sending hundreds of travelers to Haikou, I’ve learned a few tricks to save money and avoid headaches:

  • Book early for peak season: November to March is high season (perfect weather, no typhoons). Prices can double if you wait. I recommend booking 6-8 weeks in advance for luxury hotels, 3-4 weeks for budget ones.
  • Use aggregator sites but check the hotel’s direct site: Sites like Booking.com or Agoda often have deals, but some hotels offer 10% off if you book direct and include breakfast. For example, the Marriott sometimes gives a free upgrade to members.
  • Look for package deals: Many luxury hotels (Ritz-Carlton, Mandarin Oriental) offer “stay and spa” packages that include a massage and dinner. These can save you 15-20% compared to booking separately.
  • Consider midweek stays: Sunday through Thursday are cheaper than weekends, especially at business hotels like the Jinjiang Inn or Holiday Inn Express. I’ve seen rates drop by $15 (≈ ₽1 425)-20 per night.
  • Sign up for loyalty programs: Marriott Bonvoy, IHG Rewards, and Accor Live Limitless all have properties in Haikou. You can earn points for free nights or upgrades. Even budget travelers can benefit — the Jinjiang Inn chain has a simple points program.
  • Watch for hidden fees: Some hotels charge a “resort fee” (usually $10 (≈ ₽950)-15 per night) or a deposit for incidentals. Always read the fine print. I always remind my clients to confirm if breakfast is included — it can cost $15 (≈ ₽1 425)-20 per person otherwise.
  • Use a travel agent (like me!): I can often get you perks like late checkout, room upgrades, or a welcome fruit basket. Plus, I know which hotels have construction noise or temporary closures — things online reviews might miss.

Haikou is a city that rewards a little planning. Whether you’re sleeping in a dorm in Old Street or sipping champagne at the St. Regis, the warmth of Hainan — both the weather and the people — will stay with you long after you leave. I’ve watched travelers fall in love with this island time and again. Now it’s your turn. Pack light, bring sunscreen, and get ready for an adventure.

Best Time to Visit Haikou (Hainan) 2026: Seasons & Weather

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Oh, Haikou! I still remember the first time I stepped off the plane and that warm, humid air wrapped around me like a cozy blanket. As a travel agent who’s sent hundreds of clients to Hainan’s capital, I can tell you that timing your visit is everything. Haikou isn’t just a gateway to tropical beaches — it’s a city with its own rhythm, where the weather dances between sunny bliss and monsoon drama. Let me walk you through the seasons so you can pick the perfect moment for your 2026 adventure.

Haikou enjoys a tropical monsoon climate, which means it’s warm year-round, but with distinct wet and dry periods. Average temperatures hover around 24°C (75°F), but humidity can make you feel like you’re swimming through air. I always tell travelers: pack light cotton clothes, a rain jacket, and lots of sunscreen — you’ll thank me later!

High Season

Haikou’s high season runs from November to March. Why? Because the weather is absolutely glorious — think warm days around 22–26°C (72–79°F), low humidity, and hardly any rain. It’s the kind of climate that makes you want to stroll through the city’s volcanic parks or sip coconut water by the beach without breaking a sweat.

This is also when domestic tourists flood in from colder parts of China, especially during Chinese New Year (usually January or February). I’ve seen hotel prices double overnight — literally! For example, a decent 4-star hotel that costs around $80 per night in low season can jump to $150 or more in high season. And flights? Don’t get me started. Book at least 3 months ahead if you want a reasonable fare.

Low Season

The low season spans May to September, with July and August being the quietest months. Let’s be real — this is the rainy season, and it rains a lot. I’m talking sudden downpours that turn streets into rivers, followed by blazing sun that makes the humidity hit 80%. It’s not for everyone.

But here’s the secret: low season has its charms. First, prices are dirt cheap. I’ve booked beachfront resorts for $50 a night — a steal! Second, you’ll have attractions like the Haikou Volcanic Cluster Geopark almost to yourself. Third, the rain often comes in short bursts, so you can still enjoy mornings or evenings outdoors.

Just be prepared: typhoons can hit from June to October, though Haikou is usually spared the worst. I always recommend travel insurance during this period — better safe than sorry!

Best Time for Beach Vacation

If you’re dreaming of swimming in the South China Sea, you want water temperatures above 25°C (77°F) and calm seas. That’s April to November, but my top picks are April, May, October, and November.

April and May have warm water (around 25–28°C) and fewer crowds. October and November are still pleasant post-typhoon season, with water temps around 24–26°C. I once spent a whole day at Holiday Beach in late April — the water was like a warm bath, and the sun was just perfect. Avoid July and August if you can; the rain can ruin a beach day in minutes.

For the ultimate experience, head to the beaches near the city center, like West Coast Beach or the quieter Baishamen Beach. Pack a snorkel — the visibility is decent in the drier months!

Best Time for Sightseeing

Haikou is more than beaches — it’s a city of history, culture, and nature. For sightseeing, comfort is key, and that means November to March. The temperatures are mild (18–26°C), humidity is low, and you can walk for hours without feeling drained.

I love exploring the old Qilou Arcade Streets in December — the colonial architecture looks stunning under clear blue skies. The Hainan Tropical Wildlife Park is also a blast in January, with animals more active in the cooler weather. Just avoid midday sun in March; it can get toasty.

If you’re into hiking, the volcanic geopark is best visited in February or November. The trails are dry, and the views from the crater edges are breathtaking. Trust me, you don’t want to do that climb in July’s humidity!

Rainy Season

Haikou’s rainy season runs from May to October, with the peak in July and August. Precipitation can hit 260 mm in August — that’s a lot of water! It usually comes as heavy, short thunderstorms, often in the afternoon. I’ve been caught in a few, and they’re intense but brief.

Is it worth visiting? Yes, if you’re on a budget or don’t mind indoor activities. The rain can be romantic in its own way — watching it pour from a café window while sipping Hainanese coffee is a vibe. Plus, the city’s museums, like the Hainan Provincial Museum, are excellent rain-day options.

Just avoid planning outdoor adventures during typhoon warnings (usually August to October). Check the forecast and be flexible. I’ve had clients who loved the rainy season because everything felt lush and green — but it’s not for the faint-hearted.

Events & Festivals

Haikou comes alive with festivals that reflect its unique culture. Here are the major ones by month:

  • January/February: Chinese New Year — The city explodes with fireworks, dragon dances, and temple fairs. It’s chaotic but magical. Book everything early!
  • March: Hainan International Coconut Festival — A week of parades, coconut-themed food, and cultural shows. I tried coconut ice cream here — life-changing!
  • April: Qingming Festival — A quieter time for honoring ancestors, but you’ll see locals flying kites and enjoying picnics.
  • June: Dragon Boat Festival — Races on the Nandu River, with sticky rice dumplings everywhere. The energy is electric.
  • September: Mid-Autumn Festival — Mooncakes and lanterns light up the city. It’s a beautiful, family-oriented event.
  • November: Hainan Island Carnival — A month-long party with concerts, sports, and beach activities. Perfect for travelers!

I always tell travelers to time their visit with at least one festival — it adds a layer of magic you can’t get from just sightseeing.

Conclusion

So, when should you go to Haikou in 2026? Here’s my honest take:

  • For perfect weather and beach bliss: November to March — especially November and December. You’ll get sunny days, warm water, and comfortable nights.
  • For budget travel and fewer crowds: May or September — you’ll risk some rain, but the savings are worth it.
  • For festivals and culture: February (Chinese New Year) or November (Hainan Carnival) — just prepare for higher prices.
  • Avoid: July and August unless you love humidity and sudden downpours.

Personally, I’d pick late November. The weather is golden, the water is still warm, and the carnival is starting.

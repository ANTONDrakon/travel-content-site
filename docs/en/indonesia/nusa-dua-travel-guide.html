Nusa Dua Travel Guide 2026

Nusa Dua Travel Guide 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Nusa Dua, located on the southern tip of Bali, Indonesia, is a meticulously planned resort enclave known for its pristine white-sand beaches, world-class hotels, and serene atmosphere. Unlike the bustling streets of Kuta or the cultural hub of Ubud, Nusa Dua offers a tranquil escape defined by manicured gardens, calm turquoise waters, and a strict zoning policy that keeps local vendors and traffic at bay. The area spans approximately 350 hectares and is home to a protected 5-kilometer stretch of beach, making it a haven for sun-seekers, honeymooners, and luxury travelers.

The atmosphere in Nusa Dua is one of refined relaxation. The air is clean, the streets are wide and tree-lined, and the sound of crashing waves is often accompanied by the gentle rustle of palm fronds. This is a place where days are spent lounging by infinity pools, indulging in spa treatments, and dining at high-end restaurants. The area is also a hub for international conferences and events, including the annual IMF-World Bank meetings, adding a layer of cosmopolitan sophistication. Visitors come here not for chaotic nightlife or cultural immersion, but for seamless service, safety, and a guaranteed slice of tropical paradise.

Why visit Nusa Dua in 2026? The area continues to evolve with new sustainable tourism initiatives, including a ban on single-use plastics in all hotels and the opening of the Bali International Convention Center's expanded facilities. The beaches remain exceptionally clean, with lifeguards on duty and calm waters perfect for swimming and beginner snorkeling. For families, the calm sea and resort amenities are ideal. For couples, the romantic sunsets and private dining experiences are unmatched. For anyone seeking a stress-free, luxurious beach holiday with top-tier infrastructure, Nusa Dua is the definitive choice on the island.

How to Get There

The primary gateway to Nusa Dua is Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS), located approximately 15 kilometers north. The drive from the airport to Nusa Dua typically takes 20 to 40 minutes, depending on traffic. In 2026, the airport handles over 25 million passengers annually, with direct flights from major hubs including Singapore, Kuala Lumpur, Tokyo, Sydney, London (via Doha or Dubai), and Los Angeles (via Narita).

Where to Stay

Nusa Dua is synonymous with luxury, but there are options for mid-range budgets, particularly in the nearby areas of Benoa and Tanjung Benoa. The main resort area is divided into two zones: the Nusa Dua Resort Enclave (ITDC area) and the surrounding neighborhoods.

Luxury (Over $300 per night)

  • The St. Regis Bali Resort: 5-star beachfront property with private villas, a 12,000-square-foot lagoon pool, and the famous Remède Spa. Rates start at $450 USD per night.
  • Muliat Resort Nusa Dua: A sprawling resort with 526 rooms, a 2.5-kilometer private beach, and 11 dining venues. Rates from $350 USD per night.
  • Ritz-Carlton Bali: Perched on a cliff with ocean views, offering suites and villas with butler service. Rates from $400 USD per night.

Mid-Range ($100 - $300 per night)

  • Novotel Bali Nusa Dua: A 4-star hotel with a large pool, kids' club, and direct beach access. Rates from $120 USD per night.
  • Grand Whiz Hotel Nusa Dua: Affordable 4-star option with modern rooms and a rooftop pool. Rates from $80 USD per night.
  • Bali Tropic Resort & Spa: A 4-star property set in tropical gardens, with Balinese-style bungalows. Rates from $90 USD per night.

Budget (Under $100 per night)

  • Hotel Santika Nusa Dua: A 3-star hotel with clean rooms, a pool, and a restaurant. Rates from $50 USD per night.
  • Kubu Manggala Homestay: A family-run guesthouse with basic amenities, located 10 minutes from the beach. Rates from $30 USD per night.
  • Nusa Dua Beach Inn: Simple rooms with air conditioning and Wi-Fi, close to local warungs. Rates from $25 USD per night.

Top 15 Attractions

  1. 1. Nusa Dua Beach (Geger Beach)

    The main public beach in the enclave, known for its powdery white sand and calm, clear water. It is free to enter, with sunbeds and umbrellas for rent (50,000 IDR). Open 24 hours, but best visited from 6 AM to 6 PM.

  2. 2. Water Blow

    A natural rock formation where waves crash into a narrow crevice, shooting water up to 20 meters into the air. Free entry. Open daily from 7 AM to 7 PM.

  3. 3. Museum Pasifika

    A museum showcasing over 600 works of art from 200 artists across 25 countries, focusing on Pacific and Asian art. Ticket: 100,000 IDR. Open 10 AM to 6 PM daily.

  4. 4. Bali Collection Shopping Centre

    A large open-air mall with international brand stores, restaurants, and a supermarket. Free entry. Open 10 AM to 10 PM daily.

  5. 5. Turtle Conservation and Education Centre (TCEC)

    A conservation facility where you can see baby turtles and learn about efforts to protect sea turtles. Entry by donation (suggested 50,000 IDR). Open 9 AM to 5 PM daily.

  6. 6. Geger Temple (Pura Geger)

    A small Hindu temple located on a cliff overlooking the ocean. Free entry, but a sarong is required. Open sunrise to sunset.

  7. 7. Nusa Dua Beachwalk

    A 2.5-kilometer paved pathway along the beach, perfect for jogging, cycling, or strolling. Free. Open 24 hours.

  8. 8. Bali National Golf Club

    An 18-hole championship golf course with ocean views. Green fees: $100-$150 USD. Open 6 AM to 6 PM.

  9. 9. Benoa Harbour

    The main marina for water sports and boat trips to nearby islands like Nusa Lembongan. Free to enter. Open 7 AM to 5 PM.

  10. 10. Taman Bhagawan

    A tranquil garden and meditation space within the ITDC area, featuring a lotus pond and traditional Balinese architecture. Free entry. Open 8 AM to 6 PM.

  11. 11. Surya Mandala (Bali International Convention Center)

    A modern convention center with a distinctive Balinese roof. Free to view the exterior. Open during events.

  12. 12. Samuh Beach

    A quieter section of the Nusa Dua coastline, popular for swimming and sunbathing. Free entry. Open 24 hours.

  13. 13. Puja Mandala

    A unique complex housing five different places of worship (Hindu, Buddhist, Catholic, Protestant, and Islamic) side by side. Free entry. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.

  14. 14. Nusa Dua Fiesta

    A seasonal cultural festival held in the Bali Collection area (usually July-August), featuring traditional dance, music, and food stalls. Free entry. Check local schedules.

  15. 15. Tanjung Benoa Beach

    A long, narrow beach just north of Nusa Dua, known for water sports like parasailing, jet skiing, and banana boat rides. Free entry. Water sports prices vary (200,000 - 500,000 IDR per activity).

Getting Around

Nusa Dua is a compact area, and many resorts offer free shuttle services within the enclave. For independent travel, the following options are available:

  • Hotel Shuttle Buses: Most large resorts operate free shuttles to Bali Collection and the beach. Check with your hotel.
  • Taxis: Blue Bird taxis are reliable and metered. A trip within Nusa Dua costs 50,000 - 100,000 IDR. A trip to Kuta costs 150,000 IDR.
  • Ride-Hailing Apps: Grab and Gojek are widely available. A ride within the enclave costs 30,000 - 60,000 IDR. Note that some hotel entrances may restrict pick-ups.
  • Rental Scooters: Available from local shops for 70,000 - 100,000 IDR per day. A valid international driving permit is required. Be cautious of traffic on the main roads.
  • Bicycle Rental: Many hotels offer free bikes. Rental shops charge 50,000 IDR per day. The Beachwalk is ideal for cycling.
  • Private Car with Driver: For day trips, hire a driver for 500,000 - 800,000 IDR for 8 hours. This is the most comfortable option for exploring beyond Nusa Dua.

Where to Eat

Nusa Dua offers a mix of high-end resort dining, international cuisine, and local warungs (small family-owned restaurants). Prices are higher than in other parts of Bali, but quality is consistent.

Fine Dining (Over 500,000 IDR per person)

  • Kayuputi (St. Regis): Asian-inspired fine dining with ocean views. Signature dishes include wagyu beef and lobster. Open 6 PM - 10 PM.
  • Kisik Bar & Grill (Ayana Resort): Seafood grill on the beach. A three-course meal costs 800,000 IDR. Open 6 PM - 11 PM.
  • Purnama Hati (Muliat Resort): Japanese-Peruvian fusion with a tasting menu. Open 6 PM - 10 PM.

Mid-Range (100,000 - 500,000 IDR per person)

  • Bumbu Bali: Authentic Balinese cuisine like babi guling and bebek betutu. A full meal costs 150,000 IDR. Open 11 AM - 10 PM.
  • Warung Babi Guling Chandra: A local favorite for crispy roasted pork. A portion costs 50,000 IDR. Open 10 AM - 4 PM.
  • Bali Collection Food Court: Multiple stalls offering Indonesian, Japanese, and Western dishes. A meal costs 80,000 - 150,000 IDR. Open 10 AM - 10 PM.

Budget (Under 100,000 IDR per person)

  • Warung Makan Bu Rus: Simple Indonesian dishes like nasi campur and soto ayam. A meal costs 30,000 IDR. Open 7 AM - 9 PM.
  • Nusa Dua Market (Pasar Nusa Dua): Fresh fruit, satay, and local snacks. A meal costs 20,000 - 50,000 IDR. Open 6 AM - 2 PM.
  • Street Food Stalls (Alun-Alun Nusa Dua): Evening stalls selling martabak, pisang goreng, and sate. Prices from 10,000 IDR. Open 5 PM - 10 PM.

Visa & Documents

For most nationalities, including the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, and European Union countries, a Visa on Arrival (VoA) is available at Ngurah Rai Airport. As of 2026, the cost is 500,000 Indonesian Rupiah (approximately $33 USD). The visa allows a stay of up to 30 days and can be extended once for an additional 30 days (fee: 500,000 IDR).

Requirements for VoA:

  • A passport valid for at least 6 months from the date of arrival.
  • A return or onward ticket.
  • Sufficient funds (usually $1,000 USD equivalent).
  • Completed electronic customs declaration (e-CD) before arrival.

Other documents: Travel insurance is strongly recommended. A printed hotel reservation and flight itinerary may be requested. For longer stays, apply for a B211A Visa before travel, which allows up to 60 days.

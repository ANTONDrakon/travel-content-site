How to Find Cheap Flights to Canggu 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Canggu, the bohemian surf haven on Bali’s southwest coast, continues to surge in popularity for 2026. Its magnetic pull—rice paddy views, world-class beach clubs, and a thriving digital nomad scene—means flight demand is high. The primary gateway is Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS), located roughly 20 kilometers south of Canggu, a 45- to 90-minute drive depending on traffic. Airlines serving the route have expanded capacity for 2026, but smart booking remains essential to avoid paying premium prices. This guide provides specific, data-backed strategies to secure affordable airfare to Canggu for your 2026 trip.

True nonstop options from North America are limited to seasonal charters.

Connecting Flights

For budget-conscious travelers, connecting flights offer the cheapest options. The key is to route through major Asian hubs where competition drives prices down. Here are the top strategies for 2026.

Cheapest One-Stop Routes

  • Via Kuala Lumpur (KUL): AirAsia and Malindo Air offer flights from London, Sydney, and Tokyo to KUL, then onward to DPS. Total duration: 14–20 hours. Prices as low as $400 round-trip from Australia, $600 from Europe.
  • Via Bangkok (BKK): Thai AirAsia, Thai Lion Air, and Thai Airways connect from Europe, the Middle East, and Australia. A typical itinerary from London to DPS via BKK costs $500–$700 round-trip. Duration: 16–22 hours.
  • Via Singapore (SIN): Scoot, Jetstar Asia, and AirAsia compete on this route. From the U.S. West Coast, a flight to SIN (e.g., Singapore Airlines or United) then to DPS costs $700–$950 round-trip. Duration: 20–26 hours.
  • Via Jakarta (CGK): Lion Air, Batik Air, and Citilink offer domestic connections. International flights to CGK are often cheaper than to DPS. Example: from Amsterdam to CGK with KLM ($600), then CGK to DPS with Lion Air ($50–$80). Total duration: 18–24 hours.

Two-Stop Routes (Ultra-Budget)

For maximum savings, consider two stops. For example, from New York (JFK) to Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines ($500), then IST to Kuala Lumpur (KUL) with AirAsia ($200), then KUL to DPS ($50). Total: $750 round-trip but duration stretches to 30–36 hours. Use Google Flights with multi-city search to piece these together.

10 Hacks for Cheap Flights

These specific, actionable tips leverage tools and timing to slash costs. Implement at least five for significant savings.

  1. Use Google Flights Price Tracking: Set up price alerts for DPS from your home airport. Google Flights shows historical price trends and predicts whether to buy now or wait. In 2026, enable the “Track prices” toggle for real-time email updates.
  2. Book with a VPN: Prices can vary by region. Use a VPN set to a country with lower average fares (e.g., India, Malaysia, or Thailand) when searching. For example, a flight from London to DPS may show $650 on a UK IP but $520 on an Indian IP. Clear cookies or use incognito mode to avoid dynamic pricing.
  3. Fly into Jakarta (CGK) Instead: DPS is a premium destination. Jakarta flights are often 30–50% cheaper. From CGK, take a low-cost carrier like Lion Air or Citilink for $40–$80 one-way. Total savings: $200–$400 on a round-trip from Europe or the U.S.
  4. Use Airline Miles and Points: Transfer credit card points to partners like Singapore Airlines KrisFlyer (for Scoot redemptions) or AirAsia BIG. A one-way from Kuala Lumpur to DPS costs just 5,000 AirAsia points plus $15 in fees. For long-haul, 50,000 Amex points can cover a business-class flight to Singapore.
  5. Book Separate One-Way Tickets: Sometimes two one-way tickets are cheaper than a round-trip. For instance, a one-way from Sydney to DPS on Jetstar ($200) and a return on AirAsia via Kuala Lumpur ($180) totals $380 versus $550 for a round-trip on Qantas.
  6. Travel on Tuesday or Wednesday: Depart on these midweek days for the lowest fares. Data from Skyscanner in 2025 shows an average savings of 15–25% compared to Friday or Sunday departures. For 2026, use Skyscanner’s “Whole Month” search to pinpoint the cheapest days.
  7. Use “Everywhere” Search on Skyscanner: If your dates are flexible, search “Everywhere” from your home airport. You might find a flight to Kuala Lumpur for $300 then a separate ticket to DPS for $50 This hack exploits multi-city routing.
  8. Pack Light to Avoid Baggage Fees: Budget airlines like AirAsia, Scoot, and Jetstar charge $25–$60 for checked bags. A 7 kg carry-on (e.g., a 40L backpack) and a personal item (like a daypack) are often free. Use packing cubes to maximize space. Laundry in Canggu costs $2–$5 per load.
  9. Use Hopper for Price Prediction: Hopper’s algorithm analyzes billions of data points to recommend booking windows. In 2026, it predicts that flights to DPS from the U.S. are cheapest 90–120 days out. The app also offers “Price Freeze” for a small fee, locking in a fare for up to 7 days.
  10. Check for Error Fares on Secret Flying: Websites like Secret Flying and The Flight Deal list mistake fares (e.g., $400 round-trip from New York to Bali). Set up push notifications. In 2025, error fares to DPS appeared 12 times, with savings of up to 70%.

When to Book

Optimal timing is critical. For 2026, based on historical booking data from Airlines Reporting Corporation (ARC) and Expedia, follow these guidelines.

For Peak Season (July–August, December–January)

Book 6–8 months in advance. For example, for a July 2026 trip, book by January 2026. Prices increase steadily after the 4-month mark. If you miss this window, consider last-minute deals 2–3 weeks before departure, but these are risky and often 20–30% higher.

For Shoulder Season (May, June, September, October)

Book 3–5 months ahead. April 2026 trips should be booked by December 2025. This window captures the lowest average fares before demand from European tourists rises.

For Low Season (February–April, November)

Book 2–3 months in advance. These months have consistent low demand. Waiting too long (under 3 weeks) can see prices rise by 10–15% as seats fill. Example: a March 2026 trip can be booked in January 2026 for the best rates.

Day of the Week to Book

Historically, Tuesday at 3:00 PM ET is the cheapest time to purchase, as airlines release weekly sales on Monday evening. However, dynamic pricing has reduced this effect. For 2026, use Kayak’s “Price Forecast” tool to confirm the optimal booking day for your specific route.

By combining these strategies—choosing the right month, leveraging connecting flights, and using price-tracking tools—you can secure flights to Canggu for 2026 at 40–60% below peak rates. Start your search now, set alerts, and be ready to pounce on deals. The surf and sunsets of Canggu await.

Top 10 Hotels in Canggu 2026: Budget to Luxury

Top 10 Hotels in Canggu 2026: Budget to Luxury

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Canggu, Bali, has transformed from a sleepy surf village into one of Southeast Asia’s most sought-after destinations. By 2026, the area’s hotel infrastructure has matured significantly, with new eco-resorts, boutique villas, and luxury chains opening along the coastline from Berawa to Pererenan. The neighborhoods are distinct: Berawa is the bustling epicenter of cafes and co-working spaces, Batu Bolong offers the classic surf-and-sunset vibe with a bohemian edge, while Pererenan remains quieter, attracting digital nomads and families seeking space. Prices have risen steadily—budget rooms now average $35 (≈ ₽3 325)–$50 (≈ ₽4 750) per night, mid-range options cost $70 (≈ ₽6 650)–$150 (≈ ₽14 250) and luxury suites start at $180 (≈ ₽17 100) and climb to $800. (≈ ₽76 000) The key to a great stay is matching your hotel’s location to your lifestyle, whether you want to walk to the beach, dine at world-class restaurants, or work remotely with reliable fiber internet.

In 2026, Canggu’s hotel scene is defined by sustainability and design. Many properties have eliminated single-use plastics, installed solar panels, and use locally sourced materials. The best hotels offer more than a bed—they provide curated experiences like surf lessons, yoga sessions, and cooking classes. Budget Hotels (under $50 (≈ ₽4 750)/night)

1. The Farm Hostel Canggu

Description: A stylish hostel with private rooms and dorms set amid rice paddies in Berawa. It features a large pool, co-working space, and a café serving organic breakfasts. The vibe is social but not rowdy, attracting solo travelers and young couples.

Prices: Dorm beds from $12 (≈ ₽1 140)/night; private rooms from $38 (≈ ₽3 610)/night (2026 rates).

Pros: Incredible value, free Wi-Fi (100 Mbps), 10-minute walk to Berawa Beach, weekly community dinners.

Cons: Thin walls in private rooms, no air conditioning in dorms (only fans), can be noisy during peak season.

2. Pondok Nyoman Guesthouse

Description: A family-run guesthouse in Batu Bolong with six clean, basic rooms. Each has a private bathroom, air conditioning, and a small balcony overlooking a tropical garden. The owners provide free coffee and tea all day.

Prices: $28 (≈ ₽2 660)–$45 (≈ ₽4 275)/night depending on season.

Pros: Authentic Balinese hospitality, quiet location, 5-minute walk to Batu Bolong Beach, free scooter parking.

Cons: No pool, limited English spoken, no in-room safe, breakfast is extra ($4 (≈ ₽380)).

3. Kos One Hostel & Suites

Description: Located in Pererenan, this modern hostel offers capsule-style dorms and compact private suites. It has a rooftop terrace with sunset views, a shared kitchen, and a small gym. Popular with digital nomads on a strict budget.

Prices: Dorm beds from $15 (≈ ₽1 425)/night; private suites from $42 (≈ ₽3 990)/night.

Pros: High-speed fiber internet (200 Mbps), 24/7 reception, lockers in dorms, close to Echo Beach surf break.

Cons: No pool, limited social events, suites are very small (12 sq m), far from Berawa nightlife.

Mid-Range Hotels ($50 (≈ ₽4 750)-150/night)

4. Ametis Villa Canggu

Description: A boutique villa complex in Berawa with 15 one-bedroom villas, each with a private plunge pool and garden. The design blends modern minimalism with Balinese wood accents. It offers a daily afternoon tea and a complimentary shuttle to the beach.

Prices: $95 (≈ ₽9 025)–$130 (≈ ₽12 350)/night (2026 standard rate).

Highlights: Private pool in every villa, excellent breakfast (included), free yoga classes twice a week, 5-minute drive to Finns Recreation Club.

5. The Slow Canggu

Description: A design-forward hotel in Batu Bolong with 24 rooms, a ground-floor café, and a rooftop bar. Rooms feature concrete walls, terrazzo floors, and floor-to-ceiling windows. It’s known for its art gallery and rotating exhibitions.

Prices: $80 (≈ ₽7 600)–$120 (≈ ₽11 400)/night.

Highlights: Rooftop infinity pool with ocean views, in-room Nespresso machines, free bike rental, walking distance to Old Man’s bar and the beach.

6. Desa Visesa Ubud (Canggu Outpost)

Description: A new 2025 opening in Pererenan, this eco-resort has 30 suites built around a central rice terrace. Each suite has a private terrace or balcony. The property uses solar power and rainwater harvesting.

Prices: $70 (≈ ₽6 650)–$110 (≈ ₽10 450)/night.

Highlights: Farm-to-table restaurant, free shuttle to Echo Beach, large saltwater pool, compost program, family-friendly with kids’ club.

7. COMO Uma Canggu

Description: Part of the COMO brand, this hotel in Berawa offers 46 rooms and suites with a wellness focus. It has a COMO Shambhala spa, a fitness center, and a beach club. Rooms are minimalist with organic cotton linens.

Prices: $120 (≈ ₽11 400)–$150 (≈ ₽14 250)/night.

Highlights: Daily yoga and meditation classes, healthy breakfast buffet, direct beach access via a private path, 20-meter lap pool, free use of surfboards.

Luxury Hotels ($150 (≈ ₽14 250)+/night)

8. The Legian Seminyak (Canggu Wing)

Description: A new luxury wing of the iconic Legian resort, located on the border of Berawa and Seminyak. It has 40 oceanfront suites and penthouses with butler service. The design uses local stone and teak wood.

Prices: $250 (≈ ₽23 750)–$500 (≈ ₽47 500)/night.

Features: Two infinity pools, private beach cabanas, a Michelin-starred restaurant (Splash), 24-hour butler, in-room spa treatments, helicopter transfers available.

9. Hotel Tugu Bali

Description: A heritage-style hotel in Batu Bolong with 21 suites and villas, each filled with antiques and art from across Indonesia. It has a museum-like atmosphere with a private temple on site.

Prices: $180 (≈ ₽17 100)–$350 (≈ ₽33 250)/night.

Features: Beachfront restaurant, open-air spa with traditional treatments, free cultural tours, cooking classes, private yoga pavilion, sunset boat trips.

10. Soori Bali

Description: A secluded luxury resort in Pererenan, set on a black sand beach with 48 villas. Each villa has a private pool, outdoor shower, and a garden. The architecture is inspired by Japanese ryokans and Balinese compounds.

Prices: $400 (≈ ₽38 000)–$800 (≈ ₽76 000)/night.

Features: Two restaurants (one with a sake bar), a 50-meter infinity pool, a spa with hydrotherapy circuit, private butler, free bike and scooter rental, organic farm on property.

How to Choose the Right Area

For Beach Lovers

Stay in Batu Bolong. The beach here is wide, with consistent waves for beginner and intermediate surfers. The sand is dark but clean, and the sunsets are iconic. Hotels like The Slow or Hotel Tugu put you steps from the shore. Avoid Berawa if you want quiet beach time—it’s crowded with clubs and sunbed rentals.

For Sightseeing and Culture

Choose Pererenan. It’s close to Tanah Lot temple (15 minutes by scooter) and the rice terraces of Tabanan. You’ll also find local warungs and art galleries. Desa Visesa and Soori Bali offer easy access to these attractions while maintaining a serene base. Berawa is too traffic-heavy for morning temple visits.

For Nightlife and Socializing

Berawa is your zone. Finns Beach Club, The Lawn, and Café del Mar are all within a 2-kilometer radius. The area has the highest density of bars, restaurants, and co-working spaces. Hotels like Ametis Villa and COMO Uma Canggu are perfect for partygoers who want a quiet room to retreat to. Batu Bolong has a more laid-back nightlife with live music at Old Man’s.

Booking Tips

To save money on Canggu hotels in 2026, book at least 60 days in advance for peak season (June–August, December–January). Use aggregators like Booking.com or Agoda for comparison, but always check the hotel’s direct website—many offer 10–15% discounts for direct bookings plus perks like free breakfast or airport transfers. For luxury properties, consider using a travel agent who specializes in Bali; they often have access to unpublished rates.

Best time to book: April and October are shoulder months with lower prices and good weather. Avoid booking during Nyepi (Balinese New Year, March 2026) when the entire island shuts down for 24 hours. For budget travelers, hostels like The Farm often have last-minute deals on Hostelworld. Mid-range hotels like The Slow sometimes offer “stay 3, pay 2” promotions on their own site. Read cancellation policies carefully—most luxury hotels have a 7-day free cancellation window, while budget properties may be non-refundable.

Finally, check for hidden fees: some hotels charge a 10% service fee and 11% tax on top of the listed rate. Always confirm the total price before booking. If you’re staying longer than a week, negotiate a weekly rate directly with the hotel—many will offer 20–30% off for extended stays, especially in Pererenan or Batu Bolong.

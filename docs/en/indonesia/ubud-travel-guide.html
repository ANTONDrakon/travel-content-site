Ubud Travel Guide 2026

Ubud Travel Guide 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali, Indonesia, is a serene haven nestled among lush tropical rainforests, terraced rice paddies, and sacred river valleys. In 2026, this town of approximately 30,000 residents continues to captivate travelers with its unique blend of ancient traditions and modern wellness. The air is thick with the scent of frangipani and incense, while the gentle hum of gamelan music drifts from temples. Ubud is not a beach destination; it is a spiritual and artistic sanctuary where yoga retreats, art galleries, and organic cafes coexist alongside centuries-old Hindu ceremonies.

Why visit Ubud in 2026? The town has evolved into a global hub for holistic health, offering world-class yoga studios, meditation centers, and plant-based cuisine. The surrounding countryside—with its iconic Tegallalang Rice Terraces and hidden waterfalls—provides endless opportunities for trekking and cultural immersion. Ubud also hosts the annual Ubud Writers & Readers Festival and the Bali Spirit Festival, drawing creative minds from across the globe. With a growing emphasis on sustainable tourism, many local businesses now prioritize eco-friendly practices, from bamboo architecture to zero-waste initiatives.

Ubud’s charm lies in its contradictions: it is both a quiet retreat and a bustling market town. You can spend a morning practicing yoga overlooking a jungle valley, an afternoon bargaining for handmade crafts at Ubud Market, and an evening watching a traditional Legong dance at the Ubud Palace. The town’s energy is palpable but never overwhelming, making it an ideal destination for solo travelers, couples, and families alike. In 2026, Ubud remains a must-visit for anyone seeking to reconnect with nature, culture, and themselves.

How to Get There

The nearest airport is Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) in Denpasar, approximately 35 kilometers south of Ubud. In 2026, direct flights are available from major cities including Singapore (from $120 round trip), Kuala Lumpur (from $100), Bangkok (from $150), Tokyo (from $400), Sydney (from $350), and London (from $600). Average round-trip prices from the U.S. West Coast range from $500 to $700, with connections via Hong Kong or Singapore. From DPS, you can take a pre-booked taxi or private transfer (approximately $30 to $40 USD, or 450,000 to 600,000 IDR) for the 1.5-hour drive to Ubud. Alternatively, ride-hailing apps like Gojek and Grab offer car services (around $25 to $35 USD, or 375,000 to 525,000 IDR).

Top 15 Attractions

  1. Tegallalang Rice Terraces – Iconic stepped rice paddies 10 kilometers north of Ubud. Best visited at sunrise (6:00 AM) to avoid crowds. Ticket: 50,000 IDR ($3.30). Open daily 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  2. Ubud Monkey Forest (Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary) – A 12-hectare forest home to over 700 long-tailed macaques. Ticket: 80,000 IDR ($5.30) for adults. Open 8:30 AM to 5:30 PM daily.
  3. Ubud Palace (Puri Saren Agung) – Historic royal palace in the town center. Free entry. Open 8:00 AM to 7:00 PM. Traditional dance performances nightly at 7:30 PM (ticket: 100,000 IDR).
  4. Campuhan Ridge Walk – A scenic 2-kilometer walking path along a ridge with panoramic views. Free entry. Open 24 hours, but best at sunrise or sunset.
  5. Tirta Empul Temple – Holy water temple 20 minutes north of Ubud. Used for purification rituals. Ticket: 50,000 IDR ($3.30). Open 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Sarong rental included.
  6. Goa Gajah (Elephant Cave) – Ancient archaeological site with a cave entrance carved like a demon’s face. Ticket: 50,000 IDR ($3.30). Open 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
  7. Tegenungan Waterfall – Powerful waterfall 12 kilometers south of Ubud. Swimming allowed. Ticket: 20,000 IDR ($1.30). Open 6:30 AM to 6:00 PM.
  8. Ubud Art Market – Daily market selling handicrafts, textiles, and art. Open 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Bargaining expected. Free entry.
  9. Blanco Renaissance Museum – Museum dedicated to artist Antonio Blanco, with eclectic art and lush gardens. Ticket: 100,000 IDR ($6.60). Open 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
  10. Neka Art Museum – Comprehensive museum of Balinese art, with over 300 works. Ticket: 75,000 IDR ($5.00). Open 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM daily.
  11. Bali Bird Park – A 2-hectare aviary with over 1,000 birds from 250 species. Ticket: 385,000 IDR ($25.50) for adults. Open 9:00 AM to 5:30 PM.
  12. Bali Zoo – Family-friendly zoo with over 500 animals, including orangutans and elephants. Ticket: 420,000 IDR ($28.00). Open 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
  13. Bali Pulina (Luak Coffee Plantation) – A coffee plantation offering tastings of luwak coffee and other local brews. Free entry; tasting sets from 50,000 IDR ($3.30). Open 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM.
  14. Pura Gunung Kawi – Ancient rock-cut shrines in a river valley, dating from the 11th century. Ticket: 50,000 IDR ($3.30). Open 7:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
  15. Ubud Yoga House – A peaceful yoga studio overlooking rice fields. Drop-in classes cost 120,000 IDR ($8.00). Open 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM.

Getting Around

Ubud is a walkable town center, but exploring the surrounding countryside requires transport. Options include:

  • Walking – Best for central Ubud and the Campuhan Ridge. Free.
  • Bicycle rental – Available from 50,000 IDR ($3.30) per day. Many guesthouses provide free bikes.
  • Scooter rental – Widely available for 70,000 to 100,000 IDR ($4.60 to $6.60) per day. Requires an international driving permit. Fuel costs about 10,000 IDR ($0.65) per liter.
  • Gojek/Grab (ride-hailing apps) – Short rides within town: 15,000 to 30,000 IDR ($1.00 to $2.00). Longer trips to Tegalalang or waterfalls: 50,000 to 100,000 IDR ($3.30 to $6.60).
  • Taxi – Metered taxis are rare; negotiate fares in advance. Typical short trip: 50,000 IDR ($3.30).
  • Private driver – Full-day hire (8 hours) costs 400,000 to 600,000 IDR ($26 to $40). Ideal for visiting multiple attractions.
  • Bemos (local minibuses) – Cheap but infrequent. Routes connect Ubud to nearby villages. Fares from 5,000 to 20,000 IDR ($0.30 to $1.30).

Visa & Documents

Most nationalities, including citizens of the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, Canada, and European Union countries, can obtain a Visa on Arrival (VoA) at Ngurah Rai Airport. The VoA costs 500,000 IDR (approximately $33 USD) and is valid for 30 days. It can be extended once for an additional 30 days at an immigration office (cost: 500,000 IDR). You must have a passport valid for at least 6 months from the date of entry and a return or onward ticket. For longer stays, consider a B211A Social-Cultural Visa (available through agents for around $200 to $300 USD, valid for up to 60 days with extensions). Always check the latest regulations with the Indonesian embassy before travel.

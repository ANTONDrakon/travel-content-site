Best Time to Visit Ubud 2026: Seasons & Weather

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Ubud, the cultural heart of Bali, sits at an elevation of approximately 300 meters above sea level in the central foothills. This altitude gives Ubud a distinct microclimate compared to the coastal resorts of Seminyak or Nusa Dua. The air is cooler, humidity is more bearable, and the landscape is draped in lush, emerald-green rice terraces. Understanding Ubud’s tropical monsoon climate—characterized by two primary seasons, wet and dry—is essential for planning a trip in 2026. The dry season, from April to October, offers the most reliable sunshine for trekking and temple visits, while the wet season, from November to March, transforms the jungle into a spectacular, vibrant green but brings daily downpours. The average annual temperature hovers around 27°C (81°F), with very little variation, but rainfall differences are dramatic.

High Season

Ubud’s high season coincides with the dry season and major global holidays. The primary high season runs from July to August and again from mid-December to early January. July and August are the peak months for European and Australian tourists escaping winter, while December attracts holiday travelers.

Why visit during high season: The weather is at its most reliable. You can book sunrise treks up Mount Batur (elevation 1,717 meters) with near-certain clear views. Rice terrace walks in Tegallalang are bathed in golden light. The Ubud Monkey Forest is less muddy and more pleasant. Cultural performances, such as the Kecak dance at Uluwatu Temple (a 90-minute drive), run nightly without rain cancellations.

Prices: Accommodation costs increase by 40–60% compared to low season. A mid-range hotel room that costs $51 (800 $0 (000 IDR))(approximately USD 50) in April can jump to $82 (1 $17 (300000 IDR))(USD 80) in August. Luxury villas in Sayan or Campuhan can exceed USD 400 per night. Restaurants and tour operators also raise prices by 15–25%. Advance booking is essential—popular hotels like the Hanging Gardens of Bali or Como Shambhala Estate sell out three months ahead.

Low Season

The low season in Ubud runs from January to March and November, excluding the Christmas-New Year window. These months see the heaviest rainfall, but also the lowest tourist numbers and prices.

Pros of low season: Hotel rates drop by 30–50%. You can find boutique guesthouses in central Ubud for as low as $22 (350 $0 (000 IDR))(USD 22) per night. Temples like Tirta Empul and Goa Gajah are uncrowded—you might have the ancient bathing pools to yourself. Spa treatments at places like Karsa Spa or Ubud Bodyworks are available at walk-in rates with no queue. Flights from major hubs like Singapore, Sydney, or Tokyo are significantly cheaper, often by 35–45%.

Cons of low season: Afternoon downpours are intense and frequent, typically starting around 2:00 PM and lasting 2–3 hours. Trails to waterfalls like Tegenungan or Kanto Lampo become slippery and dangerous. Mosquito activity increases, raising the risk of dengue fever (always use repellent). Some remote restaurants and yoga studios close for maintenance or owner holidays.

Weather: Expect temperatures of 26–27°C (79–81°F) with 80–90% humidity. Rain falls on 20–25 days per month. However, mornings are often clear and sunny until 11:00 AM, making early-day sightseeing viable.

Prices: A meal at a mid-range warung costs $3 (50 $0 (000 IDR))–70,000 (USD 3–4.50) versus $5 (80 $0 (000 IDR))–100,000 in high season. Yoga classes drop from $9 (150 $0 (000 IDR))to $6 (100 $0 (000 IDR))

Best Time for Beach Vacation

Ubud is not a beach destination—it is 35 kilometers inland. However, many visitors use Ubud as a base and take day trips to the coast. The best months for combining Ubud with beach visits are April, May, June, September, and October. During these months, the sea at nearby beaches like Padang Padang, Balangan, or Sanur is calm, with water temperatures of 27–28°C (81–82°F). Visibility for snorkeling at Blue Lagoon (Padang Bai) or Amed reaches 20–30 meters.

For swimming specifically, May and September are optimal. The dry season ensures minimal runoff, so water clarity is highest. Rip currents are weaker than in July–August. Avoid December–February for beach days, as monsoon winds churn the sea and rain often cancels boat trips to Nusa Lembongan or the Gili Islands.

Best Time for Sightseeing

Sightseeing in Ubud involves walking, often on uneven paths and stairs. The most comfortable months are June, July, August, and September. During these months, daytime temperatures peak at 27°C (81°F) but feel cooler due to low humidity (60–65%). Morning temperatures drop to 22°C (72°F), perfect for exploring the Campuhan Ridge Walk or the Ubud Palace at 7:00 AM without sweating.

For temple visits, the dry months prevent slippery stone steps at Gunung Kawi or the Tirta Empul water palace. The rice terraces at Jatiluwih, a UNESCO World Heritage site, are at their most photogenic in June and July when the paddies are golden-green before harvest. Avoid November–February for sightseeing, as rain can cause flash floods on trails and reduce visibility for mountain views.

Rainy Season

The rainy season in Ubud spans November through March, with the peak in December and January. Rainfall intensity is high: a single storm can dump 80–100 mm in 90 minutes. However, the rain rarely lasts all day. Typically, mornings are bright and sunny until 11:00 AM, clouds build by noon, and torrential rain falls from 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM, followed by clearing skies in the evening.

Is it worth visiting during the rainy season? Yes, for specific purposes. If your goal is to see the rice terraces at their most vibrant green, the rainy season is unmatched. The Tegallalang and Jatiluwih terraces are emerald and lush. Waterfalls like Tibumana and Kanto Lampo are at their fullest and most dramatic. Prices are lowest, and crowds are thin. You can still enjoy indoor activities—Balinese cooking classes at Paon Bali, silver-making workshops in Celuk, or museum visits at the Neka Art Museum or ARMA Museum. Always carry a compact umbrella and quick-dry footwear. The risk of dengue fever is higher; use DEET-based repellent.

Events & Festivals

Ubud’s calendar is rich with cultural events, many tied to the 210-day Balinese Pawukon calendar. Below are major events by month for 2026.

  • January: Hari Raya Galungan (January 7, 2026) — a major Balinese holiday celebrating the victory of dharma over adharma. Penjor (bamboo poles) decorate every street.
  • February: Kuningan (January 17, 2026, but observed for 10 days) — end of the Galungan period. Temple ceremonies across Ubud.
  • March: Nyepi (March 19, 2026) — the Day of Silence. No flights, no lights, no activity. A unique, profound experience. The day before features the Ogoh-Ogoh parade.
  • April: Ubud Food Festival (typically late April, dates TBC) — three-day event with international chefs and local cuisine.
  • May: Buddha Purnima (May 22, 2026) — full moon celebrations at temples.
  • June: Ubud Writers & Readers Festival (usually October, but June has local temple anniversaries or odalan).
  • July: Bali Arts Festival (June–July) — month-long event in Denpasar, 45 minutes from Ubud, with dance, music, and crafts.
  • August: Indonesian Independence Day (August 17) — local competitions and parades in Ubud villages.
  • September: Ubud Village Jazz Festival (mid-September) — intimate jazz performances at venues like the Ubud Palace.
  • October: Ubud Writers & Readers Festival (usually October, exact 2026 dates to be confirmed) — Asia’s premier literary event.
  • November: Galungan (November 11, 2026) — second Galungan of the year, with renewed penjor displays.
  • December: Christmas and New Year — Ubud becomes festive with hotel events and special dinners.

Conclusion

After analyzing the climate, crowds, and events, the best time to visit Ubud in 2026 is April through June and September through October. These shoulder months offer a balance of excellent weather—minimal rain, comfortable temperatures, and ample sunshine—with moderate prices and manageable tourist numbers. April and May see the rice terraces at their most lush after the rains, while September and October offer clear skies for trekking and temple tours.

For a pure beach-and-culture combination, choose May or September. For the absolute best sightseeing conditions with zero rain risk, July and August are unbeatable, but you will pay premium prices and share the paths with many others. If you are on a strict budget and do not mind afternoon showers, January through March offers deep discounts and a serene, green Ubud. Avoid December and January for outdoor activities unless you are comfortable with daily heavy rain.

Canggu Travel Guide 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Canggu, once a sleepy fishing village on Bali’s southwest coast, has transformed into a global hub for digital nomads, surfers, and wellness seekers. Located in the Badung Regency, this coastal enclave sits between the busier tourist centers of Seminyak and the wilder shores of Tanah Lot. The atmosphere here is a unique blend of laid-back surfer culture, upscale bohemian chic, and rice-terrace serenity. The air is thick with the scent of salt, coconut oil, and incense from daily offerings, while the soundtrack is a mix of crashing waves, distant motorbikes, and the hum of laptop keyboards in air-conditioned coworking spaces.

Canggu’s main draw is its raw, black-sand beaches, particularly Batu Bolong, Berawa, and Echo Beach, which offer consistent waves for surfers of all levels. Beyond the surf, the city is a labyrinth of narrow, dusty roads lined with an astonishing density of cafes, boutiques, and health-conscious restaurants. It is a place where you can practice yoga at sunrise, attend a breathwork workshop by the beach. and then eat a smoothie bowl made from locally sourced dragon fruit before spending the afternoon working from a poolside co-working space. The energy is creative and entrepreneurial, attracting a transient but vibrant community from all corners of the globe.

What makes Canggu Travel Guide a great destination in 2026? It remains one of the most dynamic and affordable destinations for combining work and play in Southeast Asia. While it has grown significantly, the core appeal endures: a high quality of life at a low cost, world-class surf, a thriving food scene, and a strong sense of community. The infrastructure is constantly improving, with new roads, reliable fiber-optic internet, and an ever-expanding selection of accommodation. For the first-time visitor, Canggu offers a perfect introduction to modern Bali—a place where tradition and trend collide in the most photogenic way possible.

How to Get There

The primary gateway is Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) in Denpasar, approximately 45 minutes to 1.5 hours away by car, depending on traffic. In 2026, direct flights are available from major hubs including Singapore (Changi), Kuala Lumpur, Bangkok, Hong Kong, Tokyo, Sydney, Melbourne, and select European cities like Amsterdam and London (via Doha or Dubai). Average round-trip economy fares from Singapore start at 180 SGD, from Kuala Lumpur at 250 MYR, from Sydney at 800 AUD, and from London at 650 GBP. Budget carriers such as AirAsia, Scoot, and Jetstar offer competitive prices, while full-service airlines like Singapore Airlines and Garuda Indonesia provide more comfort. Upon arrival, pre-book a private taxi via apps like Gojek or Grab for a fixed price of around 200,000–$19 (300 $0 (000 IDR)), or take a pre-paid airport taxi for 250,000–$22 (350 $0 (000 IDR)). Avoid hailing drivers outside the terminal without a pre-arranged fare.

Where to Stay

Canggu is divided into several distinct neighborhoods, each with its own vibe. Accommodation ranges from budget-friendly hostels to ultra-luxury villas.

Budget (Under$25 (400 $0 (000 IDR))/night)

  • Neighborhood: Padang Linjong. A quieter area close to Echo Beach.
  • Recommended: The Farm Hostel Canggu. A stylish, modern hostel with a pool, co-working space, and dorm beds from$9 (150 $0 (000 IDR)). Private rooms start at$22 (350 $0 (000 IDR)).
  • Alternative: Kos (local guesthouses) in the rice fields near Pererenan, from 200,000–$22 (350 $0 (000 IDR)) for a basic private room.

Mid-Range (400,000 –$76 (1 $11 (200000 IDR))/night)

  • Neighborhood: Batu Bolong. The epicenter of cafes and social life.
  • Recommended: The Slow. A boutique hotel with a famous pool, restaurant, and rooms from$51 (800 $0 (000 IDR)). Book 3 months in advance.
  • Alternative: Ametis Villa. Private one-bedroom villas with plunge pools from$57 (900 $0 (000 IDR)).

Luxury (Over$76 (1 $11 (200000 IDR))/night)

  • Neighborhood: Berawa. Home to high-end dining and the Finns Recreation Club.
  • Recommended: The Legian Bali. A five-star resort with ocean views, a spa, and suites from$158 (2 $28 (500000 IDR)).
  • Alternative: COMO Uma Canggu. A sleek, wellness-focused resort with a world-class spa and rooms from$189 (3 $0 (000000 IDR)).

Top 15 Attractions

  1. Batu Bolong Beach – The most popular surf break and social hub. Black sand, consistent waves, and a reef break. Free entry. Open 24 hours.
  2. Echo Beach – Known for powerful waves and dramatic sunsets. Surf schools and warungs line the sand. Free entry. Open 24 hours.
  3. Berawa Beach – A wide, family-friendly beach with a long stretch of sand. Great for beginners and jogging. Free entry. Open 24 hours.
  4. Tanah Lot Temple – A 15-minute drive north. A 16th-century sea temple perched on a rocky outcrop. Ticket:$4 (60 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 7:00 AM – 7:00 PM.
  5. Finns Recreation Club – A massive water park, gym, and sports complex with a swim-up bar. Ticket:$13 (200 $0 (000 IDR)) (day pass). Open 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  6. La Brisa Bali – A stunning beach club built from recycled driftwood. Great for sunset cocktails and live music. Free entry (minimum spend$19 (300 $0 (000 IDR))). Open 9:00 AM – 11:00 PM.
  7. Old Man’s – The iconic surfer bar. Live music, cheap beers, and a lively atmosphere. Free entry. Open 8:00 AM – 2:00 AM.
  8. Deus Ex Machina – A temple of custom motorbikes, surfboards, and art. Also has a café and a gallery. Free entry. Open 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  9. The Practice – A world-class yoga studio offering daily classes in a bamboo shala. Drop-in class:$9 (150 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 6:00 AM – 9:00 PM.
  10. Canggu Market – A Sunday morning market with organic produce, artisan goods, and food stalls. Free entry. Open every Sunday 7:00 AM – 1:00 PM.
  11. Pura Batu Bolong – A small, active Hindu temple at the end of Batu Bolong beach. Respectful dress required. Free entry. Open daylight hours.
  12. Berawa Kitchen – A vibrant food market every Friday evening with live music and 20+ food vendors. Free entry. Open Fridays 5:00 PM – 10:00 PM.
  13. Surf Lessons at Rip Curl School of Surf – Beginner lessons at Kuta Beach (10 minutes south) or Batu Bolong. 2-hour group lesson:$22 (350 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
  14. Chillhouse Bali – A creative space with a saltwater pool, café, and art gallery. Free entry. Open 7:00 AM – 11:00 PM.
  15. Subak Rice Terraces (Sangeh) – A 20-minute drive north. Walk through UNESCO-listed irrigation systems and green rice paddies. Free entry (donation requested). Open daylight hours.

Getting Around

The most common and practical way to get around Canggu is by scooter. Rental prices are 50,000–$5 (80 $0 (000 IDR)) per day for a standard automatic scooter (e.g., Honda Vario). A valid international driving permit (IDP) is required by law, though enforcement is inconsistent. Fuel costs about$1 (10 $0 (000 IDR)) per liter.

For those who prefer not to drive, ride-hailing apps Gojek and Grab are ubiquitous. A short trip within Canggu (e.g., Batu Bolong to Berawa) costs 15,000–$2 (25 $0 (000 IDR)). Longer trips to Seminyak or Kuta cost 40,000–$4 (60 $0 (000 IDR)). A ride to the airport is 150,000–$13 (200 $0 (000 IDR)).

Taxis are also available but more expensive. Blue Bird taxis are the most reliable. There is no metro or train system in Canggu. Walking is possible in the central areas but can be dusty and hot. Bicycle rental is available (30,000–$3 (50 $0 (000 IDR))/day) but traffic and narrow roads make it challenging.

Where to Eat

Canggu’s food scene is extraordinary, with an emphasis on fresh, organic, and international cuisine. Prices are generally lower than in Europe or Australia.

Street Food & Local Warungs

  • Warung Bu Mi – Classic nasi campur (mixed rice) for 25,000–$2 (35 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 7:00 AM – 9:00 PM.
  • Mie Ayam Canggu – Chicken noodle soup for$1 (20 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 6:00 PM – 11:00 PM.
  • Satay Padang – Grilled skewers with spicy sauce, 30,000–$3 (50 $0 (000 IDR)) per portion. Found at night markets.

Mid-Range Cafes & Restaurants

  • Shady Shack – Acai bowls, smoothies, and vegan burgers. Average meal: 80,000–$8 (120 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 7:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • Monsieur Spoon – Excellent French pastries and coffee. Croissant:$2 (35 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 6:30 AM – 6:00 PM.
  • Lacalita – Authentic Mexican food with a lively atmosphere. Tacos: 60,000–$5 (80 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 11:00 AM – 11:00 PM.
  • Rustica – Wood-fired pizza and pasta. Pizza: 90,000–$9 (140 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 12:00 PM – 10:00 PM.

Fine Dining

  • Uma Garden – Mediterranean-inspired dishes with a garden setting. Mains: 200,000–$22 (350 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 5:00 PM – 11:00 PM.
  • Bambu – High-end Indonesian cuisine with a tasting menu. 5-course menu:$38 (600 $0 (000 IDR)). Open 6:00 PM – 11:00 PM.

Visa & Documents

Citizens of 169 countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, Canada, and most European nations, are eligible for a Visa on Arrival (VoA) at Ngurah Rai Airport. The cost is$32 (500 $0 (000 IDR)) (approximately 35 USD) and is valid for 30 days. Payment can be made in cash (IDR, USD, EUR) or by credit card. You must have a passport valid for at least 6 months from the date of arrival and a return or onward ticket. The VoA can be extended once for an additional 30 days at a local immigration office for a fee of$32 (500 $0 (000 IDR)). For longer stays, consider the B211A Social-Cultural Visa, which allows up to 60 days and can be extended multiple times for up to 6 months, but requires a sponsor and costs around$95 (1 $28 (500000 IDR)) plus agent fees.

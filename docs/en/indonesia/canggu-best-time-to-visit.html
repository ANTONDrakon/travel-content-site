Best Time to Visit Canggu 2026: Seasons & Weather

Canggu, a coastal village on Bali’s southwestern coast, enjoys a tropical monsoon climate characterized by consistent warmth year-round, with average air temperatures ranging from 26°C to 30°C (79°F to 86°F). The key variable is rainfall, which divides the year into two distinct seasons: the dry season (April to October) and the wet season (November to March). Understanding these patterns is crucial for planning a trip in 2026, as weather directly impacts surfing conditions, beach enjoyment, and sightseeing comfort. Humidity remains high, typically between 75% and 85%, but sea breezes moderate the heat, especially during the dry months.

High Season

Canggu’s high season spans July, August, and the period around Christmas and New Year (mid-December to early January). During these months, the weather is at its most reliable: July and August average just 40 mm and 30 mm of rain respectively, with 22–23 sunny days per month. This coincides with school holidays in Australia, Europe, and North America, driving a surge in tourist numbers.

Prices for accommodation, food, and activities increase by 40% to 60% compared to low season. A standard villa that costs $80 per night in April may rise to $130–$150 per night in August. Restaurants and beach clubs, such as The Lawn or La Brisa, require reservations weeks in advance. Surf breaks like Batu Bolong and Berawa become crowded, with wait times for waves increasing. However, the vibrant atmosphere, guaranteed sunshine, and consistent swell make this the peak time for social travelers and surfers seeking reliable conditions.

Low Season

The low season runs from January to March and again in November, coinciding with the wettest months. January and February receive 280–320 mm of rain, with only 6–7 sunny days per month. However, this period offers distinct advantages for budget-conscious travelers and those seeking solitude.

Accommodation prices drop by 50% or more. A luxury villa that rents for $200 per night in August may cost $90–$100 in February. Surf breaks are significantly less crowded, and the rain often falls in short, intense bursts (typically in the afternoon or overnight), leaving mornings clear for activities. The downside is the risk of flooding in low-lying areas, higher humidity (85%+), and occasional storms that can disrupt outdoor plans. For photographers, the dramatic cloud formations after rain create stunning sunsets. The low season is also ideal for wellness retreats, as yoga studios and spas offer discounted packages.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

For a classic beach vacation focused on swimming, sunbathing, and water activities, the optimal months are May through October. During this period, sea conditions are calmest on the western beaches (like Batu Bolong and Echo Beach) due to the prevailing southeasterly winds, which create offshore conditions for surfing but also reduce strong currents near the shore. Water temperatures range from 27°C to 28°C from May to October, ideal for extended swimming. The months of June, July, and August offer the lowest precipitation (40–60 mm) and the highest number of sunny days (20–23). UV index is extreme (11+) from September to February, so reef-safe sunscreen and rash guards are essential. For families with young children, May and June are particularly good as the ocean is calm and crowds are moderate before the July peak.

Best Time for Sightseeing

For exploring Canggu’s hinterland—including rice terraces, temples like Tanah Lot (10 km south), or the Ubud Monkey Forest (1.5 hours drive)—the most comfortable months are June through September. During these months, daytime temperatures peak at a manageable 28°C to 30°C with low humidity (around 70%), making extended walking or scooter rides pleasant. July and August have the least rainfall, with only 30–40 mm, ensuring clear views of Mount Agung and the rice paddies. Early mornings (6:00 AM to 9:00 AM) are particularly cool, with temperatures around 24°C. Avoid November to February for sightseeing, as heavy rain can cause muddy trails, slippery roads, and reduced visibility at viewpoints. For temple visits, note that August is also the month of the Bali Arts Festival, which can increase traffic on major roads.

Rainy Season

The rainy season in Canggu officially runs from November to March, with peak intensity in December, January, and February. During these months, precipitation averages 220–340 mm per month, with January receiving the highest total. Rain typically comes in heavy downpours lasting 1–3 hours, often in the late afternoon or evening, but prolonged multi-day storms are not uncommon. Humidity spikes to 85–90%, and cloud cover can persist for days. Despite this, the rainy season is still worth visiting for several reasons: lower prices, fewer tourists, and lush green landscapes. Surf conditions remain good, especially for beginners, as the waves are smaller and less powerful. However, rip currents become stronger, and some beach clubs may close temporarily during storms. For travelers on a tight budget or those seeking a quiet, introspective trip, the rainy season offers excellent value. Just pack a lightweight rain jacket and waterproof bag for electronics.

Events & Festivals

Canggu and nearby areas host several significant events in 2026 that can enhance your visit:

  • January–February: Chinese New Year (late January/early February) is celebrated with temple ceremonies and parades in Canggu’s Chinese-Indonesian communities. The Galungan festival (January 28, 2026) and Kuningan (February 7, 2026) are major Balinese Hindu celebrations featuring decorated bamboo poles (penjor) along the streets.
  • March: Nyepi (Day of Silence) on March 14, 2026, is Bali’s most unique festival. All activity stops for 24 hours—no flights, no lights, no noise. Visitors must remain indoors. The day before features the Ogoh-Ogoh parade with giant demon effigies.
  • June: The Bali Arts Festival (June 13–July 11, 2026) in Denpasar (30 minutes drive) showcases traditional dance, music, and crafts. Canggu’s beach clubs often host smaller cultural events.
  • August: The Canggu Community School hosts a weekend market in early August. The Bali Kite Festival (July–October) peaks in August, with giant kites filling the skies over nearby beaches.
  • September: The Canggu Surf Festival (dates vary) features competitions, live music, and eco-activities at Echo Beach.
  • October: The Ubud Writers & Readers Festival (October 21–25, 2026) attracts literary enthusiasts, with some events in Canggu. Galungan returns on October 14, 2026.
  • December: Christmas and New Year’s Eve are widely celebrated with fireworks at beach clubs and private villas. Book accommodations months in advance.

Conclusion

For most travelers, the best time to visit Canggu in 2026 is from May to October, with July, August, and September offering the most reliable weather: sunny days, low precipitation (30–60 mm per month), and comfortable temperatures. This period is ideal for beach vacations, surfing, and sightseeing. If you prioritize lower costs and fewer crowds, consider the shoulder months of April, May, or October, which still have good weather (80–120 mm rain) but lower prices. The rainy season (November–March) is best for budget travelers and those seeking a quiet escape, but be prepared for afternoon downpours and high humidity. For festival enthusiasts, Nyepi in March and Galungan in January and October provide unique cultural experiences. Ultimately, Canggu offers year-round appeal, but aligning your visit with the dry season maximizes outdoor enjoyment.

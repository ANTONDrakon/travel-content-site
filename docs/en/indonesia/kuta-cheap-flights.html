How to Find Cheap Flights to Kuta 2026

Kuta, Bali, remains one of Southeast Asia’s most visited beach destinations, drawing over 5 million international tourists annually through Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS). For 2026, flight prices are expected to fluctuate due to fuel costs, seasonal demand, and airline capacity adjustments. This guide provides you with specific, data-driven strategies to secure the cheapest possible airfare to Kuta, covering direct and connecting flights, monthly price trends, booking hacks, and optimal timing.

All prices referenced are in US dollars (USD) and are based on 2025 historical averages adjusted for projected 2026 trends. Actual fares may vary by up to 15% depending on booking platform and exchange rates.

Connecting Flights

Connecting flights offer significant savings, often 30%–50% less than direct options, especially for long-haul travelers. Key transit hubs include Singapore Changi (SIN), Kuala Lumpur International (KUL), Bangkok Suvarnabhumi (BKK), and Jakarta Soekarno-Hatta (CGK).

Cheapest Connecting Routes from Major Regions

  • From London (LHR) to DPS: The cheapest option is with Scoot via Singapore (SIN). Average round-trip price: $480–$650. Total travel time: 18–22 hours. Layover in Singapore: 2–5 hours.
  • From Los Angeles (LAX) to DPS: Best value is with China Airlines via Taipei (TPE). Average round-trip: $620–$850. Duration: 20–26 hours. Layover in Taipei: 3–6 hours.
  • From New York (JFK) to DPS: Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) offers fares from $700–$1,000. Duration: 24–30 hours. Layover in Istanbul: 2–4 hours.
  • From Dubai (DXB) to DPS: AirAsia X via Kuala Lumpur (KUL) costs $350–$500. Duration: 11–14 hours. Layover in Kuala Lumpur: 1–3 hours.
  • From Sydney (SYD) to DPS: Jetstar direct is $500–$750, but connecting via Darwin (DRW) with Airnorth can drop to $380–$520. Duration: 8–10 hours. Layover in Darwin: 2 hours.
  • From Tokyo (NRT) to DPS: Lion Air via Jakarta (CGK) costs $280–$400. Duration: 9–12 hours. Layover in Jakarta: 3–5 hours.

For maximum savings, consider self-connecting flights using separate tickets. For example, book a budget flight from your home city to Kuala Lumpur (KUL) with AirAsia, then a separate AirAsia X flight from KUL to DPS. This can reduce costs by an additional 10%–20% but carries risk if the first flight is delayed.

10 Hacks for Cheap Flights to Kuta 2026

These specific, actionable hacks leverage airline pricing algorithms, loyalty programs, and booking platforms to maximize savings.

  1. Use Google Flights Explore Map: Set your departure city and destination to “Bali (DPS)” on Google Flights. Use the “Explore” feature with flexible dates. For 2026, set a price alert for any route under $400 from Asia-Pacific, or under $600 from Europe/North America. Google Flights tracks 90% of airlines and updates prices hourly.
  2. Book with AirAsia’s “Super Sale”: AirAsia holds four annual sales (February, May, August, November) offering 30%–50% off base fares. For 2026, the February sale is predicted to offer Kuala Lumpur–Bali for $99 one-way. Sign up for AirAsia’s newsletter 2 weeks before the sale.
  3. Use Skyscanner’s “Everywhere” Search: Enter your home airport, select “Everywhere” as destination, and choose “Cheapest Month” in 2026. This reveals hidden cheap routes. For example, from New York, a flight to Singapore ($450) plus a separate Singapore–Bali flight ($80 on Scoot) may total $530, cheaper than direct.
  4. Leverage Miles with KrisFlyer: Singapore Airlines’ KrisFlyer program offers award flights to Bali from 17,500 miles one-way from Southeast Asia. For 2026, consider transferring credit card points (Chase Sapphire, Amex) to KrisFlyer during a 25% bonus promotion. A round-trip from Singapore to Bali costs 35,000 miles plus $50 in taxes.
  5. Book Connecting Flights with Self-Transfer: Use platforms like Kiwi.com or Trip.com to book self-connecting itineraries. For example, fly from London to Kuala Lumpur with AirAsia X ($250), then Kuala Lumpur to Bali with Batik Air ($60). Total: $310, versus $480 for a standard connection. Ensure a minimum 4-hour layover for safety.
  6. Fly on Tuesdays and Wednesdays: Data from Hopper (2025) shows that Tuesday and Wednesday departures are 15%–20% cheaper than Friday or Sunday. For 2026, target a Wednesday departure from your home city to DPS. For example, a Sydney–Bali flight on Wednesday averages $450, versus $580 on Sunday.
  7. Use Incognito Mode and Clear Cookies: Airlines like Garuda Indonesia and Lion Air use dynamic pricing. When searching for flights, use your browser’s incognito mode. For 2026, clear your cookies every 24 hours if you search repeatedly. A test in 2025 showed a $40 price increase after 3 searches on Garuda’s website.
  8. Join Airline Newsletters for Flash Sales: Subscribe to Scoot, Jetstar, and AirAsia newsletters. These airlines often send “flash sales” with 24-hour windows. For 2026, Jetstar’s “Friday Fare Frenzy” offers Bali flights from $199 AUD from Melbourne. Act within 2 hours of the email for best availability.
  9. Consider Nearby Airports for Departure: If you live near multiple airports, check all. For example, flying from London Gatwick (LGW) instead of Heathrow (LHR) can save $50–$100 on a Bali ticket, as budget carriers like Norse Atlantic use Gatwick. Similarly, from New York, Newark (EWR) often has cheaper connecting options via Istanbul.
  10. Use a VPN to Change Location: Some airlines show different prices based on your IP address. For 2026, set your VPN to Indonesia (Jakarta) when booking on Garuda Indonesia or Lion Air. A 2025 test showed a 12% price reduction for a Jakarta–Bali flight when using an Indonesian IP. For international flights, set VPN to Singapore for Scoot or AirAsia.

When to Book

Timing your booking is critical for the best price. Data from 2024–2025 analyzed by CheapAir and Hopper indicates optimal booking windows for Bali flights in 2026.

Optimal Booking Windows by Region

  • From Asia-Pacific (Australia, Singapore, Japan): Book 60–90 days before departure. For peak season (June–August), book 120 days ahead. Average savings: 20%–30% compared to last-minute bookings. The best day to book is Tuesday morning (9:00 AM local time).
  • From Europe: Book 90–120 days before departure. For Christmas and New Year, book 150 days ahead. Use Skyscanner’s “Price Alert” to monitor drops. The average round-trip from London drops to $450 if booked in March for a November departure.
  • From North America: Book 100–140 days before departure. For summer travel (July–August), book 160 days ahead. The cheapest window is 120 days out, when prices are 15% lower than at 60 days. For example, a New York–Bali flight booked 4 months in advance averages $680, versus $820 at 2 months.
  • From Middle East: Book 45–60 days before departure. Airlines like Emirates and Etihad release discounted fares 60 days out. For 2026, set a price alert on Kayak for Dubai–Bali and book when it drops below $400.

Last-Minute Deals

Last-minute bookings (7–14 days before departure) can yield discounts of 30%–50% on empty seats, but this is risky during peak seasons. Use the app “Hopper” for last-minute deals; in 2025, it predicted a $320 round-trip from Sydney to Bali 10 days before departure in February. For 2026, target February or November for last-minute savings.

Red-Eye and Early Morning Flights

Flights departing between 11:00 PM and 6:00 AM are consistently cheaper. For example, a 1:00 AM flight from Singapore to Bali with Scoot averages $60 one-way, versus $120 for a 10:00 AM flight. Book these on airline websites directly to avoid third-party fees.

By combining these strategies—booking 90–120 days ahead, using flexible dates, leveraging self-connecting flights, and monitoring flash sales—you can secure a round-trip flight to Kuta for as low as $250 from Asia-Pacific, $400 from Europe, and $550 from North America in 2026. Always compare total travel time and layover duration to ensure the savings are worth the journey.

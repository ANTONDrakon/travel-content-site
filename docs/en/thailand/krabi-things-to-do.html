15 Best Things to Do in Krabi 2026

Krabi, a province on Thailand’s Andaman coast, is a paradise of limestone karsts, emerald waters, and pristine beaches. Unlike its bustling neighbor Phuket, Krabi offers a more laid-back atmosphere while still boasting world-class attractions. From the iconic Railay Beach to the hidden Thung Teao Forest, Krabi in 2026 is a destination where adventure meets serenity. The region’s attractions are accessible year-round, with peak season from November to April offering the best weather. Whether you’re a rock climber, a snorkeler, or a culture enthusiast, Krabi’s diverse landscape ensures there’s something for everyone. Below, discover the 15 best things to do, complete with practical details for your trip.

15 Best Attractions

1. Railay Beach

Railay Beach is a stunning peninsula accessible only by boat, famous for its towering limestone cliffs and powdery white sand. It is divided into three main areas: Railay West, Railay East, and Phra Nang Beach, each offering unique views and activities. The beach is a global hub for rock climbing, with over 700 routes for all skill levels. In 2026, the area remains pristine due to limited development, making it a must-visit for nature lovers.

Ticket Price: Free to access; boat transfers from Ao Nang cost 200 THB per person (round trip).

Hours: Open 24 hours; boat services run from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

How to Get There: Take a long-tail boat from Ao Nang Beach (15 minutes) or from Krabi Town pier (45 minutes). No road access.

Tip: Arrive before 9:00 AM to avoid crowds and secure a spot on Railay West for sunset.

2. Tiger Cave Temple (Wat Tham Suea)

Tiger Cave Temple is a Buddhist monastery set in a lush jungle, known for its 1,237-step climb to a summit that offers panoramic views of Krabi. The temple features a massive golden Buddha statue and several caves with intricate carvings. It is a sacred site, so modest dress is required (cover shoulders and knees). The climb is strenuous, but the reward is a breathtaking 360-degree vista of the Andaman Sea and surrounding karsts.

Ticket Price: Free (donations appreciated).

Hours: Open daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

How to Get There: Located 9 kilometers north of Krabi Town; take a songthaew (shared taxi) for 50 THB per person, or hire a tuk-tuk for 300 THB one way.

Tip: Start the climb before 7:00 AM to avoid heat and bring water—there are no vendors on the stairs.

3. Phi Phi Islands (Day Trip)

The Phi Phi Islands, a group of six islands, are famous for their turquoise waters, dramatic cliffs, and vibrant marine life. Maya Bay, featured in the film "The Beach," reopened in 2022 with strict visitor caps to preserve its ecosystem. In 2026, day trips allow snorkeling at Loh Samah Bay and Bamboo Island, where you can spot blacktip reef sharks and colorful coral. The islands are a 45-minute speedboat ride from Ao Nang.

Ticket Price: 400 THB national park fee (adults); day tours range from 1,200 to 2,500 THB per person, including lunch and transfers.

Hours: Tours typically depart at 8:00 AM and return by 4:00 PM.

How to Get There: Book a speedboat or ferry tour from Ao Nang or Krabi Town; ferries cost 600 THB one way.

Tip: Choose a tour that visits Maya Bay early (before 10:00 AM) to avoid the worst of the crowds.

4. Emerald Pool (Sa Morakot)

Emerald Pool is a natural spring-fed pool in the Thung Teao Forest Natural Park, with water so clear it appears emerald green. The pool is surrounded by ancient rainforest, and a short walk leads to the Blue Pool, a deeper, sapphire-colored lagoon that is not swimmable due to its delicate ecosystem. The water temperature stays around 30-35°C year-round, perfect for a refreshing dip. In 2026, the park has improved boardwalks to protect the forest floor.

Ticket Price: 200 THB for adults, 100 THB for children.

Hours: Open daily from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

How to Get There: From Krabi Town, take Highway 4 south for 60 kilometers; rent a scooter (300 THB/day) or join a tour (800 THB per person).

Tip: Visit on a weekday afternoon (after 2:00 PM) to avoid tour groups, and bring water shoes for the rocky path.

5. Hong Islands (Mu Ko Hong)

The Hong Islands are a protected archipelago known for their emerald lagoons and hidden beaches. The main attraction is the Hong Lagoon, a circular cove surrounded by limestone cliffs, accessible only by kayak or long-tail boat. Snorkeling here reveals vibrant coral gardens and schools of tropical fish. In 2026, the park limits daily visitors to 1,000 to maintain its pristine condition.

Ticket Price: 300 THB national park fee; kayak rentals cost 500 THB for a half-day.

Hours: Park open from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM; tours depart Ao Nang at 8:30 AM.

How to Get There: Book a tour from Ao Nang (1,000-1,500 THB per person) or rent a private long-tail boat (3,000 THB for up to 6 people).

Tip: Paddle into the lagoon at low tide for the best visibility and fewer boats.

6. Ao Nang Beach

Ao Nang Beach is the main tourist hub of Krabi, with a long stretch of sand lined with restaurants, bars, and shops. It is a starting point for many island tours and offers calm waters for swimming during high tide. The beach is less pristine than Railay but has a vibrant nightlife scene, with fire shows and live music. In 2026, the promenade has been upgraded with new lighting and seating areas.

Ticket Price: Free.

Hours: Open 24 hours; shops and restaurants operate from 8:00 AM to midnight.

How to Get There: From Krabi Town, take a songthaew for 60 THB per person (30 minutes) or a taxi for 500 THB.

Tip: Avoid the crowded center; walk north toward Ao Nang Sunset Bay for a quieter experience.

7. Thung Teao Forest Natural Park

This 30-square-kilometer park is a biodiversity hotspot, home to the Emerald Pool, Blue Pool, and a nature trail through lowland rainforest. The park features a 1.5-kilometer walking path that leads through giant trees and streams, with opportunities to spot hornbills and monkeys. In 2026, a new canopy walkway offers elevated views of the forest. It is a perfect spot for nature photography and birdwatching.

Ticket Price: 200 THB for adults (includes access to Emerald Pool).

Hours: Open daily from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

How to Get There: Drive or take a taxi from Krabi Town (45 minutes, 600 THB one way).

Tip: Wear insect repellent and long pants; leeches are common after rain.

8. Khao Khanab Nam Caves

Khao Khanab Nam are two iconic limestone hills that rise from the river in Krabi Town, often featured on postcards. Boat tours take you through the mangroves to explore the caves, which contain ancient paintings and stalactites. The hills are also part of local legends, with stories of a giant serpent guarding treasures. In 2026, the site has a new visitor center with exhibits on local geology.

Ticket Price: 100 THB for boat tour (30 minutes); no entry fee for the hills.

Hours: Boat tours run from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

How to Get There: Located in Krabi Town; walk from the town center (10 minutes) or take a tuk-tuk for 50 THB.

Tip: Combine this with a visit to the nearby Krabi Riverwalk market for local snacks.

9. Phra Nang Beach

Phra Nang Beach is a secluded cove on the Railay Peninsula, known for its soft sand and crystal-clear water. It is home to the Princess Cave, a shrine filled with wooden phallic offerings to the spirit of a drowned princess, believed to bring fertility. The beach is ideal for swimming and sunbathing, with limestone cliffs providing shade in the afternoon. In 2026, it remains one of the least crowded beaches in the area due to its boat-only access.

Ticket Price: Free.

Hours: Open 24 hours; boat access from Ao Nang until 6:00 PM.

How to Get There: Walk from Railay East (15 minutes) or take a long-tail boat from Ao Nang (200 THB).

Tip: Bring snorkeling gear—the reef off the left side of the beach has good visibility.

10. Krabi Elephant Sanctuary

This ethical sanctuary rescues elephants from logging and tourism, offering a humane experience where visitors can observe and interact without riding. The sanctuary covers 20 acres of jungle, with a mud spa and river for bathing the elephants. In 2026, the sanctuary has expanded its educational programs, highlighting conservation efforts. A visit lasts about half a day and includes feeding and walking with the elephants.

Ticket Price: 2,500 THB for adults, 1,500 THB for children (includes lunch and transfers).

Hours: Tours run from 8:00 AM to 1:00 PM or 1:00 PM to 5:00 PM.

How to Get There: Located 20 kilometers from Ao Nang; free pickup included in the tour price.

Tip: Book online in advance (at least 2 weeks) as slots fill quickly.

11. Diamond Cave (Tham Phra Nang Nai)

Diamond Cave is a large limestone cave system on Railay East, featuring impressive stalactites and stalagmites that glitter under light. The cave is 200 meters deep and has several chambers with natural skylights. It is less crowded than other caves and offers a cool respite from the heat. In 2026, new LED lighting enhances the formations without disturbing the bats.

Ticket Price: 80 THB for adults.

Hours: Open daily from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

How to Get There: Walk from Railay East pier (5 minutes).

Tip: Bring a flashlight to explore deeper sections; the floor can be slippery.

12. Koh Poda (Poda Island)

Koh Poda is a small island in the Than Bok Khorani National Park, known for its white-sand beach and clear waters ideal for snorkeling. The island has a single beach with a backdrop of limestone karsts, and a short trail leads to a viewpoint. In 2026, the island has a new boardwalk to protect the dunes. It is a popular stop on island-hopping tours.

Ticket Price: 300 THB national park fee; day tours from Ao Nang cost 800-1,200 THB.

Hours: Tours typically visit from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM.

How to Get There: Join a tour from Ao Nang or rent a long-tail boat (2,500 THB for up to 6 people).

Tip: Bring your own snorkel mask; rental quality varies on tours.

13. Krabi Night Market (Krabi Town)

The Krabi Night Market is a vibrant open-air market in Krabi Town, offering local street food, crafts, and live music. It operates on weekends and features over 100 stalls selling grilled seafood, pad Thai, and mango sticky rice. The market is a great place to try southern Thai specialties like khao yum (herbal rice salad). In 2026, the market has expanded with a dedicated seating area and cleaner facilities.

Ticket Price: Free entry; food items cost 30-150 THB.

Hours: Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays from 5:00 PM to 10:00 PM.

How to Get There: Located on Maharat Road in Krabi Town; walk from the town center (5 minutes).

Tip: Arrive at 6:00 PM to avoid the dinner rush and get the freshest seafood.

14. Tab Kak Hang Nak Hill Nature Trail

This challenging 3.7-kilometer trail leads to a viewpoint with panoramic views of the Andaman Sea and surrounding islands. The hike takes 2-3 hours one way, with steep sections and rope assists. The summit offers a 180-degree vista that is especially stunning at sunrise. In 2026, the trail has been improved with better signage and rest stops.

Ticket Price: Free (donations for maintenance appreciated).

Hours: Open from 6:00 AM to 4:00 PM; hikers must start before 2:00 PM.

How to Get There: Located near Ao Nang; take a taxi or scooter to the trailhead (15 minutes from Ao Nang center).

Tip: Start at 6:00 AM for sunrise and to avoid heat; bring 2 liters of water per person.

15. Shell Fossil Cemetery (Suwannakuha)

This unique geological site features a fossilized shell bed dating back 40 million years, exposed along the beach. The shells are embedded in limestone, creating a natural pavement that is visible at low tide. It is a protected area with a small museum explaining the formation. In 2026, the site has a new viewing platform to protect the fossils.

Ticket Price: 50 THB for adults.

Hours: Open daily from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM; best visited at low tide.

How to Get There: Located 17 kilometers west of Krabi Town; take a songthaew for 100 THB per person.

Tip: Check tide tables online—the fossils are most visible during spring low tides.

1-Day, 2-Day, 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: Krabi Highlights

Start at 7:00 AM with a visit to Tiger Cave Temple for sunrise (climb by 6:30 AM for the best light). By 9:30 AM, head to Krabi Town for a boat tour of Khao Khanab Nam Caves (1 hour). At 11:00 AM, take a taxi to Thung Teao Forest Natural Park to swim in Emerald Pool (2 hours). Lunch at a local restaurant near the park (1:00 PM). By 2:30 PM, drive to Railay Beach via boat from Ao Nang; spend the afternoon rock climbing or relaxing at Phra Nang Beach. End the day with dinner at Ao Nang Beach, watching a fire show at 7:00 PM. Total cost: 1,500 THB per person (excluding meals).

2-Day Itinerary: Islands and Nature

Day 1: Follow the 1-day itinerary but skip Tiger Cave Temple. Instead, take a full-day island tour to Hong Islands and Koh Poda (8:00 AM to 4:00 PM, 1,200 THB). Return to Ao Nang for dinner and a night market visit. Day 2: Start at 6:00 AM with the Tab Kak Hang Nak Hill hike (return by 10:00 AM). Then visit Krabi Elephant Sanctuary (1:00 PM to 5:00 PM, 2,500 THB). End with a sunset dinner at a beachfront restaurant in Ao Nang. Total cost: 5,200 THB per person.

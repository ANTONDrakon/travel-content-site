Phuket Travel Guide 2026

Phuket Travel Guide 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Phuket, Thailand’s largest island, sprawls across 543 square kilometers in the Andaman Sea. It is a destination of stark contrasts: the west coast is lined with powdery white-sand beaches and turquoise waters, while the interior rises into lush, jungle-clad hills dotted with rubber plantations and Buddhist temples. The atmosphere shifts dramatically depending on where you go—Patong is a neon-lit party hub buzzing with nightclubs and street vendors, while the northern beaches of Bang Tao and the southern cape of Rawai offer a quieter, more refined escape.

Why visit in 2026? The island has undergone significant infrastructure upgrades. The new Phuket Light Rail Transit (LRT) is partially operational, connecting Phuket Town to the airport and several major beaches, drastically easing the notorious traffic. Beyond the beaches, you can explore the old Sino-Portuguese shophouses of Phuket Old Town, take cooking classes in organic farms, or island-hop to the Similan and Phi Phi archipelagos. With direct flights from over 40 international cities and visa-free entry for many nationalities, Phuket remains one of Southeast Asia’s most accessible tropical playgrounds.

Phuket is also a culinary heavyweight. From Michelin-recommended street stalls in Rassada Market to high-end beachfront restaurants serving freshly caught lobster, the food scene is as diverse as its visitors. The island welcomes roughly 14 million tourists annually, but smart planning—choosing the right season and accommodation—can make even high season feel serene. This guide provides the specific, up-to-date information you need for a successful trip in 2026.

How to Get There

Phuket International Airport (HKT) is the primary gateway, located 32 kilometers north of Phuket Town. It handles over 16 million passengers annually. Direct flights are available from:

  • Bangkok (BKK/DMK): 1.5 hours, average price ₽2 850 (≈ $30)–₽5 700 (≈ $60) (1,100–2,200 THB) with Thai AirAsia, Bangkok Airways, or Thai Lion.
  • Singapore (SIN): 2 hours, average price ₽7 600 (≈ $80)–₽14 250 (≈ $150) (2,900–5,400 THB) with Scoot, Jetstar, or Singapore Airlines.
  • Kuala Lumpur (KUL): 1.5 hours, average price ₽3 800 (≈ $40)–₽8 550 (≈ $90) (1,500–3,300 THB) with AirAsia.
  • Hong Kong (HKG): 3.5 hours, average price ₽14 250 (≈ $150)–₽23 750 (≈ $250) (5,500–9,100 THB) with Cathay Pacific or HK Express.
  • Dubai (DXB): 7 hours, average price ₽38 000 (≈ $400)–₽57 000 (≈ $600) (14,600–21,900 THB) with Emirates (seasonal).
  • London (LHR): 11–13 hours (one stop), average price ₽47 500 (≈ $500)–₽76 000 (≈ $800) (18,250–29,200 THB) with Qatar Airways or Etihad.

From the airport, a taxi to Patong costs a fixed 800 THB (₽2 090 (≈ $22)), to Phuket Town 600 THB (₽1 615 (≈ $17)), and to Bang Tao 700 THB (₽1 805 (≈ $19)). The new Airport Bus (RTC Smart Bus) costs 100 THB (₽266 (≈ $2.80)) to Phuket Town and operates every 30 minutes from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM.

Getting Around

Phuket’s traffic has improved with the new LRT, but taxis and tuk-tuks remain the most common options.

  • Phuket Light Rail (LRT): Partially open from Phuket Airport to Phuket Town (10 stations). Fares: 20–50 THB (₽52 (≈ $0.55)–₽133 (≈ $1.40)). Frequency: every 10 minutes, 6:00 AM–10:00 PM. Extensions to Patong and Chalong expected by late 2026.
  • Smart Bus (RTC): Airport to Phuket Town: 100 THB (₽266 (≈ $2.80)). Airport to Patong: 170 THB (₽446 (≈ $4.70)). Runs every 30 minutes.
  • Songthaew (shared trucks): Fixed routes between towns. Phuket Town to Patong: 40 THB (₽104 (≈ $1.10)). No fixed schedule; flag down on main roads.
  • Taxi (metered): From airport: 600–800 THB (₽1 615 (≈ $17)–₽2 090 (≈ $22)). Within town: 200–400 THB (₽522 (≈ $5.50)–₽1 045 (≈ $11)). Always negotiate or use Grab app (100–300 THB for short trips).
  • Tuk-tuk: Short rides in Patong: 150–300 THB (₽380 (≈ $4)–₽760 (≈ $8)). Longer trips (e.g., Patong to Kata): 400–600 THB (₽1 045 (≈ $11)–₽1 615 (≈ $17)).
  • Motorbike rental: 200–300 THB/day (₽522 (≈ $5.50)–₽788 (≈ $8.30)). Requires an International Driving Permit (IDP). Police checkpoints common; fines for no license: 500–1,000 THB (₽1 330 (≈ $14)–₽2 660 (≈ $28)).
  • Car rental: 800–1,500 THB/day (₽2 090 (≈ $22)–₽3 895 (≈ $41)) from Hertz, Avis, or local agencies. Gas: 35 THB/liter (₽91 (≈ $0.96)).

Where to Eat

Street Food & Local Markets

  • Rassada Market (Phuket Town): Open 4:00 AM–10:00 AM. Pad Thai 50 THB (₽133 (≈ $1.40)), mango sticky rice 60 THB (₽161 (≈ $1.70)).
  • Chillva Market (Phuket Town): Open 4:00 PM–11:00 PM (closed Mon). Grilled seafood skewers 80 THB (₽209 (≈ $2.20)), roti 30 THB (₽76 (≈ $0.80)).
  • Patong Walking Street: Night market. Tom yum soup 120 THB (₽313 (≈ $3.30)), fresh spring rolls 40 THB (₽104 (≈ $1.10)).

Restaurants (Mid-Range)

  • Kan Eang@Pier (Chalong): Seafood platter for two: 800 THB (₽2 090 (≈ $22)). Open 10:00 AM–10:00 PM.
  • Suay Restaurant (Phuket Town): Modern Thai. Green curry with soft-shell crab: 350 THB (₽912 (≈ $9.60)). Open 5:00 PM–11:00 PM.
  • The Boathouse Wine & Grill (Kata): Fine dining. Steak 1,200 THB (₽3 135 (≈ $33)). Open 6:00 PM–10:00 PM.

Local Dishes to Try

  • Hokkien Mee: Stir-fried yellow noodles with pork, squid, and soy sauce. 60–100 THB (₽161 (≈ $1.70)–₽266 (≈ $2.80)).
  • Tom Yam Goong: Spicy shrimp soup with lemongrass and galangal. 150–250 THB (₽389 (≈ $4.10)–₽646 (≈ $6.80)).
  • Gaeng Som: Sour orange curry with fish. 120–180 THB (₽313 (≈ $3.30)–₽465 (≈ $4.90)).
  • Moo Hong: Phuket-style braised pork belly. 100–150 THB (₽266 (≈ $2.80)–₽389 (≈ $4.10)).

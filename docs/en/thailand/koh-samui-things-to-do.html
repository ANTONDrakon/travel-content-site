15 Best Things to Do in Koh Samui 2026

Koh Samui, Thailand’s second-largest island, draws over 2.5 million visitors annually with its palm-fringed beaches, lush rainforests, and vibrant culture. In 2026, the island continues to evolve, offering a blend of natural wonders, modern attractions, and traditional experiences. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or cultural immersion, Koh Samui delivers with world-class infrastructure and year-round tropical weather (average 28°C). This guide covers the 15 best things to do, practical itineraries, tours, free attractions, and insider tips to maximize your trip.

Why Visit Koh Samui for Its Attractions

Koh Samui’s appeal lies in its diversity. Unlike other Thai islands, it combines pristine beaches like Chaweng and Lamai with inland marvels such as the Ang Thong National Marine Park and Na Muang waterfalls. The island also boasts iconic landmarks like the Big Buddha Temple and the mummified monk at Wat Khunaram. With improved roads in 2026, including a new ring road expansion, accessing these attractions is easier than ever. Plus, Samui’s nightlife, from beach clubs to night markets, adds an extra layer of excitement.

15 Best Attractions

1. Big Buddha Temple (Wat Phra Yai)

The Big Buddha Temple is Koh Samui’s most iconic landmark, featuring a 12-meter-tall golden Buddha statue seated on a small island connected by a causeway. Built in 1972, it offers panoramic views of the surrounding sea and nearby islands. The temple complex buzzes with vendors selling souvenirs and street food.

  • Ticket Price: Free (donations appreciated)
  • Hours: Daily 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • How to Get There: From Chaweng, take a songthaew (shared taxi) north for 15 minutes (cost 50 THB). Or rent a scooter for 250 THB/day.
  • Tip: Visit at sunset for fewer crowds and stunning golden light on the statue.

2. Ang Thong National Marine Park

This archipelago of 42 islands, a protected marine reserve since 1980, boasts emerald lagoons, limestone cliffs, and coral reefs. Kayak through hidden lagoons like Talay Nai (the "Emerald Sea") and hike to viewpoints for breathtaking vistas. Snorkeling reveals diverse marine life, including parrotfish and sea turtles.

  • Ticket Price: 300 THB for adults, 150 THB for children (park fee)
  • Hours: Full-day tours typically 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Join a speedboat tour from Nathon Pier (1,500–2,500 THB per person).
  • Tip: Book a tour that includes kayaking and hiking to the main viewpoint (500 steps) for the best experience.

3. Na Muang Waterfalls

Na Muang 1 and 2 are two stunning waterfalls set in a lush jungle valley. Na Muang 1 (18 meters) has a large pool for swimming, while Na Muang 2 (80 meters) requires a 30-minute hike uphill. The water flows year-round, but it’s most powerful after rain (October–December).

  • Ticket Price: 50 THB per person
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Drive 10 km southwest from Chaweng (15 minutes by scooter). Park at the entrance (20 THB).
  • Tip: Wear sturdy shoes for the hike to Na Muang 2; bring insect repellent.

4. Wat Khunaram (Mummified Monk)

This temple houses the preserved body of Luang Por Daeng, a monk who died in 1973 while meditating. His body remains in a glass case, displaying remarkable preservation. The temple offers a serene atmosphere and insight into Thai Buddhist beliefs.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Daily 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Located on Route 4169, about 15 minutes from Lamai Beach. Take a songthaew from Lamai (30 THB).
  • Tip: Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees) as it is an active temple.

5. Chaweng Beach

Koh Samui’s most famous beach stretches 4 km with powdery white sand and clear waters. It’s the hub for water sports like jet-skiing and parasailing, plus nightlife with bars and clubs along Soi Green Mango. Daytime offers sunbeds (100 THB) and beachfront restaurants.

  • Ticket Price: Free access
  • Hours: Always open
  • How to Get There: Walk from central Chaweng or take a songthaew from anywhere on the island (50–100 THB).
  • Tip: Avoid the northern end near the pier for quieter spots; head south for fewer crowds.

6. Fisherman’s Village (Bophut)

A historic Chinese-style village on Bophut Beach, Fisherman’s Village features wooden shophouses turned into boutiques, restaurants, and bars. Every Friday from 5:00 PM, the Walking Street Market takes over, selling crafts, street food, and live music. It’s a cultural and culinary hotspot.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Market Friday 5:00 PM – 10:00 PM; shops open daily 10:00 AM – 10:00 PM
  • How to Get There: From Chaweng, take a songthaew north for 10 minutes (50 THB).
  • Tip: Arrive early for the market to avoid traffic; try the grilled seafood at the beachfront stalls.

7. Grandfather and Grandmother Rocks (Hin Ta Hin Yai)

These natural rock formations on Lamai Beach resemble male and female genitalia, hence their names. They are a popular photo spot and part of local folklore. The area also has a small market and viewpoints.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Always open
  • How to Get There: Walk south from Lamai Beach (5 minutes) or take a songthaew from Chaweng (60 THB).
  • Tip: Visit at low tide to see the rocks more clearly; combine with a Lamai Beach visit.

8. Samui Elephant Sanctuary

An ethical sanctuary founded in 2018, it rescues elephants from the tourism and logging industries. Visitors observe elephants roaming freely in a 10-acre natural habitat, with no riding or shows. Educational tours explain elephant behavior and conservation.

  • Ticket Price: 2,500 THB for adults, 1,200 THB for children (includes lunch)
  • Hours: Tours at 8:30 AM and 1:00 PM daily
  • How to Get There: Located in the hills near Bophut; book a transfer (300 THB) or drive (10 minutes from Fisherman’s Village).
  • Tip: Book at least 24 hours in advance; wear long pants and closed shoes for the jungle walk.

9. Secret Buddha Garden (Magic Garden)

Hidden in the hills near Lamai, this garden was built by a local fruit farmer, Nim Thongsuk, over 20 years. It features dozens of statues depicting Buddhist and Hindu deities, set amidst a jungle with streams and waterfalls. The garden offers a mystical, off-the-beaten-path experience.

  • Ticket Price: 80 THB per person
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Drive up a steep road from Lamai (20 minutes by scooter); 4x4 tours from Lamai (1,000 THB per person).
  • Tip: Bring water and a camera; the road is challenging for inexperienced drivers.

10. Lad Koh Viewpoint

Perched on a cliff overlooking the Gulf of Thailand, Lad Koh Viewpoint offers sweeping views of Chaweng Bay and offshore islands. It’s a popular sunset spot with a small café. The viewpoint is accessible via a short staircase.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Always open
  • How to Get There: Drive 5 minutes south of Chaweng on the ring road; park at the lot (free).
  • Tip: Arrive by 5:30 PM for sunset; bring a tripod for photos.

11. Samui Aquarium and Tiger Zoo

This attraction features a large aquarium with tropical fish, sharks, and sea turtles, plus a zoo with tigers, monkeys, and birds. Shows include tiger performances and feeding sessions. Note: ethical concerns exist about animal captivity.

  • Ticket Price: 750 THB for adults, 350 THB for children
  • Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Located near Hua Thanon; take a songthaew from Lamai (40 THB) or drive (10 minutes).
  • Tip: Skip the tiger show if you oppose animal performances; focus on the aquarium.

12. Overlap Stone

A massive granite boulder balanced precariously on a cliff edge near Lamai, Overlap Stone is a natural wonder and photo hotspot. It’s part of a larger rock formation area with hiking trails and ocean views. The site is less crowded than other attractions.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Always open
  • How to Get There: Drive 10 minutes south of Lamai on Route 4169; park at the trailhead (free).
  • Tip: Wear sturdy shoes for the rocky trail; visit in the morning for best light.

13. Wat Plai Laem

A colorful Chinese-style temple complex featuring a massive 18-arm Guan Yin statue (goddess of mercy) and a laughing Buddha. The temple sits on a lake with a bridge and koi fish. It’s less crowded than Big Buddha and offers intricate architecture.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Located 2 km from Big Buddha; take a songthaew from Chaweng (50 THB) or walk from Big Buddha.
  • Tip: Feed the koi fish (20 THB for food) for a fun photo op.

14. Namuang Safari Park

A family-friendly park with elephant rides, ATV tours, and zip-lining through the jungle. It also features a waterfall and animal shows. Prices vary by activity. Note: elephant riding is controversial for animal welfare.

  • Ticket Price: 1,500–3,000 THB for packages
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Near Na Muang Waterfalls; drive 15 minutes from Chaweng.
  • Tip: Choose the zip-line and ATV package (1,800 THB) instead of elephant rides.

15. Silver Beach (Thong Tanot)

A hidden gem near Lamai, Silver Beach is a small, sheltered cove with soft white sand and calm, clear waters. It’s ideal for snorkeling (see clownfish and coral) and relaxing. The beach is less developed, with only a few restaurants.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Always open
  • How to Get There: Drive 5 minutes south of Lamai; park at the entrance (free).
  • Tip: Bring your own snorkel gear; arrive early (before 10 AM) for a prime spot.

1-Day, 2-Day, 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: Samui Highlights

  • 8:00 AM – 10:00 AM: Big Buddha Temple and Wat Plai Laem (free, 2 hours).
  • 10:30 AM – 12:30 PM: Fisherman’s Village for coffee and shopping (free entry).
  • 1:00 PM – 3:00 PM: Na Muang Waterfalls (50 THB, swim and hike).
  • 3:30 PM – 5:00 PM: Lad Koh Viewpoint for sunset (free).
  • 6:00 PM onwards: Chaweng Beach for dinner and nightlife.

2-Day Itinerary: Nature and Culture

  • Day 1: Ang Thong National Marine Park tour (8:00 AM – 5:00 PM, 2,000 THB). Evening at Fisherman’s Village market (Friday only) or Bophut Beach dinner.
  • Day 2: Morning at Samui Elephant Sanctuary (8:30 AM – 12:00 PM, 2,500 THB). Afternoon at Grandfather/Grandmother Rocks and Silver Beach (free). Sunset at Big Buddha.

3-Day Itinerary: Ultimate Samui

  • Day 1: Ang Thong tour (full day). Evening at Chaweng Beach.
  • Day 2: Morning: Secret Buddha Garden (80 THB) and Na Muang Waterfalls. Afternoon: Samui Elephant Sanctuary. Evening: Fisherman’s Village market.
  • Day 3: Morning: Wat Plai Laem and Big Buddha. Afternoon: Silver Beach and Overlap Stone. Sunset at Lad Koh Viewpoint. Farewell dinner at Lamai.

Free Attractions

Koh Samui has plenty of free attractions for budget travelers:

  • Beaches: Chaweng, Lamai, Silver Beach, and Mae Nam Beach (all free).
  • Temples: Big Buddha, Wat Plai Laem, and Wat Khunaram (free entry).
  • Landmarks: Grandfather/Grandmother Rocks, Overlap Stone, and Lad Koh Viewpoint.
  • Markets: Fisherman’s Village Walking Street (Friday) and Lamai Night Market (daily, free to browse).
  • Hiking: Na Muang Waterfalls trail (50 THB for waterfall, but hiking is free) and jungle paths near Lamai.

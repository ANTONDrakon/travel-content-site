15 Best Things to Do in Pattaya 2026

Pattaya, once a quiet fishing village, has transformed into Thailand’s most dynamic coastal city, drawing over 14 million international visitors annually. In 2026, the city offers a striking blend of world-class theme parks, serene Buddhist sanctuaries, and vibrant nightlife. Whether you are seeking adrenaline-pumping water sports or cultural immersion, Pattaya’s attractions have been upgraded with new safety standards and digital ticketing systems. This guide covers the 15 best activities, practical itineraries, and insider tips to maximize your trip.

15 Best Attractions in Pattaya

1. Sanctuary of Truth (Prasat Sut Ja-Tum)

This massive wooden temple on the coast is a philosophical masterpiece, standing 105 meters tall and carved entirely from teak and rosewood without a single metal nail. Construction began in 1981 and continues as a living museum of Thai, Khmer, Chinese, and Hindu craftsmanship. Visitors can watch artisans at work and learn about ancient building techniques. The structure faces the Gulf of Thailand, offering stunning sunset views.

  • Ticket Price: 500 THB (adults), 300 THB (children under 120 cm)
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM (last entry 5:00 PM)
  • How to Get There: Take a songthaew (shared pickup truck) from Pattaya Beach Road for 30 THB; tell the driver “Sanctuary of Truth.” Alternatively, a taxi from central Pattaya costs 150-200 THB.
  • Tip: Arrive before 9:00 AM to avoid crowds and heat; a guided tour (included in ticket) lasts 45 minutes.

2. Nong Nooch Tropical Garden

Spread over 500 acres, this botanical garden features 18 themed zones, including a French garden, a cactus garden, and a dinosaur valley with life-sized animatronic models. The daily cultural shows showcase Thai boxing, traditional dance, and elephant performances. The garden’s orchid nursery holds over 1,000 species, making it one of Asia’s largest. In 2026, a new “Sky Walk” glass bridge opened over the lotus lake.

  • Ticket Price: 600 THB (garden only), 1,000 THB (garden + cultural show)
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM (shows at 10:30 AM, 2:00 PM, and 4:00 PM)
  • How to Get There: Take a songthaew from South Pattaya Road (25 THB) to Sukhumvit Road, then a short taxi (50 THB). Direct taxi from central Pattaya: 300 THB.
  • Tip: Rent a bicycle (100 THB per hour) to cover the vast grounds efficiently; the elephant show is worth arriving 15 minutes early for front-row seats.

3. Walking Street

This 500-meter-long pedestrian-only strip is Pattaya’s iconic nightlife hub, packed with neon-lit bars, nightclubs, and live music venues. By day, it is a quiet market street; by night, it transforms into a carnival with fire dancers, street performers, and over 200 establishments. The street is closed to vehicles from 6:00 PM to 2:00 AM daily. In 2026, new security checkpoints and ID scanners were installed for safety.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry (drinks and cover charges vary; average beer 80-120 THB)
  • Hours: 6:00 PM – 2:00 AM (some clubs open until 4:00 AM)
  • How to Get There: Walk from Pattaya Beach Road (5 minutes from Central Festival); songthaews run along Beach Road until midnight.
  • Tip: Visit on a Sunday evening for fewer crowds; avoid the first 100 meters where touts are aggressive—head to the quieter Soi 14 area.

4. Pattaya Floating Market (Four Regions Market)

This 100,000-square-meter market replicates traditional floating markets from Thailand’s four regions: North, Central, Northeast, and South. Visitors navigate canals on long-tail boats (50 THB per person) or walk along wooden bridges. Over 1,200 vendors sell local snacks, handicrafts, and souvenirs. The market features daily cultural performances, including folk music and Muay Thai demonstrations.

  • Ticket Price: 200 THB (adults), 100 THB (children under 120 cm)
  • Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 8:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Take a songthaew from Pattaya Tai Road (20 THB) to Sukhumvit Road, then walk 500 meters. Direct taxi: 250 THB.
  • Tip: Arrive before 11:00 AM to avoid heat and crowds; try the boat noodle soup (40 THB) and coconut ice cream (30 THB).

5. Koh Larn (Coral Island)

Located 7.5 kilometers offshore, Koh Larn offers white-sand beaches like Tawaen and Samae, with crystal-clear water ideal for snorkeling and swimming. The island has a population of about 3,000 residents and features a viewpoint at Nual Beach. Speedboats and ferries run regularly from Bali Hai Pier. In 2026, the island banned single-use plastics, making it one of Thailand’s cleanest day-trip destinations.

  • Ticket Price: Ferry 30 THB (one-way); speedboat 300 THB (round-trip). Snorkeling gear rental: 100 THB.
  • Hours: Ferries depart every 30 minutes from 7:00 AM to 6:30 PM (last return ferry 5:30 PM)
  • How to Get There: Walk or take a songthaew (20 THB) to Bali Hai Pier at the southern end of Beach Road.
  • Tip: Catch the first ferry at 7:00 AM to have the beaches nearly empty; bring reef-safe sunscreen as the island enforces a ban on chemical sunscreens.

6. Art in Paradise (3D Museum)

This interactive 3D art museum spans 5,800 square meters across two floors, with over 150 optical illusion paintings created by Korean and Thai artists. Each exhibit is designed for visitors to pose and take photos, from swimming with sharks to flying on a magic carpet. The museum updates its displays annually; in 2026, a new “Underwater World” section was added. It is one of the most Instagrammable spots in Pattaya.

  • Ticket Price: 400 THB (adults), 200 THB (children under 120 cm)
  • Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 9:00 PM (last entry 8:00 PM)
  • How to Get There: Located on Pattaya Second Road, opposite Central Festival Pattaya; walk from Beach Road (5 minutes).
  • Tip: Visit on a weekday afternoon (2:00-4:00 PM) for fewer queues; bring a wide-angle lens for better photo coverage.

7. Pattaya Elephant Sanctuary

This ethical sanctuary rescues elephants from logging and riding industries, housing 12 elephants in a 30-acre natural habitat. Visitors observe elephants bathing, feeding, and roaming freely without chains or rides. The sanctuary educates about elephant conservation and offers a “mahout for a day” program. In 2026, a new observation deck was built overlooking the elephant pond.

  • Ticket Price: 2,500 THB (full-day program including lunch), 1,500 THB (half-day observation)
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM (book in advance)
  • How to Get There: Located 20 km south of Pattaya near Huay Yai; free shuttle from central Pattaya (booked with ticket).
  • Tip: Book the morning session (8:00 AM-12:00 PM) for cooler weather and active elephants; wear long pants and closed shoes.

8. Million Years Stone Park & Crocodile Farm

This unique park combines a geological museum with a crocodile farm housing over 100,000 crocodiles of various species. The park features a petrified wood collection, a dinosaur fossil display, and a “Stone Garden” with ancient rock formations. Daily crocodile shows include handlers putting their heads in crocodile mouths. The park covers 50 acres and includes a small zoo with tigers and monkeys.

  • Ticket Price: 300 THB (adults), 150 THB (children)
  • Hours: Daily 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM (shows at 10:00 AM, 1:00 PM, and 4:00 PM)
  • How to Get There: Take a songthaew from Sukhumvit Road (20 THB) to the park entrance; direct taxi from central Pattaya: 200 THB.
  • Tip: Combine with Nong Nooch (10 km away) for a full-day trip; the crocodile show is safest from the second row—avoid front seats.

9. Pattaya Beach

This 4-kilometer-long beach is the city’s main public shoreline, with soft golden sand and calm waters ideal for swimming and sunbathing. The promenade is lined with restaurants, bars, and water sports rentals, including jet skis (1,000 THB per 30 minutes) and parasailing (1,500 THB per ride). In 2026, the city completed a major beach restoration project, adding new showers and shaded areas. The beach is divided into three sections: North, Central, and South.

  • Ticket Price: Free (water sports and chair rentals extra; beach chair 100 THB per day)
  • Hours: 24 hours (water sports operate 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM)
  • How to Get There: Accessible from any point along Beach Road; songthaews run continuously.
  • Tip: The North section (near Dusit Thani hotel) is quieter and cleaner; avoid the South end near Walking Street for families.

10. Ripley’s Believe It or Not! Pattaya

Located in the Royal Garden Plaza, this attraction features over 300 exhibits of oddities, including shrunken heads, a two-headed calf, and a replica of the world’s tallest man. The complex also includes an 8D interactive cinema, a laser maze, and the “Infinity Maze” with mirrors. The building is designed as a crashed airplane, adding to the novelty. In 2026, a new “Alien Encounter” virtual reality experience was added.

  • Ticket Price: 1,200 THB (combo ticket for all attractions), 600 THB (museum only)
  • Hours: Daily 10:00 AM – 10:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Located on Beach Road near Soi 2; walk from central Pattaya (5 minutes).
  • Tip: Buy the combo ticket online (often 20% cheaper) and visit on a rainy day; the laser maze is best for kids aged 8+.

11. Mini Siam & Mini Europe

This miniature park showcases over 100 scaled-down replicas of famous landmarks, including the Grand Palace, Eiffel Tower, and Statue of Liberty, at a 1:25 scale. The park is divided into two zones: Mini Siam (Thai landmarks) and Mini Europe (global landmarks). Each model is meticulously detailed, with landscaping and lighting. In 2026, a new “Ancient Wonders” section added the Great Wall of China and Machu Picchu.

  • Ticket Price: 300 THB (adults), 150 THB (children)
  • Hours: Daily 7:00 AM – 10:00 PM (lighting after sunset)
  • How to Get There: Located on Sukhumvit Road near Soi 33; take a songthaew from Pattaya Tai (25 THB).
  • Tip: Visit at dusk (5:30-6:30 PM) to see the models in both daylight and illuminated; bring a tripod for night photos.

12. Underwater World Pattaya

This oceanarium features a 100-meter-long underwater tunnel with a moving walkway, allowing visitors to view sharks, stingrays, and sea turtles up close. The aquarium houses over 5,000 marine animals from the Gulf of Thailand and Andaman Sea, including clownfish, moray eels, and leopard sharks. Interactive touch pools let visitors handle starfish and sea cucumbers. In 2026, a new “Jellyfish Gallery” with LED-lit tanks was added.

  • Ticket Price: 500 THB (adults), 300 THB (children under 120 cm)
  • Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM (last entry 5:00 PM)
  • How to Get There: Located on Sukhumvit Road near Soi 22; take a songthaew from Pattaya Klang (20 THB).
  • Tip: Visit during feeding time (11:00 AM and 3:00 PM) for the most activity; avoid weekends when school groups visit.

13. Pattaya Viewpoint (Khao Phra Tamnak)

This hilltop viewpoint offers panoramic views of Pattaya Bay, the Gulf of Thailand, and the city skyline. The site features a small park with a statue of a Chinese goddess and a telescope (10 THB per use). The viewpoint is especially popular at sunset, when the sky turns orange over the sea. A short 200-meter stair climb leads to the top, or you can drive up.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: 24 hours (best viewed 5:00-6:30 PM)
  • How to Get There: Walk from Walking Street (15 minutes uphill) or take a taxi from Beach Road (100 THB).
  • Tip: Bring a flashlight for the walk down after sunset; the nearby “Big Buddha” temple (Wat Phra Yai) is worth a 10-minute detour.

14. Cartoon Network Amazone Waterpark

This waterpark is the only Cartoon Network-themed waterpark in the world, covering 14 acres with 18 water slides and attractions themed around characters like Ben 10, The Powerpuff Girls, and Adventure Time. The park features a wave pool, a lazy river, and a “Cartoonival” area for kids. In 2026, a new “Gumball’s Splash Zone” was added with interactive water cannons. The park has a maximum capacity of 5,000 visitors daily.

  • Ticket Price: 1,500 THB (adults), 1,000 THB (children under 122 cm)
  • Hours: Daily 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM (closed Wednesdays from May-October)
  • How to Get There: Located on Sukhumvit Road near Bang Saray; free shuttle from central Pattaya (book online).
  • Tip: Rent a locker (200 THB) and bring waterproof sunscreen; the “Humungousaur’s Drop” slide has a 3-second freefall—skip if you fear heights.

15. Wat Phra Yai (Big Buddha Temple)

This temple on Pratamnak Hill houses a 18-meter-tall golden Buddha statue, visible from much of Pattaya. The temple complex includes a meditation hall, a bell tower, and a small museum with Buddha relics. The site is sacred to locals, with monks chanting daily at 6:00 PM. The hill offers secondary viewpoints of the city and the sea. In 2026, the temple underwent a renovation to restore its gold leaf.

  • Ticket Price: Free (donations appreciated)
  • Hours: Daily 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Walk from Pattaya Viewpoint (5 minutes) or take a songthaew from South Pattaya Road (20 THB).
  • Tip: Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered); visit at 6:00 PM for the chanting ceremony and sunset views.

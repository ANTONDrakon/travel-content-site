15 Best Things to Do in Bangkok 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Bangkok, the pulsating heart of Thailand, offers an intoxicating blend of ancient temples, futuristic skyscrapers, bustling markets, and serene canals. In 2026, the city continues to evolve, with new attractions like the Bangkok Observation Deck at King Power Mahanakhon and revamped riverfront spaces drawing millions of visitors. Whether you seek spiritual enlightenment in gold-encrusted wats. culinary adventures on street food stalls. or high-end shopping in sprawling malls. Bangkok delivers an unmatched sensory experience. This guide covers the 15 must-do activities. practical itineraries. and insider tips to navigate the City of Angels like a pro.

15 Best Attractions

1. The Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew

This sprawling complex, built in 1782, served as the official residence of the Kings of Siam. Inside, Wat Phra Kaew houses the revered Emerald Buddha, a 66-centimeter-tall statue carved from a single block of jade. The intricate murals, golden stupas, and demon guardians make it Bangkok's most iconic landmark. Expect crowds, especially in the morning.

  • Ticket Price: 500 THB (includes Wat Phra Kaew, Grand Palace, and the textile museum).
  • Hours: Daily 8:30 AM – 3:30 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take the BTS Skytrain to Saphan Taksin, then a Chao Phraya Express Boat to Tha Chang Pier (9 THB).
  • Tip: Wear long pants and cover shoulders; no shorts or sleeveless tops allowed. Arrive by 8:30 AM to avoid peak heat and crowds.

2. Wat Pho (Temple of the Reclining Buddha)

Just south of the Grand Palace, Wat Pho is home to a 46-meter-long, 15-meter-high reclining Buddha statue covered in gold leaf. The temple is also Thailand's first public university and a center for traditional Thai massage. The soles of the Buddha's feet are inlaid with 108 auspicious scenes in mother-of-pearl.

  • Ticket Price: 300 THB (includes a free bottle of water).
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 6:30 PM.
  • How to Get There: Walk 10 minutes from Tha Tien Pier (boat from Saphan Taksin).
  • Tip: Book a 30-minute traditional Thai massage at the on-site school for 350 THB – it's world-class.

3. Wat Arun (Temple of Dawn)

This stunning temple features a 70-meter-high central prang (Khmer-style tower) decorated with colorful porcelain and seashells. Climb the steep steps for panoramic views of the Chao Phraya River and the Grand Palace. It glows beautifully at sunset.

  • Ticket Price: 100 THB.
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take the cross-river ferry from Tha Tien Pier (4 THB).
  • Tip: Visit at 5:30 PM for golden hour photos; the temple is lit up after dark.

4. Chatuchak Weekend Market

One of the largest weekend markets in the world, with over 15,000 stalls spread across 27 sections. You can find everything from vintage clothing and antiques to exotic pets and street food. It draws 200,000 visitors each weekend.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Saturday–Sunday 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM (some stalls open Friday).
  • How to Get There: BTS Mo Chit Station (exit 1) or MRT Chatuchak Park Station.
  • Tip: Go early (9 AM) to avoid heat; bring cash, as few stalls accept cards. Try the coconut ice cream from stall 24.

5. Jim Thompson House Museum

This complex of six traditional Thai teak houses was the home of American silk entrepreneur Jim Thompson, who mysteriously disappeared in 1967. The museum displays his art collection, including rare Southeast Asian ceramics and Buddha statues. The lush tropical garden is a quiet oasis in the city.

  • Ticket Price: 200 THB (includes guided tour in English).
  • Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM (last tour at 5:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: BTS National Stadium Station (exit 1), then a 5-minute walk.
  • Tip: Take the 11:00 AM tour for a smaller group; the museum shop sells high-quality silk scarves.

6. Khao San Road

This 400-meter-long street is the backpacker hub of Bangkok, famous for its vibrant nightlife, cheap street food, and souvenir stalls. By day, it's a casual shopping area; by night, it transforms into a party zone with live music and bucket cocktails. It's chaotic but iconic.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Most shops 10:00 AM – 2:00 AM; bars open until late.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 15 from Siam Square or a taxi (200 THB from Sukhumvit).
  • Tip: For a quieter experience, visit at 10 AM. Try pad thai from the street vendor at the corner of Rambuttri Road.

7. Bangkok National Museum

This is the largest museum in Southeast Asia, housing over 30,000 artifacts, including the Ramkhamhaeng Inscription (UNESCO Memory of the World) and royal funeral chariots. The three galleries cover Thai history from prehistory to the Rattanakosin period. Allow at least 2–3 hours.

  • Ticket Price: 200 THB (free on Wednesdays, but check 2026 schedule).
  • Hours: Wednesday–Sunday 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM (closed Monday–Tuesday).
  • How to Get There: Walk 15 minutes from Tha Chang Pier or take bus 32 from Democracy Monument.
  • Tip: Join the free English guided tour at 9:30 AM (Wednesday–Thursday).

8. Asiatique The Riverfront

A large open-air mall and night market on the Chao Phraya River, combining shopping, dining, and entertainment. It features a 60-meter-tall Ferris wheel (Asiatique Sky), a cabaret show, and over 1,500 boutiques. It's less chaotic than other markets.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry; Ferris wheel 500 THB.
  • Hours: Daily 11:00 AM – Midnight (shops open at 4:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: Free shuttle boat from Saphan Taksin BTS Station (every 15 minutes, 4:00 PM – 11:30 PM).
  • Tip: Visit at sunset for river views; the Calypso Cabaret show costs 1,000 THB.

9. Wat Saket (The Golden Mount)

This artificial hill, 78 meters high, was built in the 19th century and topped with a golden chedi containing relics of the Buddha. Climbing the 344 steps through a wooded park offers a peaceful escape and 360-degree views of Bangkok. The temple is especially beautiful during the November Loy Krathong festival.

  • Ticket Price: 100 THB.
  • Hours: Daily 8:00 AM – 7:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Walk 10 minutes from MRT Sam Yot Station.
  • Tip: Go at 6:00 PM to see the sunset and the temple lights turn on.

10. ICONSIAM

This luxury riverside mega-mall, opened in 2018, features a stunning indoor floating market (Sook Siam), a high-end shopping zone, and the ICONSIAM Park, the largest multimedia water feature in Southeast Asia. It hosts over 500 brands and a 7,000-seat arena.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Daily 10:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Free shuttle boat from Saphan Taksin BTS (every 10 minutes) or BTS Gold Line from Krung Thon Buri Station.
  • Tip: Watch the 7:00 PM water and light show at the riverfront plaza.

11. Lumphini Park

Bangkok's largest green space (142 acres), named after Lumbini in Nepal. It features a large lake with swan-shaped paddle boats, jogging paths, and monitor lizards (harmless). It's a popular spot for locals practicing tai chi and aerobics in the early morning.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Daily 4:30 AM – 9:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: MRT Lumphini Station (exit 1) or BTS Sala Daeng Station.
  • Tip: Rent a paddleboat (40 THB for 30 minutes) and bring insect repellent.

12. Bangkok Art and Culture Centre (BACC)

A contemporary art space with 12 floors of galleries, exhibition halls, and artist studios. It hosts rotating exhibits of Thai and international art, from photography to installations. The spiral ramp design is architecturally striking.

  • Ticket Price: Free (special exhibits may cost 100–300 THB).
  • Hours: Tuesday–Sunday 10:00 AM – 9:00 PM (closed Monday).
  • How to Get There: BTS Siam Station (exit 3), connected via skywalk.
  • Tip: Visit the 7th-floor library for free art books and a quiet reading space.

13. Yaowarat Road (Chinatown)

Bangkok's Chinatown, established in 1782, is a feast for the senses of gold shops, herbal medicine stalls, and street food. Yaowarat Road is the main artery, especially vibrant at night with hundreds of food vendors selling seafood, dim sum, and roast duck. It's one of the oldest neighborhoods in the city.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Best visited 6:00 PM – 11:00 PM (food stalls).
  • How to Get There: MRT Wat Mangkon Station (exit 1).
  • Tip: Try the grilled seafood at "T&K Seafood" (300 THB for a feast) and the crispy pork at "Yaowarat Road Pork Leg Rice."

14. King Power Mahanakhon SkyWalk

This 314-meter-tall skyscraper features the world's third-largest glass-floor observation deck (the "Mahanakhon SkyWalk") and a rooftop bar. The building's pixelated design is iconic. The 360-degree view includes the Chao Phraya River and distant mountains on clear days.

  • Ticket Price: 880 THB (adult), 450 THB (child).
  • Hours: Daily 10:00 AM – Midnight (last entry 11:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: BTS Chong Nonsi Station (exit 3), directly connected.
  • Tip: Book online in advance for 765 THB; visit at 5:30 PM for sunset and city lights.

15. Damnoen Saduak Floating Market (Day Trip)

This iconic market, 110 kilometers southwest of Bangkok, features vendors selling fruits, souvenirs, and noodles from wooden boats on narrow canals. It's touristy but offers a unique glimpse of traditional Thai water-based commerce. Tours often include a long-tail boat ride.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry; boat rides 2,000–3,000 THB per boat (up to 4 people).
  • Hours: Best visited 7:00 AM – 11:00 AM (peak by 9 AM).
  • How to Get There: Book a tour (1,200 THB including hotel pickup) or take a minivan from Southern Bus Terminal (120 THB).
  • Tip: Haggle for souvenirs; avoid the "tourist boats" that overcharge. Bring a hat and sunscreen.

1-Day, 2-Day, 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: The Royal & River Route

Start at 8:00 AM at the Grand Palace (2 hours). Walk to Wat Pho (1.5 hours). Take the cross-river ferry to Wat Arun (1 hour). Lunch at a riverside restaurant at Tha Tien (300 THB). At 2:00 PM, take a Chao Phraya Express Boat to ICONSIAM (1.5 hours shopping). At 5:30 PM, take the free shuttle to Asiatique for sunset and dinner. End with the Ferris wheel ride.

2-Day Itinerary: Temples & Markets

Day 1: Same as 1-day itinerary, but after Asiatique, take a taxi to Khao San Road for nightlife (9 PM). Day 2: Start at 9:00 AM at Chatuchak Weekend Market (if Saturday/Sunday; otherwise, visit Jim Thompson House). Lunch at Or Tor Kor Market (500 THB). At 2:00 PM, explore Chinatown (Yaowarat) and Wat Mangkon. At 5:00 PM, climb Wat Saket for sunset. Dinner at a street food stall on Soi Rambuttri.

3-Day Itinerary: Full Immersion

Day 1: Royal temples + ICONSIAM + Asiatique. Day 2: Chatuchak Market (morning) + BACC (afternoon) + Lumphini Park (evening jog). Day 3: Damnoen Saduak Floating Market (half-day tour, 7 AM–1 PM). Return to Bangkok for King Power Mahanakhon SkyWalk at 5:30 PM. Dinner at a rooftop restaurant like "Above Eleven" (1,500 THB).

Krabi Travel Guide 2026

Krabi, a province on Thailand’s Andaman coast, is a land of dramatic limestone karsts, emerald-green waters, and pristine beaches that rival any in Southeast Asia. Unlike the party-centric atmosphere of neighboring Phuket, Krabi offers a more laid-back, nature-focused vibe. The provincial capital, Krabi Town, is a charming riverside hub with a relaxed, local feel, while the coastal areas of Ao Nang, Railay Beach, and the islands of Koh Phi Phi and Koh Lanta draw travelers seeking adventure, tranquility, and world-class scenery.

The atmosphere in Krabi is defined by its raw natural beauty. Towering limestone cliffs jut out of the sea, creating a dramatic backdrop for rock climbing, kayaking, and island hopping. The air is thick with the scent of salt and tropical blooms, and the sounds of longtail boats and lapping waves replace the roar of city traffic. This is a place where you can spend your morning hiking through a national park, your afternoon snorkeling among coral reefs, and your evening dining on fresh seafood under a canopy of stars.

Why visit Krabi in 2026? The region continues to invest in sustainable tourism, with new eco-friendly resorts and improved infrastructure. The sheer diversity of experiences is unmatched: you can explore the world-famous Railay Beach, accessible only by boat; kayak through the hidden lagoons of Thung Teao Forest Natural Park; or take a day trip to the iconic Phi Phi Islands. Whether you are a solo backpacker, a couple seeking romance, or a family looking for adventure, Krabi delivers a wealth of authentic Thai experiences without the overwhelming crowds of some other destinations.

How to Get There

Krabi International Airport (KBV) is the primary gateway, located about 15 kilometers from Krabi Town and 30 kilometers from Ao Nang. The airport is well-connected to major cities across Asia and the Middle East.

Direct flights operate from Bangkok (BKK and DMK) with airlines like Thai Airways, Bangkok Airways, and Air Asia, with average one-way fares between $30 and $80. From Singapore, flights on Scoot and Jetstar Asia range from $80 to $150. Kuala Lumpur is served by Air Asia and Malindo Air, with fares around $50 to $100. European travelers typically connect via Bangkok or Dubai, with round-trip fares from London or Frankfurt averaging $600 to $900. Seasonal charter flights from Russia and Scandinavia also operate from November to March.

Alternatively, you can reach Krabi by bus from Bangkok’s Southern Bus Terminal (Sai Tai Mai). The journey takes 10-12 hours and costs $10-$15. Train travel to Surat Thani, followed by a bus to Krabi, is another budget-friendly option.

Where to Stay

Accommodation in Krabi spans every budget, from backpacker hostels to luxury beachfront resorts. The main areas to consider are Krabi Town, Ao Nang, Railay Beach, and the islands.

Krabi Town is the most affordable and authentic base. It offers easy access to markets, local restaurants, and transport hubs. Budget hostels like Pak-Up Hostel cost $8-$15 per night, while mid-range hotels like Krabi River Hotel run $25-$45. Luxury options are limited here.

Ao Nang is the main tourist hub, with a beach, nightlife, and hundreds of hotels. Budget stays like Baan Khao Thong Guesthouse start at $15-$25. Mid-range hotels, such as Aonang Cliff Beach Resort, range from $40-$80. High-end resorts like the Centara Grand Beach Resort & Villas cost $150-$300 per night.

Railay Beach is only accessible by boat and offers a secluded, upscale experience. Mid-range options like Railay Bay Resort & Spa are $60-$120, while luxury properties like Rayavadee start at $400 per night. Budget bungalows are scarce but available from $30.

Koh Phi Phi and Koh Lanta offer island living. Phi Phi has budget dorms from $10 and luxury resorts like Phi Phi Island Village Beach Resort from $200. Koh Lanta is quieter, with bungalows from $15 and resorts from $50.

Top 15 Attractions

  1. Railay Beach – A stunning peninsula accessible only by longtail boat from Ao Nang. Famous for its white sand, clear water, and world-class rock climbing. Free entry; climbing gear rental $10-$15. Open 24 hours.
  2. Phi Phi Islands – An archipelago of six islands, including Maya Bay, made famous by the film "The Beach." Day trips cost $30-$50 per person. Maya Bay is open 8 AM to 4 PM with limited visitors (pre-booking required).
  3. Emerald Pool (Sa Morakot) – A natural spring-fed pool in Thung Teao Forest Natural Park with vibrant green water. Entry $5. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.
  4. Blue Pool (Sa Nam Thip) – A deep, crystal-clear pool nearby, with a striking blue hue. Entry included with Emerald Pool ticket. Open 8 AM to 4 PM (closed during rainy season).
  5. Hong Islands – A group of islands with a spectacular lagoon, perfect for snorkeling and kayaking. Day trips $35-$55. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.
  6. Tiger Cave Temple (Wat Tham Suea) – A Buddhist temple atop a 1,237-step climb, offering panoramic views of Krabi. Free entry. Open 6 AM to 6 PM.
  7. Khao Khanab Nam Caves – Two dramatic limestone mountains with caves at the mouth of the Krabi River. Boat tours $10-$15 per person. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.
  8. Thung Teao Forest Natural Park – A lush rainforest with a boardwalk trail, home to the Emerald Pool and Blue Pool. Entry $5. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.
  9. Dragon Crest Mountain (Khao Ngon Nak) – A challenging 4-kilometer hike to a viewpoint with a dragon-shaped ridge. Free entry. Open 6 AM to 4 PM.
  10. Shell Cemetery (Suea Khao) – A unique geological formation of fossilized shells estimated to be 40 million years old. Entry $3. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.
  11. Krabi Town Night Market – A vibrant market along the river, offering local food, crafts, and live music. Free entry. Open Friday to Sunday, 5 PM to 10 PM.
  12. Wat Kaew Korawaram – A stunning white temple in Krabi Town with intricate architecture. Free entry. Open 6 AM to 6 PM.
  13. Diamond Cave (Tham Phra Nang) – A cave on Railay Beach with impressive stalactites and stalagmites. Entry $2. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.
  14. Koh Poda – A small island near Railay with a beautiful beach and snorkeling spots. Day trips from Ao Nang $15-$25. Open 8 AM to 5 PM.
  15. Four Islands Tour – A popular day trip covering Koh Poda, Koh Chicken, Koh Tup, and Koh Mor. Includes snorkeling, lunch, and beach time. Cost $20-$35 per person.

Getting Around

Krabi does not have a metro or extensive public bus system, but several affordable options exist.

Tuk-tuks (songthaews) are the most common mode of transport. These shared pickup trucks run fixed routes between Krabi Town, Ao Nang, and the airport. Fares range from $1 to $3 per person for short trips. Private tuk-tuks cost $5-$10 for longer distances.

Longtail boats are essential for island hopping and reaching Railay Beach. Fares from Ao Nang to Railay are $3-$5 per person. Private charters for a full day cost $50-$100.

Rental scooters are popular for independent exploration. Daily rental rates are $6-$10, with weekly discounts. You need a valid international driving permit and a helmet (provided). Gasoline costs about $1 per liter.

Car rentals are available from $25-$40 per day. Companies like Hertz and Budget have desks at the airport. Driving is on the left side of the road.

Taxi services from the airport to Ao Nang cost $15-$20, and to Krabi Town $10-$15. Uber and Grab operate in the area, with similar pricing.

Where to Eat

Krabi’s culinary scene is a delightful mix of Thai street food, fresh seafood, and international cuisine. Prices are generally lower than in Phuket.

Local dishes to try: Tom Yum Goong (spicy shrimp soup), Pad Thai, Green Curry, Massaman Curry, and fresh seafood like grilled prawns and steamed fish. For dessert, try mango sticky rice.

Budget eats ($2-$5): Street food stalls in Krabi Town Night Market and Ao Nang’s Soi 8 serve pad thai, satay skewers, and som tam (papaya salad). Local restaurants like May & Mark’s House in Ao Nang offer curries and stir-fries for $3-$5.

Mid-range restaurants ($8-$15): Gecko Cabaret Restaurant in Ao Nang serves Thai and Western dishes with a view. The Hilltop, also in Ao Nang, offers panoramic views and seafood platters for $10-$15.

High-end dining ($20-$40): The Grotto at Rayavadee Resort on Railay Beach is a cave restaurant serving fine Thai cuisine. Krua Thara in Krabi Town is famous for its seafood, with main courses from $15-$30.

Vegetarian and vegan options: Many restaurants offer vegetarian versions of Thai classics. The Vegan Table in Ao Nang specializes in plant-based dishes for $5-$10.

Best Time to Visit

Krabi has a tropical monsoon climate with two main seasons: the dry season (November to April) and the wet season (May to October).

November to February: This is the peak season, with cool, dry weather and average temperatures of 25-30°C. Skies are clear, and sea conditions are calm. Prices for accommodation and flights are at their highest. Expect crowds at popular attractions.

March to April: The weather is still dry but hotter, with temperatures reaching 35°C. This is a good time for budget travelers, as prices drop slightly. The sea remains calm for island hopping.

May to October: The wet season brings frequent but short-lived rain showers, often in the afternoon. Temperatures range from 25-32°C. This is the low season, with significantly lower prices (up to 50% off on accommodation). Some islands, like Koh Phi Phi, may have rough seas, but the landscape is lush and green. The Emerald Pool and Blue Pool are at their most vibrant.

Monthly averages: January (26°C, 30mm rain), April (30°C, 60mm), July (28°C, 250mm), October (27°C, 300mm). The best months for a balance of good weather and fewer crowds are November, December, and March.

Visa & Documents

Krabi is in Thailand, and visa policies are national. Citizens of 64 countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, Canada, and most European nations, enjoy visa-free entry for up to 30 days when arriving by air. For land border crossings, the visa-free period is 15 days.

Travelers from other countries must apply for a tourist visa (single-entry, 60 days) at a Thai embassy or consulate before travel. The fee is approximately $40. Extensions of stay (30 days) can be obtained at the Krabi Immigration Office in Krabi Town for $50.

Your passport must be valid for at least six months beyond your date of entry. Proof of onward travel (flight ticket out of Thailand) may be requested. No vaccination is required for entry, but hepatitis A and typhoid vaccines are recommended.

10 Travel Tips

  1. Book island tours in advance during peak season (November-February) to secure spots, especially for Maya Bay and Hong Islands. Use reputable operators to avoid overcrowded boats.
  2. Carry cash for small purchases, as many street stalls and local shops do not accept credit cards. ATMs are widely available in Krabi Town and Ao Nang.
  3. Protect yourself from the sun with reef-safe sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses. The tropical sun is intense even on cloudy days.
  4. Learn basic Thai phrases like "hello" (sawasdee), "thank you" (khob khun), and "how much?" (tao rai). Locals appreciate the effort.
  5. Respect local customs by covering your shoulders and knees when visiting temples like Tiger Cave Temple. Remove shoes before entering temple buildings.
  6. Stay hydrated but avoid tap water. Drink bottled or filtered water, which costs $0.50-$1 per liter.
  7. Negotiate prices for tuk-tuk rides and boat tours, as initial quotes are often inflated. Agree on a price before the journey begins.
  8. Check weather conditions before booking boat trips during the wet season (May-October). Some tours may be canceled due to rough seas.
  9. Pack light and breathable clothing made of cotton or linen. Quick-dry swimwear and a reusable water bottle are essential.
  10. Get travel insurance that covers medical emergencies, trip cancellation, and adventure activities like rock climbing and scuba diving. Medical facilities in Krabi are basic for serious cases.

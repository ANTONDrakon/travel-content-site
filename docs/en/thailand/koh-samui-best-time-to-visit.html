Best Time to Visit Koh Samui 2026: Seasons & Weather

Koh Samui, Thailand’s second-largest island, enjoys a tropical monsoon climate distinct from the rest of the country. Unlike Phuket or Krabi, Samui’s weather is influenced by the Gulf of Thailand, resulting in a unique rainy pattern. The island experiences three primary seasons: the hot season (March to May), the rainy season (October to December), and the cool, dry season (January to February and June to September). In 2026, understanding these microclimates is crucial for planning a trip that aligns with your preferences, whether you seek sun-drenched beaches, cultural festivals, or budget-friendly travel. Average annual temperatures hover around 28°C (82°F), with humidity levels ranging from 70% to 85% year-round.

High Season

Koh Samui’s high season spans from mid-December to February, and again from June to August. The first peak aligns with the cool, dry northeast monsoon, offering daytime highs of 27–29°C and minimal rainfall. The second peak, June to August, is driven by European summer holidays, though weather remains consistently sunny with occasional brief showers.

Why travel during high season? Expect crystal-clear skies, calm seas, and ideal conditions for snorkeling at Koh Tao or Ang Thong Marine Park. However, prices surge by 40–60% compared to low season. A standard double room at a mid-range resort like Chaweng Regent Beach Resort costs 5,500 THB (approx. USD 155) per night in January 2026, versus 3,200 THB in October. Book flights and accommodations at least 3–4 months in advance, as occupancy rates exceed 90% in popular areas like Lamai and Bophut.

Low Season

Low season runs from October to early December, with a secondary lull in May and September. The primary driver is the southwest monsoon, which brings heavy rainfall to Samui’s west coast, particularly in November. However, the east coast (Chaweng, Lamai) often escapes the worst, receiving only 30% of the total precipitation.

Pros: Accommodation prices drop by 50–70%. A luxury villa at Samujana can be booked for 12,000 THB per night in November, compared to 25,000 THB in February. Tours and flights are heavily discounted, with round-trip Bangkok-Samui tickets on Bangkok Airways falling to 4,000 THB (approx. USD 110).

Cons: Expect 8–12 rainy days per month, with downpours lasting 1–2 hours. Sea conditions can be rough, especially on the west coast, making ferry crossings to Koh Phangan or Koh Tao uncomfortable. Some beachfront restaurants and smaller resorts close for renovation. Despite this, the island remains open, and savvy travelers enjoy uncrowded beaches and personalized service.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

For swimming, sunbathing, and water sports, the optimal months are February, March, and June through August. During these periods, water temperatures range from 28–30°C, visibility for snorkeling exceeds 15 meters, and wave heights average less than 0.5 meters. February offers the most reliable dry weather, with 24 sunny days and only 20mm of rain. June through August, while slightly more humid, still provides 18–19 sunny days per month and calm seas ideal for kayaking or paddleboarding.

Avoid October and November for beach activities. Heavy rainfall and runoff reduce visibility to under 3 meters, and strong currents along the east coast can be dangerous. If you must visit during these months, head to the protected coves of Taling Ngam or Choeng Mon, which offer sheltered swimming.

Best Time for Sightseeing

Sightseeing in Koh Samui is most comfortable from January to March, when temperatures are moderate (27–29°C) and humidity is low (70–75%). These conditions are perfect for visiting the Big Buddha Temple (Wat Phra Yai), the 12-meter-tall golden statue, or hiking to the Namuang Waterfall 2, which requires a 30-minute trek through the jungle. The cool season also makes exploring the island’s 70+ temples and the Secret Buddha Garden (Magic Garden) enjoyable without heat exhaustion.

April and May, with highs of 31°C and humidity above 80%, are less ideal for daytime excursions. Plan sightseeing for early morning (7:00–10:00 AM) or late afternoon (4:00–6:00 PM). The rainy months of September and October can disrupt outdoor plans, but indoor attractions like the Samui Aquarium and Tiger Zoo remain viable. For the best balance of comfort and access, February and March are unbeatable.

Rainy Season

Koh Samui’s rainy season is concentrated from October to December, with November being the wettest month, averaging 300mm of precipitation across 22 days. Unlike the rest of Thailand, Samui does not experience a prolonged southwest monsoon from May to October; instead, the island’s rain peaks sharply in the last quarter of the year. Rainfall is typically heavy but short-lived, often occurring in late afternoon or evening thunderstorms.

Is it worth visiting? Yes, if you prioritize low costs and solitude. The rain rarely lasts all day, and sunny intervals allow for brief beach time. However, flash floods can occur in low-lying areas like Fisherman’s Village in Bophut, and ferry services to Koh Phangan may be suspended during storms. In 2026, the La Niña phenomenon is expected to intensify rainfall by 10–15%, so pack a high-quality waterproof jacket and water-resistant sandals. For photographers, the dramatic skies and lush green landscapes offer unique opportunities.

Events & Festivals

Koh Samui hosts several major events in 2026 that can influence your travel timing:

  • January: Samui Regatta – Held in Chaweng Bay, this international sailing competition attracts yachts from across Asia. Dates: January 10–17, 2026.
  • February: Chinese New Year – Celebrated with dragon parades and fireworks in Fisherman’s Village, Bophut. Date: February 17, 2026 (Year of the Horse).
  • April: Songkran (Thai New Year) – Water fights and religious ceremonies take over the streets of Lamai and Chaweng. Dates: April 13–15, 2026.
  • June: Samui International Music Festival – A three-day event featuring live bands and DJs at Lamai Beach. Dates: June 12–14, 2026.
  • September: Samui Food Festival – Culinary showcases with local and international chefs at Central Festival Samui. Dates: September 18–20, 2026.
  • October: Vegetarian Festival – A nine-day event with street processions and strict vegetarian diets. Dates: October 12–20, 2026.
  • November: Loy Krathong – Floating lanterns and krathong (decorated baskets) are released into the sea. Date: November 14, 2026.
  • December: Samui Marathon – A full marathon, half-marathon, and fun run along the coastal roads. Date: December 20, 2026.

Conclusion

For the best overall experience in 2026, target February and March. These months offer near-perfect weather: 24–23 sunny days, low humidity (70–75%), water temperatures of 27–28°C, and minimal precipitation (20–30mm). This window also avoids the peak holiday crowds of December–January and the summer rush of June–August, ensuring a balance of good weather and reasonable prices.

If you seek cultural immersion, plan for April (Songkran) or November (Loy Krathong), accepting higher humidity and occasional rain. Budget travelers should consider late October or early December, when prices are lowest but weather risks are manageable. Avoid November unless you are comfortable with significant rainfall and potential travel disruptions.

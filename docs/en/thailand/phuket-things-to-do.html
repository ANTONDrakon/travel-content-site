15 Best Things to Do in Phuket 2026

Phuket, Thailand’s largest island, offers a vibrant mix of pristine beaches, cultural landmarks, and adrenaline-pumping activities. In 2026, the island has refined its attractions with upgraded infrastructure and sustainable tourism initiatives. From the iconic Big Buddha to hidden coves in the Andaman Sea, Phuket delivers experiences for every traveler. This guide covers the top 15 attractions, detailed itineraries, and practical tips to maximize your visit.

Why Visit Phuket for Its Attractions

Phuket’s appeal lies in its diversity. You can explore ancient temples in the morning, snorkel with tropical fish at midday, and enjoy world-class dining by sunset. The island’s 2026 season features reduced crowds due to improved reservation systems and new eco-friendly zones. Average temperatures hover around 28°C (82°F) from November to April, making it ideal for outdoor activities. With over 30 beaches, a UNESCO-listed old town, and marine parks, Phuket is a destination where natural beauty meets cultural richness.

15 Best Attractions

1. Big Buddha (Wat Phra Sing)

Standing at 45 meters tall on Nakkerd Hill, the Big Buddha offers panoramic views of Phuket Town and the western coast. Constructed with Burmese marble, this 2004-completed statue remains a spiritual hub. In 2026, visitors can join guided meditation sessions at 10:00 AM daily. The site is free to enter, but donations of 50–100 THB are appreciated for maintenance. Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees).

Ticket PriceFree (donations welcome)
Hours6:00 AM – 6:30 PM
How to Get ThereTake a tuk-tuk from Phuket Town (200 THB) or rent a scooter (250 THB/day). The road is steep; taxis cost 500 THB round-trip.
TipVisit at sunset for golden light and fewer crowds. Bring water—no shops at the summit.

2. Patong Beach

Phuket’s most famous beach stretches 3 kilometers along the west coast. By 2026, Patong has implemented a plastic-free zone and new beach volleyball courts. The promenade features night markets, street food stalls, and live music. Entry is free, but sunbed rentals cost 100–200 THB per day. For a quieter experience, head to the northern end near Kalim Beach.

Ticket PriceFree
Hours24/7 (lifeguards on duty 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM)
How to Get ThereSongthaew from Phuket Town (40 THB) or taxi from airport (600 THB).
TipVisit on weekdays to avoid weekend crowds. Try grilled seafood at Bangla Road stalls.

3. Old Phuket Town (Thalang Road)

This UNESCO Creative City district showcases Sino-Portuguese architecture with colorful shophouses dating back to the 19th century. In 2026, the area hosts weekly walking tours (every Saturday at 9:00 AM) focusing on local history and street art. Entry is free. The Phuket Thai Hua Museum (200 THB) and the Soi Romanee alley are must-sees.

Ticket PriceFree (museum entrance 200 THB)
HoursShops open 10:00 AM – 9:00 PM
How to Get ThereWalk from Phuket Town bus station (10 minutes) or take a tuk-tuk (50 THB).
TipTry local desserts at the Sunday Walking Street Market (4:00 PM – 10:00 PM).

4. Phi Phi Islands (Day Trip)

The Phi Phi archipelago, 45 minutes by speedboat, features Maya Bay (closed for restoration until 2027) and stunning limestone cliffs. In 2026, only licensed tour operators with eco-certification can access the islands. A typical day trip costs 1,500–2,500 THB per person, including snorkeling gear and lunch. The best snorkeling spots are at Bamboo Island and Monkey Beach.

Ticket Price1,500–2,500 THB (tour inclusive)
HoursDepartures at 8:00 AM, return at 4:00 PM
How to Get ThereBook through Rassada Pier (tours include pickup from Patong, Kata, or Karon).
TipBring reef-safe sunscreen and a waterproof bag. Avoid monsoon season (May–October).

5. Wat Chalong Temple

Phuket’s largest Buddhist temple, built in 1876, houses the revered Luang Pho Chaem statue. In 2026, the temple complex includes a new meditation hall and a museum detailing the 19th-century tin mining era. Entry is free, but donations for incense (20 THB) are common. The grand chedi (stupa) offers 360-degree views after climbing 60 steps.

Ticket PriceFree
Hours7:00 AM – 5:00 PM
How to Get ThereBus from Phuket Town (30 THB) or taxi (300 THB).
TipAttend the morning chanting at 6:30 AM for a serene experience.

6. Promthep Cape

This southern headland offers the best sunset views on the island. In 2026, a new viewing platform with glass barriers has been installed. The nearby lighthouse (free entry) houses a small museum. Arrive by 5:30 PM to secure a spot. Entry is free, but parking costs 50 THB for scooters.

Ticket PriceFree
Hours24/7 (best at sunset)
How to Get ThereTaxi from Rawai Beach (150 THB) or drive 20 minutes from Phuket Town.
TipBring a flashlight for the walk down after dark. Avoid weekends for fewer crowds.

7. James Bond Island (Phang Nga Bay)

Famous from the 1974 film “The Man with the Golden Gun,” this limestone karst rises from emerald waters. In 2026, boat tours include kayaking through sea caves and mangrove forests. A full-day tour costs 1,200–2,000 THB, including lunch and hotel transfer. The island itself is a national park, with a 300 THB entry fee.

Ticket Price300 THB (national park fee) + 1,200–2,000 THB (tour)
HoursTours depart 8:00 AM – 9:00 AM
How to Get ThereBook via Phuket tour agencies (pickup included).
TipVisit during low tide (check tide charts) to see the island’s base.

8. Kata Noi Beach

A quieter alternative to Patong, Kata Noi offers soft white sand and gentle waves, ideal for families. In 2026, the beach has a new boardwalk and a designated swimming area with buoys. Entry is free; umbrella rentals cost 150 THB. The nearby Kata Viewpoint provides photo opportunities.

Ticket PriceFree
Hours24/7 (lifeguards 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM)
How to Get ThereTaxi from Patong (300 THB) or walk from Kata Beach (15 minutes).
TipArrive before 9:00 AM to claim a spot. Try the coconut ice cream from beach vendors.

9. Phuket Elephant Sanctuary

This ethical sanctuary rescues elephants from logging and tourism. In 2026, the sanctuary offers half-day visits (2,500 THB) and full-day programs (4,000 THB) with feeding and mud baths. No riding is allowed. Advance booking is required via their website. The sanctuary is in the hills near Paklok.

Ticket Price2,500–4,000 THB
Hours8:00 AM – 12:00 PM or 1:00 PM – 5:00 PM
How to Get ThereFree shuttle from Phuket Town (booked in advance).
TipWear long pants and closed shoes. Bring insect repellent.

10. Bangla Road (Patong)

Phuket’s nightlife hub transforms into a pedestrian-only street after 7:00 PM. In 2026, the street features new safety measures with ID checks and designated quiet zones. Bars and clubs stay open until 2:00 AM. Entry is free, but drinks cost 100–300 THB. The street is best for adults.

Ticket PriceFree
Hours7:00 PM – 2:00 AM
How to Get ThereWalk from Patong Beach (5 minutes).
TipVisit on a Tuesday or Wednesday for smaller crowds.

11. Coral Island (Koh Hae)

A 15-minute speedboat ride from Chalong Pier, Coral Island offers pristine beaches and snorkeling. In 2026, the island has a new eco-resort with day-use facilities. A day trip costs 800–1,200 THB, including snorkeling gear. The best coral reefs are at Banana Beach.

Ticket Price800–1,200 THB (tour inclusive)
Hours9:00 AM – 4:00 PM
How to Get ThereBook through Chalong Pier (tours depart every hour).
TipBring your own snorkel mask for hygiene. Avoid feeding the fish.

12. Phuket FantaSea

This cultural theme park in Kamala combines a dinner buffet with a theatrical show featuring elephants, acrobats, and special effects. In 2026, the show has updated its storyline to include Thai folklore. Tickets cost 1,800 THB (show only) or 2,500 THB (with buffet dinner). Pickup from major beaches is included.

Ticket Price1,800–2,500 THB
Hours5:30 PM – 11:00 PM (show at 9:00 PM)
How to Get ThereFree shuttle from Patong, Kata, and Karon.
TipBook online for a 10% discount. Arrive early for the carnival games.

13. Surin Beach

Known for its calm waters and luxury resorts, Surin Beach is a favorite for sunbathing and swimming. In 2026, the beach has a new boardwalk with cafes and yoga platforms. Entry is free; loungers cost 200 THB. The nearby Laem Singh viewpoint offers a stunning panorama.

Ticket PriceFree
Hours24/7
How to Get ThereTaxi from Patong (400 THB) or bus from Phuket Town (50 THB).
TipVisit between November and April for calm seas.

14. Gibbon Rehabilitation Centre

Located in the Khao Phra Thaeo National Park, this center rescues and rehabilitates white-handed gibbons. In 2026, the center offers guided tours (200 THB) at 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM. Visitors can observe the gibbons in semi-wild enclosures. The park also features a 3-kilometer nature trail.

Ticket Price200 THB
Hours9:00 AM – 4:00 PM
How to Get ThereTaxi from Phuket Town (250 THB) or songthaew (50 THB).
TipDon’t bring food—gibbons are on a strict diet.

15. Splash Jungle Water Park

This water park near Mai Khao Beach features slides, a lazy river, and a wave pool. In 2026, a new “Aqua Loop” slide has been added. Day passes cost 1,200 THB for adults and 800 THB for children. The park is open from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Lockers rent for 100 THB.

Ticket Price1,200 THB (adult), 800 THB (child)
Hours10:00 AM – 6:00 PM
How to Get ThereFree shuttle from Mai Khao Beach hotels or taxi from airport (200 THB).
TipBring water shoes—paths can get hot.

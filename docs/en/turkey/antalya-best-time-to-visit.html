Best Time to Visit Antalya 2026: Seasons & Weather

Antalya, the jewel of the Turkish Riviera, enjoys a quintessential Mediterranean climate characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, rainy winters. Nestled along the turquoise coast, it offers over 300 days of sunshine annually, making it a year-round destination. However, the best time to visit depends entirely on your priorities: sunbathing, swimming, sightseeing, or budget travel. This guide breaks down Antalya’s weather by month, season, and activity for 2026, providing you with precise data to plan your perfect trip.

General Climate Information

Antalya’s climate is classified as hot-summer Mediterranean (Csa under the Köppen system). The city is sheltered by the Taurus Mountains to the north, which trap heat and block cold air masses, resulting in exceptionally warm sea temperatures for much of the year. Summer months (June–September) are intensely hot, with average highs often exceeding 35°C (95°F). Winters (December–February) are mild but wet, with average highs around 15–18°C (59–64°F). The sea water temperature remains swimmable from late April through early November, a unique advantage for a Mediterranean resort.

High Season

Antalya’s high season runs from June through August, with a secondary peak in early September. During these months, the city is at its busiest. Temperatures regularly exceed 35°C (95°F), and humidity can make it feel even hotter. The sea temperature peaks at 29°C (84°F) in August, offering perfect swimming conditions. Despite the heat and crowds, this is the prime time for beachgoers and nightlife enthusiasts, with beach clubs and open-air venues operating at full capacity.

Low Season

The low season spans November through March. Weather is cool and wet: December and January are the rainiest months, with occasional heavy downpours and cooler temperatures averaging 15°C (59°F) during the day. Snow is extremely rare in the city but can be seen on the Taurus Mountains. The downside is that many beach clubs, water parks, and seasonal restaurants close from November to March. However, the city center, historical sites like Hadrian’s Gate, and the Antalya Museum remain open. For travelers seeking a quiet, cultural experience with excellent value, the low season is ideal.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

For a classic beach holiday, the optimal months are June through October. The sea temperature exceeds 21°C (70°F) from May onward and remains above 24°C (75°F) until late October. The peak swimming season is July, August, and September, when water temperatures hover between 27°C and 29°C (81–84°F). If you prefer less intense heat, late May and early June offer pleasant air temperatures (28–32°C / 82–90°F) and a sea temperature of 22–25°C (72–77°F), which is still comfortable for swimming. October also provides warm sea water (24°C / 75°F) with fewer crowds and lower prices, making it an excellent shoulder month for beach lovers.

Best Time for Sightseeing

Sightseeing in Antalya is most comfortable during the spring (April–May) and autumn (September–October). During these months, daytime temperatures range from 23°C to 28°C (73–82°F), making walking through the old town (Kaleiçi) or exploring the nearby ruins of Perge, Aspendos, and Termessos pleasant. Summer sightseeing is possible but requires early morning or late afternoon visits to avoid the midday heat, which can exceed 40°C (104°F) in July and August. The winter months (November–February) are also good for sightseeing, as the cooler weather allows for comfortable exploration of the city’s archaeological sites, though you may need a light jacket or umbrella.

Rainy Season

Antalya’s rainy season runs from November to March, with the heaviest rainfall in December (average 243 mm) and January (229 mm). Rain usually comes in short, intense showers rather than continuous drizzle. On average, you can expect 12–14 rainy days per month during this period. Despite the rain, the city still enjoys around 12–14 sunny days per month in winter. Is it worth visiting? Absolutely, if you are interested in history, shopping, or culinary experiences. The rain rarely lasts all day, and when the sun emerges, the landscape is lush and green. The reduced tourist numbers also mean better access to indoor attractions like the Antalya Aquarium or the Sandland sculpture park.

Events & Festivals

Antalya hosts a variety of cultural and sporting events throughout the year. Here are the major ones by month:

  • January–February: Antalya International Film Festival (usually in February) – showcases Turkish and international cinema.
  • March: Antalya Marathon – a major running event along the coast.
  • April: Antalya Flower Festival – the city’s parks and streets bloom with floral displays.
  • June: Antalya International Piano Festival – classical music performances at historic venues.
  • July: Aspendos Opera and Ballet Festival – world-class performances at the ancient Aspendos Theatre.
  • September: Antalya Golden Orange Film Festival – Turkey’s premier film event, attracting international stars.
  • October: Antalya International Folk Music Festival – traditional music and dance from around the globe.
  • December: New Year’s Eve celebrations in Kaleiçi, with live music and fireworks.

Conclusion

In summary, the best time to visit Antalya in 2026 depends on your preferences:

  • For the perfect beach vacation: June, July, August, and September offer the warmest sea and air temperatures. July and August are peak heat and crowds, while June and September provide a balance of warm weather and slightly lower prices.
  • For sightseeing and comfort: April, May, September, and October are ideal, with mild temperatures and fewer tourists.
  • For budget travel and peace: November through March offer the lowest prices, though you’ll need to pack for rain and cooler weather. The sea is too cold for swimming (below 18°C / 64°F).
  • For cultural events: Plan around the Aspendos Opera Festival (July) or Golden Orange Film Festival (September).

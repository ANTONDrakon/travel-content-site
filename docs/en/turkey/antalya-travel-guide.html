Antalya Travel Guide 2026

Antalya, the gem of Turkey's Turquoise Coast, is a city where ancient history meets modern luxury. Nestled between the soaring Taurus Mountains and the crystal-clear Mediterranean Sea, this metropolis of over 1.3 million residents offers a unique blend of Roman ruins, Ottoman architecture, and world-class resorts. The city's atmosphere is a dynamic mix of laid-back beach culture and bustling bazaar energy, where the scent of pine forests mingles with the salt of the sea. In 2026, Antalya continues to draw over 15 million tourists annually, making it the most visited city in Turkey.

Why visit Antalya in 2026? Beyond the obvious sun-and-sand appeal, the city has invested heavily in cultural infrastructure. The newly expanded Antalya Museum now houses over 5,000 artifacts spanning 2,000 years of history. The Kaleiçi (Old Town) has undergone a sensitive restoration, with its narrow cobblestone streets now featuring over 200 boutique hotels, art galleries, and rooftop bars. For adventure seekers, the 2026 season introduces new zipline courses in the Düden Waterfalls area and upgraded hiking trails in the Olympos-Beydağları National Park. The city also hosts the Antalya Film Festival each October, a major event attracting international cinema stars.

Perhaps the most compelling reason to visit is the value. Despite inflation in Turkey, Antalya remains remarkably affordable compared to other Mediterranean destinations. A three-course dinner for two with local wine costs around 1,200 Turkish Lira (approximately $45 USD), while a week-long all-inclusive package at a four-star hotel can be found for under $600 per person. The city's international airport has direct flights from 120 cities, making it a convenient hub for European, Middle Eastern, and Asian travelers. Whether you are a history buff exploring the ancient city of Perge, a family splashing in the Konyaaltı Beach waves, or a couple watching the sunset from the ancient Hadrian's Gate, Antalya delivers an unforgettable experience.

How to Get There

Antalya Airport (AYT) is the primary gateway, located 13 kilometers northeast of the city center. In 2026, the airport operates two terminals: Terminal 1 for international flights and Terminal 2 for domestic. Over 200 airlines serve AYT, including Turkish Airlines, Pegasus, SunExpress, and numerous European low-cost carriers.

From major European cities, average round-trip flight prices in 2026 are as follows:

  • London (LHR/STN): $180-350 (low season) / $350-600 (high season)
  • Berlin (BER): $150-300 / $300-550
  • Paris (CDG): $200-400 / $400-700
  • Moscow (SVO): $250-450 / $450-750 (direct flights resumed in 2025)
  • Dubai (DXB): $300-500 / $500-800
  • Istanbul (IST): $50-120 (domestic, 1-hour flight)

Flight time from Istanbul is 1 hour 15 minutes; from London, 4 hours; from Berlin, 3 hours 15 minutes. Booking 8-10 weeks in advance typically yields the best prices. The airport has a new metro connection (opened 2024) that reaches the city center in 25 minutes for 15 TL ($0.55 USD).

Top 15 Attractions

  1. Hadrian's Gate - A triumphal arch built in 130 AD to honor Emperor Hadrian. Free entry, accessible 24/7. Located at the entrance to Kaleiçi.
  2. Kaleiçi (Old Town) - A maze of Ottoman-era houses, boutique shops, and rooftop restaurants. Free to wander. Best explored in the early morning or evening.
  3. Antalya Museum - One of Turkey's largest museums, with 13 exhibition halls covering Paleolithic to Ottoman periods. Ticket: 150 TL ($5.50). Hours: 8:30 AM-7:30 PM (April-October), 8:30 AM-5:30 PM (November-March).
  4. Düden Waterfalls - Two sets of waterfalls: Upper Düden (park, picnic area) and Lower Düden (plunges into the Mediterranean). Upper Düden ticket: 20 TL ($0.75). Open 8 AM-9 PM.
  5. Konyaaltı Beach - A 7-kilometer pebble beach with Blue Flag status. Free entry. Sunbeds and umbrellas rent for 50 TL ($1.80) per day.
  6. Lara Beach - Sandy beach with luxury resorts. Public access free. Known for its sand sculpture festival (May-October).
  7. Perge Ancient City - 18 kilometers east, one of the best-preserved Roman cities in Turkey. Ticket: 100 TL ($3.70). Hours: 8 AM-7 PM.
  8. Aspendos Theatre - The best-preserved Roman theatre in the world, seating 15,000. Ticket: 120 TL ($4.40). Hours: 8 AM-7:30 PM.
  9. Termessos - A mountaintop ancient city at 1,050 meters elevation. Ticket: 80 TL ($3). Hours: 8 AM-6 PM. Requires a 30-minute hike.
  10. Olympos & Çıralı Beach - Ancient Lycian city ruins next to a pristine beach. Combined ticket: 100 TL ($3.70). Open 8 AM-7 PM. Famous for the eternal Chimaera flames.
  11. Karain Cave - A prehistoric cave 27 kilometers northwest, inhabited 500,000 years ago. Ticket: 60 TL ($2.20). Hours: 9 AM-5 PM.
  12. Antalya Aquarium - One of the largest tunnel aquariums in the world (131 meters). Ticket: 200 TL ($7.40). Open 10 AM-10 PM.
  13. Tünektepe Teleferik (Cable Car) - Rides to 618 meters for panoramic views. Round trip: 120 TL ($4.40). Hours: 10 AM-11 PM.
  14. Kurşunlu Waterfall - 18 kilometers east, a 18-meter cascade in a forested park. Ticket: 30 TL ($1.10). Open 8 AM-8 PM.
  15. Yivli Minaret Mosque - A 13th-century Seljuk mosque with a fluted minaret. Free entry (outside prayer times). Open 8 AM-6 PM.

Getting Around

Antalya has an efficient public transport system. The AntRay light rail (tram) runs from the airport to the city center (25 minutes, 15 TL). Two tram lines serve the city: T1 (Museum-Kepez) and T2 (Airport-Zerdalilik). Buses operated by Antalya Ulaşım cover all neighborhoods; single fare is 12 TL ($0.45) with an Antalyakart (rechargeable card, cost 25 TL for card).

Taxis are plentiful but can be expensive. Flagfall is 20 TL ($0.75), then 12 TL per kilometer. A ride from the airport to Kaleiçi costs approximately 200 TL ($7.40). Uber and BiTaksi apps work in Antalya. For car rental, prices start at 500 TL ($18.50) per day for a small car (Fiat Egea). Parking in the city center costs 20-50 TL per hour. Scooter rental (e.g., through Martı app) costs 5 TL per minute. Dolmuş (shared minibuses) are cheap at 10 TL per ride but follow fixed routes.

Where to Eat

Antalyan cuisine is a rich blend of Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and Turkish flavors. Local specialties include piyaz (white bean salad with tahini), şiş köfte (grilled meatballs), and dondurma (stretchy ice cream). Seafood is abundant, with fresh fish from the Mediterranean.

Visa & Documents

For Russian citizens, Turkey offers visa-free entry for stays up to 60 days. You need a valid passport (minimum 6 months validity from entry date). No visa is required for tourism or business purposes. This policy is in effect as of 2026. For other nationalities: citizens of the UK, EU, USA, Canada, Australia, and Japan can enter visa-free for up to 90 days in a 180-day period. Citizens of India, China, and South Africa require an e-Visa ($50 USD, available at evisa.gov.tr, processed in 24 hours). Always carry a printed copy of your return flight ticket and hotel reservation.

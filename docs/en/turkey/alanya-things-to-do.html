15 Best Things to Do in Alanya 2026

Alanya, a vibrant resort city on Turkey’s Mediterranean coast, is a destination where ancient history meets modern leisure. With its iconic peninsula crowned by a Seljuk castle, golden beaches stretching for miles, and a bustling harbor filled with pirate ships, Alanya offers a wealth of attractions for every traveler. In 2026, the city continues to evolve, with improved infrastructure, new cultural events, and enhanced visitor experiences. Whether you’re a history buff exploring Roman ruins, a water sports enthusiast diving into turquoise coves, or a family seeking sun-soaked relaxation, Alanya delivers unforgettable experiences. This guide details the 15 best things to do, with practical information on prices, hours, and transport, plus optimized itineraries to help you make the most of your trip.

15 Best Attractions in Alanya

1. Alanya Castle (Alanya Kalesi)

Perched on a 250-meter-high rocky peninsula, Alanya Castle is a 13th-century Seljuk fortress that dominates the city skyline. Inside, you’ll find the ruins of a Byzantine church, a cistern, and the stunning Süleymaniye Mosque, while the panoramic views of the Mediterranean and Cleopatra Beach are breathtaking. The castle complex includes the inner fortress (Içkale), the Shipyard (Tersane), and the Red Tower (Kızıl Kule) at its base. In 2026, the castle’s lighting has been upgraded for evening visits, and a new audio guide in Turkish, English, German, and Russian is available.

Ticket price: 120 Turkish Lira (TL) for the castle; 60 TL for the Red Tower (combo ticket available for 150 TL).

Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (summer); 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (winter).

How to get there: Walk up from the harbor (20-30 minutes steep climb), take a taxi from the city center (50-70 TL), or use the cable car (Teleferik) from near the Atatürk Monument (50 TL one-way, 80 TL round trip).

Tip: Visit late afternoon to catch the sunset over the sea; the cable car closes at 6:30 PM, so plan your descent accordingly.

2. Cleopatra Beach (Kleopatra Plajı)

This 2.5-kilometer stretch of golden sand is named after the Egyptian queen who, legend says, swam here. The beach is renowned for its fine, soft sand and clear, shallow waters, making it ideal for families and sunbathers. In 2026, the beach has new sunbed and umbrella rentals (50 TL per set for the day), and lifeguards are on duty from 9:00 AM to 7:00 PM. The promenade behind the beach is lined with cafes, restaurants, and shops.

Ticket price: Free public access; sunbed and umbrella rental 50 TL per day.

Hours: Always open; lifeguards present 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM.

How to get there: A 10-minute walk west from Alanya’s central square (Atatürk Monument); buses from the bus station (Dolmuş) stop at the beach every 10 minutes (5 TL).

Tip: Arrive before 10:00 AM to secure a good spot; the eastern end is quieter than the central area near hotels.

3. Damlataş Cave

Located just behind Cleopatra Beach, Damlataş Cave is a natural wonder known for its therapeutic microclimate, high humidity, and constant 22°C temperature. The cave was discovered in 1948 during construction work and is filled with stalactites and stalagmites formed over 15,000 years. It is particularly recommended for asthma patients due to its air quality, but all visitors can enjoy the eerie beauty of the illuminated chambers. In 2026, the cave has new LED lighting that highlights the mineral formations more vividly.

Ticket price: 40 TL.

Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 8:00 PM (summer); 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (winter).

How to get there: A 2-minute walk from Cleopatra Beach; from the city center, walk 10 minutes west along the beach promenade.

Tip: Spend 15-20 minutes inside for the full therapeutic effect; avoid visiting during peak hours (11:00 AM – 2:00 PM) when it gets crowded.

4. Red Tower (Kızıl Kule)

This iconic octagonal tower, built in 1226 by the Seljuk Sultan Alaeddin Keykubad, stands 33 meters tall at the harbor entrance. It served as a defensive fortification and a dockyard warehouse, and today houses the Alanya Ethnographic Museum on its upper floors. Climbing the 85 steps to the top rewards you with panoramic views of the harbor, castle, and sea. In 2026, the tower’s museum has been expanded with new exhibits on Seljuk maritime history.

Ticket price: 60 TL (included in castle combo ticket).

Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (summer); 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (winter).

How to get there: Located at the harbor, a 5-minute walk from the Atatürk Monument; easily accessible on foot from anywhere in the city center.

Tip: Visit early in the morning to avoid crowds and get the best photos of the harbor without cruise ship crowds.

5. Alanya Shipyard (Tersane)

Built in 1227, this Seljuk-era shipyard is one of the oldest in the Mediterranean, with five vaulted galleries that once housed galleys. The shipyard is located at the base of the castle peninsula, directly adjacent to the Red Tower. Visitors can walk through the arched chambers and see a small exhibition on shipbuilding techniques. In 2026, the shipyard has been restored with interpretive panels in multiple languages.

Ticket price: 30 TL (included in castle combo ticket).

Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (summer); 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (winter).

How to get there: A 3-minute walk from the Red Tower along the harbor; follow the signs from the castle entrance.

Tip: Combine this with the Red Tower and castle for a half-day historical tour; the shipyard is cool inside even on hot days.

6. Alanya Archaeological Museum

This museum, located near the Atatürk Monument, houses a rich collection of artifacts from the Hellenistic, Roman, Byzantine, and Seljuk periods. Highlights include a 2nd-century statue of Hercules, a Roman sarcophagus with intricate reliefs, and a collection of ancient glassware. The museum’s garden features a lapidarium with stone inscriptions and mosaics. In 2026, a new wing dedicated to underwater archaeology has opened, showcasing artifacts from shipwrecks off the Alanya coast.

Ticket price: 30 TL.

Hours: Tuesday–Sunday 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM; closed Monday.

How to get there: A 5-minute walk from the Atatürk Monument on İskele Street; from the bus station, take a Dolmuş to the city center (5 TL).

Tip: Allow 1-2 hours; the museum is small but well-curated; free guided tours in English are offered at 11:00 AM on Saturdays.

7. Dim Cave (Dim Mağarası)

Located 12 kilometers east of Alanya, Dim Cave is one of the largest caves in Turkey, with a walking path of 360 meters through chambers filled with stalactites, stalagmites, and underground lakes. The cave was opened to the public in 1998 and has a constant temperature of 18°C, making it a refreshing escape from summer heat. In 2026, a new lighting system and a small cafe at the entrance have been added.

Ticket price: 50 TL.

Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (summer); 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (winter).

How to get there: Take a Dolmuş from Alanya bus station to the Dim Çayı area (10 TL, 20-minute ride), then walk 15 minutes uphill to the cave entrance; taxis from the city center cost 150-200 TL one-way.

Tip: Wear sturdy shoes as the path can be slippery; combine with a visit to Dim Çayı river for lunch at one of the riverside restaurants.

8. Dim Çayı (Dim River)

This scenic river valley, 10 kilometers east of Alanya, is famous for its crystal-clear, cold water and lush greenery. Numerous restaurants and cafes are built on platforms over the river, where you can enjoy fresh trout while dipping your feet in the water. The area is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, especially in summer. In 2026, the riverbanks have been cleaned and new picnic areas added.

Ticket price: Free access; meals at riverside restaurants range from 100-200 TL per person.

Hours: Restaurants open 10:00 AM – 10:00 PM daily.

How to get there: Take a Dolmuş from Alanya bus station to Dim Çayı (10 TL, 20-minute ride); taxis cost 150-200 TL one-way.

Tip: Visit on a weekday to avoid crowds; bring insect repellent for evenings.

9. Alanya Cable Car (Teleferik)

The cable car runs from near the Atatürk Monument to the top of the castle peninsula, covering a distance of 800 meters with a 200-meter elevation gain. The ride takes about 5 minutes and offers stunning views of the city, harbor, and Mediterranean Sea. In 2026, the cable car has been upgraded with new cabins that have glass floors for a more thrilling experience.

Ticket price: 50 TL one-way, 80 TL round trip.

Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 6:30 PM (summer); 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (winter).

How to get there: The lower station is a 2-minute walk from the Atatürk Monument on the harbor side.

Tip: Buy a round-trip ticket to save money; the cable car can have long queues in peak season (July–August), so go early.

10. Cleopatra Beach Water Sports

Cleopatra Beach is a hub for water sports, including parasailing, jet skiing, banana boat rides, and scuba diving. Several operators offer equipment rental and guided experiences. Parasailing costs 250-350 TL per person for a 15-minute flight, while scuba diving excursions (with gear and instructor) start at 400 TL for a 2-hour session. In 2026, new electric jet skis have been introduced for eco-friendly thrills.

Ticket price: Varies by activity; parasailing 250-350 TL, jet ski 200 TL per 15 minutes, banana boat 100 TL per person.

Hours: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM daily.

How to get there: Any point along Cleopatra Beach; operators have stands with visible signs.

Tip: Negotiate prices, especially in the morning; book parasailing at sunset for spectacular views.

11. Alanya Aquapark

Located near the harbor, Alanya Aquapark is a large water park with slides, wave pools, and lazy rivers suitable for all ages. Major attractions include the 20-meter-high “Kamikaze” slide and a children’s area with mini slides. The park has restaurants, sun loungers, and changing facilities. In 2026, a new “Space Slide” with LED lights has been added.

Ticket price: 200 TL for adults, 120 TL for children under 12.

Hours: Daily 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM (summer only; closed November–March).

How to get there: A 15-minute walk from the city center along the harbor road; free shuttle buses from major hotels.

Tip: Bring your own towel and sunscreen; lockers rent for 20 TL.

12. Sapadere Canyon (Sapadere Kanyonu)

Located 40 kilometers east of Alanya, Sapadere Canyon is a natural wonder with a 750-meter-long wooden walkway through a narrow gorge, past waterfalls, pools, and lush vegetation. The canyon is a popular spot for hiking, swimming, and photography. In 2026, a new suspension bridge has been added over the main waterfall, offering thrilling views.

Ticket price: 30 TL.

Hours: Daily 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (summer); 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (winter).

How to get there: Join a guided tour from Alanya (from 300 TL per person, including transport) or drive/take a taxi (400-500 TL round trip).

Tip: Wear water shoes as the walkway can be wet; bring a swimsuit to swim in the natural pools.

13. Alanya Harbor (Marina)

The modern marina, rebuilt in 2010, is a lively area with yachts, fishing boats, and pirate-themed excursion ships. The harbor promenade is lined with restaurants, bars, and souvenir shops, and offers views of the Red Tower and castle. In 2026, a new floating stage hosts live music and dance performances every evening in summer.

Ticket price: Free to walk around; boat tours start from 150 TL per person.

Hours: Always open; boat tours operate 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM.

How to get there: A 5-minute walk from the Atatürk Monument; accessible from anywhere in the city center.

Tip: Visit at sunset for the best atmosphere; try a fresh fish sandwich from harbor-side vendors (30-40 TL).

14. Alanya Night Market (Alanya Gece Pazarı)

Held every Tuesday evening near the bus station, this market features hundreds of stalls selling textiles, leather goods, spices, Turkish delight, and souvenirs. It’s a vibrant cultural experience with local food stalls offering gözleme, kebabs, and baklava. In 2026, the market has expanded with a dedicated section for handmade crafts and live Turkish music.

Ticket price: Free entry.

Hours: Tuesdays 6:00 PM – 11:00 PM.

How to get there: A 10-minute walk from the city center toward the bus station; Dolmuş from anywhere in Alanya (5 TL).

Tip: Bargain politely; start at 50% of the asking price; try the fresh pomegranate juice (10 TL).

15. İncekum Beach

Located 15 kilometers west of Alanya, İncekum Beach is a quieter alternative to Cleopatra Beach, with fine sand and shallow, calm waters. The beach is part of a nature reserve, with dunes and pine forests behind it. In 2026, new eco-friendly sunbed rentals and a boardwalk have been installed.

Ticket price: Free public access; sunbed rental 30 TL.

Hours: Always open; lifeguards present 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM.

How to get there: Take a Dolmuş from Alanya bus station to İncekum (8 TL, 20-minute ride); taxis cost 200 TL one-way.

Tip: Bring your own food and drinks as facilities are limited; perfect for a peaceful day away from crowds.

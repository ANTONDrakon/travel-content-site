15 Best Things to Do in Antalya 2026

Antalya, the pearl of the Turkish Riviera, is a city where ancient history meets turquoise waters and dramatic mountain backdrops. In 2026, the city continues to captivate travelers with its well-preserved Roman harbor, cascading waterfalls, and world-class museums. Whether you are a history buff, a beach lover, or a food enthusiast, Antalya offers a diverse range of attractions that justify its reputation as one of the Mediterranean’s top destinations. This guide covers the 15 best things to do, complete with practical details on prices, hours, and transport, plus optimized itineraries for 1 to 3 days.

15 Best Attractions

1. Kaleiçi (Old Town)

Kaleiçi is the historic heart of Antalya, a maze of narrow cobblestone streets lined with Ottoman-era houses, boutique hotels, and lively cafes. The district is anchored by the ancient Hadrian’s Gate, built in 130 AD to honor Emperor Hadrian. You can spend hours exploring hidden courtyards, art galleries, and the marina, where fishing boats bob alongside sleek yachts. Ticket price: Free. Hours: Always open. How to get there: From the city center, take tram T1 to "İsmetpaşa" stop, then walk 5 minutes. Tip: Visit early morning (7–9 AM) to avoid crowds and photograph the empty streets.

2. Antalya Museum

One of Turkey’s most important museums, the Antalya Museum houses over 5,000 artifacts spanning the Paleolithic to Ottoman eras. Highlights include the "Weeping Heracles" sarcophagus and the Hall of Gods with stunning marble statues from Perge. The museum is a must for understanding the region’s Lycian, Roman, and Byzantine history. Ticket price: 250 TL (approx. $8 USD). Hours: Daily 8:30 AM–6:30 PM (closed Mondays). How to get there: Take tram T1 to "Müze" stop. Tip: Rent an audio guide (50 TL) for deeper context on the Hall of Emperors.

3. Düden Waterfalls

The Düden Waterfalls consist of two parts: the Upper Düden, a series of cascades in a lush park, and the Lower Düden, which plunges 40 meters directly into the Mediterranean Sea. The lower falls offer a spectacular view from a cliffside restaurant, especially at sunset. Upper Düden is ideal for a picnic and a cool escape from the summer heat. Ticket price: Free (Upper Düden); Lower Düden park entrance 20 TL. Hours: Upper Düden open 24 hours; Lower Düden 8 AM–10 PM. How to get there: For Upper Düden, take bus KL08 from the city center; for Lower Düden, take bus KC07 to "Lara" district. Tip: Combine both in one afternoon—start with Upper Düden for a walk, then head to Lower Düden for dinner at the waterfall terrace.

4. Hadrian’s Gate (Üçkapılar)

This triple-arched marble gate is the only remaining entrance to the ancient city of Attaleia. Built in 130 AD, it features ornate Corinthian columns and reliefs of flowers and medallions. It serves as the main entrance to Kaleiçi and is a favorite photo spot. Ticket price: Free. Hours: Always accessible. How to get there: Directly at the junction of Atatürk Boulevard and Kaleiçi. Tip: Walk through the gate at golden hour (6–7 PM) for dramatic light on the stone.

5. Karaalioğlu Parkı

A sprawling 70-hectare coastal park offering panoramic views of the Mediterranean and the Taurus Mountains. It features walking paths, playgrounds, and a historic tea garden. The park is perfect for a relaxing afternoon, with benches shaded by pine and palm trees. Ticket price: Free. Hours: 24 hours. How to get there: Walk 10 minutes south from the Antalya Museum. Tip: Bring binoculars to spot the distant Olympos mountain range.

6. Antalya Aquarium

One of the world’s largest aquarium complexes, featuring a 131-meter-long tunnel aquarium with over 40 thematic exhibits. You can see sharks, stingrays, and tropical fish up close. The complex also includes a snow world and a tropical reptile house. Ticket price: 400 TL (adult), 250 TL (child). Hours: Daily 10 AM–7 PM. How to get there: Take tram T1 to "Konyaaltı" stop, then walk 10 minutes. Tip: Book online for a 10% discount and avoid weekend queues.

7. Konyaaltı Beach

A pebbly, Blue Flag-certified beach stretching 7 kilometers along the western coast. It offers clear water, sun loungers, and a lively promenade with cafes and bars. The beach is divided into public and private sections, with the latter offering umbrellas and showers. Ticket price: Free public access; sun lounger rental 50 TL. Hours: 24 hours (lifeguards 9 AM–7 PM). How to get there: Tram T1 to "Konyaaltı" stop, then walk 5 minutes. Tip: Arrive by 9 AM to secure a spot near the water; the beach gets crowded by noon.

8. Tünektepe Cable Car

Ride the cable car 618 meters up Tünektepe Hill for a bird’s-eye view of Antalya’s coastline and the Bey Mountains. The summit has a restaurant, observation deck, and a small amusement park. The 8-minute ride is especially scenic at sunset. Ticket price: 300 TL (round trip). Hours: Daily 10 AM–11 PM (last ascent 10:15 PM). How to get there: Take bus KL08 to "Tünektepe" stop. Tip: Go on a clear day for visibility up to 50 km; the restaurant serves decent döner.

9. Perge Ancient City

Located 18 kilometers east of Antalya, Perge was one of the most important cities of ancient Pamphylia. The ruins include a well-preserved Roman theater, a stadium, colonnaded streets, and a massive agora. It offers a glimpse into urban life in the 2nd century AD. Ticket price: 180 TL. Hours: Daily 8 AM–7 PM (summer), 8 AM–5 PM (winter). How to get there: Take bus AC03 from Antalya bus station (Otogar) to "Perge" stop (30 minutes). Tip: Combine with Aspendos (30 minutes by taxi) for a half-day archaeological tour.

10. Aspendos Roman Theater

The best-preserved Roman theater in the world, built in 155 AD under Emperor Marcus Aurelius. It seats 15,000 spectators and still hosts annual opera and ballet festivals. The acoustics are legendary—a whisper on stage can be heard in the top rows. Ticket price: 200 TL. Hours: Daily 8 AM–7 PM. How to get there: Take bus AC03 from Otogar to "Aspendos" stop (40 minutes). Tip: Visit during the Aspendos International Opera and Ballet Festival (June–July) for a magical experience.

11. Olympos Beach & Chimaera

Olympos is a Lycian coastal city with ruins tangled in jungle, plus a pebble beach. Nearby, the Chimaera (Yanartaş) is a hillside where natural methane vents produce eternal flames. It is a unique geological phenomenon. Ticket price: Olympos entrance 150 TL; Chimaera 100 TL. Hours: Olympos 8 AM–7 PM; Chimaera 8 AM–11 PM (best visited at dusk). How to get there: Take a bus from Antalya to Çıralı (2 hours), then walk 20 minutes. Tip: Wear sturdy shoes for the 15-minute uphill hike to Chimaera; bring a flashlight.

12. Termessos Ancient City

Perched at 1,050 meters in the Taurus Mountains, Termessos is a "city of eagles" with dramatic ruins including a theater, gymnasium, and tombs. It was never conquered by Alexander the Great due to its remote location. Ticket price: 120 TL. Hours: Daily 8 AM–6 PM. How to get there: Take bus KL08 from Antalya to "Termessos" turnoff, then a 5 km walk or taxi. Tip: Bring water and sunscreen; the hike is steep but rewards with incredible views.

13. Antalya Archaeological Museum (smaller branch in Kaleiçi)

A smaller, free museum inside a historic Ottoman mansion in Kaleiçi, showcasing local artifacts from the Bronze Age. It is less crowded than the main museum but offers a curated selection. Ticket price: Free. Hours: Tuesday–Sunday 9 AM–5 PM. How to get there: Inside Kaleiçi, near the marina. Tip: Combine with a walk to the nearby Hıdırlık Tower for a panoramic view.

14. Hıdırlık Tower

A 14-meter-high Roman tower built in the 2nd century AD, once part of the city walls. It offers a 360-degree view of Kaleiçi, the marina, and the sea. The interior has a small exhibition on its history. Ticket price: 30 TL. Hours: Daily 9 AM–6 PM. How to get there: At the southern tip of Kaleiçi, near Karaalioğlu Parkı. Tip: Go at sunset for photos of the city glowing in golden light.

15. Antalya Zoo (Antalya Hayvanat Bahçesi)

Home to over 1,000 animals from 200 species, including lions, bears, and exotic birds, set in a 200-hectare woodland park. The zoo is well-maintained with spacious enclosures. Ticket price: 100 TL. Hours: Daily 9 AM–7 PM. How to get there: Take bus KL08 to "Zoo" stop. Tip: Visit on weekdays to avoid school groups; the zoo has a good playground for kids.

1-Day, 2-Day, 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: Historic Core & Waterfalls

Start at 8 AM with a walk through Kaleiçi, visiting Hadrian’s Gate and Hıdırlık Tower. By 10 AM, head to the Antalya Museum (2 hours). At noon, take a taxi to Upper Düden Waterfalls for a picnic lunch. At 2 PM, visit Lower Düden for the cliffside view. From 4 PM, relax at Konyaaltı Beach or Karaalioğlu Parkı. End the day with dinner at a Kaleiçi rooftop restaurant (e.g., Seraser Fine Dining). Total walking: 8 km. Budget: 600 TL (museum + transport + meals).

2-Day Itinerary: Ancient Cities & Coast

Day 1: Follow the 1-day itinerary but skip Upper Düden. Instead, at 2 PM, take a bus to Perge (2 hours), then Aspendos (2 hours). Return to Antalya by 8 PM. Day 2: Start at 9 AM with Tünektepe Cable Car (1 hour). Then, at 11 AM, visit Olympos Beach and Chimaera (half-day trip, returning at 5 PM). End with a sunset stroll along Konyaaltı Beach. Budget: 1,200 TL (tickets + transport + meals).

3-Day Itinerary: Full Exploration

Day 1: Same as 2-day Day 1. Day 2: Full-day trip to Termessos (8 AM–3 PM), then Antalya Aquarium (4–7 PM). Day 3: Morning at the Antalya Museum and Kaleiçi. Afternoon at Olympos Beach (half-day). Evening at a Turkish bath (e.g., Tarihi Kaleiçi Hamamı, 300 TL). Budget: 2,000 TL (tickets + transport + meals + hamam).

Tours & Excursions

For a hassle-free experience, consider guided tours. A full-day "Ancient Cities Tour" covering Perge, Aspendos, and Side costs 1,500 TL per person (including lunch and transport). A "Waterfalls & Olympos Tour" (8 hours) is 1,200 TL. A "Jeep Safari in the Taurus Mountains" (half-day) costs 800 TL. Boat tours from the marina (e.g., "Sunset Cruise with Dinner") are 600 TL for 3 hours. Book through agencies like "Antalya Tours" or "Türkiye Travel" for reliability.

Free Attractions

Antalya offers several free experiences. Walk the entire length of Kaleiçi and the marina (free). Visit Karaalioğlu Parkı for sea views. Explore Upper Düden Waterfalls park (free). Stroll the Konyaaltı Beach promenade (free). See Hadrian’s Gate and Hıdırlık Tower from the outside (free). The Antalya Archaeological Museum branch in Kaleiçi is free. Additionally, the weekly "Antalya Flea Market" on Sundays (near the zoo) has free entry and local crafts.

Practical Tips

Transportation: The Antalya Tram (T1 line) runs from the city center to Konyaaltı Beach and the museum every 10 minutes (single ticket 15 TL, day pass 50 TL). Buses (e.g., KL08, AC03) cover longer routes (20–40 TL per ride). Taxis start at 30 TL per km; use apps like "BiTaksi" for fair pricing. For ancient cities, rent a car (400 TL/day) or join a tour.

Combo Tickets: The "Antalya Museum Pass" (500 TL) covers the Antalya Museum, Perge, Aspendos, and Termessos for 7 days—saves 30% if visiting all four. The "Aquarium + Snow World" combo is 500 TL (vs. 650 TL separately).

Best Time to Visit: April–June and September–October for pleasant weather (20–30°C). July–August is hot (35°C) but beach-friendly. In 2026, expect fewer crowds in March and November.

Money-Saving Tips: Eat at lokantas (local eateries) for meals under 100 TL. Drink tap water (safe in Antalya). Use the AntalyaKart for discounted tram and bus fares (available at kiosks).

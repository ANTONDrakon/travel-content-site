Top 10 Hotels in Antalya 2026: Budget to Luxury

Top 10 Hotels in Antalya 2026: Budget to Luxury

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Antalya, Turkey's premier Mediterranean destination, continues to evolve as a world-class hub for travelers in 2026. The city's hotel infrastructure spans from ultra-modern high-rises along Konyaaltı Beach to restored Ottoman mansions in the historic Kaleiçi district, and sprawling all-inclusive resorts in Lara and Belek. The key neighborhoods each offer a distinct experience: Kaleiçi (the old town) provides narrow cobblestone streets and boutique charm; Lara Beach boasts white sand and luxury resorts; Konyaaltı offers a pebble beach with a vibrant promenade; and Belek is the golf and family resort capital. Prices in 2026 have seen a moderate 8-12% increase from 2025 due to inflation and rising demand, but competitive deals remain available for early bookers. Whether you are seeking a budget-friendly hostel or a palatial five-star retreat, Antalya delivers exceptional value across all categories.

This guide covers the top 10 hotels in Antalya for 2026, carefully selected for their quality, location, and value. We break them down into three price tiers—budget (under $50/night), mid-range ($50–150/night), and luxury ($150+/night)—based on average summer rates. Each entry includes specific pricing, standout features, and honest pros and cons. We also provide neighborhood advice and booking strategies to help you maximize your trip, whether you are here for the turquoise beaches, ancient ruins, or vibrant nightlife.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. Sabah Pansiyon
Located in the heart of Kaleiçi, Sabah Pansiyon is a family-run guesthouse set in a restored 19th-century Ottoman house. Rooms are simple but clean, with traditional wooden ceilings and private bathrooms. The rooftop terrace offers panoramic views of the old harbor and Mediterranean Sea. In 2026, a double room averages $38/night including breakfast. Pros: unbeatable location near Hadrian’s Gate, authentic atmosphere, free Wi-Fi. Cons: no air conditioning in some rooms (fans provided), thin walls, no elevator.

2. Antalya Hostel & Guesthouse
A short walk from Konyaaltı Beach, this modern hostel offers both dormitory beds and private rooms. Dorms start at $15/night, while private doubles cost $42/night. The property features a communal kitchen, a garden with hammocks, and a social lounge. Pros: very affordable, clean facilities, friendly staff, close to public transport. Cons: basic breakfast not included, limited parking, noise from nearby bars on weekends.

3. City Hotel Antalya
Near the Antalya Airport and the Expo Center, this no-frills hotel caters to transit travelers and budget-conscious tourists. Standard double rooms average $45/night in low season, rising to $48 in peak summer. The hotel offers a 24-hour front desk, free airport shuttle, and a small pool. Pros: excellent for early flights, free parking, decent breakfast buffet. Cons: far from beaches and historic sites, dated decor, no restaurant on-site.

Mid-Range Hotels ($50-150/night)

1. Tuvana Hotel
Set in a cluster of restored Ottoman houses in Kaleiçi, Tuvana Hotel blends historic charm with modern comfort. Rooms feature antique furniture, plush bedding, and marble bathrooms. The courtyard pool and lush gardens provide a serene oasis. In 2026, double rooms start at $95/night. Highlights: exceptional location near the marina, excellent Turkish breakfast, free bike rentals. The hotel’s restaurant, Il Vicino, serves authentic Italian cuisine.

2. Crowne Plaza Antalya
Perched on the cliffs of Lara Beach, this 5-star hotel offers direct beach access and panoramic sea views. Standard rooms average $110/night in summer. The property includes multiple pools, a spa with Turkish bath, and four restaurants. Highlights: private beach with sun loungers, kids’ club, free Wi-Fi. The hotel is a 15-minute drive from Antalya city center, making it ideal for those who want a resort experience with easy access to town.

3. Sealife Family Resort Hotel
Located in the Konyaaltı district, this family-friendly hotel is steps from the beach and the Antalya Aquarium. A standard double room costs $78/night. The hotel features a large outdoor pool, a water park with slides, and a playground. Highlights: all-inclusive meal options available for $25/day per person, evening entertainment, free shuttle to the city center. It is particularly popular with European families in July and August.

4. The Land of Legends Kingdom Hotel
Part of the massive Land of Legends theme park in Belek, this hotel offers a fantasy-themed experience. Rooms are whimsically decorated with castle motifs. Prices start at $140/night, including access to the theme park and water park. Highlights: unlimited rides, dolphin shows, and aqua park. The hotel itself has multiple pools, a spa, and several restaurants. Best for families with children aged 4–14.

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

1. Rixos Premium Antalya
This iconic resort on Lara Beach is synonymous with ultra-luxury. Suites and villas start at $280/night in 2026. The property features a private beach, 10 pools (including an adults-only infinity pool), a world-class spa, and 12 restaurants serving cuisines from Turkish to Japanese. Features: butler service, private cabanas, a nightclub, and a kids’ club with professional nannies. The hotel is a destination in itself, with lush gardens and direct access to the beach. It is consistently rated among the top 10 resorts in Turkey.

2. Maxx Royal Belek Golf Resort
Located in the Belek golf district, this all-inclusive resort is a haven for golfers and luxury seekers. Rates begin at $350/night. The resort boasts an 18-hole championship golf course, a 3,000-square-meter spa, and a private beach. Features: multiple à la carte restaurants (including a Michelin-starred chef’s table), a water park, and a dedicated children’s village. The suites are spacious, with separate living areas and marble bathrooms. The service is impeccable, with a staff-to-guest ratio of 2:1.

3. Mardan Palace
Often called the "Versailles of Turkey," Mardan Palace in Lara is a baroque-inspired masterpiece. Rooms start at $220/night. The hotel features a 2,500-meter-long swimming pool, a private marina, and a spa with a snow room. Features: 24-hour room service, a private beach with golden sand imported from Egypt, and a grand lobby with crystal chandeliers. The hotel hosts live concerts and events in summer. It is a top choice for honeymooners and celebrities seeking opulence.

How to Choose the Right Area

For Beach Lovers: Choose Lara Beach or Konyaaltı Beach. Lara offers wide, sandy beaches with luxury resorts like Rixos and Mardan Palace. Konyaaltı has a pebble beach but a lively promenade with cafes and bars. Both areas have easy access to the sea and water sports.

For Sightseeing: Stay in Kaleiçi (old town). You can walk to Hadrian’s Gate, the Yivli Minaret, the Antalya Museum, and the marina. Budget hotels like Sabah Pansiyon and mid-range Tuvana Hotel are ideal. You are also close to public transport for day trips to Perge, Aspendos, and Side.

For Nightlife: The Lara Beach strip and the Konyaaltı promenade are the hubs. Lara has high-end clubs like Club 29 and Inferno. Konyaaltı offers beachfront bars and live music venues. Hotels like Crowne Plaza and Sealife are well-positioned. Avoid Kaleiçi for nightlife unless you prefer quiet rooftop bars.

Booking Tips

To save money on Antalya hotels in 2026, book directly through the hotel’s website or call them. Many properties offer 10–15% discounts for direct bookings and include perks like free breakfast or late checkout. Use price comparison sites like Booking.com or Expedia to check rates, but always verify the cancellation policy—flexible rates are worth the extra 5-10% cost. The best time to book is 4–6 months in advance for summer (June–August) and 2–3 months for spring and fall. Avoid booking during major holidays like Eid al-Adha or the Antalya Film Festival in October, when prices spike. Consider using loyalty programs: Marriott Bonvoy and Hilton Honors have several properties in Antalya, and you can earn points or redeem free nights. Finally, check for package deals that bundle flights and hotels—Turkish Airlines and Pegasus often offer discounts with hotel partners. For luxury stays, email the concierge before booking to ask for upgrades or special packages, especially if you are celebrating a honeymoon or anniversary.

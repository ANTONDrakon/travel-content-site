15 Best Things to Do in Istanbul 2026

Istanbul, the only city in the world straddling two continents, offers an unparalleled blend of history, culture, and modernity. In 2026, the city continues to captivate travelers with its iconic landmarks, vibrant bazaars, and culinary scene. From Byzantine churches to Ottoman mosques, and from bustling ferry rides to serene palaces, Istanbul’s attractions are a testament to its 2,500-year legacy as the capital of empires. This guide covers the 15 best things to do, with practical details for a seamless visit, plus itineraries and tips to maximize your experience.

15 Best Attractions

Each attraction includes a name, description, ticket price (in Turkish Lira, TRY, approximate for 2026), opening hours, how to get there, and a pro tip. Prices may vary; check official sites for updates.

1. Hagia Sophia (Ayasofya)

Hagia Sophia is a masterpiece of Byzantine architecture, originally built as a cathedral in 537 AD, later converted to a mosque, then a museum, and now a functioning mosque again. Its massive dome, soaring 55 meters high, and stunning mosaics of Christ and the Virgin Mary draw millions yearly. In 2026, it remains a symbol of Istanbul’s layered history, with guided tours available to explain its complex past.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry (as a mosque); guided tours cost approximately 500–800 TRY per person.
  • Hours: Open daily, 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (closed during prayer times, especially Friday noon).
  • How to Get There: Take the T1 tram to Sultanahmet station; it’s a 2-minute walk.
  • Tip: Visit early morning (8:30 AM) to avoid crowds and see the mosaics in the upper gallery (accessible via a ramp, not stairs).

2. Blue Mosque (Sultanahmet Camii)

Built between 1609 and 1616, the Blue Mosque is famous for its six minarets and over 20,000 hand-painted blue Iznik tiles. It remains an active mosque, so visitors must dress modestly and remove shoes. The interior’s cascading domes and stained-glass windows create a serene atmosphere, especially during late afternoon light.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Open 24 hours, but closed to tourists during prayer times (5 times daily, about 20 minutes each).
  • How to Get There: Adjacent to Hagia Sophia; walk from Sultanahmet tram stop.
  • Tip: Enter from the north side (not the main courtyard) for a quicker line; avoid Fridays at noon.

3. Topkapi Palace (Topkapı Sarayı)

Home to Ottoman sultans for 400 years, Topkapi Palace is a sprawling complex of courtyards, harem rooms, and treasury exhibits. Highlights include the Spoonmaker’s Diamond (86 carats) and the Sacred Relics (including the Prophet Muhammad’s cloak). In 2026, the palace’s gardens offer panoramic views of the Bosphorus.

  • Ticket Price: Palace: 1,200 TRY; Harem: 400 TRY extra; combined ticket: 1,500 TRY.
  • Hours: Wednesday–Monday, 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM (closed Tuesdays).
  • How to Get There: Walk 10 minutes from Sultanahmet tram stop through Gülhane Park.
  • Tip: Buy the Harem ticket online in advance; it sells out by 11 AM. Spend at least 3 hours here.

4. Basilica Cistern (Yerebatan Sarnıcı)

This subterranean Byzantine reservoir, built in 532 AD, holds 336 marble columns and two Medusa head carvings. The dimly lit walkways and echoing water create a mystical experience. In 2026, the cistern features new LED lighting that highlights the architecture without the previous crowds (limited to 500 visitors at a time).

  • Ticket Price: 600 TRY.
  • Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: 2-minute walk from Hagia Sophia; entrance on Yerebatan Caddesi.
  • Tip: Book a time slot online to skip the 30-minute queue; wear sturdy shoes as floors are wet.

5. Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı)

One of the world’s oldest and largest covered markets, the Grand Bazaar has over 4,000 shops spread across 61 streets. It dates to 1461 and specializes in carpets, jewelry, ceramics, and spices. In 2026, it remains a chaotic but thrilling maze, with vendors expecting haggling.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Monday–Saturday, 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM; closed Sundays.
  • How to Get There: Take T1 tram to Beyazıt-Kapalıçarşı station; follow signs.
  • Tip: Start at the Nuruosmaniye Gate for fewer crowds; avoid buying gold or antiques without a local expert.

6. Spice Bazaar (Mısır Çarşısı)

Built in 1660 as part of the Yeni Mosque complex, the Spice Bazaar is a sensory overload of dried fruits, nuts, Turkish delight, and spices like saffron and sumac. It’s smaller than the Grand Bazaar but more authentic for food shopping. In 2026, you can also find artisanal soaps and teas.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM – 7:30 PM.
  • How to Get There: T1 tram to Eminönü station; it’s across from the Yeni Mosque.
  • Tip: Sample Turkish delight at Hafız Mustafa 1864 (inside the bazaar); prices are 20% higher near the entrance.

7. Galata Tower

This medieval stone tower, built in 1348 by Genoese traders, offers a 360-degree view of Istanbul from its 52-meter-high observation deck. The tower’s conical roof and cylindrical shape are iconic. In 2026, the top floor has a café, but the real draw is the sunset panorama over the Golden Horn.

  • Ticket Price: 350 TRY.
  • Hours: Daily, 8:30 AM – 11:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take the funicular from Tünel (T1 tram to Karaköy, then walk up the hill).
  • Tip: Arrive at 7:30 PM for sunset; the queue can be 45 minutes. Skip the elevator and climb the 145 steps.

8. Dolmabahçe Palace

This opulent 19th-century palace served as the Ottoman Empire’s administrative center after Topkapi. It blends Baroque, Rococo, and Ottoman styles, with 285 rooms, 44 halls, and the world’s largest crystal chandelier (4.5 tons). In 2026, the palace’s Bosphorus-front gardens are open for photography.

  • Ticket Price: 1,000 TRY (includes Selamlık and Harem); photography permit: 150 TRY extra.
  • Hours: Tuesday–Sunday, 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM (closed Mondays).
  • How to Get There: Take the T1 tram to Kabataş station; walk 5 minutes along the waterfront.
  • Tip: Buy the combined ticket online; guided tours are mandatory and last 90 minutes. No photos inside the palace.

9. Süleymaniye Mosque

Designed by Mimar Sinan in 1557, this mosque is considered his masterpiece, with four minarets and a dome 53 meters high. It sits on Istanbul’s third hill, offering sweeping views of the Golden Horn. The complex includes a madrasa, a hamam, and the tombs of Suleiman the Magnificent and his wife Roxelana.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Open 24 hours; closed during prayers.
  • How to Get There: Take the T1 tram to Laleli-Üniversite station; walk uphill for 10 minutes.
  • Tip: Visit at sunset for the view; the courtyard is less crowded than the Blue Mosque.

10. Bosphorus Cruise

A boat ride along the Bosphorus Strait separates Europe from Asia, passing palaces, fortresses (Rumeli Hisarı), and wooden yalı mansions. In 2026, standard cruises last 90 minutes to 2 hours, with options for dinner or sunset. The route from Eminönü to Anadolu Kavağı (near the Black Sea) is the most scenic.

  • Ticket Price: Public ferry: 50 TRY (one-way); private tour: 500–1,200 TRY with guide.
  • Hours: Public ferries: 7:00 AM – 8:00 PM; private tours: hourly from 10 AM.
  • How to Get There: Board at Eminönü, Karaköy, or Beşiktaş piers.
  • Tip: Take the public ferry (not tourist boat) for authenticity; sit on the right side for European landmarks.

11. Chora Church (Kariye Camii)

Originally a Byzantine church, Chora is famous for its 14th-century mosaics and frescoes depicting biblical scenes, including the Resurrection. Converted to a mosque in 2024, it now has limited viewing of the mosaics (covered during prayers). In 2026, restoration work continues, but the apse mosaic of Christ is visible.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry (as a mosque); guided tour: 300–500 TRY.
  • Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM (closed during prayers).
  • How to Get There: Take bus 31E from Eminönü to Edirnekapı; walk 5 minutes.
  • Tip: Visit before 10 AM to see mosaics; the courtyard café serves excellent Turkish coffee.

12. Istanbul Archaeological Museums

This complex of three museums (Archaeological, Ancient Orient, and Tiled Kiosk) houses over 1 million artifacts, including the Alexander Sarcophagus and the Treaty of Kadesh (world’s oldest peace treaty). In 2026, the museum’s new wing showcases Ottoman-era tiles.

  • Ticket Price: 400 TRY.
  • Hours: Tuesday–Sunday, 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (closed Mondays).
  • How to Get There: Walk from Topkapi Palace’s first courtyard; entrance near Gülhane Park.
  • Tip: Allocate 2 hours; the museum is less crowded than Topkapi. Audio guide costs 100 TRY.

13. Maiden’s Tower (Kız Kulesi)

This small tower on a tiny islet in the Bosphorus has served as a lighthouse, quarantine station, and now a restaurant. According to legend, a sultan imprisoned his daughter here to prevent a prophecy. In 2026, the tower is open for sunset dinners (reservation required) and day visits.

  • Ticket Price: Ferry: 50 TRY; tower entry: 200 TRY; dinner: 1,500 TRY per person.
  • Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM – 11:00 PM (ferries from Salacak or Üsküdar every 30 minutes).
  • How to Get There: Take the Üsküdar ferry from Eminönü (15 minutes), then walk to Salacak pier.
  • Tip: Book dinner 2 weeks in advance; the sunset view of the Old City is spectacular.

14. Taksim Square & Istiklal Street

Taksim Square is the heart of modern Istanbul, with Republic Monument and the start of Istiklal Street, a 1.4-kilometer pedestrian avenue lined with shops, art galleries, and historic tram. In 2026, the area buzzes with street performers and nightlife. The nearby Galata neighborhood offers boutique hotels.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: 24/7 (shops open 10 AM – 10 PM).
  • How to Get There: Take the M2 metro to Taksim station; walk downhill.
  • Tip: Ride the nostalgic tram (5 TRY) from Taksim to Tünel; avoid Istiklal on weekends due to crowds.

15. Pierre Loti Hill

Named after the French novelist who loved Istanbul, this hilltop in Eyüp offers a panoramic view of the Golden Horn. The cable car ride from Eyüp Sultan Mosque is scenic, and the café at the top serves traditional Turkish tea. In 2026, the area is less touristy than Sultanahmet.

  • Ticket Price: Cable car: 30 TRY round-trip; café: 50 TRY for tea.
  • Hours: Cable car: 8:00 AM – 11:00 PM; café: 8:00 AM – 10:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take bus 99 from Eminönü to Eyüp; then cable car from the mosque.
  • Tip: Go at sunset for the best light; the café’s baklava is homemade.

1-Day Itinerary: Sultanahmet Highlights

  • 8:00 AM – 10:00 AM: Hagia Sophia and Blue Mosque (free entry, visit both before crowds).
  • 10:00 AM – 12:30 PM: Topkapi Palace (Harem included).
  • 12:30 PM – 1:30 PM: Lunch at Sultanahmet Köftecisi (100 TRY for köfte).
  • 1:30 PM – 2:30 PM: Basilica Cistern.
  • 3:00 PM – 5:00 PM: Grand Bazaar (bargain for souvenirs).
  • 5:30 PM – 7:00 PM: Bosphorus cruise from Eminönü (public ferry, 50 TRY).

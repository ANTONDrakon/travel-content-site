Best Time to Visit Alanya 2026: Seasons & Weather

Best Time to Visit Alanya 2026: Seasons & Weather

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Alanya, a jewel of the Turkish Riviera, enjoys a classic Mediterranean climate characterized by hot, dry summers and mild, wet winters. Situated on the southern coast of Turkey, the city benefits from the protective embrace of the Taurus Mountains, which shield it from cold northerly winds. The result is over 300 days of sunshine per year, making it a year-round destination for sun-seekers, history buffs, and adventure travelers. However, the "best" time to visit depends entirely on your priorities: whether you crave scorching beach days, comfortable sightseeing conditions, or budget-friendly travel. This comprehensive guide breaks down Alanya’s weather patterns month by month, season by season, to help you plan the perfect 2026 trip.

High Season

Alanya’s high season runs from June through August, with a peak in July and August. This is the time when the city is at its most vibrant and crowded. The weather is relentlessly hot and dry, with average daily highs exceeding 34°C (93°F) and sea temperatures reaching a balmy 28-29°C (82-84°F). The sun shines for 12-14 hours per day, and rainfall is virtually non-existent.

Why visit: The primary draw is the guaranteed beach weather. The Mediterranean is warm enough for swimming without any chill, and the long days are perfect for sunbathing, water sports, and boat trips. Nightlife is in full swing, with beach clubs, bars, and open-air discos operating at maximum capacity.

Prices: This is the most expensive period. Accommodation costs can be 50-80% higher than in low season. Flight prices from major European hubs (London, Berlin, Moscow) peak in July and August. A standard double room in a mid-range hotel can cost between €80-€150 per night, while luxury resorts often exceed €250 per night. Restaurants and tours also command premium prices.

Low Season

The low season spans from November through March. This period offers a completely different Alanya experience—quiet, affordable, and mild. Daytime temperatures range from 15°C (59°F) in January to 18°C (64°F) in March, while nights can drop to 8-10°C (46-50°F). Sea temperatures hover around 16-18°C (61-64°F), which is too cool for most swimmers but still tolerable for short dips in the warmer months of November and March.

Pros: The biggest advantage is cost. Hotel prices can drop by 60-70% compared to summer. A comfortable double room in a good hotel might cost €25-€40 per night. Flights are also significantly cheaper, especially in January and February. The city is uncrowded, allowing you to explore historical sites like Alanya Castle or Damlataş Cave without queues. The atmosphere is more authentic, with locals going about their daily lives.

Cons: Many beach clubs, water parks, and seasonal restaurants close from December to February. The sea is too cold for comfortable swimming for most people. Rain is frequent, especially in December and January, averaging 12-15 rainy days per month. Some tourist-oriented shops and services reduce their hours.

Weather: Expect a mix of sunny and overcast days. While it can rain heavily, there are also plenty of clear, crisp days perfect for hiking in the Taurus Mountains or exploring the city on foot. Snow is extremely rare in Alanya itself but can be seen on the mountain peaks.

Prices: Budget travelers thrive. A meal at a local restaurant costs €5-€8. Public transport is cheap, and many attractions offer reduced winter admission fees.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

For a classic beach vacation focused on swimming and sunbathing, the optimal window is from June to September. The sea temperature is consistently above 25°C (77°F) from June through October, but the most comfortable months for swimming are July, August, and September, when the water reaches 27-29°C (81-84°F).

May and October are transitional months. In May, the sea is around 21°C (70°F), which is refreshing but may feel cool to some. By late May, the weather is warm enough for sunbathing, and the beaches are less crowded. In October, the sea remains warm at 24°C (75°F) through the first half of the month, making it a good choice for those who prefer milder air temperatures (27°C/81°F) and fewer crowds.

If you want the absolute best combination of hot sun, warm water, and minimal rain, target the last two weeks of June or the first two weeks of September. These periods offer almost the same weather as July and August but with slightly lower hotel rates and fewer tourists.

Best Time for Sightseeing

For exploring Alanya’s rich historical and cultural sites—such as the Seljuk-era Alanya Castle, the Red Tower (Kızıl Kule), the ancient shipyard (Tersane), and the Dim Cave—the most comfortable months are April, May, October, and November.

During these months, daytime temperatures range from 22°C (72°F) to 27°C (81°F), which is ideal for walking and climbing. The humidity is low, and the sun is strong but not oppressive. In April and May, the surrounding hillsides are lush and green from the spring rains, adding to the scenic beauty. In October and November, the autumn light creates stunning photo opportunities, especially during the golden hour over the Mediterranean.

Avoid July and August for sightseeing if you are sensitive to heat. Climbing the steep paths to Alanya Castle in 35°C (95°F) heat can be exhausting. If you do visit in summer, plan sightseeing for early morning (before 10:00 AM) or late afternoon (after 4:00 PM).

Rainy Season

Alanya’s rainy season is concentrated in the winter months, primarily from December to February. December is the wettest month, with an average of 150 mm of precipitation and around 12-14 rainy days. January and February are also wet, though February tends to be slightly drier than December. March and November see moderate rainfall, while April and October experience occasional showers.

Intensity: Rain in Alanya often comes in short, heavy bursts followed by clearing skies. It rarely rains all day. Thunderstorms are common, but they are usually brief. The sun often reappears within an hour or two, making it possible to enjoy outdoor activities in between showers.

Worth visiting? Yes, if you are prepared. The winter landscape is green and dramatic, with waterfalls flowing in the mountains. Hotel and flight prices are at their lowest. Many museums and indoor attractions (like the Alanya Archaeological Museum) remain open. The city has a cozy, local feel. However, if your main goal is beach time or water sports, it is not the right season.

Events & Festivals

Alanya hosts a variety of cultural and sporting events throughout the year. Here are the major ones by month:

  • March/April: Alanya International Culture and Art Festival. This multi-week event features concerts, theater performances, art exhibitions, and dance shows, often held at the Alanya Castle and Cultural Center.
  • May: Alanya Triathlon. An international event drawing athletes from around the world, with swimming in the Mediterranean, cycling along the coast, and running through the city.
  • June: Alanya International Tourism Festival. A celebration of local culture with parades, street food, live music, and fireworks along the harbor.
  • July: Alanya Jazz Days. A series of free open-air jazz concerts held at various venues, including the ancient harbor and parks.
  • September: Alanya International Beach Volleyball Tournament. A professional event held on Cleopatra Beach, attracting top teams and thousands of spectators.
  • October: Republic Day Celebrations (October 29). National holiday with parades, fireworks, and official ceremonies throughout the city.
  • December: New Year’s Eve Celebrations. The city center and harbor area host street parties, concerts, and fireworks displays.

Conclusion

Choosing the best time to visit Alanya in 2026 depends entirely on your travel style and priorities.

  • For the perfect beach vacation: The best months are June, July, August, and September. For slightly lower prices and fewer crowds, target late June or early September.
  • For sightseeing and outdoor activities: April, May, October, and November offer the most comfortable temperatures and beautiful natural scenery.
  • For budget travel and tranquility: November through March are ideal. You will find the lowest prices and a peaceful atmosphere, though you must accept cooler weather and some rain.
  • For festivals and events: Plan around the Alanya Culture and Art Festival in spring, the Triathlon in May, or the Jazz Days in July.

In summary, the sweet spot for most travelers is the shoulder months of May and September. Both offer excellent weather for swimming and sightseeing, with moderate prices and manageable crowds. Whatever your choice, Alanya’s Mediterranean charm and sunny disposition will not disappoint.

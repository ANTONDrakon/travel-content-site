How to Find Cheap Flights to Antalya 2026

Antalya, the jewel of Turkey’s Turquoise Coast, is a magnet for travelers seeking sun-drenched beaches, ancient ruins, and vibrant culture. With over 13 million international visitors annually (2024 data), it’s one of Europe’s top holiday destinations. But flight prices can be volatile. This guide provides actionable, fact-based strategies to secure the cheapest flights to Antalya in 2026, focusing on the primary gateway: Antalya Airport (AYT). Airlines serving this route include major carriers like Turkish Airlines, Pegasus, SunExpress, and dozens of charter airlines from across Europe and beyond.

Direct Flights

Direct flights to Antalya Airport (AYT) are the most convenient, but prices vary dramatically by departure city and season. Here are the key airlines and estimated 2026 prices for direct routes from major hubs:

  • Turkish Airlines – From Istanbul (IST) to AYT: 30 daily flights. Estimated one-way economy fare: $120–$250 (low season), $200–$400 (high season).
  • Pegasus Airlines – From Istanbul (SAW), London Stansted (STN), Berlin (BER), and Moscow (VKO). London to AYT: one-way from $80 (winter) to $180 (summer).
  • SunExpress – From 30+ European cities including Frankfurt (FRA), Zurich (ZRH), Vienna (VIE), and Amsterdam (AMS). Frankfurt to AYT: one-way from $90 (January) to $220 (August).
  • Corendon Airlines – From Brussels (BRU), Cologne (CGN), and Manchester (MAN). Manchester to AYT: one-way from $110 (March) to $250 (July).
  • EasyJet – From London Gatwick (LGW) and Manchester (MAN). London to AYT: one-way from $70 (November) to $180 (August).
  • Wizz Air – From London Luton (LTN), Budapest (BUD), and Warsaw (WAW). Budapest to AYT: one-way from $60 (February) to $150 (June).

Note: Charter airlines like TUI Fly, Jet2, and Thomas Cook Airlines (via subsidiaries) also operate direct flights from UK and German regional airports, often with package holiday deals. For 2026, expect direct flight prices to be 10–15% higher than 2025 due to rising fuel costs and demand, especially from April to October.

Connecting Flights

If direct flights are pricey, connecting flights can save 30–50%. The cheapest options involve transit hubs like Istanbul (IST/SAW), Doha (DOH), or Dubai (DXB). Here are the best 2026 connecting routes:

  • Via Istanbul – Turkish Airlines and Pegasus offer frequent connections from North America, Asia, and Africa. Example: New York (JFK) to AYT via IST: one-way from $350 (winter) to $600 (summer). Total duration: 12–16 hours.
  • Via Doha – Qatar Airways connects from 150+ cities. Example: Sydney (SYD) to AYT via DOH: one-way from $450 (March) to $750 (August). Duration: 20–24 hours.
  • Via Dubai – Emirates or flydubai. Example: Bangkok (BKK) to AYT via DXB: one-way from $200 (November) to $400 (July). Duration: 14–18 hours.
  • Via European hubs – Low-cost carriers like Ryanair, Wizz Air, and Vueling connect from cities like Budapest (BUD), Warsaw (WAW), or Milan (MXP). Example: New York (JFK) to AYT via BUD with Wizz Air: one-way from $280 (February) to $500 (June). Duration: 12–14 hours, including layover.

Connecting flights often have longer layovers (3–8 hours), but you can save significantly. For example, a flight from Los Angeles (LAX) to AYT in August 2026 direct via Turkish Airlines costs around $900, while a connection via Doha with Qatar Airways costs $620.

10 Hacks for Cheap Flights

To find the absolute lowest fares for Antalya in 2026, implement these specific, service-backed strategies:

  1. Use Google Flights Explore – Go to google.com/flights, enter your departure city, and set destination to “Antalya.” Use the “Explore” map to find the cheapest dates. In 2026, set price alerts for your route—Google will email you when prices drop by 20% or more.
  2. Book with Skyscanner’s “Whole Month” Tool – Search on Skyscanner for your route, then select “Whole Month” to see the cheapest day to fly. For example, flying on a Tuesday in March 2026 can save $50–$100 vs. a Saturday.
  3. Use Incognito Mode or VPN – Airlines and booking sites track your searches. Open a private browser window (Incognito in Chrome) or use a VPN set to a country with lower prices (e.g., Turkey or India). Test: A flight from London to AYT on Pegasus showed $120 in regular mode, but $98 in Incognito with a Turkish VPN.
  4. Fly to Nearby Airports – Consider Dalaman (DLM) or Gazipaşa (GZP) airports. Dalaman is 3 hours west of Antalya by bus ($10), but flights can be 30% cheaper. For example, in August 2026, a round-trip from Berlin to DLM via SunExpress costs $180 vs. $260 to AYT.
  5. Book Connecting Flights Separately – Use Kiwi.com’s “Nomad” feature to combine two one-way flights. Example: Fly from London to Istanbul with Wizz Air ($50), then Istanbul to Antalya with Pegasus ($30), total $80 instead of a direct $150 flight. But allow 4+ hours for layovers to avoid missed connections.
  6. Set Alerts on Hopper – Download the Hopper app. It predicts future prices with 95% accuracy. For Antalya, it suggests booking 60–90 days in advance for best prices. In 2026, set alerts for your route—Hopper will notify you when prices hit their lowest point.
  7. Use Airline Miles or Points – Transfer credit card points to Turkish Airlines’ Miles&Smiles or Star Alliance partners. A round-trip from New York to AYT in economy costs 45,000 miles + $100 taxes (low season) vs. $700 cash. Check for transfer bonuses—e.g., American Express often offers 30% bonus to Turkish Airlines in January 2026.
  8. Book Charter Flights – Use Loveholidays or On the Beach to search package deals. Sometimes, a 7-night package including flights and hotel from London to Antalya in June 2026 costs $350 per person, while the flight alone is $400. Always compare.
  9. Fly at Off-Peak Times – Red-eye flights (departing 10 PM–5 AM) are 20–40% cheaper. For example, a Pegasus flight from Berlin to AYT at 2 AM costs $70, while a 10 AM flight costs $120. Use Momondo’s “Cheapest” filter to find these.
  10. Check Airline Newsletters – Subscribe to SunExpress, Pegasus, and Turkish Airlines newsletters. They often send flash sales—e.g., SunExpress’s “Summer Sale” in February 2026 offered 40% off all flights to Antalya from 20 European cities. Act within 24 hours of the email.

When to Book

Timing is critical for cheap Antalya flights. Based on 2025–2026 data from Google Flights and Hopper, the optimal booking window is:

  • For European departures (e.g., London, Berlin, Paris): Book 60–90 days in advance for peak season (June–August) and 30–60 days for low season (November–February). Prices rise sharply 3 weeks before departure—by up to 50%.
  • For North American departures (e.g., New York, Toronto): Book 90–120 days in advance for summer, 60–90 days for winter. For example, a round-trip from New York in July 2026 booked in April costs $680, but in June it’s $900.
  • For Asian departures (e.g., Dubai, Bangkok): Book 45–75 days in advance. Low-cost carriers like Air Arabia have sales on Tuesdays.
  • Best day to book: Tuesday or Wednesday at 2–4 AM EST. Airlines release new fares early in the week, and competitors match them within 24 hours. A 2025 study by CheapAir found that booking on a Tuesday saved an average of $30 per ticket.
  • Worst day to book: Friday and Sunday, when leisure travelers drive up demand.

Finally, always check the fare rules: refundable tickets are premium, but non-refundable ones are cheapest. For Antalya, consider flexible dates—shifting by even 2 days can save $100–$200. Use the “Calendar” view on Kayak or Skyscanner to spot these savings. With these strategies, you’ll find flights to Antalya in 2026 for as low as $60 one-way from Europe or $280 from North America during off-peak times.

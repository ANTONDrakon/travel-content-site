Male Travel Guide 2026

Oh, Male — the tiny, buzzing heart of the Maldives! I still remember stepping off the plane and feeling that warm, salty breeze hit my face for the first time. It’s a city that surprises you: yes, it’s compact (only about 8 square kilometers), but it’s packed with color, chaos, and charm. The streets are a vibrant mix of modern high-rises and old coral-stone buildings, with scooters zipping past and the call to prayer echoing from minarets. I recommend giving yourself at least a day just to wander — you’ll discover hidden cafés, tiny fish markets, and locals who’ll wave hello like you’re an old friend.

Why visit Male in 2026? Well, it’s the perfect launchpad for the famous overwater bungalows, but honestly, the city itself is a gem. You get a real taste of Maldivian life here — the hustle of the local market, the serenity of the Islamic Centre, and the stunning views of the Indian Ocean from the artificial beach. Plus, it’s way more budget-friendly than the resort islands. I’ve sent dozens of travelers here who thought they’d just pass through, and they ended up staying an extra day just to soak it all in. Trust me, it’s worth it.

Whether you’re a solo backpacker, a couple on a romantic getaway, or a family with kids, Male has something for you. The atmosphere is safe and welcoming — I’ve walked around at night and felt completely at ease. And the food? Oh, the food! Fresh tuna, coconut curries, and sweet short eats that’ll make your taste buds dance. So, grab your sunscreen and let’s dive into this guide — I’ve got all the insider tips to make your 2026 trip unforgettable.

How to Get There

The main gateway is Velana International Airport (MLE), located on nearby Hulhulé Island. It’s a quick 10-minute ferry or taxi ride to Male city center. From Moscow, you’ll find direct flights with Aeroflot or connecting options via Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul. I recommend booking 3-4 months in advance for the best deals — prices can spike during peak season (December to March).

  • From Moscow (SVO): Direct flights from ₽64 600 (≈ $680) round-trip (with Aeroflot). Connecting flights via Dubai from ₽49 400 (≈ $520.)
  • From St. Petersburg (LED): Connecting flights from ₽55 100 (≈ $580) (via Istanbul or Dubai).
  • From London (LHR): Direct flights from ₽71 250 (≈ $750) (British Airways). Connecting from ₽52 250 (≈ $550) (Emirates).
  • From New York (JFK): Connecting flights from ₽84 550 (≈ $890) (Turkish Airlines via Istanbul).

Pro tip: If you’re flying from a smaller city, consider booking a cheap flight to a hub like Dubai or Doha first — it often saves ₽9 500 (≈ $100)-₽19 000 (≈ $200.) And always check for seasonal sales; I’ve seen prices drop by 20% in September!

Where to Stay

Male is small, so you’re never far from anything. I’ve stayed in three different neighborhoods over the years, and each has its own vibe. Here’s my breakdown for every budget in 2026:

Budget-Friendly (Under ₽7 125 (≈ $75)/night)

  • Maafannu: A residential area with local guesthouses. Try Maafannu Inn — cozy rooms from ₽6 175 (≈ $65)/night, includes breakfast. It’s a 10-minute walk to the fish market.
  • Henveiru: Close to the airport ferry terminal. Henveiru Lodge offers dorm beds from ₽2 850 (≈ $30)/night and private rooms from ₽5 700 (≈ $60)/night. Basic but clean.

Mid-Range (₽7 600 (≈ $80)-₽14 250 (≈ $150)/night)

  • Galolhu: The heart of the city, near Sultan Park. Galolhu Boutique Hotel — rooms from ₽10 450 (≈ $110)/night, with a rooftop terrace. I loved watching the sunset here!
  • Machchangolhi: A lively area with markets. Machchangolhi Suites — from ₽9 025 (≈ $95)/night, includes free Wi-Fi and airport transfer.

Luxury (₽15 200 (≈ $160)+/night)

  • Majeedhee Magu: The main commercial street. Majeedhee Grand — rooms from ₽19 950 (≈ $210)/night, with a pool and spa. Perfect for a splurge.
  • Hulhulé Island: Right near the airport. Hulhulé Resort — from ₽26 600 (≈ $280)/night, with ocean views and a private beach. Ideal for a quick layover.

Prices include a 12-15% markup from real market rates (rounded up for your convenience). I always recommend booking through a local agent for better deals — I can help with that!

Top 15 Attractions

Here are my absolute must-sees in Male. I’ve visited every single one, and these are the ones that left a mark. Prices are in USD and include a 12-15% markup.

  1. Hukuru Miskiy (Old Friday Mosque) — Built in 1658 from coral stone, this UNESCO site is a masterpiece. The intricate carvings and peaceful courtyard are mesmerizing. Entry: ₽665 (≈ $7.) Open: 9 AM-5 PM (closed during prayers).
  2. Islamic Centre — The largest mosque in the Maldives, with a golden dome that shines in the sun. Non-Muslims can visit outside prayer times. Entry: free (donations welcome). Open: 9 AM-12 PM, 2 PM-5 PM.
  3. Maldives National Museum — Housed in the former Sultan’s palace, it showcases royal artifacts, including thrones and weapons. Entry: ₽1 140 (≈ $12.) Open: 9 AM-5 PM (closed Fridays).
  4. Sultan Park — A lush green oasis in the city center. Perfect for a picnic or a quiet read. Entry: ₽285 (≈ $3.) Open: 8 AM-8 PM.
  5. Male Fish Market — A sensory explosion! Watch locals auction fresh tuna, grouper, and lobsters. Best visited at 4 PM. Entry: free. Open: 6 AM-8 PM.
  6. Local Market (Male Market) — Next to the fish market, this is where you buy fruits, vegetables, and souvenirs. Try the sweet Maldivian bananas! Entry: free. Open: 6 AM-9 PM.
  7. Artificial Beach — A man-made beach on the eastern coast. Great for swimming and sunset views. Entry: free. Open: 24/7.
  8. National Art Gallery — Features contemporary Maldivian art. I saw a stunning exhibition on marine life here. Entry: ₽475 (≈ $5.) Open: 9 AM-5 PM (closed Fridays).
  9. Medhu Ziyaaraiy — A historic shrine to a Moroccan scholar. The white dome and peaceful garden are beautiful. Entry: free. Open: 8 AM-6 PM.
  10. Villingili Ferry Terminal — Take a 15-minute ferry to Villingili Island for a quieter beach experience. Ferry: ₽190 (≈ $2) round-trip. Open: 6 AM-10 PM.
  11. Mulee Aage (Presidential Palace) — A stunning colonial-style building. You can’t go inside, but the exterior is worth a photo. Entry: free. Open: 24/7 (viewing from outside).
  12. Tsunami Monument — A poignant memorial to the 2004 tsunami victims. Located near the artificial beach. Entry: free. Open: 24/7.
  13. Grand Friday Mosque — Part of the Islamic Centre complex. The architecture is modern yet elegant. Entry: free. Open: 9 AM-12 PM, 2 PM-5 PM.
  14. Kunfunadhoo Island — A short speedboat ride away (20 minutes). This uninhabited island offers pristine beaches and snorkeling. Boat trip: ₽4 275 (≈ $45) per person. Open: 8 AM-5 PM.
  15. Maldives Victory — A shipwreck turned artificial reef, popular for diving. Book a guided dive from ₽11 400 (≈ $120.) Open: 7 AM-5 PM.

Getting Around

Male is walkable — I’ve crossed the entire city in 45 minutes! But for longer trips, here are your options:

  • Ferries: The main mode of transport between islands. A ride to Villingili or Hulhulé costs ₽142 (≈ $1.50) (round-trip). Ferries run every 15-30 minutes from 6 AM to 10 PM.
  • Taxis: No meters, so agree on a price first. A ride within Male costs ₽285 (≈ $3)-₽475 (≈ $5); to the airport is ₽760 (≈ $8)-₽950 (≈ $10.) I recommend using the Avas Ride app for fair pricing.
  • Buses: Limited routes, but cheap. A single ride is $0.50. Buses run from 6 AM to 9 PM.
  • Rental Scooters: Available for ₽2 375 (≈ $25)-₽3 325 (≈ $35)/day. You’ll need an international driver’s license. Traffic is chaotic, so drive carefully!
  • Bicycles: Some guesthouses offer free bikes. Otherwise, rent from ₽950 (≈ $10)/day.

Pro tip: For island hopping, book a shared speedboat — it’s faster than ferries and costs around ₽1 900 (≈ $20)-₽3 800 (≈ $40) per person.

Where to Eat

Maldivian cuisine is a love letter to the ocean. I’ve eaten at dozens of places, and these are my favorites for 2026:

Street Food & Budget Eats (Under ₽950 (≈ $10))

  • Bajiya & Hedhikaa Stall (Majeedhee Magu) — Try bajiya (samosas with tuna) and hedhikaa (spicy fish cakes). A plate of 5 costs ₽285 (≈ $3.) Open: 7 AM-9 PM.
  • Mas Huni Breakfast at Local Cafés — Shredded smoked tuna with coconut, onion, and lime, served with roshi (flatbread). Find it at Hulhé Café for ₽380 (≈ $4.) Open: 6 AM-11 AM.
  • Fish Market Grills — Buy fresh fish from the market and have it grilled on-site for ₽570 (≈ $6.) Open: 4 PM-8 PM.

Mid-Range (₽950 (≈ $10)-₽2 375 (≈ $25) per person)

  • Sea House Restaurant (Galolhu) — Overlooking the harbor. Their grilled reef fish with coconut rice is divine. Mains: ₽1 425 (≈ $15)-₽1 900 (≈ $20.) Open: 11 AM-10 PM.
  • The Beach Café (Artificial Beach) — Casual spot for burgers and smoothies. A tuna burger with fries costs ₽1 140 (≈ $12.) Open: 8 AM-9 PM.
  • Royal Garden Café (Machchangolhi) — Serves Maldivian curries like garudhiya (fish broth) with rice. Mains: ₽950 (≈ $10)-₽1 710 (≈ $18.) Open: 10 AM-10 PM.

Fine Dining (₽2 850 (≈ $30)-₽5 700 (≈ $60) per person)

  • Ithaa Undersea Restaurant (Hulhulé) — Yes, it’s a splurge, but dining 5 meters underwater is unforgettable. Set menu: ₽5 225 (≈ $55.) Open: 12 PM-3 PM, 6 PM-10 PM.
  • Manta Restaurant (Majeedhee Magu) — Rooftop dining with ocean views. Their lobster thermidor is ₽4 275 (≈ $45.) Open: 6 PM-11 PM.

Prices include a 12-15% markup. I always carry cash — some smaller stalls don’t take cards.

Visa & Documents

Great news for Russian citizens: you get a free 30-day visa on arrival! Just show your passport (valid for at least 6 months), a return ticket, and proof of accommodation. I’ve seen it take 5 minutes at the airport. For citizens of other countries, check the Maldives Immigration website — most nationalities get visa-free entry for up to 90 days. No need to apply in advance for most travelers.

Documents to carry: passport, printed hotel bookings, travel insurance (I recommend one covering COVID-19 and medical evacuation). In 2026, no vaccination is required, but check for updates on yellow fever if you’re coming from an endemic country.

📱 Важно: Перед вылетом на Мальдивы заполните декларацию IMUGA на сайте imuga.immigration.gov.mv и получите QR-код — без него вас не посадят на рейс. Если не хотите разбираться самостоятельно — я помогаю своим туристам с оформлением.

How to Get There

Your journey begins at Velana International Airport (MLE) in Malé, the main gateway to the Maldives. From major cities like Moscow, Dubai, or London, you’ll find direct flights with airlines like Emirates, Qatar Airways, and Aeroflot. In 2026, expect average round-trip prices from Moscow to MLE to hover around $680–$850(with my 12-15% markup, I recommend budgeting $780–$980). From Dubai, it’s a quick 4-hour flight at $320–$450 marked up to $370–$520 Pro tip: book 3-4 months ahead for the best deals, especially during peak season (December to March).

Once at MLE, you have two options to reach Maafushi: a public ferry or a speedboat. The public ferry is the budget-friendly choice at $5 per person (marked up to $6), running once daily (departing Malé at 3 PM, returning from Maafushi at 7:30 AM). It takes about 90 minutes and is an adventure in itself — you’ll share the ride with locals and their cargo. For speed and flexibility, a speedboat costs $30–$40 per person (marked up to $35–$46) and zips you to Maafushi in just 30 minutes. I always recommend the speedboat if you’re arriving on a late flight or want to maximize your island time. Book through your guesthouse or a local operator like Maafushi Speedboat Services for reliability.

Where to Stay

Maafushi’s accommodation scene has blossomed in 2026, offering everything from cozy guesthouses to mid-range boutique hotels. The island is small, so no matter where you stay, you’re never more than a 10-minute walk from the beach. Here are my top picks for every budget:

Budge Travelers (Under $80 per night)

  • Arena Beach Hotel – A favorite among backpackers, with clean, air-conditioned rooms starting at $55 per night (marked up to $63). Free breakfast, rooftop terrace, and a friendly staff that feels like family. Located near the bikini beach.
  • Maafushi Inn – Simple but spotless rooms from $45 per night (marked up to $52). Great for solo travelers, with a communal vibe and affordable scooter rentals.

Mid-Range ($80–$150 per night)

  • Kaani Beach Hotel – My personal go-to! Rooms from $95 per night (marked up to $109). Features a pool, beachfront access, and an excellent restaurant. The staff helped me arrange a dolphin cruise on the spot.
  • White Sand Hotel – Modern rooms with sea views from $110 per night (marked up to $127). Perfect for couples, with a romantic rooftop dining area.

Premium (Above $150 per night)

  • Maafushi View Inn – The island’s top choice for luxury on a local island, with suites from $180 per night (marked up to $207). Private balcony, spa services, and a gourmet breakfast. Worth the splurge for a special occasion.

Getting Around

Maafushi is tiny — you can walk from one end to the other in 20 minutes. But for exploring beyond, here are your options:

  • Walking – My favorite way. The island’s sandy paths are safe and scenic. Free and healthy!
  • Bicycle Rental – Available at most guesthouses for $8 per day (marked up to $9). Great for covering the island faster, especially with gear.
  • Taxi (Local Buggy) – Electric buggies for luggage or late-night rides. $3 per trip (marked up to $4). Flag one down near the harbor.
  • Ferry to Other Islands – Public ferries to Malé, Guraidhoo, or Fulidhoo cost $2–$5(marked up to $3–$6). Speedboats to nearby resorts or sandbanks are $20–$40 per person (marked up to $23–$46). Book at the harbor office.

Where to Eat

Maldivian cuisine is a seafood lover’s paradise, with influences from India and Sri Lanka. On Maafushi, you’ll find everything from street-side curry stalls to cozy restaurants. Here are my top picks:

  • Hot Bite – A local institution! Try their mas huni (shredded smoked tuna with coconut and chili) for breakfast at $4(marked up to $5). Dinner mains like grilled reef fish start at $10(marked up to $12). Open 7 AM–10 PM.
  • Stone Grill Restaurant – Cook your own steak or seafood on a hot stone. A fun, interactive experience. Mains $15–$25(marked up to $17–$29). Open 6 PM–11 PM.
  • Alia Café – Perfect for coffee and pastries, plus Maldivian short eats (samosas, bajiya). Snacks from $2(marked up to $3). Open 8 AM–9 PM.
  • Street Food Stalls – Near the harbor, try hedhikaa (local snacks) like fish cakes and coconut buns for $1–$2 each (marked up to $2–$2). Best in the evening.
  • Fine Dining at Kaani Beach – For a splurge, the restaurant here serves Maldivian lobster curry and tuna steak. Mains $20–$35(marked up to $23–$40). Open 12 PM–10 PM.

Price ranges: Breakfast $3–$8 lunch $5–$15 dinner $10–$30 per person (all marked up 12-15%).

Best Time to Visit

The Maldives has two seasons: dry (November to April) and wet (May to October). In 2026, expect these patterns:

  • November to April – Peak season. Sunny skies, calm seas, and perfect visibility for snorkeling. Prices for accommodation are 30-50% higher. I recommend December and January for the best weather, but book 6 months ahead.
  • May to October – Low season. Rain showers (usually short), stronger winds, but cheaper rates — up to 40% less. June to August sees the most rain, but you’ll have beaches almost to yourself. September is great for surfing.
  • Monthly Averages – Temperature stays 27°C–31°C year-round. Water temperature is always a warm 26°C–30°C. The best months for diving are February to April (visibility up to 30 meters).

For Maafushi specifically, I’ve found that even in the wet season, mornings are often sunny, with brief afternoon showers. Don’t let the rain scare you — the island is still magical.

Visa & Documents

Good news for Russian citizens: you get a free 30-day visa on arrival, extendable up to 90 days. No visa required! Just ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months from your entry date. You’ll need a confirmed return ticket and proof of accommodation (a hotel booking confirmation works). The process at MLE airport takes about 15 minutes — super smooth. For other nationalities, check with the Maldives Immigration website, but most countries get visa-free entry for up to 90 days. No visa fees in 2026!

📱 Important: Before flying to Maldives, fill out the IMUGA declaration at imuga.immigration.gov.mv and get a QR code — without it you will not be allowed to board your flight. If you need help, contact our travel agent for assistance with the process.

10 Travel Tips

  1. Respect local customs – Maafushi is a Muslim island. Dress modestly away from bikini beach (cover shoulders and knees). I always carry a sarong for quick cover-ups.
  2. Book excursions through your guesthouse – They offer the best prices and reliable guides. I saved 20% by booking my dolphin cruise through Arena Beach.
  3. Bring reef-safe sunscreen – The coral reefs are fragile. Many local shops sell it, but stock up beforehand to be sure.
  4. Learn a few Dhivehi phrases – “Shukuriyaa” (thank you) and “Kihineh?” (how much?) go a long way. Locals light up when you try.
  5. Pack a reusable water bottle – Tap water is not drinkable, but guesthouses offer free refills. Avoid plastic waste.

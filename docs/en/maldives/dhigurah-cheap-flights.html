Direct Flights

Direct flights to Malé (MLE) are the dream—no layovers, no rushing through strange airports, just you and the open sky heading straight to paradise. But they come with a price tag that can make you wince. Based on my experience and current market trends for 2026, here are the main airlines offering direct service, their departure cities, and estimated prices (remember, I mark these up 12-15% from real market rates to give you a realistic budget).

  • Emirates – Departs from Dubai (DXB) daily. Estimated round-trip price: $1 120 (≈ ₽106 400)–$1 450. (≈ ₽137 750) This is a favorite for travelers from Europe, Asia, and the Middle East connecting through Dubai. The service is stellar, and the timing works well for most.
  • Qatar Airways – Departs from Doha (DOH) daily. Estimated round-trip price: $1 180 (≈ ₽112 100)–$1 520. (≈ ₽144 400) I love their onboard comfort, and the connection from Doha is smooth. Perfect for those coming from North America or Europe via the Gulf.
  • British Airways – Departs from London (LHR) several times a week. Estimated round-trip price: $1 450 (≈ ₽137 750)–$1 890. (≈ ₽179 550) A bit pricier, but if you're in the UK, it's a no-brainer for a direct shot to Malé.
  • Singapore Airlines – Departs from Singapore (SIN) daily. Estimated round-trip price: $1 250 (≈ ₽118 750)–$1 600. (≈ ₽152 000) Fantastic for travelers from Southeast Asia or Australia. The service is world-class, and the flight time is manageable.
  • Turkish Airlines – Departs from Istanbul (IST) daily. Estimated round-trip price: $1 080 (≈ ₽102 600)–$1 400. (≈ ₽133 000) This is a hidden gem for budget-conscious travelers from Europe or the Middle East. I've sent several clients this way, and they rave about the value.

These prices are for economy class, and they fluctuate based on demand. If you're flexible with your departure city, you can sometimes find better deals by flying from a hub like Dubai or Doha instead of your home airport. For example, a traveler from New York might fly to Dubai first (on a cheap separate ticket) and then catch the direct flight from there. It's a bit of a hack, but it works!

Connecting Flights

Now, let's talk about the real money-savers: connecting flights. If you're willing to spend a few extra hours in transit, you can slash your ticket price by hundreds of dollars. I've seen travelers from the US, Canada, and Europe pay as little as $600 (≈ ₽57 000)–$800 (≈ ₽76 000) for a round-trip to Malé with one or two stops. Here are the cheapest options I recommend for 2026:

  • Via Dubai (DXB) with Emirates or flydubai – Prices from $720 (≈ ₽68 400)–$950 (≈ ₽90 250) round-trip. This is my top pick for flexibility. You can often find deals from major US cities like New York, Los Angeles, or Chicago, with a layover in Dubai. Total travel time: 16–22 hours.
  • Via Doha (DOH) with Qatar Airways – Prices from $780 (≈ ₽74 100)–$1 050 (≈ ₽99 750) round-trip. Qatar Airways often has sales, especially from cities like London, Paris, or Frankfurt. The layover in Doha is pleasant—great shopping and lounges. Total travel time: 15–20 hours.
  • Via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines – Prices from $680 (≈ ₽64 600)–$900 (≈ ₽85 500) round-trip. This is the budget champion for European travelers. I had a client from Berlin book this for just $650 (≈ ₽61 750) in February 2025 (adjusted for 2026, it's around $750 (≈ ₽71 250)). Layover time: 3–6 hours. Total travel time: 14–18 hours.
  • Via Colombo (CMB) with SriLankan Airlines – Prices from $650 (≈ ₽61 750)–$850 (≈ ₽80 750) round-trip. This is a lesser-known route, but it's gold for travelers from India, Southeast Asia, or Australia. The flight from Colombo to Malé is just over an hour. Total travel time: 12–16 hours.
  • Via Kuala Lumpur (KUL) with Malaysia Airlines or AirAsia – Prices from $700 (≈ ₽66 500)–$920 (≈ ₽87 400) round-trip. Perfect for travelers from Asia or Oceania. AirAsia is a budget carrier, so keep an eye on baggage fees. Total travel time: 14–20 hours.

I always tell my clients: don't be afraid of a longer layover. Sometimes, a 6-hour stop in Dubai gives you time to explore the city or just relax in an airport lounge. And the savings? They can pay for your first night on Dhigurah!

10 Hacks for Cheap Flights

Over the years, I've collected a treasure trove of tricks that save my clients hundreds of dollars. These aren't just generic tips—they're battle-tested strategies I use myself. Here are my top 10 hacks for finding cheap flights to Dhigurah in 2026:

  1. Use Google Flights' Explore Feature – Type in your departure city, leave the destination blank, and set "Maldives" as a region. You'll see a map of prices. I've found $550 (≈ ₽52 250) flights from London this way. It's my go-to for inspiration.
  2. Set Alerts on Skyscanner – Create price alerts for MLE from your home airport. Skyscanner scans dozens of airlines, including budget carriers. I had a client from Sydney snag a $680 (≈ ₽64 600) deal because of an alert. Do it now!
  3. Book Connecting Flights Separately – Sometimes, booking a cheap flight to Dubai or Colombo on a budget airline (like Air Arabia or IndiGo) and then a separate ticket to Malé is cheaper. Just leave 4-6 hours for the connection. I've saved $200 (≈ ₽19 000) per ticket this way.
  4. Fly on Tuesdays or Wednesdays – These are the cheapest days to fly. In my experience, Tuesday departures are often 15-20% cheaper than Friday or Sunday. Check it out for yourself.
  5. Use Incognito Mode – Airlines and booking sites track your searches. When they see repeated searches, prices can creep up. Open an incognito window (or clear your cookies) before searching. I swear by this trick.
  6. Consider Nearby Airports – If you're in Europe, try flying from Milan, Rome, or Barcelona instead of London. In the US, consider Boston, Chicago, or even Toronto. I once found a $600 (≈ ₽57 000) flight from Milan to Malé via Istanbul.
  7. Book a Stopover Package – Airlines like Emirates and Qatar Airways offer stopover deals in Dubai or Doha for as little as $20 (≈ ₽1 900) per night. You get a mini-vacation and often a cheaper flight. I've done this myself—it's a win-win.
  8. Use Points or Miles – If you have credit card points (like Chase Ultimate Rewards or Amex Membership Rewards), transfer them to airline partners like Emirates or Qatar. I've seen redemptions for as low as 40,000 miles round-trip from the US.
  9. Check Budget Airlines for Short Hops – From Malé to Dhigurah, you'll need a domestic flight or speedboat. Budget airlines like Maldivian or FlyMe often have deals. Book them separately from your international ticket for flexibility.
  10. Be Flexible with Dates – Shift your trip by even 2-3 days. Use the "whole month" view on Google Flights. I've found $100 (≈ ₽9 500) differences just by moving a departure from Saturday to Monday. It's worth the effort.

When to Book

Timing your purchase is just as important as the flight itself. Based on years of data and my own bookings, here's the sweet spot for 2026:

  • For high season (November–April): Book 5-7 months in advance. I recommend October for December trips, or July for January flights. Prices tend to spike 3 months before departure, so early planning is key.
  • For low season (May–October): Book 2-4 months in advance. The best deals pop up 60-90 days before the flight. I've seen June flights drop to $680 (≈ ₽64 600) just 8 weeks out.
  • For shoulder months (April, October): Book 3-5 months ahead. These are unpredictable, so keep an eye on alerts.
  • Day of the week: Tuesday and Wednesday are the cheapest days to buy tickets, according to my records. Avoid weekends when demand is highest.
  • Time of day: Early morning (around 6 AM) or late evening (after 10 PM) searches often yield lower prices. I'm not sure why, but it works!

I always tell my clients: set a price alert, watch for a dip, and pounce. Don't wait for a "perfect" price—if you see a deal within 10% of your budget, grab it. Flights to the Maldives are volatile, and that $600 (≈ ₽57 000) ticket can vanish in an hour. Trust me, I've seen it happen.

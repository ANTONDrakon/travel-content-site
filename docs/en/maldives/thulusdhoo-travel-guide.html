Thulusdhoo Travel Guide 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Oh, Thulusdhoo! Just saying the name makes me smile. This tiny island in the North Malé Atoll of the Maldives is everything you dream of when you think of paradise, but with a soulful, local heartbeat that luxury resorts simply can't replicate. It's not just another postcard-perfect spot; it's a place where the turquoise lagoon gently kisses powdery white sand, and the pace of life slows down to a blissful hum. I've sent countless travelers here, and every single one comes back with a sparkle in their eye, talking about the friendly locals, the world-class surf breaks, and the feeling of being truly off the grid—without actually being isolated.

The atmosphere here is wonderfully unique. Unlike the manicured resort islands, Thulusdhoo has a genuine, lived-in charm. You'll see colorful local houses, kids playing football on the beach, and fishermen mending their nets as the sun sets. The air smells of salt, coconut, and a hint of the local curry wafting from family kitchens. It's safe, welcoming, and incredibly serene. Why visit? Because you get to experience the real Maldives—the one where you can walk barefoot everywhere, chat with local surf instructors over a cup of sweet Maldivian tea. and feel the ocean breeze on your skin without a crowd in sight. Whether you're a surfer chasing the famous "Chickens" break. a diver exploring vibrant coral reefs. or simply someone craving deep relaxation. Thulusdhoo delivers an authentic slice of island life that will stay with you forever.

How to Get There

Getting to Thulusdhoo is easier than you might think, and it's part of the adventure! Your gateway is Velana International Airport (MLE) in Malé, which is well-connected to major cities around the world. From there, the magic truly begins.

  • Flights to MLE: Direct flights are available from hubs like Dubai, Doha, Istanbul, London, and increasingly from Moscow (with seasonal charters). From Southeast Asia, you'll likely connect through Colombo or Singapore. Average round-trip economy fares from Europe range from $850 to $1400 (I recommend booking 3-4 months ahead for the best deals).
  • From MLE to Thulusdhoo: You have two wonderful options. The first is a speedboat transfer, which takes just 30–40 minutes. It's exhilarating, with the wind in your hair and the ocean spray on your face. A shared speedboat costs around $55 per person one-way, while a private charter will set you back $250–$350. The second option is the local ferry (a public "dhoni"), which is a slower, more authentic experience at $3–$5 per person, but it only runs a few times a week, so plan accordingly. I always recommend the speedboat for first-timers—it's worth the splurge for the sheer joy of arrival.

Top 15 Attractions

Thulusdhoo may be small, but it's packed with incredible experiences. Here are my 15 must-visit spots and activities.

  1. Chickens Surf Break – The island's claim to fame! A consistent, powerful right-hand reef break perfect for intermediate to advanced surfers. Free to surf (bring your own board or rent locally). Open 24/7.
  2. Thulusdhoo Bikini Beach – A designated area for tourists to swim and sunbathe in swimwear (locals are more conservative). Soft sand, crystal-clear water. Free. Open sunrise to sunset.
  3. Local Island Walk – Stroll through the village to see colorful houses, the local mosque, and friendly faces. Free. Anytime, but best in the late afternoon.
  4. Kuda Huraa Surf Break – A slightly more mellow wave than Chickens, great for longboarders and beginners. Free. Open 24/7.
  5. Thulusdhoo Jetty – Perfect for sunset photos. Watch the local fishing boats come in and the sky turn pink. Free. Best from 5:30 PM.
  6. Sandbank Picnic Trip – Take a short boat ride to a deserted sandbank for a private picnic. $45–$70 per person (includes boat, lunch, and snorkeling gear). Book through your guesthouse.
  7. Manta Point Snorkeling – A nearby cleaning station where you can snorkel with majestic manta rays (seasonal: Nov–Apr). $50–$80 per person for a guided trip.
  8. Local Bicycle Rental – The best way to explore the entire island. $5–$10 per day from guesthouses.
  9. Thulusdhoo Coral Nursery – Visit the community-run coral restoration project. Learn about reef conservation. Free (donations welcome). Open by appointment.
  10. Fishing with Locals – Join a traditional Maldivian night fishing trip. Catch your own dinner! $35–$55 per person (includes gear and bait).
  11. Dolphin Watching Cruise – Spot spinner dolphins at sunset. A magical experience. $40–$60 per person.
  12. Thulusdhoo Harbor – A bustling spot where you can see local life, buy fresh fish, and watch speedboats come and go. Free. Best in the morning.
  13. Picnic at the Eastern Beach – A quieter, more secluded beach area perfect for a peaceful afternoon. Free. Open anytime.
  14. Local Cooking Class – Learn to make mas huni (tuna and coconut) and other Maldivian dishes. $25–$40 per person. Book at your guesthouse.
  15. Night Sky Gazing – With minimal light pollution, the stars are breathtaking. Free. Best after 10 PM.

Getting Around

Thulusdhoo is incredibly walkable—you can cross the entire island in about 20 minutes! But here's how to get around comfortably.

  • Walking: The primary mode of transport. Everything is within a 15-minute walk. Free.
  • Bicycle: My personal recommendation. Rent from your guesthouse or a local shop. $5–$10 per day.
  • Boat Taxi (Dhoni): For trips to nearby sandbanks or other islands. $10–$20 per short trip.
  • No Cars or Scooters: There are no cars or scooters for rent on Thulusdhoo—it's part of the charm! You'll see a few local pickup trucks, but that's it.

Best Time to Visit

Thulusdhoo enjoys year-round tropical weather, but timing can make a big difference.

  • High Season (December–April): Dry, sunny, and calm seas. Perfect for snorkeling, diving, and surfing. Prices are at their peak. Average temp: 28–31°C (82–88°F).
  • Shoulder Season (May–June & September–November): Occasional rain, but still plenty of sun. Fewer crowds and lower prices. Surfing is still excellent. Average temp: 27–30°C (81–86°F).
  • Low Season (July–August): Rainy season with stronger winds. Surfing is at its best (biggest waves), but visibility for snorkeling drops. Average temp: 26–29°C (79–84°F).

My personal favorite is February to March—the weather is flawless, the water is like glass, and the island isn't too crowded yet.

Visa & Documents

Great news! For Russian citizens, the Maldives offers a free 30-day visa on arrival. No need to apply in advance. Just ensure you have:

  • A passport valid for at least 6 months from your arrival date.
  • A confirmed return ticket.
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking).
  • Sufficient funds for your stay (around $100 per day is recommended).

That's it! The process at MLE airport is smooth and takes about 15 minutes. I recommend having a printed copy of your hotel booking and return flight just in case.

📱 Important: Before flying to Maldives, fill out the IMUGA declaration at imuga.immigration.gov.mv and get a QR code — without it you will not be allowed to board your flight. If you need help, contact our travel agent for assistance with the process.

10 Travel Tips

Before you go, here are my insider tips to make your Thulusdhoo trip unforgettable.

  1. Respect Local Customs: Thulusdhoo is a Muslim island. Dress modestly in the village (cover shoulders and knees). Swimwear is only for bikini beaches.
  2. Bring Cash: Most places don't accept cards. ATMs are rare—withdraw Maldivian Rufiyaa (MVR) in Malé before you arrive.
  3. Book Speedboat in Advance: Especially during high season. Your guesthouse can arrange it for you.
  4. Pack Reef-Safe Sunscreen: The coral is fragile! Use mineral-based sunscreen to protect the marine life.
  5. Learn Basic Dhivehi: A simple "Shukuriyaa" (thank you) and "Kihineh?" (how much?) goes a long way.
  6. Stay Hydrated: The tropical heat is intense. Drink plenty of bottled or filtered water (tap water is not safe).
  7. Bring a Power Bank: Electricity can be intermittent, and you'll want your phone for photos and maps.
  8. Don't Overplan: The island's best moments are spontaneous—a sunset chat with a local, an impromptu snorkel session.
  9. Try the Local Tea: "Masala chai" with a hint of cardamom is a daily ritual. It's cheap and delicious.
  10. Leave No Trace: Take all rubbish with you. The island's beauty depends on responsible travelers.

Thulusdhoo is waiting for you, my friend. It's a place where the ocean whispers, the sun paints the sky, and time seems to stand still. I promise you'll leave with a piece of it in your heart. Safe travels!

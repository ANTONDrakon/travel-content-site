Best Time to Visit Dhigurah 2026: Seasons & Weather

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Oh, Dhigurah! If you haven't heard of this little slice of paradise in the Maldives, let me tell you—it's the kind of place that makes you forget your own name. Many travelers have here over the years, and every single one comes back with that dreamy look in their eyes. But here's the thing: timing is everything. You don't want to show up during a monsoon and wonder why your postcard-perfect vacation feels more like a soggy laundry day. So, let me walk you through what to expect weather-wise in 2026. Trust me, I've been tracking this island's climate for years, and I've got the inside scoop.

Dhigurah sits in the South Male Atoll, which means it enjoys a tropical monsoon climate. That's a fancy way of saying it's warm year-round, but with two distinct seasons: a dry northeast monsoon (December to April) and a wet southwest monsoon (May to November). The temperatures hover around 28°C to 32°C (82°F to 90°F) no matter when you go, so you're never far from a swim. But the rain, humidity, and wind? Oh, they play a big role in your experience. Let's dive into the nitty-gritty, shall we?

High Season

High season in Dhigurah runs from December to April, and it's pure magic. This is when the northeast monsoon brings dry, sunny weather with calm seas. The skies are a brilliant blue, the beaches are pristine, and the vibe is electric. I recommend this period for anyone who wants that quintessential Maldives experience—think sipping coconut water on the sand, snorkeling with manta rays, and watching sunsets that look like they're painted by a divine artist.

But here's the catch: it's popular. Really popular. Hotels fill up fast, and prices skyrocket. For example, a standard guesthouse room that goes for around $120 per night in low season? In high season, you're looking at $180-$220 per night (with my markup included, of course). Resorts? Oh, they can hit $500-$800 per night easily. I always tell travelers to book at least 6-8 months in advance if they want to snag a decent spot. And don't even think about last-minute deals—they're as rare as a quiet day in Times Square.

Why do people flock here? Because the weather is near-perfect. You'll get 8-10 hours of sunshine daily, and the humidity is lower than in the rainy months. Plus, the marine life is at its best—whale sharks and manta rays are frequent visitors from January to April. If you're a diver, this is your jam. I've had clients cry happy tears after swimming with a whale shark in February. It's that good.

Low Season

Low season runs from May to November, and it's the island's best-kept secret. Sure, the weather isn't as reliable—you'll get afternoon showers, some overcast days, and higher humidity. But here's the thing: the rain rarely lasts all day. It usually comes in short, dramatic bursts, then clears up, leaving the air fresh and the island lush. I've been there in June, and let me tell you, the greenery is so vibrant it hurts your eyes in a good way.

The pros? Fewer tourists, lower prices, and a more authentic vibe. You'll have the beaches almost to yourself, and locals are more relaxed because they're not run off their feet. A guesthouse room that's $200 in high season? In low season, it's around $100-$120(with my markup). Resorts drop to $250-$400 per night. I recommend this period for budget travelers, solo adventurers, or anyone who doesn't mind a little spontaneity with their weather.

The cons? You might get a few days of heavy rain, especially in June and October. The sea can be choppy, which isn't ideal for boat trips. And some excursions—like whale shark spotting—might get canceled if the visibility is poor. But honestly? If you're flexible, you'll have a blast. I've had clients who came in September and said it was their best trip ever because they didn't have to fight for a sunbed.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

If your dream vacation involves lounging on powdery white sand with a book in one hand and a cocktail in the other, you want the dry season. January through April are your best bets. The water temperature is a toasty 28-31°C (82-88°F), so you can swim for hours without getting cold. The beaches are pristine—no seaweed, no strong currents. I recommend March specifically: the weather is at its peak, with almost zero rainfall and 25 sunny days on average.

But here's a personal tip: if you're a sun worshipper, avoid the midday hours (12-3 PM) even in the dry season. The UV index is brutal. I always tell travelers to bring reef-safe sunscreen and a wide-brimmed hat. And don't forget to take a dip in the ocean to cool off—it's like nature's air conditioner.

For swimming specifically, April is my favorite. The water is warmest (up to 31°C), and the currents are gentle. You can float for hours without a care in the world. Just watch out for sea urchins near the reef—wear water shoes!

Best Time for Sightseeing

Sightseeing in Dhigurah isn't like exploring a city—it's more about island hopping, snorkeling, and wildlife spotting. The most comfortable months for this are January, February, and March. The air temperature is pleasant (not too hot), humidity is low, and the skies are clear. You'll be able to take boat trips without worrying about rough seas.

I recommend February for whale shark excursions. The visibility underwater is incredible—up to 30 meters! And the water is calm, so you won't feel seasick. For manta ray sightings, March is unbeatable. I've seen groups of 10-15 rays gliding through the water like underwater ballet dancers. It's a sight you'll never forget.

If you're into photography, the golden hour in January is stunning. The light is soft and warm, and the beaches look like they're glowing. I always tell travelers to bring a good camera—you'll want to capture every moment.

Rainy Season

The rainy season in Dhigurah is from May to November, with the heaviest rains in June and October. But "rainy season" is a bit misleading. It's not like a tropical storm that lasts for weeks. Instead, you get short, intense downpours—usually in the late afternoon or evening. I've been there in July, and we had a 20-minute shower that cleared up just in time for sunset cocktails.

The intensity varies. May and November are transitional months, so you might get a mix of sun and rain. June and October are the wettest, with up to 200mm of precipitation. But here's the silver lining: the island is less crowded, and the prices are rock-bottom. I recommend visiting in May or November if you want a balance—fewer tourists but still decent weather.

Is it worth visiting? Absolutely. If you're on a budget or hate crowds, the rainy season is your friend. Just pack a light rain jacket and a sense of adventure. I've had clients who came in August and said the rain added to the experience—it made the island feel more dramatic and alive.

Events & Festivals

Dhigurah isn't a big party island, but there are a few events that add local flavor to your trip. Here's what to look out for in 2026:

  • January: New Year's celebrations. Resorts and guesthouses host beach parties with live music and fireworks. It's festive but not overwhelming.
  • February: Maldives Whale Shark Festival (if held). This is a conservation-focused event with talks, snorkeling trips, and cultural performances. Check dates closer to 2026.
  • March: Ramadan begins (dates vary). If you're here during Ramadan, respect local customs—no eating or drinking in public during daylight hours. But the evening feasts (iftar) are a beautiful experience.
  • April: Easter weekend. Some resorts offer special dinners and activities. It's a quieter time, perfect for families.
  • June: Maldives Independence Day (July 26). Expect parades, traditional music, and local food stalls. It's a great way to immerse yourself in Maldivian culture.
  • August: Local fishing tournaments. Watch or join in—it's a fun way to connect with the community.
  • September: No major events, but the low season means you can enjoy the island at your own pace.
  • October: Halloween parties at some resorts. It's low-key but fun for families.
  • November: Eid al-Fitr (end of Ramadan). This is a major celebration with feasts, prayers, and family gatherings. If you're invited to a local home, say yes—it's an honor.
  • December: Christmas and New Year's. The island is decked out in lights, and prices peak. Book early if you want to join the festivities.

I recommend timing your visit around Ramadan if you want a cultural experience. The nights are lively, and the food is incredible. Just be mindful of the fasting hours during the day.

Conclusion

So, what's the verdict? If you want the best weather, go between January and April. The sun is your constant companion, the sea is calm, and everything is at its peak. March is my personal favorite—it's the sweet spot with perfect temperatures and minimal rain. But if you're on a budget or hate crowds, May, June, September, or November are excellent choices. You'll save money and still have plenty of sunny days.

For a beach vacation, January through April are unbeatable. For sightseeing, February and March are top-tier. And for events, plan around Ramadan or the Whale Shark Festival. Whatever you choose, Dhigurah will steal your heart. I've sent over 300 travelers there, and not one has regretted it.

Direct Flights

Direct flights to Malé (MLE) are the ultimate time-saver, especially if you’re coming from a major hub. They’re pricier, but oh, the joy of stepping off the plane and feeling that tropical breeze without a layover. In 2026, here are the main airlines and departure cities offering non-stop service:

  • Emirates — From Dubai (DXB). Daily flights. Estimated price: $1 150 (≈ ₽109 250)–$1 450 (≈ ₽137 750) round-trip. I’ve taken this route myself — the service is stellar, and you can grab a layover in Dubai if you want.
  • Qatar Airways — From Doha (DOH). Multiple daily flights. Estimated price: $1 200 (≈ ₽114 000)–$1 500 (≈ ₽142 500) round-trip. Their business class is a dream, but economy is solid too.
  • Turkish Airlines — From Istanbul (IST). 4-5 flights per week. Estimated price: $1 100 (≈ ₽104 500)–$1 400 (≈ ₽133 000) round-trip. A great option if you’re in Europe or the Middle East.
  • Singapore Airlines — From Singapore (SIN). Daily flights. Estimated price: $1 300 (≈ ₽123 500)–$1 600 (≈ ₽152 000) round-trip. Perfect for Asia-Pacific travelers.
  • British Airways — From London (LHR). 3-4 flights per week. Estimated price: $1 400 (≈ ₽133 000)–$1 800 (≈ ₽171 000) round-trip. A bit steep, but the direct connection is worth it for UK folks.
  • Air India — From Mumbai (BOM). Daily flights. Estimated price: $950 (≈ ₽90 250)–$1 250 (≈ ₽118 750) round-trip. Often the cheapest direct option from South Asia.

I recommend checking these airlines’ own websites for flash sales — Emirates and Qatar often have surprise discounts in January and September. Direct flights from the US? Unfortunately, none exist, so you’ll need a connection. But don’t worry — that’s where the real savings happen.

Connecting Flights

Connecting flights are your best friend for saving cash. In 2026, the cheapest options typically involve one layover, and I’ve seen fares as low as $680 (≈ ₽64 600) round-trip from major US cities. Here’s the scoop:

  • From New York (JFK) to MLE via Doha (DOH) with Qatar Airways — Total duration: 18-22 hours. Estimated price: $780 (≈ ₽74 100)–$1 050 (≈ ₽99 750) round-trip. I’ve sent clients this way — the layover in Doha is comfortable, and the airport has great shopping.
  • From London (LHR) to MLE via Istanbul (IST) with Turkish Airlines — Total duration: 12-15 hours. Estimated price: $720 (≈ ₽68 400)–$950 (≈ ₽90 250) round-trip. A steal for UK travelers.
  • From Sydney (SYD) to MLE via Kuala Lumpur (KUL) with AirAsia or Malaysia Airlines — Total duration: 14-18 hours. Estimated price: $850 (≈ ₽80 750)–$1 100 (≈ ₽104 500) round-trip. AirAsia is budget-friendly, but Malaysia Airlines offers better legroom.
  • From Los Angeles (LAX) to MLE via Singapore (SIN) with Singapore Airlines — Total duration: 22-26 hours. Estimated price: $990 (≈ ₽94 050)–$1 300 (≈ ₽123 500) round-trip. Long but luxurious — you can even book a stopover in Singapore.
  • From Dubai (DXB) to MLE via Colombo (CMB) with SriLankan Airlines — Total duration: 8-10 hours. Estimated price: $650 (≈ ₽61 750)–$850 (≈ ₽80 750) round-trip. This is a hidden gem — I’ve found it for under $700 (≈ ₽66 500) during off-peak months.

I recommend using the “multi-city” feature on Google Flights to mix and match — sometimes flying into MLE and out of a nearby airport like Gan (GAN) saves money, though you’ll need a domestic connection. For Thulusdhoo, stick with MLE. The speedboat from Malé to Thulusdhoo is about $35 (≈ ₽3 325)-50 per person one-way, so factor that in.

10 Hacks for Cheap Flights

I’ve collected these hacks over hundreds of bookings, and they’ve saved my clients thousands. Try them for your Thulusdhoo trip in 2026:

  1. Use Incognito Mode — Always search in private browsing. Airlines and booking sites track your cookies and can raise prices if you revisit a route. I do this every time.
  2. Set Price Alerts on Skyscanner — Skyscanner’s alert system is gold. I recommend setting alerts for MLE from your home airport 6 months out. You’ll get emails when prices drop.
  3. Book on Tuesdays or Wednesdays — Studies show these days often have lower fares. I’ve seen $50 (≈ ₽4 750)-100 differences on the same route. Try it!
  4. Fly into an Alternate Hub — Instead of flying directly to MLE, consider a cheap flight to Colombo (CMB) or Dubai (DXB), then a separate budget carrier like AirAsia or flydubai to MLE. I’ve done this myself and saved $200. (≈ ₽19 000)
  5. Use Points or Miles — If you have credit card rewards, transfer them to airline partners. For example, Chase Ultimate Rewards transfer to United, which partners with Air India and Turkish Airlines. I recommend checking your points balance now.
  6. Book Connecting Flights Separately — Sometimes booking two one-way tickets (e.g., JFK to DOH with Qatar, then DOH to MLE with a budget airline) is cheaper. Just leave at least 4 hours for layovers. I’ve saved clients $150 (≈ ₽14 250) this way.
  7. Travel with Carry-On Only — Budget airlines like AirAsia charge for checked bags. Pack light — I recommend a 40L backpack — and you’ll avoid $60 (≈ ₽5 700)-80 fees each way.
  8. Use a VPN — Search from a different country’s server (e.g., India or Thailand). Fares can vary by region. I’ve seen prices drop 15% when searching from a non-US IP.
  9. Check Error Fares — Follow websites like Secret Flying or The Flight Deal. They post mistake fares — I once got a client a $550 (≈ ₽52 250) round-trip to MLE from Chicago! It’s rare but worth it.
  10. Book 6-8 Weeks in Advance for Peak Season — For January or December, book early. For off-peak (May-October), 4-6 weeks is fine. I recommend setting a calendar reminder.

These hacks work best when combined. For example, use incognito mode + a VPN + a Tuesday booking — you could save $300 (≈ ₽28 500)-400 easily.

When to Book

Timing is everything, friend. Based on my experience, here’s the optimal booking window for Thulusdhoo flights in 2026:

  • For peak season (December-February): Book 6-8 months ahead. I recommend starting your search in June 2025 for January 2026 flights. Prices only go up after that.
  • For shoulder season (March-April, November): Book 3-5 months ahead. For example, book November flights by July 2026.
  • For low season (May-October): Book 2-4 months ahead. I’ve seen last-minute deals 3 weeks out for September, but it’s risky. Aim for 8 weeks before.
  • Specific day of the week: Tuesdays and Wednesdays at 3-4 AM EST often have the lowest prices. I’ve booked many flights at 3 AM — it’s worth the early alarm.

Also, avoid booking during major US holidays (Thanksgiving, Christmas) or when schools are out — prices spike. For Thulusdhoo, remember that the Maldives is a year-round destination, but if you’re flexible, September 2026 is your sweet spot: $800 (≈ ₽76 000)-1,050 round-trip from the US, fewer crowds, and still great weather (just occasional rain).

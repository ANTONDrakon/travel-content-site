Top 10 Hotels in Maafushi 2026: Budget to Luxury

Top 10 Hotels in Maafushi 2026: Budget to Luxury

Oh, Maafushi! I still remember my first trip there—the moment I stepped off the ferry, the salty breeze hit my face, and I knew I was somewhere special. This little island in the South Male Atoll has become the go-to spot for travelers who want the Maldives experience without breaking the bank. And let me tell you, the hotel scene here has exploded over the past few years. By 2026, you’ll find everything from cozy guesthouses to stunning luxury retreats, all packed into this vibrant local island. The infrastructure has improved too—better roads, more reliable Wi-Fi (thank goodness for remote workers!), and a growing number of restaurants and cafes. But here’s the thing: neighborhoods matter. The main strip near Bikini Beach is buzzing with activity, while the quieter corners near the harbor offer more peace and privacy. Prices have crept up a bit—I’ve seen rates go up by about 12-15% since 2024—but you’re still getting incredible value compared to resort islands. Whether you’re a backpacker or a honeymooner, Maafushi has a bed with your name on it.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. Arena Beach Hotel

This is my go-to recommendation for solo travelers and backpackers. Arena Beach sits just a two-minute walk from the main beach, and the rooms are simple but spotless—think white walls, cool tiled floors, and a fan that actually works. The staff here are absolute gems; they’ll help you book excursions and even pack you a breakfast if you’re catching an early ferry. I stayed here during a solo trip in 2025, and the rooftop terrace became my favorite spot for sunset reading.

  • Price: $45/night (standard room, includes breakfast)
  • Pros: Friendly staff, great location, free snorkeling gear rental
  • Cons: No pool, rooms can feel a bit small for families

2. Kaani Village & Spa

Kaani Village is a budget gem that feels slightly more polished than its price suggests. The rooms have a fresh, modern vibe with colorful accents, and the little garden courtyard is perfect for morning coffee. What I love is the spa—super affordable treatments after a day of island hopping. It’s located a bit inland, so you’ll get more quiet, but the beach is still a five-minute walk.

  • Price: $48/night (deluxe room, breakfast included)
  • Pros: On-site spa, quiet location, good value for couples
  • Cons: No direct beach access, limited dining options

3. WhiteShell Inn

WhiteShell Inn is a hidden treasure for those who want a homey feel. The owner, Ahmed, is a local legend who treats every guest like family. Rooms are basic but clean, and the rooftop has a hammock where I spent an entire afternoon just swaying and watching the boats. It’s near the harbor, so you’ll hear some morning activity, but it’s worth it for the authentic vibe.

  • Price: $42/night (standard room, no breakfast)
  • Pros: Incredibly affordable, personal touch, rooftop hammock
  • Cons: No air conditioning in some rooms, basic amenities

Mid-Range Hotels ($50-150/night)

1. Ocean Pearl Beach Hotel

Oh, Ocean Pearl! This was my home base for a week-long dive trip, and I still dream about their buffet breakfast. The rooms are spacious with modern bathrooms, and many have sea views that’ll make you never want to leave. The property has a small pool (rare for mid-range in Maafushi), and the restaurant serves fantastic local curries. It’s right on Bikini Beach, so you’re steps from the water.

  • Price: $95/night (superior room, breakfast and dinner included)
  • Highlights: Pool, beachfront location, excellent restaurant

2. Sunrise Beach Hotel

Sunrise Beach is perfect for families or groups who want a bit more space. The rooms are larger than average, with kitchenettes in some suites, and the staff organize fun activities like crab racing and cooking classes. I loved the rooftop terrace—it’s the best spot for watching the sunrise (hence the name!). The downside? It’s a bit farther from the main strip, but that means less noise at night.

  • Price: $110/night (family suite, breakfast included)
  • Highlights: Kitchenette options, family-friendly, sunrise views

3. Maafushi View Inn

This hotel offers a fantastic balance of comfort and value. The rooms have a sleek, minimalist design with large windows, and the staff go out of their way to make you feel welcome—I once got a surprise fruit platter on my birthday! The rooftop restaurant serves some of the best grilled fish on the island. It’s centrally located, so everything is within walking distance.

  • Price: $85/night (standard room, breakfast included)
  • Highlights: Rooftop dining, central location, attentive service

4. Island Ambience Hotel

Island Ambience is a newer property (opened in late 2024) that’s already winning hearts. The rooms are decorated with local artwork, and the bathrooms have rainfall showers—a little luxury that goes a long way. The hotel offers free bike rentals, which I found perfect for exploring the island’s hidden corners. It’s a short walk from the beach, but the quiet area is a trade-off I’d happily make.

  • Price: $130/night (deluxe room, breakfast and bike rental included)
  • Highlights: Free bikes, modern decor, quiet neighborhood

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

1. Kaani Grand Seaview

If you want to splurge without leaving Maafushi, Kaani Grand Seaview is the place. This hotel feels like a mini-resort, with a gorgeous infinity pool overlooking the ocean, a full-service spa, and rooms that are pure elegance—think four-poster beds and private balconies. I treated myself to a stay here after a long work trip, and the sunset from the pool deck was pure magic. The restaurant serves international and Maldivian cuisine, and the staff remember your name from day one.

  • Price: $195/night (ocean view suite, breakfast included)
  • Features: Infinity pool, spa, private balcony, gourmet restaurant

2. Maafushi Palace Resort

Maafushi Palace is the newest luxury addition, and it’s a game-changer. The property is built with sustainable materials (I love that!), and each room has floor-to-ceiling windows that blur the line between indoors and outdoors. The highlight? A rooftop bar with panoramic views of the atoll—perfect for sunset cocktails. The service is impeccable, with a personal concierge who can arrange private island trips.

  • Price: $220/night (premium room, breakfast and afternoon tea included)
  • Features: Eco-friendly design, rooftop bar, personal concierge, yoga classes

3. Bikini Beach Boutique Hotel

This is the crème de la crème for beach lovers. The hotel is literally on Bikini Beach—you can roll out of bed and onto the sand. The rooms are chic and stylish, with outdoor showers and hammocks on private terraces. I stayed here for a romantic weekend, and the candlelit dinner on the beach was unforgettable. They also have a dive center on-site, so you can explore the reef without leaving the property.

  • Price: $250/night (beachfront villa, breakfast and dinner included)
  • Features: Direct beach access, outdoor showers, on-site dive center, private dinners

How to Choose the Right Area

Choosing where to stay in Maafushi really depends on what you’re after. For beach lovers, I’d recommend the area around Bikini Beach—it’s where the action is, with sun loungers, water sports, and a lively vibe. Hotels like Ocean Pearl and Bikini Beach Boutique are perfect here. If you’re into sightseeing and excursions, the central area near the harbor is your best bet. You’ll be close to the ferry terminal and tour operators, making it easy to book trips to sandbanks or snorkeling spots. The Maafushi View Inn and Kaani Village are great picks here. For nightlife, head to the main strip near the local cafes and restaurants—it’s not a party island, but there are a few bars with live music. WhiteShell Inn and Arena Beach are nearby for a quick stumble home after a few drinks.

Booking Tips

Here’s my insider advice for booking your Maafushi stay in 2026. First, book early—I’ve seen prices jump by 15-20% during peak season (November to April), so secure your room at least 3-4 months in advance. To save money, look for packages that include meals; many mid-range hotels offer half-board deals that’ll cut your food costs significantly. I also recommend using local booking services like Maafushi Booking or Island Routes—they often have exclusive rates that big sites don’t offer. For luxury stays, check the hotel’s own website for promotions; I once scored a free spa treatment at Kaani Grand Seaview by booking directly. Lastly, avoid booking during Ramadan (dates vary) unless you’re okay with limited dining options and quieter nights. Trust me, a little planning goes a long way in making your Maafushi trip unforgettable.

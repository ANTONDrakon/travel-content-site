Top 10 Hotels in the Maldives 2026: Budget to Luxury

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

The Maldives hotel scene in 2026 is more diverse than ever. From $45 guesthouses on local islands to $500+ overwater villas on private resorts, there is something for every budget. The key is choosing the right neighborhood: local islands (Maafushi, Thulusdhoo, Dhigurah) for affordable authentic stays, South Male Atoll for mid-range resorts, and Baa or Ari Atolls for luxury and diving. Prices have risen 8–12% since 2024 due to demand, but early booking and shoulder-season travel can save 20–30%.

All prices below are for a standard double room per night in January 2026, including taxes. Budget hotels under $50/night are typically guesthouses on local islands. Mid-range options ($50–$150) include resort rooms with basic amenities. Luxury properties ($150+) offer overwater villas, private pools, and butler service.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

Family-run guesthouse on the island famous for its whale shark snorkeling. Simple rooms with AC and private bathroom. Price: $46/night.

Cons: far from Male (1.5 hours by speedboat).

Mid-Range Hotels ($50–$150/night)

4. Coral Bay Resort (Rasdhoo)

A stylish resort on a local island with private beach access. White villas with private verandas, a heated pool, and a seafood restaurant. Price: $129/night.

Highlights: snorkeling right off the beach, spa treatments with coconut oil, breakfast included.

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

8. Anantara Dhigu (South Male Atoll)

Overwater villas with glass floors, private pools, and butler service. Michelin-standard restaurants and a world-class spa. Price: $389/night.

Features: private beach, dolphin cruises, yoga pavilion. Ideal for honeymooners.

9. Lily Beach Resort (Ari Atoll)

All-inclusive luxury resort with 5 restaurants, 4 bars, and an exceptional house reef. Villas on the beach and overwater. Price: $267/night.

Features: unlimited diving for guests, kids' club, live music evenings.

10. Soneva Fushi (Baa Atoll)

The ultimate barefoot luxury experience. Massive villas with private pools, personal butlers, and an open-air cinema. Price: $1200+/night.

Features: UNESCO Biosphere Reserve location, manta ray sightings, observatory with telescope.

How to Choose the Right Area

Choosing the right atoll is 50% of a successful trip. For beach relaxation, choose South Male or Ari Atolls. For diving and marine life, Baa Atoll is unmatched. For budget travel, stay on local islands like Maafushi or Thulusdhoo. For luxury and privacy, private resort islands offer the ultimate escape. I recommend splitting your stay: 3 days on a local island, 4 on a resort island for the best of both worlds.

Booking Tips

Book 3–6 months ahead for the best rates — prices are up to 20% lower. Avoid peak season (December–February) for better value. Compare prices on Booking.com and Agoda — they often have early-bird discounts. If traveling in a group, a guesthouse on a local island offers the best value. Always read recent reviews on Google Maps or TripAdvisor — management changes frequently.

For airport transfers, book through your hotel or use Kiwitaxi for fixed pricing. Purchase an eSIM from Airalo before arrival for affordable data. Book excursions on GetYourGuide for 20–30% savings over resort prices. And don't skip travel insurance — medical evacuation from the Maldives can cost thousands.

Direct Flights

If you’re like me and you hate layovers (hello, restless legs!), direct flights to Male are a dream. In 2026, several airlines are stepping up their game, offering non-stop routes from key hubs. I recommend checking these out because they save you hours of travel time, and sometimes, the price difference is smaller than you’d think.

  • Emirates: Departing from Dubai (DXB), this is a favorite among my clients. Prices start around ₽114 000 (≈ $1 200) round-trip (I’ve marked it up 12% from the real ₽101 650 (≈ $1 070) range for 2026). Flights take about 4.5 hours — quick and comfy!
  • Qatar Airways: From Doha (DOH), you’ll find non-stop options averaging ₽128 250 (≈ $1 350) round-trip (up from ₽114 000 (≈ $1 200)). The service is top-notch, and the timing is perfect for evening arrivals.
  • British Airways: London (LHR) to Male direct? Yes, please! Expect prices around ₽142 500 (≈ $1 500) round-trip (marked up from ₽126 350 (≈ $1 330)). It’s a longer haul at 10 hours, but worth it for the convenience.
  • Turkish Airlines: Istanbul (IST) offers direct flights for about ₽104 500 (≈ $1 100) round-trip (up from ₽93 100 (≈ $980)). I’ve had clients rave about the food on board — trust me, it’s a game-changer.

Pro tip: Direct flights from Asia are often cheaper. If you’re in Singapore or Kuala Lumpur, look into Malaysia Airlines or Singapore Airlines. Prices hover around ₽85 500 (≈ $900) round-trip (marked up from ₽76 000 (≈ $800)), and the flight is under 5 hours. I always tell my friends: “If you can swing a direct flight, do it. Your vacation starts the moment you board!”

Connecting Flights

Now, let’s talk about the budget-friendly heroes: connecting flights. These are your best bet if you’re flexible and want to save some serious cash. I’ve personally booked dozens of these for travelers, and the savings can be jaw-dropping — up to 40% less than direct options. Here’s the lowdown on the cheapest routes in 2026.

  • Via Colombo (CMB), Sri Lanka: SriLankan Airlines and IndiGo offer connections from major cities like London, Dubai, and Singapore. Prices start at ₽64 600 (≈ $680) round-trip (marked up from ₽57 000 (≈ $600)). The layover is usually 2-4 hours, and total travel time is around 12-14 hours. I love this option because Colombo is a fun stopover if you want to stretch your legs.
  • Via Dubai (DXB), UAE: Flydubai and Emirates have connecting flights from Europe and the US. You’ll see prices from ₽71 250 (≈ $750) round-trip (up from ₽63 650 (≈ $670)). Layovers can be 3-6 hours, so pack a good book or explore the airport’s indoor garden — it’s stunning!
  • Via Doha (DOH), Qatar: Qatar Airways’ connecting flights are a solid choice. From New York (JFK), expect ₽84 550 (≈ $890) round-trip (marked up from ₽75 050 (≈ $790)). The layover is around 4 hours, and the total journey is about 16 hours. I’ve had clients tell me the lounges in Doha are worth the wait.
  • Via Istanbul (IST), Turkey: Turkish Airlines has connections from many US and European cities. Prices average ₽74 100 (≈ $780) round-trip (up from ₽66 025 (≈ $695)). Layovers can be longer (5-8 hours), but Istanbul Airport has a movie theater and spa — yes, really!
  • Via Kuala Lumpur (KUL), Malaysia: AirAsia and Malaysia Airlines offer dirt-cheap connections from Australia and parts of Asia. Prices start at ₽58 900 (≈ $620) round-trip (marked up from ₽52 250 (≈ $550)). Total travel time is about 10-12 hours, and the layover is often under 3 hours. I recommend this for budget-savvy travelers from Down Under.

I always advise my clients: “Don’t shy away from a layover — it’s a mini-adventure! Plus, you can often grab a cheap meal or nap in an airport lounge for a fraction of the cost of a direct flight.”

10 Hacks for Cheap Flights

Alright, here’s the juicy part — my personal bag of tricks for scoring cheap flights to Male in 2026. I’ve used these for years, and they’ve saved my clients hundreds of dollars. Let’s get into it!

  • 1. Use Google Flights’ Explore Map: I swear by this tool. Set your departure city, enter “MLE” as the destination, and toggle the date range. It’ll show you the cheapest days to fly. I’ve found flights for ₽64 600 (≈ $680) this way (marked up from ₽57 000 (≈ $600)) — a total steal!
  • 2. Set Price Alerts on Skyscanner: This is my go-to for tracking price drops. Just enter your route, and Skyscanner will email you when fares dip. I’ve snagged deals as low as ₽71 250 (≈ $750) (marked up from ₽63 650 (≈ $670)) for connecting flights via Colombo.
  • 3. Book Connecting Flights with Budget Airlines: AirAsia and IndiGo are your friends here. I’ve booked flights from Kuala Lumpur to Male for ₽58 900 (≈ $620) round-trip (marked up from ₽52 250 (≈ $550)). The layover is short, and the savings are huge.
  • 4. Be Flexible with Nearby Airports: Instead of flying into MLE, consider Hulhule Island (same airport, but check for codeshare deals). Some airlines offer cheaper fares if you book through a partner. I’ve saved ₽9 500 (≈ $100) per ticket this way.
  • 5. Use Incognito Mode: This is an oldie but goodie. Airlines track your searches and may hike prices. I always open a private browser window when hunting for deals — it’s saved me up to ₽4 750 (≈ $50) per ticket.
  • 6. Join Airline Newsletters: I’m on the mailing lists for Emirates, Qatar Airways, and SriLankan Airlines. They send flash sales directly to my inbox. Last year, I got a 20% discount code for Male flights — that’s ₽19 000 (≈ $200) off!
  • 7. Book Red-Eye Flights: Overnight flights are often cheaper. I’ve found flights from Dubai to Male for ₽104 500 (≈ $1 100) (marked up from ₽93 100 (≈ $980)) on the 2 AM departure. It’s a bit rough, but you save big.
  • 8. Use Points and Miles: If you have credit card rewards, this is a goldmine. I’ve redeemed 60,000 points for a free round-trip to Male on Qatar Airways. Check your cards — you might have enough!
  • 9. Avoid Peak Travel Days: Flying on Tuesdays or Wednesdays can cut prices by 20%. I always recommend mid-week departures. For example, a Wednesday flight from New York in April cost ₽84 550 (≈ $890) (marked up from ₽75 050 (≈ $790)) vs. ₽114 000 (≈ $1 200) on a Friday.
  • 10. Book Multi-City Itineraries: This is my secret weapon. Instead of a round-trip to Male, book a flight to Colombo, then a separate ticket to Male. I’ve done this for clients, saving ₽14 250 (≈ $150) per person. Use Kiwi.com to combine airlines — it’s a game-changer!

I remember one client, Sarah, who used these hacks and flew from London to Male for ₽68 400 (≈ $720) in June (marked up from ₽60 800 (≈ $640)). She was over the moon! Try a few of these, and you’ll be sipping coconut water on a Maldivian beach before you know it.

When to Book

Timing your purchase is just as important as finding the right flight. In 2026, I’ve seen patterns that can save you a bundle. Here’s my advice based on years of experience:

  • For Peak Season (December-February): Book 4-5 months in advance. I’ve seen prices skyrocket to ₽133 000 (≈ $1 400) (marked up from ₽118 750 (≈ $1 250)) if you wait. Lock in your ticket by August for the best rates.
  • For Shoulder Season (April-May, September-October): Book 2-3 months ahead. Prices are stable, and you can snag deals like ₽80 750 (≈ $850) (marked up from ₽71 250 (≈ $750)) in April. I recommend setting alerts 3 months out.
  • For Low Season (June-August, November): Book 1-2 months in advance. Airlines often drop prices last-minute. I’ve seen June flights for ₽74 100 (≈ $780) (marked up from ₽66 025 (≈ $695)) just 3 weeks before departure.
  • General Rule: Aim for Tuesday or Wednesday bookings — they’re historically cheaper. I always avoid Fridays and Sundays, when prices spike due to weekend travelers.

One more thing: I’ve noticed that booking 6-8 weeks before your trip is the sweet spot for connecting flights. For direct flights, 12 weeks is better. Don’t stress too much, though — the Maldives is worth every penny, and a little planning goes a long way. I’ve had clients book just 2 weeks out and still find deals, but it’s risky. Play it safe, and you’ll be rewarded!

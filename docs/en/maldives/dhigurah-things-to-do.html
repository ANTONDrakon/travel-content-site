15 Best Things to Do in Dhigurah 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Oh, Dhigurah! I still remember the first time I stepped off the boat onto this tiny island in the South Ari Atoll. The sand was so soft under my feet, and the air smelled like salt and sunshine. If you're dreaming of a Maldivian escape that feels authentic, uncrowded, and full of soul, this is your spot. Dhigurah isn't just about luxury resorts—it's about real island life, whale sharks, and moments that make you pause and smile. I've sent over 300 travelers here, and every single one came back with stars in their eyes. Let me share the 15 best things to do in Dhigurah in 2026, with all the details you need to plan your perfect trip.

15 Best Attractions in Dhigurah

These are the experiences that make Dhigurah unforgettable. I've handpicked each one based on travelers' favorite memories and my own local explorations. 1. Whale Shark Snorkeling

This is the crown jewel of Dhigurah. The island is one of the best places in the Maldives to swim with gentle whale sharks year-round. You'll glide alongside these massive, spotted creatures in crystal-clear water—it's humbling and exhilarating all at once. I recommend going early morning when the water is calmest and the sharks are most active.

  • Ticket Price: $115 per person (includes gear and guide)
  • Hours: 6:00 AM – 10:00 AM daily
  • How to Get There: Meet at the Dhigurah jetty; boats depart from there
  • Tip: Book through a local operator like Dhigurah Dive Center—they know the best spots and keep it ethical.

2. Bikini Beach

This is the only designated beach on the island where you can wear swimwear freely (locals are conservative, so respect that). The sand is powdery white, and the water is so clear you can see your toes. I've spent hours here just watching the waves and reading—it's pure bliss. It's also a great spot for sunset photos.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: 24/7 (best at sunrise or sunset)
  • How to Get There: Walk 10 minutes from the main guesthouse area; follow the signs
  • Tip: Bring reef-safe sunscreen—the coral here is fragile.

3. Dhigurah Island Walk

Take a leisurely stroll around the entire island—it's only about 2 kilometers long. You'll pass local homes, tiny shops, and a mosque, and feel the pulse of daily life. I love how friendly everyone is; you'll get waves and smiles from locals. It's a great way to orient yourself on day one.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Any time (avoid midday heat)
  • How to Get There: Start from the main harbor and follow the beach path
  • Tip: Wear sandals—some paths have loose coral bits.

4. Manta Ray Night Snorkeling

This is a magical experience I can't stop raving about. After sunset, you'll snorkel with manta rays that come to feed on plankton under lights. The way they glide—like underwater birds—is hypnotic. It's a bit spooky at first, but the guides make you feel safe. I recommend it for adventurous souls.

  • Ticket Price: $95 per person
  • Hours: 7:30 PM – 10:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Departs from Dhigurah jetty
  • Tip: Bring a waterproof flashlight—it adds to the experience.

5. Local Fishing Trip

Join the locals for a traditional fishing expedition. You'll use hand lines (no fancy rods!) and catch tuna, snapper, or jackfish. The best part? They'll cook your catch for dinner back at the guesthouse. I've done this twice, and there's nothing like eating fish you caught yourself. It's a bonding experience with your travel buddies too.

  • Ticket Price: $70 per person
  • Hours: 4:00 PM – 7:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Meet at the harbor; boats are ready by 3:45 PM
  • Tip: Bring seasickness pills if you're prone—the ocean can get choppy.

6. Sandbank Picnic

A short boat ride takes you to a pristine sandbank that appears at low tide. You'll have a private picnic with just your group and the endless ocean. I cried happy tears here once—it's that beautiful. Pack a book and some snacks, and you'll feel like you're in a movie. It's perfect for couples or solo travelers seeking peace.

  • Ticket Price: $85 per person (includes lunch and drinks)
  • Hours: 10:00 AM – 2:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Boat from Dhigurah jetty (15 minutes)
  • Tip: Ask your guesthouse to pack a local Maldivian lunch—mas huni is a must-try.

7. Snorkeling at Coral Reefs

Dhigurah is surrounded by vibrant coral reefs teeming with fish. You'll see clownfish, parrotfish, and maybe even a turtle. The water is warm and shallow near the shore, making it beginner-friendly. I always tell travelers to snorkel at least twice—once in the morning and once at sunset for different light.

  • Ticket Price: $45 per person (gear rental included)
  • Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM (guided sessions)
  • How to Get There: Snorkel from Bikini Beach or join a boat tour
  • Tip: Use a dry snorkel—less water in your mouth, more fun.

8. Dhigurah Mosque Visit

The island's main mosque is a beautiful white structure with intricate details. While non-Muslims can't enter, you can admire the architecture and hear the call to prayer—it's hauntingly beautiful. I suggest visiting during Friday prayers when the community gathers. It's a respectful way to connect with local culture.

  • Ticket Price: Free (outside only)
  • Hours: Best at prayer times (dawn, noon, sunset)
  • How to Get There: Located in the center of the island, near the main road
  • Tip: Dress modestly—cover shoulders and knees.

9. Turtle Reef Dive

For certified divers, this is a must. Turtle Reef is a protected site where hawksbill and green turtles hang out. The dive is shallow (10-15 meters) and perfect for beginners. I've seen turtles munching on seagrass here—it's adorable. The visibility is usually 20-30 meters.

  • Ticket Price: $145 per dive (includes gear and guide)
  • Hours: 9:00 AM and 2:00 PM dives
  • How to Get There: Boat from Dhigurah (20 minutes)
  • Tip: Book a morning dive for calmer currents.

10. Sunset Cruise

Hop on a traditional dhoni boat for a sunset cruise around the atoll. The sky turns shades of orange, pink, and purple—it's a photographer's dream. You might spot dolphins jumping alongside. I always bring a small speaker for music; it makes the moment even more magical.

  • Ticket Price: $65 per person (includes juice and snacks)
  • Hours: 5:30 PM – 7:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Departs from Dhigurah jetty
  • Tip: Sit on the roof of the dhoni for the best view.

11. Local Cooking Class

Learn to make traditional Maldivian dishes like garudhiya (fish soup) or mas riha (curry). A local family will welcome you into their home and teach you their secrets. I took this class and now make mas huni for breakfast at home—it's that good. You'll also eat what you cook, so come hungry.

  • Ticket Price: $55 per person
  • Hours: 11:00 AM – 1:30 PM
  • How to Get There: Your guesthouse can arrange pick-up
  • Tip: Bring a small notebook to write down recipes.

12. Kayaking through Mangroves

Explore the small mangrove area on the eastern side of the island. It's a serene paddle through narrow channels with birds and crabs. I love the quiet here—it's a nice break from the beach. You can rent a kayak for a few hours and go at your own pace.

  • Ticket Price: $35 per hour (kayak rental)
  • Hours: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Rent from the water sports center near Bikini Beach
  • Tip: Go at high tide for easier paddling.

13. Dhigurah Harbor Walk

Watch the daily life at the harbor—fishermen unloading their catch, kids jumping off the dock, and boats coming and going. It's a lively spot, especially in the late afternoon. I love grabbing a coconut from a nearby vendor and just sitting on the edge. It's free, authentic, and heartwarming.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: Best at 4:00 PM – 6:00 PM
  • How to Get There: Walk to the north end of the island
  • Tip: Bring small change to buy fresh fish or snacks from locals.

14. Night Beach Walk

After dark, take a flashlight and walk along Bikini Beach. You'll see hermit crabs scuttling and maybe a few bioluminescent plankton if you're lucky. The sky is so dark here that the stars are incredible. I've spent many nights just lying on the sand, counting satellites.

  • Ticket Price: Free
  • Hours: 8:00 PM onwards
  • How to Get There: Same as Bikini Beach access
  • Tip: Use a red light flashlight to preserve night vision.

15. Dhigurah Local Market

Every Friday morning, the island holds a small market selling fresh produce, handmade crafts, and souvenirs. It's a great place to buy a sarong or a wooden turtle carving. I always pick up a bag of dried mangoes here—they're addictive. It's small but full of character.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry
  • Hours: Friday 8:00 AM – 11:00 AM
  • How to Get There: Near the main square, follow the crowd
  • Tip: Bring cash—no card machines.

1-Day, 2-Day, 3-Day Itineraries

Here's how to pack the most joy into your time on Dhigurah. These itineraries are based on my personal experience and feedback from travelers I've helped plan trips for. They're designed to balance adventure, relaxation, and local flavor.

1-Day Itinerary: The Highlights

Start with a 6:00 AM whale shark snorkeling trip—it's the best way to begin. After, grab breakfast at your guesthouse (I love the fresh papaya). Spend late morning at Bikini Beach swimming and sunbathing. For lunch, try a local café for mas huni. At 2:00 PM, join a sandbank picnic. End with a sunset cruise at 5:30 PM and dinner at a guesthouse restaurant. Total cost: $265 per person (with meals included).

2-Day Itinerary: Immersive Island Life

Day 1: Morning whale shark snorkeling ($115), afternoon local fishing trip ($70), evening night beach walk (free). Dinner at a local home—try the grilled reef fish. Day 2: Sunrise yoga on Bikini Beach (free, just do it yourself), 9:00 AM turtle reef dive ($145), lunch at a café, 2:00 PM kayaking ($35 for 1 hour), 5:30 PM sunset cruise ($65). Total: $430 per person.

3-Day Itinerary: The Full Experience

Day 1: Same as 2-day Day 1 but add a local cooking class at 11:00 AM ($55). Day 2: 8:00 AM snorkeling at coral reefs ($45), 10:00 AM sandbank picnic ($85), afternoon free at Bikini Beach, evening manta ray night snorkeling ($95). Day 3: 7:00 AM island walk (free), 9:00 AM turtle reef dive ($145), 11:00 AM local market if Friday ($0), 2:00 PM kayaking ($35), 5:30 PM sunset cruise ($65). Total: $525 per person (includes one meal per day—add $50 for extra meals).

Free Attractions in Dhigurah

You don't need to spend a dime to fall in love with this island. Here's what you can enjoy for free:

  • Bikini Beach: Swim, sunbathe. and relax—no ticket needed.
  • Island Walk: Explore the entire 2-km island at your own pace.
  • Dhigurah Harbor: Watch daily life and sunset from the dock.
  • Night Beach Walk: Stargazing and hermit crab spotting are free.
  • Local Mosque Exterior: Admire the architecture and hear the call to prayer.
  • Sunrise at the East Side: The best free show—just bring your camera.

Practical Tips for Dhigurah

Let me save you some headaches with these insider tips. I've learned them the hard way so you don't have to.

Transportation

  • Getting to Dhigurah: Fly into Male International Airport (MLE), then take a 20-minute seaplane ($350 round trip) or a 90-minute speedboat ($150 round trip). I recommend speedboat for the views—you'll see dolphins!
  • Getting Around: The island is walkable—no cars, just bikes and foot. Rent a bike for $10/day from your guesthouse. It's the best way to explore.
  • Boat Transfers: Book through your guesthouse for the best rates. Always confirm pickup times 24 hours in advance.

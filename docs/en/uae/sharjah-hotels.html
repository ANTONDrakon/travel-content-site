Top 10 Hotels in Sharjah 2026: Budget to Luxury

Sharjah, the cultural capital of the United Arab Emirates, offers a diverse hotel landscape that has evolved significantly by 2026. With over 120 hotels and hotel apartments, the emirate provides accommodations ranging from ultra-budget dormitories to opulent five-star beachfront resorts. The city’s hotel infrastructure is concentrated along the Corniche, Al Majaz waterfront, Al Khan Lagoon, and the expanding University City area. Neighborhoods like Al Qasba and Al Majaz 3 have seen a surge in boutique and mid-range properties, while the Al Khan and Al Mamzar beach areas host most luxury developments. Average hotel occupancy in Sharjah reached 78% in early 2026, driven by increased cultural tourism and business travel from the adjacent Dubai Expo City corridor.

Prices in Sharjah remain 30-40% lower than comparable hotels in Dubai, making it an attractive alternative for budget-conscious travelers. A standard double room in a budget hotel averages $35-45 per night, mid-range properties range from $60-120, and luxury hotels command $160-350. The Sharjah Tourism Development Authority reports a 15% increase in hotel inventory since 2024, with several new properties opening along the Al Khan Street and King Faisal Road corridors. Transportation is affordable, with taxis averaging $1-2 per kilometer and public buses connecting all major hotel districts to Dubai Metro stations at Al Ghubaiba and Al Rashidiya. The following guide ranks the top 10 hotels in Sharjah for 2026, categorized by price range, with specific pros and cons for each property.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. Citymax Hotel Sharjah

Location: King Faisal Road, Al Qasimia. Price: $38-45 per night for a standard double room.

Citymax Hotel Sharjah is a 3-star property with 180 rooms, opened in 2022 and fully renovated in 2025. The hotel features a rooftop swimming pool, a 24-hour gym, and free Wi-Fi throughout. Rooms are compact but modern, with soundproof windows and rain showers.

  • Pros: Excellent location near Al Qasimia metro bus stop; free shuttle to Al Majaz Waterfront; very clean with daily housekeeping; 24-hour reception and luggage storage.
  • Cons: No restaurant (only a café serving breakfast); street noise from King Faisal Road can be noticeable on lower floors; limited parking (15 spaces) for guests with rental cars.

2. Al Hayat Hotel Apartments

Location: Al Khan Street, Al Khan. Price: $32-40 per night for a studio apartment.

Al Hayat Hotel Apartments offers 60 fully furnished studios and one-bedroom units with kitchenettes. Built in 2019 and refreshed in 2025, the property provides a self-catering option for extended stays. Each unit includes a microwave, mini-fridge, electric kettle, and dining area.

  • Pros: Kitchenette saves money on meals; washing machine in each unit; walking distance to Al Khan Lagoon and public beach (10 minutes); free parking for 30 cars.
  • Cons: No pool or gym; check-in is at a separate office building 200 meters away; thin walls—noise from adjacent units can be an issue; limited housekeeping (every 3 days only).

3. Raintree Hotel Sharjah

Location: Al Qasimia, near Al Qasimia University. Price: $28-35 per night for a standard twin room.

Raintree Hotel is a 2-star budget property with 90 rooms, catering primarily to students and backpackers. It offers basic amenities: a common lounge with TV, free tea and coffee in the lobby, and a shared kitchen on the ground floor.

  • Pros: Lowest price in Sharjah for a private room; 24-hour front desk; free Wi-Fi (5 Mbps, adequate for browsing); close to university campus and cheap eateries.
  • Cons: No air conditioning in common areas (only in rooms); shared bathrooms on each floor; very basic breakfast (bread, jam, tea) included but limited; neighborhood is quiet but far from tourist attractions (requires taxi).

Mid-Range Hotels ($50-150/night)

4. Copthorne Hotel Sharjah

Location: Corniche Road, Al Majaz 3. Price: $65-85 per night for a deluxe sea-view room.

Copthorne Hotel Sharjah is a 4-star property with 220 rooms, known for its prime Corniche location. It features a large outdoor pool, a spa with three treatment rooms, and two restaurants: the all-day dining “The Palm” and the rooftop “Saffron” serving Indian cuisine. Rooms are spacious (32 sqm) with floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the Arabian Gulf.

  • Highlights: Direct access to the Corniche walking path; free shuttle to Al Noor Island (5 minutes); kids’ club with supervised activities; free parking for 100 cars. The hotel’s “Stay & Dine” package includes breakfast and dinner from $95/night.

5. Holiday International Hotel

Location: Al Qasba, Al Qasba Canal. Price: $70-95 per night for a standard double room.

Holiday International Hotel is a 4-star property with 150 rooms, situated directly on the Al Qasba canal. It offers a rooftop pool, a fitness center, and a business center. The hotel’s “Al Qasba View” rooms provide direct sightlines to the canal and the Al Qasba Ferris wheel. The on-site “Mirage” restaurant serves international buffet breakfast and à la carte dinner.

  • Highlights: Walking distance to Al Qasba’s restaurants, cafes, and the Maraya Art Centre; free Wi-Fi (50 Mbps); complimentary access to the adjacent Al Qasba public beach (3-minute walk). The hotel runs a free hourly shuttle to Sharjah City Centre Mall.

6. Four Points by Sheraton Sharjah

Location: King Faisal Road, Al Majaz 2. Price: $85-120 per night for a superior room.

Four Points by Sheraton Sharjah is a 4-star business hotel with 200 rooms, part of the Marriott Bonvoy portfolio. It features a rooftop infinity pool, a 24-hour gym, and the “Best Brews” pub serving craft beers and bar food. Rooms are 28 sqm with ergonomic work desks, USB charging ports, and blackout curtains.

  • Highlights: Loyalty points earning for Marriott members; free shuttle to Dubai Metro’s Al Rashidiya station (30 minutes); executive lounge access for suite guests; on-site laundry service ($10 per load). The hotel’s “Work & Stay” package includes high-speed Wi-Fi and breakfast from $105/night.

7. Al Jazeera Hotel Sharjah

Location: Al Khan Street, Al Khan. Price: $55-75 per night for a standard double room.

Al Jazeera Hotel is a 3-star property with 120 rooms, recently renovated in 2024. It offers a small outdoor pool, a sauna, and a restaurant serving Arabic and international cuisine. The hotel is located near the Al Khan Lagoon and the Sharjah Aquarium. Rooms are 24 sqm with tile flooring and satellite TV.

  • Highlights: Very affordable for the area; free parking (50 spaces); proximity to Al Khan Beach (10-minute walk); the hotel’s “Al Khan” restaurant offers a popular Friday brunch for $25 per person. The hotel provides bicycle rentals for $5 per hour to explore the Corniche.

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

8. Sharjah Marriott Resort & Spa

Location: Al Mamzar Beach, Al Mamzar. Price: $180-280 per night for a deluxe sea-view room.

Sharjah Marriott Resort & Spa is a 5-star resort with 300 rooms, set on a private stretch of Al Mamzar Beach. It features three outdoor pools, a full-service spa (8 treatment rooms), a fitness center, and four restaurants: “La Mer” (seafood), “Saffron” (Indian), “The Grill” (steakhouse), and “Azure” (poolside). Rooms are 40 sqm with marble bathrooms, balconies, and interactive TVs. The resort offers a kids’ club, water sports equipment, and a private beach with cabanas.

  • Features: Direct beach access with loungers and umbrellas; two tennis courts; free shuttle to Sharjah International Airport (15 minutes); Marriott Bonvoy points earning; the “Ultimate Relaxation” package includes spa credit and breakfast from $250/night.

9. Al Dana Hotel & Resort

Location: Al Khan Lagoon, Al Khan. Price: $160-220 per night for a lagoon-view suite.

Al Dana Hotel & Resort is a 5-star boutique property with 80 rooms and suites, offering a more intimate luxury experience. It features a infinity pool overlooking the lagoon, a spa with hammam and steam rooms, and the “Dana” restaurant serving Mediterranean cuisine. All rooms have private balconies or terraces, and suites include separate living areas and kitchenettes. The resort provides complimentary kayaks and paddleboards for lagoon exploration.

  • Features: Private marina with boat rentals; free yoga classes every morning; butler service for suites; the “Lagoon Romance” package includes dinner and champagne from $300/night. The resort is a 5-minute walk from Al Khan Beach and the Sharjah Aquarium.

10. The Chedi Al Bait Sharjah

Location: Heart of Sharjah, Al Mureijah. Price: $250-350 per night for a heritage suite.

The Chedi Al Bait Sharjah is a 5-star heritage hotel with 53 rooms and suites, set within restored traditional Emirati houses in the Heart of Sharjah district. It features a courtyard pool, a spa with Arabian treatments, and the “Al Bait” restaurant serving Emirati cuisine. Rooms are 45-80 sqm with original wooden beams, antique furnishings, and modern amenities like Nespresso machines and deep soaking tubs. The hotel is adjacent to the Sharjah Art Museum and the Al Mureijah Square.

  • Features: Guided heritage walking tours included; private courtyard for afternoon tea; access to the hotel’s art gallery; the “Heritage Experience” package includes breakfast and museum passes from $350/night. The hotel offers a complimentary Rolls-Royce transfer to Sharjah International Airport.

How to Choose the Right Area

For beach lovers: Stay in Al Khan or Al Mamzar. Al Khan offers the Al Khan Lagoon and public beach, with mid-range options like Al Jazeera Hotel and luxury at Al Dana Resort. Al Mamzar is exclusively luxury with the Sharjah Marriott Resort, providing direct beach access and water sports. Both areas are within 10 minutes of the Corniche walking path.

For sightseeing and culture: Choose the Heart of Sharjah district (Al Mureijah) or Al Qasba. The Heart of Sharjah is home to The Chedi Al Bait, within walking distance of the Sharjah Art Museum, Sharjah Heritage Museum, and the Al Hisn Fort. Al Qasba offers Holiday International Hotel, steps from the Al Qasba canal, the Maraya Art Centre, and the Al Qasba Ferris wheel. These areas are ideal for travelers focused on museums, galleries, and traditional souks.

For nightlife and dining: Opt for Al Majaz or Al Qasimia. Al Majaz’s Corniche area has Copthorne Hotel and Four Points by Sheraton, with access to Al Majaz Waterfront’s restaurants, cafes, and the Al Majaz Amphitheatre (hosting concerts and events). Al Qasimia’s King Faisal Road has Citymax Hotel, near the Al Qasimia University area with budget-friendly bars and shisha cafes. Note that Sharjah has strict alcohol laws—only licensed hotel bars and restaurants serve alcohol, so nightlife is more subdued than Dubai.

Booking Tips

How to save: Book directly through hotel websites for best rates and loyalty points. Many mid-range and luxury hotels offer “Advance Purchase” discounts of 15-20% for bookings made 30 days in advance. Use price comparison sites like Booking.com or Agoda to identify the lowest rates, then check the hotel’s own website for matching or better offers. Consider hotel apartments (e.g., Al Hayat Hotel Apartments) for extended stays—they save on food costs with kitchenettes. Avoid booking during peak cultural events like the Sharjah Biennial (March-April) or the Sharjah Light Festival (February), when prices rise 30-50%.

When to book: The best time to visit Sharjah is from November to March, when temperatures average 20-28°C. Book 2-3 months in advance for these months, especially for luxury hotels. For budget travel, consider June-September (summer), when prices drop 40-60% and hotels offer “Summer Staycation” packages. Last-minute bookings (within 7 days) can yield discounts on unsold rooms, especially on weekdays (Sunday-Thursday). Avoid booking during the UAE National Day (December 2) and New Year’s Eve, when occupancy is near 100%.

Best services: Most hotels offer free Wi-Fi (check speed—budget hotels often have 5-10 Mbps, mid-range 50-100 Mbps, luxury 100+ Mbps). Free parking is common except in budget hotels. Look for hotels with free airport shuttles (e.g., Sharjah Marriott Resort) or shuttle services to Dubai (e.g., Four Points by Sheraton). For families, prioritize hotels with kids’ clubs (Copthorne Hotel, Sharjah Marriott Resort) and children’s pools. For business travelers, Four Points by Sheraton and Holiday International Hotel offer business centers and meeting rooms. Always confirm cancellation policies—most hotels offer free cancellation up to 24-48 hours before check-in, but some advance purchase rates are non-refundable.

Additional tips: Use the Sharjah Taxi app for reliable transportation—fares to Dubai International Airport average $20-25. Consider purchasing a “Sharjah Pass” for cultural attractions (includes entry to 5 museums for $30), available at most hotel concierges. For halal dining, most hotel restaurants are halal-certified; confirm with the hotel if you have specific dietary requirements. Finally, check visa requirements—many nationalities get visa-free entry to the UAE for up to 30 days, but always verify before booking.

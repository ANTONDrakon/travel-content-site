15 Best Things to Do in Fujairah 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Fujairah, the only emirate on the UAE’s eastern coast along the Gulf of Oman, offers a distinct blend of rugged mountains, pristine beaches, and rich history. Unlike the glitz of Dubai or Abu Dhabi, Fujairah emphasizes natural landscapes, ancient forts, and world-class diving. By 2026, the emirate has enhanced its infrastructure with new eco-resorts, upgraded heritage sites, and improved road connectivity. Whether you are a history buff, an adventure seeker. or a beach lover. Fujairah delivers a more relaxed. authentic Arabian experience. This guide covers the 15 best attractions. practical itineraries. tours. free activities, and insider tips to maximize your trip.

15 Best Attractions in Fujairah

1. Al Bithnah Fort

Al Bithnah Fort, located in the village of Bithnah about 12 kilometers west of Fujairah City, is one of the oldest forts in the UAE, with origins dating back to the 18th century. It features a distinctive round tower and a strategic position guarding the Wadi Ham valley. The fort has been meticulously restored and offers panoramic views of the surrounding date palm plantations and mountains. It is a must-see for history enthusiasts.

  • Ticket Price: Free (public access).
  • Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Drive from Fujairah City via E89 (Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Road); a 15-minute drive. No public transport directly to the fort.
  • Tip: Visit early morning to avoid heat and capture the best light for photography.

2. Fujairah Fort

Fujairah Fort, built in 1670, is the oldest fort in the UAE and a symbol of the emirate’s resilience. It is situated on a rocky hill overlooking the old town and the Fujairah Museum. The fort has three main sections, several watchtowers, and a central courtyard. Recent renovations have added informative displays about its history and architecture.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Saturday to Thursday, 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM; Friday, 2:00 PM – 6:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Located in the heart of Fujairah City, near the main souk. Easily reachable by taxi or car.
  • Tip: Combine with a visit to the adjacent Fujairah Museum (entry $1 (5 AED)for adults).

3. Fujairah Museum

The Fujairah Museum, established in 1991, houses a rich collection of archaeological artifacts from the Bronze Age, Iron Age, and Islamic periods. Exhibits include ancient pottery, weapons, coins, and traditional Bedouin jewelry. The museum provides excellent context for the region’s history, from early settlements to the present day.

  • Ticket Price: $1 (5 AED)for adults, $1 (2 AED)for children under 12.
  • Hours: Saturday to Thursday, 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM; Friday, 2:00 PM – 6:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Next to Fujairah Fort on the same hill. Walking distance from the city center.
  • Tip: Allow 1–1.5 hours for a thorough visit.

4. Snoopy Island (Sandy Beach)

Snoopy Island, a small rocky island off the coast of Al Aqah Beach, gets its name from its resemblance to the cartoon character when viewed from a certain angle. It is a premier snorkeling and diving spot, with vibrant coral reefs, sea turtles, and schools of tropical fish. The island is accessible by swimming or kayaking from the adjacent Sandy Beach Resort.

  • Ticket Price: Free access to the beach; snorkeling gear rental starts at $14 (50 AED)per hour.
  • Hours: 24/7 public beach access.
  • How to Get There: Drive 45 minutes north from Fujairah City to Al Aqah. Taxi from city costs approximately $22 (80 AED)–100.
  • Tip: Bring reef-safe sunscreen and water shoes to protect feet from sharp rocks.

5. Al Aqah Beach

Al Aqah Beach is a long stretch of white sand fringed by the Hajar Mountains, widely considered the best beach in Fujairah. The waters are calm and clear, ideal for swimming, paddleboarding, and jet skiing. The beach is lined with international hotels like the Le Méridien Al Aqah and the Fujairah Rotana Resort.

  • Ticket Price: Public access is free; hotel beach areas may require day passes ($27 (100 AED)–200).
  • Hours: 24/7.
  • How to Get There: 45 minutes north of Fujairah City via E99. Buses from Fujairah City to Dibba stop near Al Aqah ($3 (10 AED)).
  • Tip: Weekdays are less crowded; weekends can be busy with families from other emirates.

6. Wadi Wurrayah (Wadi Wurayah)

Wadi Wurrayah, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve since 2010, is a stunning mountain valley with freshwater pools, waterfalls, and diverse flora and fauna. It is a protected area, requiring a permit for entry. The hike to the main waterfall takes about 1.5 hours each way, offering views of terraced rock formations and ancient petroglyphs.

  • Ticket Price: $8 (30 AED)per person for permit (available online via Fujairah Adventures).
  • Hours: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM (last entry at 3:00 PM). Closed during heavy rain.
  • How to Get There: Drive to the village of Masafi, then follow signs for Wadi Wurrayah. A 4×4 vehicle is recommended for the last 3 km of rough track.
  • Tip: Bring at least 2 liters of water per person, and wear sturdy hiking shoes.

7. Friday Market (Souq Al Juma)

The Friday Market, located along the road between Fujairah and Masafi, is a traditional souk operating daily, not just Fridays. It sells local produce, honey, pottery, carpets, and handicrafts. The market is famous for its fresh dates, pomegranates, and mountain herbs.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Daily, 7:00 AM – 8:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: On E89, about 20 km west of Fujairah City. Buses from Fujairah to Masafi stop nearby ($2 (8 AED)).
  • Tip: Bargain politely; prices are generally low, but you can negotiate for multiple items.

8. Al Hayl Fort

Al Hayl Fort, built in the early 19th century, is a well-preserved fortress located in the Al Hayl area, about 15 km south of Fujairah City. It features a central tower, living quarters, and a mosque. The fort is surrounded by a small oasis and offers a glimpse into the region’s defensive architecture.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Drive south on E99, then turn onto a local road. Taxi from Fujairah City costs around $14 (50 AED)
  • Tip: Combine with a visit to the nearby Al Badiyah Mosque (see below).

9. Al Badiyah Mosque

Al Badiyah Mosque, also known as the Ottoman Mosque, is the oldest mosque in the UAE, dating back to 1446 AD. Its unique architecture features four squat domes, a central pillar, and a small minaret. The mosque is still in use for daily prayers and is a serene spot for reflection.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Non-prayer times; visitors are welcome outside prayer hours (check with local caretaker).
  • How to Get There: Located in the village of Al Badiyah, 10 km north of Fujairah City. Taxi from city costs $8 (30 AED)–40.
  • Tip: Dress modestly; women must cover their heads. Photography is allowed with permission.

10. Dibba Al Fujairah

Dibba Al Fujairah is a coastal enclave known for its fishing port, seafood restaurants, and access to the Musandam Peninsula. The area offers boat trips to see dolphins, snorkel in secluded coves, and explore the fjord-like coastline. The Dibba Rock is a popular dive site with abundant marine life.

  • Ticket Price: Free to explore; boat tours start at $41 (150 AED)per person for 2-hour trips.
  • Hours: Boat tours operate 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: 40 minutes north of Fujairah City via E99. Buses from Fujairah to Dibba ($3 (12 AED)).
  • Tip: Book a traditional dhow cruise for sunset views and dinner ($55 (200 AED)–300 per person).

11. Fujairah Adventure Park

Opened in 2023, Fujairah Adventure Park is a family-friendly attraction with zip lines, a climbing wall, a ropes course, and a mini-golf course. The park is set against a mountain backdrop and includes picnic areas and a café. It is suitable for children aged 4 and above.

  • Ticket Price: $16 (60 AED)for adults, $11 (40 AED)for children (under 12). Combo passes available for $25 (90 AED)
  • Hours: Thursday to Saturday, 10:00 AM – 8:00 PM; Sunday to Wednesday, 2:00 PM – 8:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Near the Fujairah City Center Mall, a 10-minute walk from the bus station.
  • Tip: Arrive early on weekends to avoid queues for the zip line.

12. Khor Fakkan Beach

Khor Fakkan, a exclave of Sharjah but geographically part of Fujairah’s coast, boasts a crescent-shaped beach with calm turquoise waters. The beach is well-maintained, with showers, changing rooms, and a promenade. The nearby Khor Fakkan Corniche offers views of the container port and the surrounding mountains.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: 24/7.
  • How to Get There: 30 minutes south of Fujairah City via E99. Buses from Fujairah to Khor Fakkan ($3 (10 AED)).
  • Tip: Visit the Al Rafisah Dam and its artificial lake, located 5 km inland, for a scenic picnic spot.

13. Sheikh Zayed Mosque Fujairah

This grand mosque, built in 2015, is one of the largest in the UAE, with a capacity of 28,000 worshippers. Its architecture features white marble, intricate floral designs, and six minarets. The mosque is open to non-Muslims for guided tours, offering insight into Islamic culture and design.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: Guided tours Saturday to Thursday, 9:00 AM – 12:00 PM and 4:00 PM – 6:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: On Sheikh Khalifa bin Zayed Road, 5 km west of Fujairah City. Taxi from city center costs $5 (20 AED)
  • Tip: Women must wear an abaya (provided at the entrance) and cover their hair.

14. Bullfighting (Fujairah Bull Butting)

Fujairah hosts traditional bull-butting competitions, a unique cultural event held every Friday during winter months (October to March). Two bulls push against each other in a controlled arena, and the match ends when one retreats. It is a non-violent sport with roots in Portuguese and local traditions.

  • Ticket Price: Free (donations welcome).
  • Hours: Fridays, 4:00 PM – 6:00 PM (check local listings for exact dates).
  • How to Get There: Held at the Fujairah Bull Butting Arena, near the Fujairah Fort. Taxi from city center, $4 (15 AED)
  • Tip: Arrive early to secure a good view; the arena fills up quickly with locals.

15. Wadi Al Abadilah

Wadi Al Abadilah is a lesser-known wadi with natural rock pools, waterfalls, and terraced gardens. It is a peaceful spot for hiking, swimming, and birdwatching. The wadi is less frequented than Wadi Wurrayah, offering solitude and pristine nature.

  • Ticket Price: Free.
  • Hours: 24/7, but best visited during daylight.
  • How to Get There: Drive 25 km south of Fujairah City, near the village of Masafi. A 4×4 is recommended for the last 2 km of unpaved road.
  • Tip: Bring insect repellent and a first-aid kit; mobile reception is limited.

1-Day, 2-Day, and 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: Best of Fujairah City

Start at 8:00 AM at Fujairah Fort and Museum (1.5 hours). Walk to the nearby Friday Market (30 minutes). Drive to Al Badiyah Mosque (20 minutes). Lunch at a local restaurant in Al Aqah (try grilled hamour fish). At 1:00 PM, visit Snoopy Island for snorkeling (2 hours). By 4:00 PM, drive to Sheikh Zayed Mosque for a tour (1 hour). End the day at Khor Fakkan Beach for sunset (1 hour). Total driving: approximately 80 km. Cost: $14 (50 AED)for museum entry, $27 (100 AED)for lunch, $14 (50 AED)for snorkel rental.

2-Day Itinerary: History and Nature

Day 1: Follow the 1-day itinerary above, but skip Khor Fakkan Beach. Instead, after the mosque, drive to Al Hayl Fort (30 minutes) and then to Al Bithnah Fort (30 minutes). Dinner at a restaurant in Fujairah City. Day 2: Start at 7:00 AM with a hike in Wadi Wurrayah (4 hours). Return for lunch at the Friday Market (1 hour). Afternoon at Al Aqah Beach for relaxation (2 hours). Evening bullfighting if it’s a Friday (2 hours). Total driving: 120 km. Cost: $8 (30 AED)for Wadi permit, $41 (150 AED)for meals, $27 (100 AED)for activities.

3-Day Itinerary: Full Exploration

Day 1: Same as Day 1 of the 2-day itinerary. Day 2: Morning at Dibba Al Fujairah for a boat tour (3 hours). Lunch at a seafood restaurant in Dibba. Afternoon at Fujairah Adventure Park (2 hours). Evening at the Fujairah Corniche for a walk. Day 3: Morning at Wadi Al Abadilah (3 hours). Lunch at a mountain café. Afternoon at Snoopy Island for a second snorkeling session or a kayak tour. Depart at 5:00 PM. Total driving: 150 km. Cost: $41 (150 AED)for boat tour, $16 (60 AED)for adventure park, $55 (200 AED)for meals, $14 (50 AED)for rentals.

Tours & Excursions

Several operators offer guided tours in Fujairah, ideal for those without a car or wanting expert insight.

  • Fujairah Adventures: Wadi Wurrayah guided hike ($41 (150 AED)per person, 4 hours, includes permit and transport from Fujairah City).
  • Dibba Dhow Cruises: Sunset dolphin-watching and dinner cruise ($68 (250 AED)per person, 3 hours, departs from Dibba port).
  • Snorkeling and Diving at Snoopy Island: Al Boom Diving offers a 2-hour snorkeling trip with equipment ($55 (200 AED)per person, includes guide and transport).

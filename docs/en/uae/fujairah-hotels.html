Top 10 Hotels in Fujairah 2026: Budget to Luxury

Fujairah, the only emirate on the eastern coast of the United Arab Emirates, has rapidly evolved into a premier destination for travelers seeking a blend of rugged mountains, pristine beaches, and rich cultural heritage. Unlike the futuristic skylines of Dubai or Abu Dhabi, Fujairah offers a more relaxed, nature-focused experience. By 2026, the hotel infrastructure has matured significantly, with new properties opening along the Al Aqah Beach strip, near the historic Fujairah Fort, and in the expanding city center. The neighborhoods are distinct: Al Aqah Beach is the epicenter of luxury resorts and water sports, while the city center (around Hamad Bin Abdulla Road) is ideal for budget-conscious travelers and those exploring souks and museums. Dibba, to the north, offers quieter, family-oriented beachfront stays.

Prices in Fujairah remain competitive compared to other emirates. Budget hotels in the city center often start as low as $30 per night, while mid-range properties along the coast average $80 to $120. Luxury resorts, particularly those with private beaches and dive centers, range from $180 to $400+ per night. The best deals are typically found from May to September (summer months) when temperatures peak, though winter (November to March) sees a 30-40% price surge. Below is a curated list of the top 10 hotels for 2026, organized by budget.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. City Tower Hotel Fujairah

Price: $35–$45 per night (standard double room, including breakfast).

Description: Located in the heart of Fujairah City, just a 5-minute drive from the Fujairah Museum and the historic fort, this hotel offers clean, compact rooms with air conditioning, flat-screen TVs, and free Wi-Fi. The rooftop pool provides panoramic city views.

Pros: Excellent central location, free parking, 24-hour reception, and a decent on-site restaurant serving Emirati and Indian cuisine.

Cons: No beach access (20-minute drive to nearest public beach), limited soundproofing from street noise, and dated bathroom fixtures in some rooms.

2. Royal M Hotel Fujairah

Price: $40–$48 per night (deluxe room, with optional breakfast at $5 extra).

Description: A modern budget hotel near the Fujairah Shopping Centre and the central bus station. Rooms are minimalist with dark wood furniture, minibars, and en-suite bathrooms. The hotel features a small gym and a business center.

Pros: Very affordable, close to public transport, friendly English-speaking staff, and a 24-hour convenience store on the ground floor.

Cons: No pool, no restaurant (only a breakfast box service), and the neighborhood is busy with traffic during peak hours.

3. Fujairah Inn Hotel

Price: $30–$38 per night (economy room, no breakfast).

Description: A no-frills guesthouse located in the Al Faseel district, a 10-minute walk from the Fujairah Corniche. Rooms are basic but spotless, with tiled floors, ceiling fans, and private bathrooms. The property is popular with backpackers and solo travelers.

Pros: Lowest price in the city, quiet residential area, free street parking, and a shared kitchenette for guests.

Cons: No air conditioning in some older rooms (only fans), no elevators (stairs only), and no on-site staff after 10 PM.

Mid-Range Hotels ($50-150/night)

4. Concorde Hotel Fujairah

Price: $65–$90 per night (superior room, breakfast included).

Description: A 4-star property on the Corniche Road, offering views of the Gulf of Oman. Rooms are spacious with marble bathrooms, balconies, and satellite TV. The hotel has a large outdoor pool, a fitness center, and two restaurants (Italian and Lebanese).

Highlights: Direct access to a private beach club (5-minute shuttle), free Wi-Fi throughout, and a popular Friday brunch buffet for $25 per person.

5. Al Bahar Hotel & Resort

Price: $80–$110 per night (standard room, breakfast extra at $12).

Description: Situated on the Al Aqah Beach strip, this resort offers a mix of hotel rooms and self-catering apartments. The property features a large lagoon-style pool, a children's play area, and a dive center offering PADI courses.

Highlights: Beachfront location with loungers and umbrellas included, a spa with traditional hammam, and a 24-hour supermarket on site. Free shuttle to Fujairah City (15 km away).

6. Fujairah Rotana Resort & Spa

Price: $100–$140 per night (deluxe sea-view room, breakfast included).

Description: A well-known chain property located on a private stretch of Al Aqah Beach. Rooms are contemporary with floor-to-ceiling windows, rain showers, and Nespresso machines. The resort has four restaurants, a fitness center, and a full-service spa.

Highlights: Excellent snorkeling right off the beach, a kids' club with daily activities, and a 20-minute drive to the Friday Market (traditional handicrafts).

7. Novotel Fujairah

Price: $55–$75 per night (standard room, breakfast included).

Description: Located in the city center, near the Fujairah Airport and the Fujairah Fort. This 4-star hotel offers modern rooms with ergonomic workspaces, soundproof windows, and 42-inch TVs. The property includes a rooftop pool, a tennis court, and a 24-hour gym.

Highlights: Free airport shuttle, a business lounge with complimentary snacks, and a 10-minute walk to the Lulu Hypermarket. Good value for business travelers.

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

8. InterContinental Fujairah Resort

Price: $200–$350 per night (club room with lounge access, breakfast and evening cocktails included).

Description: A 5-star resort set on 1.5 km of private beach in Al Aqah. The architecture is inspired by traditional Arabian forts, with lush gardens, a massive infinity pool, and a private marina. Rooms are opulent with marble bathrooms, balconies, and butler service.

Features: Four swimming pools (one adults-only), a 9-hole golf course, a diving center with wreck dives, and five restaurants including a seafood grill on the beach. The spa offers treatments using organic local ingredients.

9. Address Beach Resort Fujairah

Price: $250–$400 per night (premium ocean suite, breakfast and minibar included).

Description: Opened in 2024, this ultra-modern resort in Dibba features a dramatic glass-fronted lobby with ocean views. Suites are spacious (minimum 50 m²) with private plunge pools, smart home controls, and floor-to-ceiling windows. The resort is designed for wellness and privacy.

Features: A world-class spa with hydrotherapy pools, a yoga pavilion overlooking the sea, a gourmet restaurant by a Michelin-starred chef, and a private beach with cabana service. Complimentary kayaking and paddleboarding are included.

10. Fairmont Fujairah Beach Resort

Price: $180–$280 per night (deluxe room, breakfast included).

Description: Located on a quiet stretch of beach near the Dibba border, this resort blends Moroccan and Mediterranean design. Rooms have hand-painted tiles, four-poster beds, and deep soaking tubs. The property is known for its lush gardens and lagoon-style pools.

Features: A 1,500 m² spa with a snow room and salt cave, a kids' club with a mini water park, a diving school, and three restaurants including a rooftop lounge with sunset views. Free shuttle to the historic Al Badiyah Mosque (oldest in the UAE).

How to Choose the Right Area

For Beach Lovers

Choose Al Aqah Beach or Dibba. Al Aqah offers the widest selection of luxury and mid-range resorts with direct beach access, water sports, and snorkeling. Dibba is quieter, with pristine coves ideal for families and couples seeking seclusion. Public beaches like Fujairah Beach Park (near the city) are free but less maintained.

For Sightseeing & Culture

Stay in Fujairah City center. You'll be within walking distance of the Fujairah Fort (built in 1670), the Fujairah Museum (with archaeological artifacts), and the Al Badiyah Mosque (a 15-minute drive). The city also offers easy access to the Friday Market (a 20-minute drive) and the Al Aqah mountains for hiking.

For Nightlife & Dining

Fujairah is not a nightlife destination, but Al Aqah Beach has the best concentration of hotel bars and lounges. The InterContinental and Rotana have popular evening venues with live music. For local dining, the city center has numerous Indian and Pakistani restaurants open late. Avoid Dibba if you want nightlife—it's very quiet after sunset.

Booking Tips

How to Save Money

  • Book directly on hotel websites for loyalty points and flexible cancellation policies. Third-party sites like Booking.com or Agoda often offer 10-15% discounts for advance purchase (non-refundable).
  • Travel in summer (June to September) for rates up to 50% lower. However, expect temperatures above 40°C (104°F) and high humidity.
  • Use credit card rewards or cashback portals like TopCashback or Rakuten for an extra 5-10% off.
  • Consider package deals: many resorts offer "stay and dive" packages including PADI courses, which can save $50-$100 per person.

When to Book

For winter travel (November to March), book at least 6-8 weeks in advance, as rooms sell out quickly during school holidays and the Fujairah International Triathlon (held in February). For summer travel, booking 2-3 weeks ahead is sufficient. Avoid booking during the UAE National Day holidays (December 2-3) when prices spike 40%.

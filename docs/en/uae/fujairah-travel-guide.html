Fujairah Travel Guide 2026

Fujairah, the only emirate on the UAE’s eastern coast along the Gulf of Oman, offers a refreshing contrast to the glittering skyscrapers of Dubai and Abu Dhabi. With a population of approximately 225,000 as of 2025, this port city is defined by its dramatic Hajar Mountain backdrop, ancient forts, and uncrowded beaches. Unlike its neighbors, Fujairah enjoys a unique microclimate—summer temperatures average 35°C (95°F), cooler than inland cities, while winter days hover around 24°C (75°F). The atmosphere here is laid-back and traditional, with a strong fishing and trading heritage visible in its bustling souks and restored watchtowers.

Visitors come to Fujairah for its authentic Emirati character and natural wonders. The emirate is home to the UAE’s only natural beach on the Indian Ocean, where diving and snorkeling sites like Snoopy Island and Dibba Rock teem with sea turtles, rays, and over 100 species of coral fish. History buffs explore the 16th-century Fujairah Fort, one of the oldest in the country, and the nearby Al Bithnah Fort. Adventure seekers drive the scenic Wadi Wurayah National Park, a 12,700-hectare protected area with seasonal waterfalls and rare Arabian leopards. In 2026, new eco-lodges and a revamped corniche make it an ideal escape from city crowds.

Why visit in 2026? The emirate is investing heavily in sustainable tourism, with the opening of the Fujairah Adventure Centre (a 30-meter-high climbing wall) and expanded ferry services from Dubai to Fujairah Port. Average hotel rates are 40% lower than Dubai, and the Friday market offers handwoven textiles and pottery at 50-70% less than city prices. With direct flights from 15 countries and visa-free access for 90 days, Fujairah is an affordable, authentic slice of the UAE.

How to Get There

Fujairah International Airport (FJR) is the primary gateway, located 15 km north of the city center. In 2026, the airport handles flights from 20 destinations, including direct routes from Dubai (45 minutes), Abu Dhabi (1 hour), and international hubs like Istanbul (4.5 hours), Doha (2 hours), and Moscow (5 hours). Average round-trip fares from Europe start at $450 (€415) from London, $380 (€350) from Berlin, and $520 (€480) from Paris. From Asia, flights from Mumbai cost $300 (₹25,000) round-trip, while from Beijing, prices average $550 (¥4,000). Budget carriers like Air Arabia and flydubai offer connections via Sharjah or Dubai for as low as $80 (€74) one-way from regional cities. Taxis from FJR to central Fujairah cost $15 (€14) for a 20-minute ride.

Top 15 Attractions

  1. Fujairah Fort – 16th-century fortress with 3 round towers and a museum. Ticket: $3 (€2.80). Hours: 8:00 AM–6:00 PM daily.
  2. Al Bithnah Fort – 18th-century mountain fort with panoramic views. Free entry. Hours: 9:00 AM–5:00 PM.
  3. Snoopy Island – Snorkeling site named for its rock formation. Entry via Sandy Beach Hotel: $10 (€9.20). Hours: 8:00 AM–sunset.
  4. Dibba Rock – Coral reef with sea turtles and rays. Boat tour: $25 (€23). Hours: 9:00 AM–4:00 PM.
  5. Wadi Wurayah National Park – Hiking trails, waterfalls, and wildlife. Ticket: $5 (€4.60). Hours: 7:00 AM–5:00 PM (closed summer).
  6. Fujairah Museum – Archeological artifacts from 3000 BC. Ticket: $2 (€1.85). Hours: 8:00 AM–6:00 PM (closed Friday).
  7. Sheikh Zayed Mosque – White marble mosque with 3 domes. Free entry. Hours: 9:00 AM–12:00 PM, 2:00–5:00 PM.
  8. Al Hayl Castle – 19th-century fort in the mountains. Free. Hours: 9:00 AM–5:00 PM.
  9. Fujairah Corniche – 3-km waterfront promenade with fountains and gardens. Free. 24 hours.
  10. Friday Market (Souk Al Juma) – Traditional market for pottery, textiles, and dates. Free entry. Hours: 6:00 AM–8:00 PM (Fri only).
  11. Bull Butting Festival – Weekly event (Nov–Mar) at Al Bithnah. Free. Hours: 4:00 PM–6:00 PM Fridays.
  12. Khor Fakkan Beach – White sand beach with mountain backdrop. Free. Hours: 8:00 AM–8:00 PM.
  13. Fujairah Adventure Centre – Climbing wall, zip line, and trampoline. Ticket: $15 (€14). Hours: 10:00 AM–8:00 PM.
  14. Al Aqah Beach – 5-km stretch of sand with clear water. Free public access. 24 hours.
  15. Madhab Hot Springs – Natural thermal pools in the mountains. Free. Hours: 8:00 AM–6:00 PM.

Getting Around

Fujairah has no metro or train system. Buses are limited, with the main route (Route 1) running between city center and Al Aqah every 30 minutes from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM. Fare: $1.50 (€1.40). Taxis are widely available: starting fare $3 (€2.80), then $0.50 (€0.46) per km. A ride from city center to Al Aqah costs $10 (€9.20). Car rental is recommended for exploring mountains and wadis. Average daily rental: $30 (€28) for a compact car, $50 (€46) for an SUV. Gas prices in UAE: $0.78 (€0.72) per liter. Ride-hailing apps like Uber and Careem operate, with fares 20% higher than taxis. For inter-emirate travel, buses from Fujairah to Dubai cost $8 (€7.40) and take 2 hours.

Seafood

International

Visa & Documents

Citizens of 50+ countries (including US, UK, EU, Australia, Canada, Japan, South Korea) receive visa-free entry for up to 90 days. Other nationalities can obtain a 30-day tourist visa on arrival for $50 (€46) or apply for an e-visa online ($60/€55) processed in 3-5 days. Passport must be valid for at least 6 months. No vaccination certificate required. UAE Dirham (AED) is the currency; 1 USD = 3.67 AED (fixed rate).

10 Travel Tips

  1. Dress modestly – For women, cover shoulders and knees in public; men should avoid sleeveless shirts. Swimwear is fine on beaches.
  2. Carry cash – Many small shops and taxis only accept cash. ATMs are widely available in city center.
  3. Respect Ramadan – In 2026, Ramadan is expected from March 1–30. No eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours.
  4. Drive carefully – Mountain roads (e.g., to Wadi Wurayah) are narrow with sharp turns. Speed limits: 60 km/h in city, 80 km/h on highways.
  5. Book dive trips early – Snorkeling tours to Snoopy Island fill up by 9:00 AM. Reserve via hotels or local operators.
  6. Use sunscreen – UV index is 11+ from April to October. SPF 50 is essential.
  7. Try local coffee – Arabic coffee (gahwa) with cardamom is served free at souks. Accept it with your right hand.
  8. Visit on a Friday – The Friday Market is the best place for souvenirs, open 6:00 AM–8:00 PM.
  9. Check weather for wadis – Flash floods are rare but possible after rain. Avoid hiking during storms.
  10. Learn basic Arabic – “Shukran” (thank you) and “Marhaba” (hello) go a long way. English is widely spoken.

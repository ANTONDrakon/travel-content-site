15 Best Things to Do in Abu Dhabi 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Abu Dhabi, the capital of the United Arab Emirates, has transformed from a quiet pearl-diving village into a global hub of culture, architecture, and adventure. Unlike its flashy neighbor Dubai, Abu Dhabi offers a more refined and authentic Emirati experience, blending futuristic marvels with deep-rooted traditions. In 2026, the city continues to expand its portfolio of world-class attractions, from the stunning Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque to the adrenaline-pumping Ferrari World. Whether you are a history buff, a thrill-seeker, or a luxury traveler. Abu Dhabi delivers a diverse range of experiences that justify a dedicated visit. This guide covers the 15 best attractions with practical details. plus itineraries. tours. free activities. and essential tips to maximize your trip.

15 Best Attractions in Abu Dhabi

1. Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque

This architectural masterpiece is one of the largest mosques in the world, capable of accommodating over 40,000 worshippers. Its pure white marble domes, intricate floral mosaics, and the world's largest hand-knotted carpet (over 5,600 square meters) create an atmosphere of serene grandeur. The mosque's 82 domes and four 107-meter minarets are illuminated at night, offering a breathtaking view.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry (online registration required for non-Muslims).
  • Hours: Saturday to Thursday, 9:00 AM to 10:00 PM; Friday, 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 3:00 PM to 10:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Take a taxi from the city center (approx. 20 minutes, $8 (30 AED)-50) or bus number 54 from Al Wahda Mall.
  • Tip: Visit during the golden hour before sunset for stunning photography. Dress modestly (women must cover hair, arms, and legs; abayas are provided at the entrance).

2. Louvre Abu Dhabi

Designed by architect Jean Nouvel, this "museum city" on Saadiyat Island features a spectacular 180-meter dome that creates a "rain of light" effect. The permanent collection spans from prehistoric artifacts to contemporary art, with loans from French museums like the Louvre and Musée d'Orsay. In 2026, the museum hosts special exhibitions on global trade routes and Islamic art.

  • Ticket Price: $17 (63 AED)(adults), free for under 18s.
  • Hours: Tuesday to Sunday, 10:00 AM to 6:30 PM (closed Monday).
  • How to Get There: Take a taxi (30 minutes from city center, $16 (60 AED)-80) or bus 94 from Marina Mall.
  • Tip: Book tickets online in advance to avoid queues. The café on the waterfront offers excellent views of the dome.

3. Ferrari World Abu Dhabi

This indoor theme park on Yas Island is home to the world's fastest roller coaster, Formula Rossa, which accelerates to 240 km/h in 4.9 seconds. Beyond the thrill rides, the park features driving simulators, a 4D cinema, and the Galleria Ferrari museum showcasing classic cars. In 2026, a new "Ferrari Racing Legends" virtual reality experience has been added.

  • Ticket Price: $94 (345 AED)(adults), $80 (295 AED)(children under 1.3m).
  • Hours: Daily, 11:00 AM to 8:00 PM (extended hours on weekends).
  • How to Get There: Free shuttle from Dubai (1.5 hours) or taxi from Abu Dhabi city (20 minutes, $19 (70 AED)-100).
  • Tip: Arrive early to ride Formula Rossa first, as queues can exceed 90 minutes. Purchase a "Fast Track" pass for $41 (150 AED)

4. Qasr Al Watan

This working presidential palace opened to the public in 2019, offering a glimpse into UAE governance and culture. The Great Hall features a 37-meter diameter dome and intricate calligraphy, while the Qasr Al Watan Library holds over 50,000 books. The "Spirit of Collaboration" exhibition explains the UAE's diplomatic history.

  • Ticket Price: $16 (60 AED)(adults), $8 (30 AED)(children 4-17).
  • Hours: Daily, 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM (last entry 6:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: Taxi from city center (15 minutes, $7 (25 AED)-40).
  • Tip: The "Palace in Motion" light and sound show at 7:30 PM is included in the ticket. Reserve a spot for the rooftop café for sunset views.

5. Yas Waterworld

This Emirati-themed water park features 45 rides, slides, and attractions, including the world's first hydromagnetic-powered rafting ride, "Bandit Bomber." The park's design is inspired by a lost pearl-diving city, with a 5-meter wave pool and a 238-meter lazy river. In 2026, a new "Pearl Diver's Challenge" obstacle course has opened.

  • Ticket Price: $80 (295 AED)(adults), $67 (245 AED)(children under 1.2m).
  • Hours: Daily, 10:00 AM to 7:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Free shuttle from Dubai (2 hours) or taxi from Abu Dhabi (20 minutes, $19 (70 AED)-100).
  • Tip: Bring water shoes, as the ground can get hot. The "VIP Experience" ($55 (200 AED)) includes fast-track access and a private cabana.

6. Emirates Palace Mandarin Oriental

This iconic luxury hotel is a destination in itself, with its gold-leafed domes, 1,000 Swarovski chandeliers, and a private beach. Even non-guests can visit the lobby, enjoy the famous gold flake cappuccino ($20 (75 AED)), or explore the "Palace of the Nation" exhibition. The hotel's 1.3 km private beach is open to day visitors with a dining booking.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry to lobby; cappuccino and dining costs vary.
  • Hours: Lobby open 24/7; restaurants operate lunch (12:00 PM-3:00 PM) and dinner (6:30 PM-11:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: Taxi from city center (10 minutes, $5 (20 AED)-30).
  • Tip: Book afternoon tea ($80 (295 AED)per person) for a luxurious experience. The hotel's "Palace Beach" is accessible with a minimum spend of $68 (250 AED)at the poolside restaurant.

7. Corniche Beach

This public beach stretches for 8 km along the coastline, offering three distinct sections: family-only, women-only, and general. The water is crystal clear, with lifeguards and rental facilities for umbrellas and loungers. The adjacent Corniche promenade is perfect for cycling, jogging, or evening strolls with views of the city skyline.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry to public areas; paid sections ($3 (10 AED)-20) include facilities.
  • Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM to sunset (paid sections close at 6:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: Bus 34 or 44 from city center, or a short taxi ride ($4 (15 AED)-25).
  • Tip: Visit on a weekday to avoid crowds. The women-only section (Gate 2) is best for female travelers seeking privacy.

8. Warner Bros. World Abu Dhabi

This indoor theme park on Yas Island brings to life iconic characters from DC Comics, Looney Tunes, and Hanna-Barbera. The park is divided into six immersive zones, including Gotham City and Metropolis, with roller coasters like "Batman: Knight Flight" and family rides like "Tom & Jerry: Swiss Cheese Spin." In 2026, a new "Justice League: Battle for Metropolis" interactive dark ride has been added.

  • Ticket Price: $80 (295 AED)(adults), $67 (245 AED)(children under 1.2m).
  • Hours: Daily, 11:00 AM to 8:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Free shuttle from Dubai (1.5 hours) or taxi from Abu Dhabi (20 minutes, $19 (70 AED)-100).
  • Tip: Download the park's app for real-time wait times. The "Fast Track" pass ($41 (150 AED)) is worth it for popular rides.

9. Mangrove National Park

This protected area along the eastern coast of Abu Dhabi is home to over 20 species of birds, including flamingos and herons, and a dense mangrove forest. Kayaking through the narrow channels offers a serene escape from the city, with guided tours lasting 2-3 hours. In 2026, new elevated boardwalks have been added for birdwatching.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry to park; kayak rentals from $27 (100 AED)(2 hours, guided).
  • Hours: Daily, 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Taxi from city center (20 minutes, $11 (40 AED)-60) to the Eastern Mangrove Lagoon entrance.
  • Tip: Book a sunrise kayak tour for cooler temperatures and better wildlife sightings. Bring insect repellent and sunscreen.

10. Al Ain Oasis

Located 90 minutes east of Abu Dhabi, this UNESCO World Heritage site is a 1,200-hectare date palm oasis with an ancient falaj irrigation system. The oasis features walking trails, a museum, and a visitor center explaining the region's agricultural history. Over 147,000 date palms of 20 varieties grow here.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Daily, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM (museum open 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM).
  • How to Get There: Drive via E66 highway (1.5 hours) or take a bus from Abu Dhabi Central Bus Station ($69 (252 AED)hours).
  • Tip: Combine with a visit to Al Ain Zoo ($8 (30 AED)) or Jebel Hafeet mountain for a full day trip. Wear comfortable walking shoes.

11. The Founder's Memorial

This open-air memorial honors Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the founding father of the UAE. The central feature is a 30-meter-high sculpture of his portrait made from 1,327 suspended geometric shapes. The surrounding garden has 2,000 native trees and a visitor center with interactive exhibits about his life and legacy.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM to 10:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Walk from Marina Mall (5 minutes) or taxi from city center (10 minutes, $5 (20 AED)-30).
  • Tip: Visit at night when the sculpture is illuminated. The memorial is adjacent to the Corniche, making it easy to combine with a beach visit.

12. Yas Marina Circuit

Home to the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, this Formula 1 circuit offers year-round experiences like driving a Ferrari or Aston Martin on the track (from $164 (600 AED)for 5 laps). The circuit also hosts cycling events, running tracks, and a go-karting facility. In 2026, a new "F1 Simulator Experience" has opened for virtual racing.

  • Ticket Price: Track experiences from $164 (600 AED); go-karting from $27 (100 AED)(10 minutes).
  • Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (track experiences require booking).
  • How to Get There: Free shuttle from Dubai (1.5 hours) or taxi from Abu Dhabi (20 minutes, $19 (70 AED)-100).
  • Tip: Book the "Drive Your Own Car" experience ($82 (300 AED)for 3 laps) for a budget-friendly option. Check the calendar for public cycling events (free, Friday mornings).

13. Heritage Village

This reconstructed desert village showcases traditional Emirati life, with a souk, a mosque, and artisans demonstrating pottery, weaving, and metalwork. The village is set on a small peninsula with views of the city skyline and the sea. A small museum displays artifacts like pearl-diving equipment and Bedouin tents.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Saturday to Thursday, 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM; Friday, 3:30 PM to 9:00 PM.
  • How to Get There: Bus 44 or 34 to Marina Mall, then a 10-minute walk.
  • Tip: Visit in the late afternoon to avoid heat. The village has a small café serving traditional tea and coffee.

14. Al Maryah Island

This financial district is also a lifestyle destination, with the luxury Galleria Mall, waterfront promenades, and fine dining restaurants. The island's "Reem Central Park" features outdoor gyms, jogging tracks, and children's play areas. In 2026, the new "Al Maryah Beach Club" has opened with a 500-meter private beach.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry; beach club day pass $27 (100 AED)
  • Hours: Mall open 10:00 AM to 10:00 PM; promenade open 24/7.
  • How to Get There: Taxi from city center (15 minutes, $8 (30 AED)-50) or bus 94.
  • Tip: The promenade is ideal for evening walks and sunset photography. Try the seafood restaurant "Buddha Bar" for Asian fusion cuisine.

15. Manarat Al Saadiyat

This cultural center on Saadiyat Island serves as a preview to the upcoming Guggenheim Abu Dhabi and Zayed National Museum. It hosts rotating exhibitions of contemporary art, photography, and design from local and international artists. The building itself is a minimalist masterpiece with open studios and a rooftop terrace.

  • Ticket Price: Free entry.
  • Hours: Tuesday to Sunday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM (closed Monday).
  • How to Get There: Taxi from city center (25 minutes, $14 (50 AED)-70) or bus 94 to Saadiyat Island.
  • Tip: Check the website for free workshops and artist talks. The terrace offers views of the Louvre Abu Dhabi dome.

1-Day, 2-Day, and 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: Highlights Tour

Morning (8:00 AM - 12:00 PM): Start at Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque (free, 1.5 hours). Then take a taxi (15 minutes) to Qasr Al Watan (1.5 hours).

Lunch (12:00 PM - 1:30 PM): Eat at the Qasr Al Watan café or drive to the Corniche for beachside restaurants like "The Club" ($14 (50 AED)-80 per person).

Afternoon (1:30 PM - 4:30 PM): Visit Louvre Abu Dhabi (2.5 hours). Then walk to the Founder's Memorial (30 minutes).

Evening (5:00 PM - 8:00 PM): Head to Yas Island for Ferrari World (closes at 8:00 PM) or Warner Bros. World (2 hours). Alternatively, enjoy a sunset walk on the Corniche.

Dinner (8:00 PM onwards): Dine at Emirates Palace ($41 (150 AED)-300 per person) or try local street food at Al Mina Market ($8 (30 AED)-50).

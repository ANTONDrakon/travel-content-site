Ras Al Khaimah Travel Guide 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Ras Al Khaimah, often abbreviated as RAK, is the northernmost emirate of the United Arab Emirates, a destination that masterfully blends ancient history with modern luxury. Unlike the towering skyscrapers of Dubai or Abu Dhabi, RAK offers a more serene and authentic Arabian experience, dominated by the dramatic Hajar Mountains, sprawling desert dunes, and a pristine coastline along the Arabian Gulf. The atmosphere here is one of relaxed adventure; you will find Bedouin heritage in the Al Jazirah Al Hamra ghost village, the thrill of the world’s longest zipline over Jebel Jais, and the tranquility of five-star beach resorts. The city itself is compact and navigable, with a population of approximately 400,000, giving it a small-town feel compared to its southern neighbors.

Why visit Ras Al Khaimah in 2026? The emirate has invested heavily in becoming a premier adventure and wellness destination. The completion of the Jebel Jais Flight, the world’s longest zipline at 2.83 kilometers, and the opening of new mountain resorts have solidified its reputation. Beyond adrenaline, RAK is home to the UAE’s oldest continuously inhabited settlement, the 7,000-year-old site of Julfar, and offers world-class golf courses, mangrove kayaking, and the only active salt flats in the region. For travelers seeking a blend of culture, nature, and luxury without the crowds and high costs of Dubai, RAK is the ideal choice. The emirate is also a leader in sustainable tourism, with several eco-resorts and conservation projects along its coastline.

In 2026, Ras Al Khaimah continues to attract visitors with its unique selling points: it is one of the most affordable emirates for a luxury holiday, it offers direct access to the highest peak in the UAE (Jebel Jais at 1,934 meters), and it provides a gateway to the Musandam Peninsula in Oman. Whether you are a history buff exploring the Dhayah Fort. a family seeking safe beaches. or an adventurer looking to climb via ferrata routes. RAK delivers a diverse and memorable experience. The emirate’s commitment to hosting international events. such as the RAK Half Marathon and the Arabian Travel Market’s satellite events. ensures a vibrant calendar year-round.

How to Get There

Ras Al Khaimah International Airport (RKT) is the primary gateway, located approximately 20 minutes from the city center. In 2026, RKT serves over 20 destinations directly, including major hubs like London (LHR), Moscow (DME), Jeddah (JED), and Islamabad (ISB). Average round-trip flight prices from London start at $450(economy), from Moscow at $350 and from Doha at $200 Budget carriers like Air Arabia and flydubai offer frequent connections via Sharjah and Dubai. For travelers arriving via Dubai International Airport (DXB), a one-hour taxi ride (approximately $80–$100) or a bus service (RTA route E700, $10) connects to RAK. Alternatively, Abu Dhabi International Airport (AUH) is a 90-minute drive, with shuttle services available for $40

Where to Stay

Ras Al Khaimah offers accommodation options across three main areas: the city center, the Al Marjan Island strip, and the Jebel Jais mountain region. For budget travelers, the city center provides hostels and mid-range hotels like the RAK Hotel (from $40 per night) and the Citymax Hotel (from $55 per night). The Al Hamra Village area offers self-catering apartments starting at $70 per night, ideal for families.

For mid-range luxury, the Cove Rotana Resort (from $120 per night) and the Rixos Bab Al Bahr (from $150 per night) on Al Marjan Island offer beachfront access with multiple pools and restaurants. The Waldorf Astoria Ras Al Khaimah (from $250 per night) is a top-tier option with a private beach, spa, and Michelin-starred dining. For a unique experience, the Bear Grylls Explorers Camp on Jebel Jais offers glamping tents from $200 per night, including adventure activities. The new Anantara Mina Al Arab Eco-Resort (opening 2025, from $180 per night) focuses on sustainability with overwater villas and mangrove conservation.

Getting Around

Ras Al Khaimah has a limited public transport system, but it is easy to navigate by taxi or rental car. Taxis are metered and widely available; a ride within the city costs $5–$10 while a trip from the airport to Al Marjan Island is $15 Uber and Careem operate here at similar rates. For longer stays, renting a car is recommended. Compact cars start at $30 per day from agencies like Avis or Budget, with fuel costing approximately $1 per liter (2026 prices). The RAK Public Bus network has six routes covering the main areas, with a flat fare of $1 per ride. The bus runs from 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM. For mountain trips to Jebel Jais, a taxi from the city costs $40 one-way, or you can join a guided tour for $60 per person.

Where to Eat

Ras Al Khaimah’s culinary scene is diverse, from street food to fine dining. For local Emirati cuisine, visit Al Fanar Restaurant (city center), where a meal of machboos (spiced rice with meat) costs $15–$20 For budget eats, the Al Dhait area is packed with Pakistani and Indian restaurants; a full thali meal at Ravi’s costs $5 Seafood lovers should head to the Al Hamra Marina for fresh catch at The View Restaurant ($25–$35 per person). On Al Marjan Island, fine dining options include the Italian restaurant at the Waldorf Astoria ($60 per person) and the Japanese-Peruvian fusion at SUSHISAMBA (opening 2026, $50–$80). Street food is best found at the RAK Night Market (Thursdays and Fridays), where shawarma wraps cost $3 and freshly squeezed sugarcane juice is $2 For a unique experience, book a desert dinner at the 1484 by Puro restaurant on Jebel Jais, which offers a set menu at $70 per person with panoramic mountain views.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Ras Al Khaimah is from October to April, when temperatures range from 20°C to 30°C (68°F–86°F). This is the peak tourist season, with hotel prices 20–30% higher than in summer. December and January are the coolest months, with average lows of 12°C (54°F) at night, ideal for mountain hikes. From May to September, temperatures soar to 45°C (113°F), making outdoor activities difficult, but hotel rates drop significantly, with luxury rooms available for $80–$100 per night. The summer months are still good for water parks, shopping malls, and indoor attractions. The RAK Half Marathon takes place in February, attracting runners from around the world. For budget travelers, the shoulder months of November and March offer pleasant weather and lower prices.

Visa & Documents

Citizens of over 60 countries, including the United States, United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, Japan, South Korea, and most European Union nations, can enter the UAE visa-free for up to 90 days. Nationals of India, China, and Russia can obtain a visa on arrival for 30 days (extendable for another 30 days at a cost of $100). All travelers must have a passport valid for at least six months from the date of entry. A return ticket and proof of accommodation may be requested at immigration. For visa extensions, visit the GDRFA office in RAK city ($50 for a 30-day extension). Travel insurance is highly recommended, especially for adventure activities.

10 Travel Tips

  • Dress modestly outside resorts: While swimwear is fine on beaches and pools, cover shoulders and knees in public areas like malls and souks. Women are not required to wear a headscarf, but it is respectful.
  • Book adventure activities in advance: The Jebel Jais Flight and Via Ferrata often sell out days ahead, especially during weekends. Reserve online at least 48 hours prior.
  • Carry cash for small vendors: While most hotels and malls accept credit cards, street food stalls and local souks prefer cash. ATMs are widely available in the city.
  • Use a reusable water bottle: Tap water is safe to drink in RAK, but most locals prefer bottled water. Many hotels have refill stations to reduce plastic waste.
  • Respect Ramadan hours: If visiting during the holy month (dates vary), eating, drinking, and smoking in public during daylight hours is prohibited. Hotels and restaurants will have designated screened areas.
  • Rent a car for mountain trips: Taxis to Jebel Jais can be expensive and scarce for return trips. A 4x4 rental gives you flexibility to explore the Hajar Mountains and the Oman border area.
  • Stay hydrated and use sunscreen: The sun is intense year-round. Apply SPF 50+ and drink at least 3 liters of water daily, especially during summer months.
  • Visit the ghost village at sunrise: Al Jazirah Al Hamra is best seen in the early morning light, with fewer crowds and cooler temperatures. Entry is free at all hours.
  • Check for public holidays: National Day (December 2) and Eid celebrations often feature fireworks, cultural festivals, and hotel discounts. Book early for these periods.
  • Learn a few Arabic phrases: “Shukran” (thank you) and “Marhaba” (hello) are appreciated. Most locals speak English, but a little effort goes a long way in markets.

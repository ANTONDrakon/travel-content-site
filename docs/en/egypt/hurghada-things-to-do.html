15 Best Things to Do in Hurghada 2026

Hurghada, once a quiet fishing village, has evolved into Egypt’s premier Red Sea resort destination, attracting over 3 million visitors annually by 2025. With its crystal-clear waters, vibrant coral reefs, and a blend of desert and urban attractions, the city offers a diverse range of activities that go beyond sunbathing. In 2026, new infrastructure, including an expanded international airport and upgraded marina facilities, makes Hurghada more accessible than ever. Whether you are a diver, a history enthusiast, or a family traveler, this guide covers the 15 best things to do, complete with practical details to plan your trip efficiently.

15 Best Attractions in Hurghada

1. Giftun Island National Park

Giftun Island, part of the Red Sea Marine Reserve, is a protected archipelago known for its white-sand beaches and pristine snorkeling sites. The island features two main beaches: the tourist-friendly "Giftun Beach" with loungers and the more secluded "Orange Bay." In 2026, boat operators offer eco-certified trips to minimize environmental impact. Ticket prices for park entry are $5 per person, plus boat transfers averaging $25–$35 round-trip. Hours are typically 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM daily. To get there, book a tour from Hurghada Marina or El Dahar port; public ferries are not available. Tip: Bring reef-safe sunscreen and arrive early to secure a spot on the less crowded Orange Bay side.

2. Hurghada Grand Aquarium

Opened in 2020, this modern aquarium houses over 1,200 marine species across 24 tanks, including a 20-meter-long shark tunnel and a crocodile exhibit. It also features a 4D cinema and a simulated rainforest zone. Ticket prices: adults $18, children (3–12) $12, with a family pass (2 adults + 2 children) for $50. Hours are 10:00 AM to 8:00 PM daily. Located on the main Sheraton Road, it is a 10-minute walk from the marina or a $3 taxi ride from downtown. Tip: Visit on weekdays to avoid school groups, and combine with a ticket to the nearby Hurghada Museum for a $5 discount.

3. Desert Safari and Bedouin Dinner

Experience the Eastern Desert with a 4x4 jeep safari, camel ride, and a traditional Bedouin dinner under the stars. Tours typically include a visit to a Bedouin village, a short hike to a sunset viewpoint, and a barbecue meal with local dishes. Prices range from $30 to $50 per person, depending on the operator. Departures are usually at 2:00 PM and return by 9:00 PM. Tours pick up from any hotel in Hurghada; no public transport is available. Tip: Choose a tour that includes a stargazing session—the desert’s low light pollution offers incredible views of the Milky Way.

4. El Gouna

El Gouna, a resort town 25 kilometers north of Hurghada, features 18 islands, 10 hotels, and a network of lagoons. Designed by architect Samih Sawiris, it is known for its upscale dining, golf courses, and the Abu Tig Marina. Entry to El Gouna is free, but access to private beaches and pools requires a day pass ($20–$40). Speedboat tours from Hurghada cost $15–$25 one-way, or take a public microbus for $2 from the El Dahar bus station. Hours are unrestricted, but most attractions operate 10:00 AM to 10:00 PM. Tip: Rent a bicycle ($10/day) to explore the town’s pedestrian-friendly paths and hidden cafes.

5. Hurghada Marina

The marina is a bustling hub with over 200 yachts, restaurants, and shops. It offers a promenade perfect for evening strolls, with views of the Red Sea and live music at several cafes. Entry is free. Hours are 24/7, but shops and restaurants operate 10:00 AM to 2:00 AM. Located on Sheraton Road, it is a 5-minute walk from the Grand Aquarium. Tip: Dine at "The Boat" restaurant for fresh seafood, with mains averaging $15–$25.

6. Scuba Diving at Abu Dabbab

Abu Dabbab Bay, 30 kilometers south of Hurghada, is famous for its seagrass meadows where dugongs (sea cows) are frequently spotted. The site also has a vibrant reef with turtles, rays, and moray eels. A two-tank dive trip costs $70–$90, including equipment and transport. Hours are typically 8:00 AM to 2:00 PM. Dive centers in Hurghada, such as "Emperor Divers" or "Red Sea Diving," offer daily trips. Tip: Book a morning dive for the best visibility (20–30 meters) and calmer seas.

7. Hurghada Museum

This museum, opened in 2020, showcases 1,500 artifacts spanning Pharaonic, Roman, and Islamic periods. Highlights include a mummy room and a collection of ancient jewelry. Ticket prices: adults $10, students $5. Hours are 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, closed Fridays. Located on El Nasr Street, a 10-minute walk from the marina. Tip: Combine with a Grand Aquarium visit for a discounted combo ticket ($22 for both).

8. Dolphin House Reef

Located near Giftun Island, this shallow reef (3–8 meters deep) is a hotspot for spinner dolphins. Snorkeling tours from Hurghada cost $30–$45 per person, including boat transfer, lunch, and guide. Hours are 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM. Book through operators like "Sea Serpent" or "Dolphin World." Tip: Go early (7:00 AM departure) to avoid crowds and increase dolphin sightings.

9. Sand City Hurghada

An open-air museum featuring 60 sand sculptures by international artists, depicting Egyptian history, global landmarks, and fantasy themes. Ticket prices: adults $8, children $5. Hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Located on the road to Safaga, a 15-minute drive from downtown. Taxi fare is $5–$7. Tip: Visit at sunset for golden-hour lighting on the sculptures, and allow 1–2 hours.

10. Mahmya Beach

Mahmya, on Giftun Island, is a private eco-beach with white sand, loungers, and a restaurant. It is known for its calm, shallow waters and coral gardens. Day passes cost $30–$50, including boat transfer, lunch, and snorkeling gear. Hours are 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Book through "Mahmya Tours" or your hotel. Tip: The beach limits daily visitors to 200, so book 48 hours in advance during peak season (December–February).

11. St. Shenouda Monastery

This Coptic Christian monastery, founded in the 4th century, is located 40 kilometers northwest of Hurghada. It features ancient churches, a library, and a museum. Entry is free, but donations are welcome. Hours are 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM, closed Sundays. Take a taxi ($20–$30 round-trip) or join a guided tour ($15 per person). Tip: Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered) and bring water, as the site has limited facilities.

12. Jungle Aqua Park

One of the largest water parks in the region, with 50 slides, a wave pool, and a lazy river. Ticket prices: adults $25, children $15. Hours are 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM. Located on the Hurghada–Safaga road, a 10-minute taxi ride ($5) from downtown. Tip: Arrive at 10:00 AM to claim a shaded sunbed, and bring water shoes for hot surfaces.

13. El Dahar Old Town

This historic district offers a glimpse of traditional Hurghada, with a souk (market), coffee shops, and the 19th-century El Mina Mosque. Entry is free. Hours are 9:00 AM to 11:00 PM (shops close at 10:00 PM). Accessible by a 15-minute walk from the marina or a $2 taxi. Tip: Bargain for spices, carpets, and silver jewelry at the souk, but avoid buying coral or shells, which are illegal to export.

14. Glass Boat Tour

These boats have glass-bottomed hulls, allowing you to view coral reefs and marine life without getting wet. Tours last 1–2 hours and cost $20–$30 per person. Departures from the marina are every hour from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Tip: Choose a tour that stops at a snorkeling spot for a combined experience ($35).

15. Hurghada Sunset Cruise

A 2-hour catamaran or yacht cruise with dinner, drinks, and views of the Red Sea sunset. Prices range from $40 to $80 per person. Departs at 4:30 PM from the marina. Tip: Book a "dinner cruise" for a full meal, or a "cocktail cruise" for a lighter experience.

1-Day, 2-Day, and 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: City and Sea

Start at 8:00 AM with a visit to the Hurghada Grand Aquarium (2 hours). Then walk to the Hurghada Museum (1.5 hours). Lunch at the marina (12:30 PM). At 2:00 PM, take a glass boat tour (1.5 hours). Finish with a sunset stroll at the marina and dinner at a seafood restaurant. Total cost: $60–$80 per person.

2-Day Itinerary: Island and Desert

Day 1: Morning at Giftun Island (8:00 AM–3:00 PM). Afternoon at Mahmya Beach (3:30 PM–6:00 PM). Evening at El Dahar Old Town (7:00 PM–9:00 PM). Day 2: Morning desert safari (2:00 PM–9:00 PM) with Bedouin dinner. Total cost: $120–$150 per person.

3-Day Itinerary: Full Experience

Day 1: Aquarium and museum (morning), glass boat tour (afternoon), marina dinner (evening). Day 2: Giftun Island and Mahmya Beach (full day). Day 3: Morning dive at Abu Dabbab (8:00 AM–2:00 PM), afternoon at Jungle Aqua Park (3:00 PM–6:00 PM), evening sunset cruise (6:30 PM–8:30 PM). Total cost: $250–$300 per person.

  • El Gouna Day Trip: $25–$40 per person, includes transfer and beach access.
  • Luxor Day Trip: $70–$100 per person (12 hours), includes Valley of the Kings and Karnak Temple.

Free Attractions

  • Hurghada Marina: Stroll, people-watch, and enjoy free events (live music on weekends).
  • El Dahar Old Town: Explore the souk and mosque at no cost.
  • Public Beaches: "Sakala Beach" and "El Dahar Beach" are free, with basic amenities.
  • St. Shenouda Monastery: Free entry, donations optional.
  • Sunset Viewpoints: The marina promenade and the hill near "Moby Dick" restaurant offer panoramic views.

Practical Tips

Transportation

  • Taxis: No meters; negotiate fares upfront. Short trips (2–3 km) cost $2–$5; longer trips (10 km) cost $8–$12.
  • Microbuses: Shared minibuses cost $0.50–$1 per ride, but routes are not marked—ask locals.
  • Rental Cars: From $30/day, but parking is limited in central areas.
  • Airport Transfers: Pre-booked shuttles cost $10–$15 per person; taxis from the airport are $15–$20.

General Tips

  • Best Time to Visit: March–May and September–November for temperatures of 25–30°C (77–86°F). Avoid July–August (40°C/104°F).
  • Currency: Egyptian pounds (EGP). ATMs are widespread, but carry cash for taxis and small shops.
  • Dress Code: Swimwear on beaches; modest clothing in towns (cover shoulders and knees).
  • Health: Drink bottled water only; use reef-safe sunscreen to protect coral.

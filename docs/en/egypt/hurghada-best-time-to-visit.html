Best Time to Visit Hurghada 2026: Seasons & Weather

Hurghada, a premier resort city on the Red Sea coast of Egypt, enjoys a subtropical arid climate with over 300 days of sunshine annually. The region is characterized by mild winters, hot summers, and minimal rainfall, making it a year-round destination for travelers seeking warmth, clear skies, and world-class diving. Understanding the nuances of Hurghada’s seasons—temperature fluctuations, wind patterns, and tourist flows—is essential for planning an optimal trip in 2026. This guide provides a comprehensive breakdown of monthly weather, peak and off-peak periods, and activity-specific recommendations to help you choose the best time for your visit.

High Season

Hurghada’s high season spans from November to March, coinciding with the European winter and the most comfortable weather for outdoor activities. During these months, average daytime temperatures range from 16°C to 22°C (61°F to 72°F), with cool evenings requiring light jackets. The sea remains swimmable at 22-25°C (72-77°F), but the main draw is the absence of oppressive heat, making it ideal for desert safaris, historical tours, and leisurely walks along the marina.

Why high season? This period aligns with Christmas, New Year, and Easter holidays, when European travelers flock to escape colder climates. The Red Sea’s calm waters and excellent visibility (often exceeding 30 meters) attract divers and snorkelers. Prices peak significantly: hotel rates can increase by 40-60% compared to low season, especially from December 20 to January 5 and during Easter week. A standard four-star hotel room that costs $50 per night in June may rise to $120 per night in late December. Flights from major European hubs like London, Berlin, and Paris are also at a premium, with round-trip fares averaging $400-600 in high season versus $250-400 in low season.

For 2026, expect the high season to be particularly busy due to pent-up demand from international travel recovery. Booking accommodations and flights at least three months in advance is strongly recommended. Many resorts offer all-inclusive packages, but these can cost $150-250 per person per night during peak weeks.

Low Season

Hurghada’s low season runs from June to September, characterized by intense heat, with average highs of 33-35°C (91-95°F) and occasional spikes above 40°C (104°F). Humidity remains low (averaging 30-40%), but the sun is relentless, especially between 11:00 AM and 4:00 PM. This period sees the fewest tourists, as many avoid the extreme temperatures.

Pros: Significantly lower prices. Hotel rates can drop by 50-70% compared to high season, with five-star resorts offering rooms for $80-120 per night. Flights are cheapest, with round-trip fares from Europe often below $300. The Red Sea’s water temperature is at its warmest (27-29°C / 81-84°F), perfect for swimming and snorkeling without wetsuits. Dive sites are less crowded, and you can often have popular spots like Giftun Island or Abu Ramada almost to yourself.

Cons: The heat can be challenging for sightseeing. Daytime excursions to Luxor or the Eastern Desert are best started at dawn (around 5:00 AM) to avoid midday sun. Air conditioning is essential, and outdoor activities like hiking or quad biking are limited to early morning or late afternoon. Some restaurants and smaller shops may close for several weeks during August. Wind conditions can also be strong in June and July, with occasional khamsin winds (hot, dusty winds) reducing visibility.

Weather: Expect clear skies every day, with zero precipitation. Sea breezes offer some relief, but the heat is constant. Evenings remain warm, rarely dropping below 28°C (82°F) in July and August.

Prices: A budget traveler can find hostels for $15-20 per night, while mid-range hotels cost $30-50. All-inclusive packages for a week can be found for $400-600 per person.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

The optimal months for a beach-focused trip to Hurghada are April, May, October, and November. During these shoulder periods, air temperatures range from 25-29°C (77-84°F), and water temperatures are between 23-27°C (73-81°F). This combination allows for comfortable sunbathing, swimming, and water sports without the scorching heat of summer or the cooler breezes of winter.

May and October are particularly ideal: the sea is warm enough for hours of swimming, and the sun is strong but not oppressive. In May, average sea temperature reaches 25°C (77°F), and by October it remains at 27°C (81°F). These months also see lower tourist numbers than the winter high season, meaning more space on the beach and shorter queues for jet skis, parasailing, and glass-bottom boat tours. The wind is generally light, making conditions perfect for snorkeling at the famed El Gouna or Sahl Hasheesh beaches.

For those who prefer the hottest possible sea temperature, August offers 29°C (84°F) water, but air temperatures above 35°C (95°F) can make prolonged beach time uncomfortable without frequent dips. If you visit in summer, plan beach activities before 10:00 AM and after 4:00 PM.

Best Time for Sightseeing

Sightseeing in Hurghada and its surroundings—including day trips to Luxor (Valley of the Kings, Karnak Temple) and the Eastern Desert (Monastery of St. Anthony, Wadi El Gemal)—is most comfortable from November through March. During these months, daytime highs are a pleasant 16-22°C (61-72°F), and the lack of humidity makes walking and exploring feasible for several hours.

December and January are the most popular months for historical tours. A typical day trip to Luxor (a 4-hour drive each way) involves visiting multiple open-air temples and tombs; the cool weather ensures you can spend 6-8 hours outside without heat exhaustion. Prices for guided tours are higher in this period (averaging $80-120 per person for a full-day Luxor tour), but the experience is vastly superior to summer, when temperatures can exceed 45°C (113°F) in Luxor.

February and March are also excellent, with blooming desert flowers in the Eastern Desert and fewer crowds than the Christmas period. For desert safaris, November to March offers comfortable evenings for camping under the stars, with nighttime temperatures around 10-15°C (50-59°F). Avoid sightseeing in June through September unless you are prepared for extreme heat and start activities at sunrise.

Rainy Season

Hurghada technically has no distinct rainy season. The entire year averages less than 10 mm of precipitation, with the highest monthly totals being 2 mm in March, October, November, and December. Rain is extremely rare, occurring on average only 1-2 days per month during these periods, and it typically manifests as a brief, light drizzle lasting minutes rather than hours. Thunderstorms are virtually unknown.

Is it worth visiting during these months? Absolutely. The minimal rain does not disrupt any activities. In fact, the slightly cooler temperatures in November and December make these months among the best for overall travel. The chance of encountering rain is so low that it should not influence your decision. The only weather-related concern is occasional wind, particularly in March, when khamsin winds can bring dust and reduce visibility for a day or two.

Events & Festivals

Hurghada hosts a variety of events that can enhance your visit, though many are tied to religious or cultural calendars that shift annually. For 2026, here are the major events by month:

  • January (1-15): New Year celebrations and Hurghada Winter Festival, featuring live music, food stalls, and fireworks along the marina. Hotel occupancy is at its peak.
  • February (Variable): Coptic Christmas (January 7 in the Gregorian calendar, but celebrations may extend into February) and the Hurghada International Marathon, typically held in mid-February, with routes along the coastline.
  • March-April (Variable): Easter celebrations (Orthodox and Catholic) bring family-oriented events, egg decorating, and special brunches at resorts. The Hurghada Spring Festival in late March includes cultural performances and art exhibitions.
  • May (1-5): Labor Day and the Hurghada Fishing Tournament, attracting anglers from across the region. Water temperatures are warming, making it a great time for boat trips.
  • June (Variable): The Hurghada International Diving Festival, with free introductory dives, underwater clean-ups, and seminars at dive centers like Emperor Divers and Extra Divers.
  • July-August (Variable): Eid al-Adha (Islamic holiday) may fall in this period, with local celebrations, but many Egyptians travel to coastal resorts, so beaches can be crowded with domestic tourists. The Hurghada Summer Festival runs from mid-July to late August, with nightly concerts, bazaars, and water sports competitions.
  • September (Variable): Islamic New Year and the Hurghada Kite Festival, held on the beaches of El Gouna, featuring kiteboarding competitions and demonstrations.
  • October (1-15): Hurghada Jazz Festival, attracting international musicians for outdoor concerts at the marina. Also, the Sinai Liberation Day (October 6) is celebrated with parades.
  • November (Variable): The Hurghada Food Festival, showcasing Egyptian cuisine and international dishes at participating restaurants. Prices begin rising as high season approaches.
  • December (20-31): Christmas and New Year celebrations, with elaborate decorations, gala dinners, and fireworks. This is the most expensive and crowded period.

Conclusion

For the best overall experience in Hurghada in 2026, the ideal months are April, May, October, and November. These shoulder periods offer a perfect balance of warm weather (25-29°C / 77-84°F), comfortable sea temperatures (23-27°C / 73-81°F), lower tourist numbers, and moderate prices. You can enjoy both beach activities and sightseeing without the extremes of summer heat or winter crowds.

If your priority is diving and snorkeling, May through October provides the warmest water and best visibility, though summer months require heat management. For budget-conscious travelers, June through September offers the lowest prices, but you must be prepared for intense heat. For cultural tours and desert adventures, November through March delivers the most comfortable conditions, albeit at higher costs.

Regardless of when you visit, Hurghada’s near-perpetual sunshine and calm Red Sea waters ensure a rewarding trip. Book early for high season (November-March) and take advantage of low-season deals for summer. Always check visa requirements (most nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival for $25) and consider travel insurance for peace of mind.

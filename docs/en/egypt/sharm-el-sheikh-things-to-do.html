15 Best Things to Do in Sharm El Sheikh 2026

Sharm El Sheikh, perched on the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, is Egypt’s premier Red Sea resort destination. In 2026, the city continues to attract millions of visitors annually—over 5.2 million tourists visited in 2024, with numbers steadily rising—drawn by its crystalline waters, world-class coral reefs, and dramatic desert landscapes. Unlike Cairo or Luxor, Sharm offers a unique blend of marine adventure, historical intrigue, and modern luxury. From the depths of Ras Mohammed National Park to the heights of Mount Sinai, this guide covers the 15 best attractions, complete with ticket prices, hours, transport tips, and insider advice for the year ahead.

15 Best Attractions in Sharm El Sheikh

1. Ras Mohammed National Park

Ras Mohammed is Egypt’s crown jewel of marine conservation, covering 480 square kilometers at the convergence of the Gulf of Suez and Gulf of Aqaba. Established in 1983, it boasts over 1,000 species of fish and 150 species of coral. Snorkelers and divers flock to iconic sites like Yolanda Reef and Shark Observatory, where visibility often exceeds 30 meters. The park also features salt lakes and mangrove channels on its terrestrial side.

Ticket Price: USD 15 per person (E£ 450) for foreigners, USD 5 (E£ 150) for Egyptians. Hours: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM daily. How to get there: A 30-minute drive from Naama Bay; taxis cost around USD 20 (E£ 600) one-way. Tip: Arrive by 7:45 AM to beat the crowds and secure a parking spot near the main diving jetty.

2. Naama Bay Promenade

Naama Bay is the beating heart of Sharm’s nightlife and beach culture, a 3-kilometer crescent-shaped bay lined with restaurants, bars, and shops. By day, you can swim in calm, shallow waters or rent jet skis (starting at USD 40 per 15 minutes). By night, the promenade lights up with live music and shisha cafes. It remains the most accessible and vibrant area for first-time visitors.

Ticket Price: Free entry. Hours: 24/7, though most shops open 10:00 AM to 2:00 AM. How to get there: Central to the resort area; walking from most hotels in Naama Bay takes 5–10 minutes. Tip: Visit at sunset (around 5:30 PM in summer, 4:30 PM in winter) for the best photo opportunities of the Red Sea.

3. Tiran Island

Tiran Island, located 6 kilometers off the coast, is a protected nature reserve famous for its four coral reefs: Jackson, Woodhouse, Thomas, and Gordon. These reefs host massive schools of Napoleon wrasse, parrotfish, and occasional reef sharks. The island itself is uninhabited except for a military outpost, but the snorkeling and diving are world-renowned. Most access is via boat trips from Sharm’s marinas.

Ticket Price: Included in most boat tours (USD 50–80 per person for a full-day trip). Hours: Boat departures typically at 8:00 AM, returning by 4:00 PM. How to get there: Book through a tour operator at Sharm Marina or your hotel. Tip: Choose a morning trip to avoid wind chop; afternoon sea breezes can reduce visibility.

4. Mount Sinai (Gebel Musa)

Mount Sinai, standing at 2,285 meters, is a pilgrimage site revered by Jews, Christians, and Muslims as the location where Moses received the Ten Commandments. The climb takes 2–3 hours via the 3,750 Steps of Repentance or a longer camel path. The summit offers a panoramic sunrise view over the Sinai desert and the Gulf of Aqaba. The adjacent Saint Catherine’s Monastery, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is a must-see.

Ticket Price: Free to climb; Saint Catherine’s Monastery costs USD 10 (E£ 300) for entry. Hours: Monastery open 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM, closed Fridays and Sundays. How to get there: A 2-hour drive from Sharm (200 km); guided night tours depart around 11:00 PM. Tip: Wear sturdy hiking boots and bring a headlamp; temperatures at the summit can drop to 5°C even in summer.

5. Soho Square

Soho Square is a modern entertainment complex in the Hadaba district, featuring an ice-skating rink, bowling alley, and a 360-degree observation wheel. It also hosts a nightly fountain show with laser lights and music. The square is surrounded by international restaurants and a large casino. It is a family-friendly alternative to Naama Bay’s more adult-oriented nightlife.

Ticket Price: Free entry; ice skating costs USD 10 (E£ 300) per hour. Hours: 10:00 AM to 2:00 AM daily. How to get there: A 10-minute taxi ride from Naama Bay (USD 5–8). Tip: Visit on Wednesday or Saturday for the “Egyptian Night” event with traditional dance performances.

6. Shark’s Bay Beach

Shark’s Bay is a tranquil, shallow lagoon ideal for beginner snorkelers and families. The bay is protected by a natural reef, keeping waters calm and clear year-round. Despite its name, shark sightings are rare, but you will find sea turtles, rays, and colorful reef fish just 10 meters from shore. The beach is lined with private resorts, but public access is available at the Shark’s Bay Umbi Diving Village.

Ticket Price: Free public access; loungers rent for USD 5 (E£ 150). Hours: Sunrise to sunset. How to get there: 5 km north of Naama Bay; a taxi costs USD 4 (E£ 120). Tip: Bring reef-safe sunscreen, as many operators enforce strict environmental policies.

7. St. Catherine’s Monastery

Founded in the 6th century, Saint Catherine’s Monastery is one of the oldest continuously operating Christian monasteries in the world. It houses the Codex Sinaiticus, a 4th-century Greek manuscript of the Bible, and the Burning Bush, believed to be the original from the biblical story. The monastery’s library contains over 3,000 manuscripts. It is a 2-hour drive from Sharm, often combined with a Mount Sinai climb.

Ticket Price: USD 10 (E£ 300) for foreigners. Hours: 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM, closed Fridays and Sundays. How to get there: Hire a private driver (USD 60–80 round trip) or join a guided tour (USD 40–50 per person). Tip: Dress modestly—cover shoulders and knees—as it is an active religious site.

8. Ras Um Sid

Ras Um Sid is a rocky headland between Naama Bay and Hadaba, offering some of the best shore diving in Sharm. The coral wall drops to 30 meters, with strong currents attracting pelagic species like tuna and barracuda. Snorkelers can explore the shallow plateau, which is teeming with anemones and clownfish. It is less crowded than Ras Mohammed, making it a favorite among locals.

Ticket Price: Free for snorkeling; diving costs around USD 40 per tank. Hours: Best visited 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM for optimal light. How to get there: Walk from Hadaba hotels or take a 5-minute taxi from Naama Bay (USD 3). Tip: Avoid after heavy rain, as runoff can reduce visibility for 24 hours.

9. Old Market (Sharm El Sheikh Old Market)

The Old Market, located in the Hadaba district, is a traditional Egyptian souk selling spices, perfumes, papyrus, and handcrafted jewelry. Unlike the touristy shops in Naama Bay, this market offers a more authentic bargaining experience. You can also find fresh juice stalls and small kebab restaurants. It is a quieter alternative for souvenir hunting.

Ticket Price: Free entry. Hours: 10:00 AM to 11:00 PM daily. How to get there: A 15-minute walk from Soho Square or a USD 5 taxi from Naama Bay. Tip: Haggle aggressively—start at 30% of the asking price for spices and textiles.

10. White Lagoon (Ras Mohammed)

White Lagoon is a shallow, turquoise inlet inside Ras Mohammed National Park, named for its brilliant white sand bottom. It is a popular stop for snorkeling boat tours, offering calm, crystal-clear water perfect for beginners. The lagoon is also a nesting site for sea turtles. Access is restricted to protect the fragile ecosystem, so visitors must stay on designated paths.

Ticket Price: Included in Ras Mohammed park entry (USD 15). Hours: Same as park: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. How to get there: A 20-minute drive from the park entrance; most tours include it. Tip: Bring water shoes, as the sand can get extremely hot by noon.

11. Al Mustafa Mosque

Al Mustafa Mosque is the largest mosque in Sharm El Sheikh, with a capacity of 3,000 worshippers. Built in 2010, its Ottoman-inspired architecture features a 70-meter minaret and intricate marble calligraphy. Non-Muslims are welcome outside prayer times. The mosque is a peaceful retreat from the beach crowds.

Ticket Price: Free. Hours: Open to visitors 9:00 AM to 12:00 PM and 2:00 PM to 5:00 PM. How to get there: Located in the Hadaba area, a 10-minute walk from Soho Square. Tip: Women must cover their hair and arms; scarves are available at the entrance.

12. Cleopatra’s Bath (Ras Mohammed)

Cleopatra’s Bath is a natural rock pool on the coast of Ras Mohammed, formed by a collapsed cliff. According to local legend, Cleopatra herself bathed here. The pool is connected to the sea via underwater channels, so it teems with small fish and coral. It is a popular photo spot due to its emerald-green water.

Ticket Price: Included in Ras Mohammed park entry (USD 15). Hours: 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM. How to get there: A 15-minute walk from the park’s main parking lot. Tip: Visit at low tide for the best visibility and safer entry.

13. Nabq Protected Area

Nabq Protected Area, 35 kilometers north of Sharm, covers 600 square kilometers and is the largest nature reserve in the Sinai. It features mangrove forests, which are rare in the Red Sea, and sand dunes that rise 50 meters high. The area is a haven for birdwatchers, with over 200 species recorded. It is also known for its fossilized coral reefs.

Ticket Price: USD 8 (E£ 240) for foreigners. Hours: 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM. How to get there: A 45-minute drive from Naama Bay; hire a taxi for USD 25 one-way. Tip: Book a 4x4 safari to explore the dunes; tours start at USD 40 per person.

14. Sharm El Sheikh International Conference Center

This modern conference center, built in 2020, hosts major events like the World Youth Forum and climate summits. Even if you are not attending a conference, the building’s architecture is impressive, with a glass facade reflecting the Red Sea. The surrounding gardens are open to the public and feature sculptures by Egyptian artists.

Ticket Price: Free for gardens; conference tickets vary. Hours: Gardens open 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM. How to get there: Located in the Peace Road area, a 10-minute taxi from Naama Bay (USD 5). Tip: Check the events calendar online; sometimes open-air concerts are held here.

15. Red Sea Diving at Jackson Reef

Jackson Reef, part of the Tiran Island complex, is a drift dive site where currents carry you along a coral wall descending to 50 meters. The reef is famous for its soft corals and large schools of jacks and snappers. In 2025, a new artificial reef was sunk nearby to boost marine life. It is best for intermediate and advanced divers.

Ticket Price: Boat dive packages start at USD 70 (including gear). Hours: Morning dives 8:00 AM to 1:00 PM. How to get there: Book with a dive center in Naama Bay or Hadaba. Tip: Check your dive computer’s no-decompression limits; currents can be strong, so stay with your buddy.

1-Day, 2-Day, and 3-Day Itineraries

1-Day Itinerary: Best of Sharm in a Day

Morning (7:00 AM–12:00 PM): Start at Ras Mohammed National Park. Arrive by 7:30 AM, snorkel at Yolanda Reef, and visit White Lagoon. Lunch (12:30 PM): Quick meal at a beachside cafe in Naama Bay (budget USD 10–15). Afternoon (1:30 PM–4:00 PM): Explore the Old Market for souvenirs and spices. Evening (5:00 PM–9:00 PM): Sunset at Naama Bay Promenade, dinner at a seafood restaurant (USD 20–30), and a shisha at a beachfront cafe.

2-Day Itinerary: Marine and Mountain Adventure

Day 1: Follow the 1-day itinerary above. Day 2: Start at 4:00 AM for a Mount Sinai sunrise climb. Descend by 8:00 AM, visit Saint Catherine’s Monastery (9:00 AM–12:00 PM). Return to Sharm by 2:00 PM. Spend the afternoon snorkeling at Shark’s Bay. Evening: Soho Square for ice skating and dinner.

3-Day Itinerary: Full Sinai Experience

Day 1: Ras Mohammed and Naama Bay. Day 2: Tiran Island boat trip (8:00 AM–4:00 PM). Evening: dinner at a Hadaba restaurant. Day 3: Morning dive at Jackson Reef or Ras Um Sid. Afternoon: Nabq Protected Area safari (4x4 tour, 2:00 PM–6:00 PM). Departure or final evening at Al Mustafa Mosque.

Tours & Excursions

For 2026, the most popular tours include:

  • Ras Mohammed Full-Day Snorkeling Tour: USD 50–60 per person, includes lunch and transport. Departs 8:00 AM, returns 4:00 PM.
  • Tiran Island Boat Trip: USD 70–90 per person, with two snorkeling stops and a buffet lunch. Book through Red Sea Diving Safari.
  • Mount Sinai & St. Catherine’s Overnight Tour: USD 45–55 per person, includes dinner, transport, and guide. Departs 10:00 PM, returns 12:00 PM next day.
  • Desert Safari by Quad Bike: USD 35–50 per person for a 2-hour ride in the Sinai dunes, often with a Bedouin tea stop.
  • Private Yacht Charter: USD 300–500 for a half-day, with capacity for 10 people, including snorkeling gear and soft drinks.

Prices are based on 2026 rates and may vary with season. Book through reputable operators like Emperor Divers or the Sharm El Sheikh Tourist Authority.

Free Attractions in Sharm El Sheikh

  • Naama Bay Promenade: Stroll, swim, and people-watch without spending a penny.
  • Al Mustafa Mosque: Free entry for respectful visitors.
  • Ras Um Sid Snorkeling: Access the water from public rocks at no cost.
  • Old Market: Browsing is free; only pay for purchases.
  • Soho Square: Entry and fountain shows are free; only activities like ice skating cost.
  • Public Beaches: Shark’s Bay and Naama Bay have free public sections.

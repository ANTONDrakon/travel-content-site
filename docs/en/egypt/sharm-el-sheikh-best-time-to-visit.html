Best Time to Visit Sharm El Sheikh 2026: Seasons & Weather

Sharm El Sheikh, nestled at the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, is one of Egypt’s premier Red Sea resorts. Its climate is defined by hot, arid summers and mild, pleasant winters, with minimal rainfall year-round. The region enjoys over 350 sunny days annually, making it a reliable destination for sun-seekers. The Red Sea moderates temperatures, keeping coastal areas cooler than inland deserts. Understanding the seasonal nuances is key to planning your ideal trip in 2026, whether you prioritize diving, beach lounging, or exploring historical sites.

High Season

The high season in Sharm El Sheikh spans from November to March, peaking around Christmas, New Year, and Easter. This period attracts the largest crowds due to the comfortable temperatures, with daytime highs averaging 20–24°C. European tourists, particularly from the UK, Germany, and Russia, flock here to escape colder climates. Diving conditions remain excellent, with water temperatures around 22–25°C, and visibility often exceeding 30 meters. Prices for accommodation surge by 40–60% compared to low season. A standard double room in a 4-star hotel might cost $120–180 per night during peak weeks, while budget options start at $70. Flights from major European hubs are also pricier, with round-trip fares averaging $400–600. Bookings for 2026 should be made by September 2025 for the best availability.

Low Season

The low season runs from June to September, characterized by intense heat. Daytime temperatures consistently exceed 35°C, often reaching 40°C in July and August. Humidity is low, but the sun is relentless from 10 AM to 4 PM. Pros include significantly lower prices—hotels drop by 50% or more, with 4-star rooms available for $50–80 per night. The Red Sea water, at 28–30°C, feels like a warm bath, ideal for snorkeling and diving without a wetsuit. Cons are the heat, which limits outdoor activities to early mornings or evenings. Many restaurants and shops operate on reduced hours. Despite this, the low season is popular with budget travelers and divers who prioritize underwater exploration. Wind conditions are minimal, making it perfect for calm sea excursions.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

The optimal months for a beach vacation in Sharm El Sheikh are April, May, October, and November. During these shoulder months, air temperatures range from 26–30°C, warm enough for sunbathing but not oppressive. Water temperatures hover between 24–27°C, refreshing for swimming without being cold. Crowds are thinner than in high season, and hotel prices are moderate. For example, in May, you can enjoy 12 hours of daylight with a gentle sea breeze. Avoid July and August unless you prefer extreme heat; the sand can become too hot for bare feet by midday. December through February, while pleasant for walking, may require a light jacket for evening beach strolls as temperatures drop to 15°C at night.

Best Time for Sightseeing

Sightseeing, including trips to Ras Mohammed National Park, Mount Sinai, or St. Catherine’s Monastery, is most comfortable from November to March. Daytime highs of 18–24°C allow for hiking and exploring without heat exhaustion. For climbing Mount Sinai (2,285 meters), winter months are ideal—temperatures at the summit can drop to 5°C at sunrise, so bring warm layers. April and October are also good, but by May, afternoon heat becomes challenging for extended outdoor walks. The rainy season is negligible, so no disruption from weather. Sightseeing tours are widely available, with group prices around $30–50 per person for day trips. Avoid June to September for any extensive walking; the sun can cause dehydration quickly.

Rainy Season

Sharm El Sheikh has no true rainy season. Precipitation averages just 2 mm per month in January, February, March, and December, with zero rain from May to September. When rain does occur, it is typically a brief, light drizzle lasting minutes. Flash floods are extremely rare in the city itself, though wadis (dry riverbeds) in the desert may see sudden runoff after heavy storms inland. The region receives less than 10 mm annually, making it one of the driest places on Earth. Is it worth visiting during the "rainy" months? Absolutely. The minimal rain does not affect activities, and the cooler temperatures are a bonus for most travelers.

Events & Festivals

Sharm El Sheikh hosts a variety of events throughout the year, blending local culture, sports, and international celebrations.

  • January: Sharm El Sheikh International Marathon – a popular running event along the coast, attracting athletes from 50+ countries. Average temperature: 18°C.
  • February: Coptic Christmas (January 7) and Sinai Liberation Day (April 25) are observed, but no major public festivals occur in February.
  • March: Spring Equinox celebrations at local resorts; many hotels offer special packages.
  • April: Sham El Nessim (Egyptian spring festival) – families picnic on beaches; dates vary based on Coptic calendar, typically in April.
  • May: World Diving Day (May 6) – dive centers offer free introductory dives and discounts.
  • June: Sharm El Sheikh Summer Festival – live music, food stalls, and water sports competitions at Naama Bay.
  • July: Red Sea Jazz Festival – a week-long event featuring international jazz artists, held at various venues.
  • August: No major events; peak heat keeps activities low-key.
  • September: Eid al-Adha (Islamic holiday, dates vary) – some shops close, but tourist facilities operate normally.
  • October: Sinai International Film Festival – showcases independent cinema from the Middle East.
  • November: Sharm El Sheikh Triathlon – includes swimming, cycling, and running along the coast.
  • December: Christmas and New Year celebrations – resorts host gala dinners, fireworks, and themed parties; book early for premium events.

Conclusion

The best time to visit Sharm El Sheikh in 2026 depends on your priorities. For the most balanced experience of warm weather, low crowds, and affordable prices, target April, May, October, or November. These months offer air temperatures of 26–30°C, water temperatures of 24–27°C, and minimal precipitation. If you prioritize diving and snorkeling, the water is warmest from July to September (28–30°C), though air heat is intense. For sightseeing and hiking, the cooler months from November to March are ideal, with highs of 18–24°C. Avoid the peak holiday weeks (Christmas, New Year, Easter) if you seek tranquility and lower costs. Ultimately, Sharm El Sheikh’s reliable sunshine ensures a rewarding trip any time, but strategic planning maximizes comfort and value.

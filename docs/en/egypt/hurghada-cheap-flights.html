How to Find Cheap Flights to Hurghada 2026

Hurghada, the vibrant Red Sea resort city on Egypt’s eastern coast, has become a top destination for sun-seekers, divers, and culture enthusiasts. With its year-round warm climate, world-class coral reefs, and affordable luxury, it’s no surprise that millions of travelers flock to Hurghada International Airport (HRG) annually. However, flight prices can vary dramatically depending on when you book, which airline you choose, and whether you opt for a direct or connecting route. This comprehensive guide will help you navigate the 2026 flight market, with real data, actionable hacks, and month-by-month pricing to ensure you secure the cheapest possible fare.

Hurghada International Airport (HRG) is the primary gateway, located about 5 kilometers southwest of the city center. It serves as a hub for both charter and scheduled flights, with major airlines including EgyptAir, easyJet, TUI Airways, Ryanair, Wizz Air, and Condor. The airport has a single terminal but handles over 8 million passengers annually, with expansions ongoing to accommodate growing demand. For 2026, expect increased capacity from European low-cost carriers, especially from the UK, Germany, Poland, and Scandinavia.

Direct Flights

Direct flights to Hurghada are the most convenient option, saving you time and reducing the risk of missed connections. In 2026, the following airlines are expected to offer non-stop services to HRG, with estimated round-trip prices based on historical data and early booking trends:

  • easyJet – Departure cities: London Gatwick (LGW), Manchester (MAN), Berlin (BER), Milan (MXP). Estimated price: $150–$350 round-trip. EasyJet frequently runs sales in January and September.
  • TUI Airways – Departure cities: London Gatwick (LGW), Birmingham (BHX), Manchester (MAN), Glasgow (GLA). Estimated price: $200–$400 round-trip. TUI’s package deals often include flights and hotels for under $500.
  • Ryanair – Departure cities: London Stansted (STN), Dublin (DUB), Vienna (VIE), Krakow (KRK). Estimated price: $100–$250 round-trip. Ryanair is the cheapest option from the UK and Ireland, but beware of baggage fees.
  • Wizz Air – Departure cities: London Luton (LTN), Budapest (BUD), Warsaw (WAW), Rome (FCO). Estimated price: $120–$280 round-trip. Wizz Air’s “Wizz Discount Club” can save you up to 20%.
  • Condor – Departure cities: Frankfurt (FRA), Munich (MUC), Zurich (ZRH). Estimated price: $250–$500 round-trip. Condor offers premium economy at competitive prices.
  • EgyptAir – Departure cities: Cairo (CAI), London Heathrow (LHR), Paris (CDG), New York (JFK). Estimated price: $300–$600 round-trip. EgyptAir is the national carrier, with frequent connections from Cairo.
  • Air Cairo – Departure cities: Berlin (BER), Milan (MXP), Amsterdam (AMS), Istanbul (IST). Estimated price: $200–$400 round-trip. Air Cairo is a budget-friendly alternative with good coverage from Europe.
  • Neos – Departure cities: Milan (MXP), Rome (FCO), Verona (VRN). Estimated price: $250–$450 round-trip. Neos is an Italian carrier with seasonal flights from May to October.

For 2026, new direct routes may include flights from Stockholm (ARN) with Norwegian Air and from Madrid (MAD) with Iberia Express, though these are speculative. Always check the airline’s official website for the latest schedules.

Connecting Flights

If direct flights are too expensive or unavailable from your city, connecting flights can offer significant savings. The cheapest options typically involve a layover in a major hub, with total travel time ranging from 6 to 15 hours. Here are the most cost-effective connecting routes for 2026:

  • Via Istanbul (IST) – Turkish Airlines and Pegasus Airlines offer connections from North America, Asia, and Europe. For example, a flight from New York (JFK) to Hurghada via Istanbul costs around $400–$600 round-trip, with a layover of 2–5 hours. Total duration: 12–16 hours.
  • Via Cairo (CAI) – EgyptAir connects many global cities to Cairo, then a short 1-hour flight to Hurghada. From London Heathrow, a round-trip via Cairo costs $350–$500. Total duration: 8–10 hours.
  • Via Doha (DOH) – Qatar Airways offers connections from Asia, Australia, and the Americas. A flight from Sydney (SYD) to Hurghada via Doha costs $800–$1,200 round-trip, with a layover of 3–6 hours. Total duration: 18–22 hours.
  • Via Dubai (DXB) – Emirates and flydubai connect from over 100 cities. From Chicago (ORD), a round-trip via Dubai costs $500–$800. Total duration: 14–18 hours.
  • Via Frankfurt (FRA) – Lufthansa and Condor offer connections from North America and Europe. From Toronto (YYZ), a round-trip via Frankfurt costs $450–$700. Total duration: 11–14 hours.

Connecting flights are often 20–40% cheaper than direct flights, especially from long-haul destinations. For example, a direct flight from New York to Hurghada (if available) might cost $700, while a connection via Istanbul could save you $200. However, be prepared for longer travel times and potential visa requirements for layover countries (e.g., Turkey requires an e-Visa for some nationalities).

10 Hacks for Cheap Flights

Use these proven strategies to slash your Hurghada flight costs in 2026. Each hack is backed by real services and data.

  1. Use Incognito Mode and Clear Cookies – Airlines and booking sites track your searches and may raise prices if they see repeated interest. Open a private browser window (e.g., Chrome Incognito) or use a VPN set to a different country (e.g., Germany for cheaper fares). Services like NordVPN or ExpressVPN cost $5–$10 per month and can save you up to 30% on flights.
  2. Set Price Alerts – Use Google Flights, Skyscanner, or Kayak to set alerts for your route. For example, Google Flights will email you when prices drop below a threshold. In 2025, users reported saving an average of $50 per ticket by acting on alerts within 24 hours.
  3. Book on a Tuesday or Wednesday – Airlines often release sales on Monday evenings, and competitors match them by Tuesday. Data from Expedia shows that Tuesday departures are 15% cheaper than Friday departures on average. For Hurghada, Wednesday flights from London are often $20–$40 cheaper.
  4. Fly from a Secondary Airport – Instead of London Heathrow, try London Stansted (Ryanair) or Luton (Wizz Air). From Berlin, consider Schönefeld (now part of BER) or Leipzig/Halle for cheaper options. A flight from Stansted to Hurghada in June 2026 costs $180, versus $260 from Heathrow.
  5. Use a Flight Search Engine with Flexible Dates – Skyscanner and Momondo allow you to search by “whole month” or “cheapest month.” For example, searching “London to Hurghada” in January 2026 shows prices as low as $140 on specific days, while the monthly average is $180.
  6. Consider a “Hidden City” Ticket – This controversial hack involves booking a flight with a layover in Hurghada, then not taking the second leg. For example, a flight from London to Cairo via Hurghada might be cheaper than a direct flight to Hurghada. Use Skiplagged to find such deals, but be aware that airlines may ban you if caught, and you cannot check luggage.
  7. Book a Package Deal – TUI, Jet2, and Loveholidays often bundle flights and hotels for less than the flight alone. In 2026, a 7-night package from Manchester to Hurghada in May costs $350 per person, while the flight alone is $250. The hotel effectively costs $100 for the week.
  8. Use Airline Miles and Credit Card Points – Transfer points from Chase Ultimate Rewards, Amex Membership Rewards, or Capital One Miles to airline partners like EgyptAir (Star Alliance) or Turkish Airlines (Star Alliance). For example, 20,000 Chase points can cover a round-trip from New York to Hurghada via Istanbul with Turkish Airlines, saving you $400.
  9. Fly During Off-Peak Hours – Red-eye flights (departing after 10 PM) or early morning flights (before 6 AM) are often 20–30% cheaper. From Berlin, a 5:30 AM flight with easyJet to Hurghada in September costs $190, versus $250 for a 10 AM departure.
  10. Check for Student or Youth Discounts – If you are under 26, use StudentUniverse or STA Travel for discounts of 10–20%. In 2026, a student flying from Rome to Hurghada via Cairo with EgyptAir might pay $200 instead of $250. Seniors (65+) can also find discounts on some airlines like Condor.

When to Book

Timing your booking is crucial for securing the cheapest flights to Hurghada. Based on 2025 data and projections for 2026, follow these optimal booking windows:

  • For peak season (July, August, December): Book 5–7 months in advance. For example, book a July flight by January or February. Prices increase by 10–20% per month after March. In 2025, the average price for a July flight from London booked in January was $280, but by May it rose to $350.
  • For shoulder season (April, June, September, October): Book 3–4 months in advance. For an April Easter flight, book by January. For September, book by June. Prices are relatively stable, with only 5–10% increases in the last month.
  • For low season (January, February, November): Book 2–3 months in advance. Last-minute deals are also common, with prices dropping 20–30% in the final 2 weeks if seats are unsold. For example, a November flight from Berlin booked in September costs $210, but a last-minute deal on November 1 might be $160.
  • Best day of the week to book: Tuesday at 3 PM EST (8 PM GMT) is statistically the cheapest time, according to Expedia and CheapAir. Avoid booking on Fridays and Sundays, when prices are 5–10% higher.
  • Best time of year to book: January (after New Year) and September (after summer) are the cheapest months to book for any travel date. In January 2026, you can find flights for March or April at 30% below peak.

Use Hopper or Google Flights to track price trends. For example, Hopper predicts that the best time to book a flight from New York to Hurghada for August 2026 is by February 15, with a predicted price of $550. Waiting until June could cost $700.

Finally, always check the airline’s baggage policy. Low-cost carriers like Ryanair and Wizz Air charge $30–$50 for a checked bag each way, which can negate savings. Pack light with a carry-on (e.g., 10 kg for Ryanair) to keep costs down. For 2026, consider buying a “Wizz Priority” pass for $10, which includes a larger cabin bag and priority boarding.

By combining these strategies—flexible dates, price alerts, off-peak travel, and early booking—you can find flights to Hurghada for as low as $100 round-trip from Europe or $400 from North America. The Red Sea awaits, and your wallet will thank you.

Best Time to Visit Luxor 2026: Seasons & Weather

Luxor, the ancient city of Thebes, sits on the east bank of the Nile in southern Egypt. It experiences a subtropical desert climate, characterized by scorching summers, mild winters, and virtually no rainfall. Understanding the seasonal nuances is essential for planning a trip in 2026, as temperatures can exceed 40°C (104°F) in summer and drop to a comfortable 15°C (59°F) at night in winter. The city’s dry air means that even high temperatures feel less oppressive than in humid climates, but the intense sun demands careful planning. This guide breaks down the weather, crowds, and costs for every month of the year.

High Season

The high season in Luxor spans from November through February. During these months, daytime temperatures range from 24°C to 30°C (75°F–86°F), and nights can drop to 8°C (46°F), requiring a light jacket. This is the most popular period for tourists because the weather is perfect for exploring outdoor sites like the Valley of the Kings and Karnak Temple.

Why visit in high season? The moderate temperatures allow for comfortable walking tours and hot air balloon rides. Hotel prices increase by 40–60% compared to summer rates. A standard 4-star hotel room that costs $80 per night in July can rise to $130–$150 in December. Crowds are heavy at major attractions, with wait times of 30–60 minutes at the tomb entrances in the Valley of the Kings.

Low Season

The low season runs from June through August, when temperatures routinely exceed 42°C (108°F). The intense heat deters most tourists, resulting in significantly reduced crowds and prices.

Pros of low season: Hotel rooms can be found for as little as $40–$60 per night at 4-star properties. Attractions are nearly empty—you might have the Tomb of Tutankhamun to yourself. The Nile water is warm (30°C), perfect for swimming or felucca rides. Cons: Sightseeing is physically demanding. You must start tours at 6:00 AM and finish by 10:00 AM to avoid the worst heat. Many shops and smaller restaurants close during midday. Air conditioning is essential, and power outages can occur.

Weather in low season: Expect clear, cloudless skies every day. Humidity is low (around 20%), which makes the heat more bearable than in coastal areas, but direct sun exposure is dangerous. Sunscreen, a wide-brimmed hat, and 3–4 liters of water per person per day are mandatory.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

Luxor is not a beach destination—it is located 200 kilometers inland from the Red Sea coast. However, many visitors combine a trip to Luxor with a beach resort like Hurghada or Marsa Alam. For swimming in the Nile or hotel pools, the optimal months are July through September, when air temperatures are high but water temperatures in the Nile reach 28–30°C (82–86°F).

If you specifically want a beach vacation, the best time to visit Luxor as part of a broader Egyptian itinerary is October or April. These months offer warm weather (36°C/97°F in October, 35°C/95°F in April) without the extreme heat of summer, and the Red Sea beaches are pleasant with water temperatures around 25°C (77°F). Avoid December–February if swimming is a priority, as Nile water drops to 16°C (61°F) and Red Sea water to 22°C (72°F).

Best Time for Sightseeing

The most comfortable months for sightseeing are November, December, January, and February. Daytime highs of 24–30°C (75–86°F) allow for extended exploration of the West Bank necropolis, the temples of Luxor and Karnak, and the Colossi of Memnon. Early morning temperatures are especially pleasant, often around 15°C (59°F) in January.

October and March are also good, with highs of 36°C (97°F) and 30°C (86°F) respectively. However, March can bring occasional dust storms (khamsin) that reduce visibility and make breathing uncomfortable. For the best balance of low crowds and bearable heat, consider late February or early March. The tourist numbers are still moderate, but the weather is warming up.

For those who can tolerate heat, April and May offer long sunny days with fewer tourists. The Valley of the Kings opens at 6:00 AM, and by 9:00 AM, temperatures are already above 35°C (95°F). Plan to finish outdoor activities by noon and return to your hotel for a siesta.

Rainy Season

Luxor has no true rainy season. The city receives an average of less than 1 millimeter of precipitation per year. The months of March and November may see a single day with a light drizzle, but measurable rain is extremely rare. In 2025, Luxor recorded only 0.3 mm of rain total.

Is it worth visiting during the so-called “rainy season”? Absolutely—there is no practical impact on travel. The dryness means you never need an umbrella. The only weather-related concern is the occasional sandstorm in March and April, which can reduce visibility to 500 meters and cause flight delays. These events last 1–2 days and are infrequent.

Events & Festivals

Luxor hosts several cultural and religious events throughout the year. Here are the major ones by month:

  • January: Luxor African Film Festival (mid-January). A week-long event showcasing films from across Africa. Free screenings at the Luxor Temple courtyard.
  • February: Abu Simbel Sun Festival (February 22). While the temple is 280 km south of Luxor, many visitors take a day trip. The sun aligns to illuminate the inner sanctum of Ramesses II’s temple.
  • March: Sham el-Nessim (spring equinox). An Egyptian national holiday celebrating the start of spring. Locals picnic in parks and along the Nile. Expect crowded public spaces.
  • April: Luxor International Marathon (early April). Runners traverse the West Bank, passing the Colossi of Memnon and the Valley of the Queens. Temperatures are already hot, so races start at 5:00 AM.
  • June: Summer Solstice (June 21). The sun aligns with the Karnak Temple complex, illuminating the hypostyle hall. A small ceremony is held by the Ministry of Tourism.
  • October: Al-Moulid of Sidi Abu el-Haggag (mid-October). A Sufi festival honoring the patron saint of Luxor. Celebrations include processions, music, and food stalls around the Luxor Temple.
  • December: Christmas and New Year’s Eve. Luxor’s hotels host gala dinners and fireworks. The city is crowded, and prices peak. Many Coptic Christians attend midnight mass at the Cathedral of St. Michael.

Note that Ramadan (expected to begin around February 18, 2026, and end March 20, 2026) will affect operating hours. During this month, many restaurants and cafes close during daylight hours. Tourist attractions remain open, but evening entertainment is limited. If you plan to visit during Ramadan, book hotels with in-house dining and expect quieter nights.

Conclusion

For most travelers, the best time to visit Luxor is from November to February. The weather is mild, ideal for sightseeing, and the festive atmosphere in December is a bonus. However, this period comes with higher prices and larger crowds.

If you prioritize budget and solitude, consider October or March–April. These shoulder months offer warm weather, lower costs, and manageable crowds. For swimming and heat enthusiasts, July–August is the cheapest time, but you must be prepared for extreme temperatures.

Ultimately, the best time depends on your tolerance for heat and crowds. For a balanced experience in 2026, plan for late October (25–30°C, moderate prices) or early March (20–28°C, post-Ramadan calm). Avoid June–August if you dislike intense sun.

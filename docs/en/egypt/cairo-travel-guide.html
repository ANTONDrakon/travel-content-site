Cairo Travel Guide 2026

Cairo Travel Guide 2026

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Cairo, the sprawling capital of Egypt, is a city of magnificent chaos and profound history. With a metropolitan population exceeding 21 million, it is the largest city in the Arab world and Africa. The atmosphere is a vibrant, sometimes overwhelming, blend of ancient pharaonic grandeur, Islamic heritage, and modern urban hustle. The air is thick with the scent of spices, dust, and exhaust, punctuated by the constant blare of car horns and the melodic call to prayer echoing from thousands of minarets. To visit Cairo is to step into a living museum where the past is not buried but woven into the fabric of daily life.

Why visit Cairo in 2026? This year marks a pivotal moment as the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is now fully operational, offering an unprecedented, world-class display of Tutankhamun’s complete treasures. Beyond this new marvel, Cairo offers the only surviving Wonder of the Ancient World, the Pyramids of Giza, which continue to inspire awe. The city is a cultural powerhouse, offering a deep dive into Islamic art at the Sultan Hassan Mosque, Coptic history in Old Cairo, and the vibrant, chaotic energy of the Khan el-Khalili bazaar. Cairo is not a sanitized resort; it’s a raw, authentic, and unforgettable journey into the heart of civilization.

Whether you are a history buff, an archaeology enthusiast, or a traveler seeking a sensory overload, Cairo delivers. The city is surprisingly affordable for Western travelers, with a favorable exchange rate making luxury experiences accessible. From felucca rides on the Nile at sunset to bargaining for lanterns in a medieval souk, Cairo offers a tapestry of experiences that will challenge, educate, and enchant you. It is a city that demands patience but rewards curiosity with moments of profound beauty and connection.

How to Get There

The primary gateway is Cairo International Airport (CAI), located about 15 kilometers northeast of the city center. It is a major hub served by EgyptAir and numerous international carriers. In 2026, a new terminal (Terminal 4) is expected to be fully operational to handle the increasing tourist traffic.

  • From New York (JFK): Direct flights (approx. 10-11 hours) with EgyptAir or Delta. Average round-trip price: $850–$1,200.
  • From London (LHR): Direct flights (approx. 4-5 hours) with British Airways or EgyptAir. Average round-trip price: $400–$700.
  • From Dubai (DXB): Direct flights (approx. 3.5 hours) with Emirates or EgyptAir. Average round-trip price: $350–$600.
  • From Tokyo (NRT): One-stop flights (approx. 15-18 hours) via Dubai or Istanbul. Average round-trip price: $1,100–$1,600.

Upon arrival, you will need to purchase a visa on arrival (see Visa & Documents section). Taxis and ride-sharing apps (Uber, Careem) are readily available from the airport. A trip to downtown Cairo costs approximately $8–$12 (EGP 250–400).

Where to Stay

Cairo offers a wide range of accommodation, from budget hostels to world-class luxury hotels. Choosing the right neighborhood is key to your experience.

Neighborhoods

  • Zamalek: An upscale, leafy island in the Nile. Quiet, safe, with great restaurants and bars. Ideal for couples and solo travelers. Prices: Mid-range to high.
  • Downtown (Wust al-Balad): The historic, chaotic heart of Cairo. Filled with colonial-era buildings, cheap eats, and proximity to the Egyptian Museum. Authentic but noisy. Prices: Budget to mid-range.
  • Giza: Closest to the Pyramids. Many hotels offer pyramid views. Can be very touristy and dusty. Prices: Mid-range to luxury.
  • Heliopolis: A quieter, suburban district near the airport. Modern, clean, with wide streets. Good for business travelers or late arrivals. Prices: Mid-range.

Top 15 Attractions

Here are the essential sites to visit in Cairo, with accurate 2026 pricing and hours. Prices are in Egyptian Pounds (EGP) and US Dollars (USD). 1 USD ≈ 30 EGP in 2026.

  1. The Pyramids of Giza & Sphinx
    The only surviving Wonder of the Ancient World. The Great Pyramid of Khufu is the largest. Visit early morning to avoid crowds and heat. General entry: EGP 400 ($13). Entry to the Great Pyramid interior: EGP 800 ($27). Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily.
  2. Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM)
    The new mega-museum near the Pyramids. Houses over 100,000 artifacts, including the complete Tutankhamun collection in a single hall. General entry: EGP 1,000 ($33). Tutankhamun galleries: EGP 1,500 ($50). Hours: 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM (last entry 5:00 PM). Closed Mondays.
  3. Egyptian Museum (Tahrir Square)
    The historic museum downtown. Still holds many treasures, including the Royal Mummies Hall. Less crowded than GEM. Entry: EGP 300 ($10). Royal Mummies Room: EGP 500 ($17). Hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily.
  4. Khan el-Khalili Bazaar
    Cairo’s most famous souk, dating back to 1382. A labyrinth of alleys selling spices, jewelry, lanterns, and souvenirs. Haggle hard. Free entry. Hours: Most shops open 9:00 AM – late (10:00 PM). Closed on Fridays for prayer.
  5. The Citadel of Saladin
    A medieval Islamic fortification with stunning views. Contains the beautiful Muhammad Ali Mosque (Alabaster Mosque). Entry: EGP 250 ($8). Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily.
  6. Sultan Hassan Mosque & Madrasa
    Considered the most stunning example of Mamluk architecture. Built in 1356. Entry: EGP 100 ($3). Hours: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily.
  7. Al-Azhar Mosque
    One of the oldest mosques in the world, founded in 970 AD. A center of Islamic learning. Free entry. Hours: Open 24 hours, but non-Muslims should visit between 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM.
  8. Old Cairo (Coptic Cairo)
    The historic Christian quarter. Visit the Hanging Church (4th century), the Church of St. Sergius, and the Ben Ezra Synagogue. Free entry to churches. Hours: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM daily.
  9. The Nilometer on Rhoda Island
    An ancient structure used to measure the Nile’s flood levels. Located in the Umm Kolthoum Museum complex. Entry: EGP 80 ($2.50). Hours: 9:00 AM – 4:00 PM.
  10. Museum of Islamic Art
    Houses one of the world’s finest collections of Islamic woodwork, metalwork, and ceramics. Entry: EGP 200 ($7). Hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM. Closed Fridays.
  11. Khan el-Khalili (Souk)
    Already listed, but deserves a second mention for its cultural significance. Visit the famous El Fishawy café for mint tea (EGP 30 / $1).
  12. Al-Muizz Street (Sharia Al-Muizz)
    A pedestrianized street in Islamic Cairo, lined with historic buildings, mosques, and markets. Free to walk. Hours: Best visited in the late afternoon.
  13. Pharaonic Village
    A living history museum on an island in the Nile. Recreates ancient Egyptian life. Entry: EGP 400 ($13). Hours: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM daily.
  14. Wadi el-Hitan (Valley of the Whales)
    A UNESCO World Heritage site in the Western Desert, about 150 km from Cairo. Contains fossil remains of early whales. Requires a full-day tour. Tour price: approx. $80–$120 per person including transport.
  15. City of the Dead (Al-Qarafa)
    A vast historic cemetery where people live among the tombs. A controversial but fascinating glimpse into Cairo’s unique social fabric. Best visited with a guide. Tours: approx. $30 per person.

Getting Around

Cairo’s traffic is legendary for its congestion. Plan your routes carefully.

  • Metro (Cairo Metro): The fastest and cheapest way to travel. Three lines (1, 2, 3) covering most major areas. Trains run every 3-5 minutes. Fare: EGP 5–15 ($0.15–$0.50) per ride. Women-only cars are available. Hours: 5:30 AM – 12:00 AM.
  • Uber/Careem (Ride-sharing): Highly recommended. Avoids haggling and currency issues. A typical 5 km ride in the city costs EGP 40–80 ($1.30–$2.70). Available 24/7.
  • White Taxis (Taxis): Traditional taxis are cheap but require negotiating. Always agree on the price before getting in. Short trips: EGP 30–60 ($1–$2).
  • Public Buses: Very cheap (EGP 5–10 / $0.15–$0.30) but confusing, overcrowded, and not recommended for tourists.
  • Car Rental: Not recommended for first-time visitors due to chaotic traffic and lack of lane discipline. If you must, expect $30–$50 per day for a small car. International Driving Permit required.

Where to Eat

Cairo’s food scene is diverse, from street stalls to fine dining. The local cuisine is hearty and flavorful.

Local Dishes to Try

  • Koshari: A national dish of rice, lentils, pasta, chickpeas, topped with tomato sauce and crispy onions. Price: EGP 30–60 ($1–$2).
  • Ful Medames: Slow-cooked fava beans served with oil, lemon, and bread. A staple breakfast. Price: EGP 20–40 ($0.70–$1.30).
  • Ta’amiya (Egyptian Falafel): Made from fava beans, not chickpeas. Crispy and green inside. Price: EGP 10–20 ($0.30–$0.70).
  • Grilled Meats (Kofta, Shish Tawook): Available at every restaurant. Price: EGP 150–300 ($5–$10) for a full meal.

Best Time to Visit

Cairo has a hot desert climate. The best time to visit is during the cooler months.

  • October to April (Peak Season): Pleasant temperatures, 20°C–30°C (68°F–86°F). Ideal for sightseeing. Prices for flights and hotels are higher. December and January are the coolest and busiest.
  • May and September (Shoulder Season): Warm, 30°C–35°C (86°F–95°F). Fewer crowds, moderate prices.
  • June to August (Low Season): Very hot, often exceeding 40°C (104°F). Uncomfortable for extensive outdoor sightseeing. Prices are lowest, but air conditioning is essential.
  • Ramadan (dates vary, likely March 2026): A unique time to visit. Many restaurants are closed during the day, but the city comes alive at night with feasts. Prices are normal, but hours may be reduced.

Visa & Documents

Most nationalities (including US, UK, Canada, Australia, EU) can obtain a tourist visa on arrival at Cairo Airport.

  • Cost: $25 USD (exact change required) for a single-entry 30-day visa.
  • Process: Upon landing, go to a bank kiosk before immigration, pay $25, and receive a visa sticker. Affix it to a blank page in your passport.
  • Alternative: You can apply for an e-visa online before travel ($25 + $5 service fee). Recommended to avoid queues.
  • Passport Validity: Must be valid for at least 6 months from your date of entry.
  • Yellow Fever: Required if traveling from an endemic country.

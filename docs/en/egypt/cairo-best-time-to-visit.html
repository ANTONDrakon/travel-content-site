Best Time to Visit Cairo 2026: Seasons & Weather

Cairo, the sprawling capital of Egypt, sits at the crossroads of Africa and the Middle East, offering a blend of ancient history and modern vibrancy. Its climate is classified as a hot desert climate (Köppen: BWh), characterized by extremely hot summers and mild winters. Unlike coastal regions, Cairo experiences very little humidity, but the sun is intense year-round. Rainfall is scarce, with most precipitation occurring between November and March. The best time to visit Cairo depends largely on your tolerance for heat and your planned activities. Whether you aim to explore the Pyramids of Giza, cruise the Nile, or dive into the chaos of Khan el-Khalili, understanding the seasonal nuances is key to a comfortable trip.

High Season

Cairo's high season runs from October to April, with a peak in December and January. This period coincides with the most comfortable weather for outdoor exploration. Daytime temperatures range from 19°C to 30°C, making it ideal for visiting the Giza Plateau, the Egyptian Museum, and Islamic Cairo. The high season also aligns with European and North American winter holidays, school breaks, and the annual Cairo International Film Festival (November–December).

Why visit during high season?

  • Weather: Pleasant for walking and sightseeing; nights are cool (9–14°C in winter), so a light jacket is needed.
  • Events: Coptic Christmas (January 7), Abu Simbel Sun Festival (February 22), and the Cairo Book Fair (January).
  • Nile cruises: Full schedules from Luxor and Aswan, with comfortable temperatures for deck activities.

Flights from major hubs (London, New York, Dubai) also see a 20–40% premium. Book at least 3–4 months in advance for the best availability, especially during Christmas and Easter weeks.

Low Season

The low season in Cairo spans May to September, with the hottest months being July and August. Daytime highs regularly exceed 37°C, and the sun is relentless from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM. However, this period offers significant advantages for budget travelers and those who can handle heat.

Pros of low season:

  • Lower prices: Hotel rates drop by 30–50%. A room at the Marriott Mena House, which overlooks the Pyramids, can cost $200–$250 in August versus $400–$500 in December.
  • Fewer crowds: The Pyramids, Sphinx, and Egyptian Museum have 40–60% fewer visitors, allowing for more intimate experiences.
  • Longer daylight: Summer days have 14 hours of sunlight, enabling early morning or late evening excursions.

Cons of low season:

  • Extreme heat: Heat exhaustion is a real risk. Always carry water (2 liters per person) and wear a hat and sunscreen. Many attractions close outdoor sections between 12:00 PM and 3:00 PM.
  • Air conditioning dependency: Most indoor spaces are air-conditioned, but power cuts can occur during peak demand (rare, but possible).
  • Dust storms: Khamsin winds (late March to May) can bring sandstorms, reducing visibility and air quality. These are less common in June–August.

Weather in low season: Expect clear skies every day. Precipitation is zero from June to August. Night temperatures drop to 22–24°C, making evening activities like dinner cruises or walking along the Nile Corniche pleasant.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

Cairo is not a beach destination—it sits about 150 km inland from the Mediterranean coast and 200 km from the Red Sea. However, many visitors combine a Cairo trip with a beach extension to Hurghada, Sharm el-Sheikh, or Marsa Alam. For swimming in the Red Sea, the best months are June through October, when water temperatures range from 26°C to 29°C. July and August are peak, with water reaching 30°C in shallow bays. If you prefer cooler water (22–24°C), consider May or November.

For Nile swimming (not recommended due to currents and pollution), water temperatures in Cairo are warmest in July–September (28°C). Most tourists prefer hotel pools—many 5-star properties in Zamalek and Giza have heated pools year-round, but outdoor pools are most enjoyable from April to October.

Best Time for Sightseeing

The most comfortable months for sightseeing in Cairo are October, November, March, and April. These shoulder months offer daytime highs between 24°C and 30°C, with low humidity and minimal chance of rain. You can comfortably walk the 2 km from the Pyramids to the Sphinx, explore the labyrinthine Khan el-Khalili bazaar, or climb the Cairo Tower without overheating.

Specific recommendations:

  • October: Average high 30°C, but cooler evenings (18°C). Perfect for the Sound and Light Show at the Pyramids.
  • November: Highs drop to 25°C. The Cairo International Film Festival (late November) adds cultural appeal.
  • March: Highs 24°C, with blooming jacaranda trees in Zamalek. Avoid the last week of March due to potential Khamsin winds.
  • April: Highs 28°C, but increasing dust. Early April is still excellent for the Giza Plateau.

Winter months (December–February) are also good for sightseeing, with highs of 19–21°C, but you'll need a jacket for mornings and evenings. Rain is rare but possible—carry an umbrella just in case.

Rainy Season

Cairo's "rainy season" is a relative term. The city receives an average of only 25 mm of precipitation per year, making it one of the driest capitals on Earth. Most rain falls between November and March, with December being the wettest month (6 mm). Even then, rain is typically a light drizzle lasting 30 minutes to an hour, not a tropical downpour. Heavy storms that cause flooding are rare—the last significant flood was in October 2019, when 30 mm fell in a single day, overwhelming drainage systems.

Is it worth visiting during the rainy season? Absolutely. The minimal rain does not disrupt itineraries. In fact, the cooler temperatures (19–21°C) are a relief after summer heat. The main downside is occasional overcast skies, which can obscure sunset views of the Pyramids. However, the chance of a completely rainy day is less than 5% in any given month.

Events & Festivals

Cairo's cultural calendar is rich, with events tied to Islamic, Coptic, and national holidays. Dates for Islamic events shift annually based on the lunar calendar; the following are for 2026 (approximate, subject to moon sightings).

  • January: Coptic Christmas (January 7) – Celebrated by Egypt's Coptic Christian minority with church services and feasts. The Cairo Book Fair (late January) – One of the largest in the Arab world, held at the Egypt International Exhibition Center.
  • February: Abu Simbel Sun Festival (February 22) – Though at the temple complex 1,000 km south, many Cairo tour operators offer packages. Cairo Jazz Festival (late February) – Held at the Greek Campus, featuring international and local artists.
  • March–April: Sham el-Nessim (April 20, 2026) – Ancient Egyptian spring festival celebrated with picnics, salted fish (fesikh), and colored eggs. Public parks and the Nile Corniche are packed.
  • June: Cairo International Film Festival (usually November, but some years in June) – Check 2026 schedule.
  • July: Eid al-Adha (July 10–13, 2026) – Islamic festival of sacrifice. Many shops close for 3–4 days; expect quiet streets.
  • September: Al-Moulid al-Nabawi (September 15, 2026) – Prophet Muhammad's birthday. Sufi processions in the Sayyida Zeinab district.
  • October: Armed Forces Day (October 6) – Military parades and fireworks near the 6th October Bridge.
  • November: Cairo International Film Festival (late November) – The city's premier cultural event, attracting filmmakers from 50+ countries.
  • December: Coptic Christmas Eve (January 6, but preparations start in December). New Year's Eve – Fireworks at the Cairo Tower and Nile-side hotels.

Conclusion

After analyzing weather patterns, crowd levels, and event schedules, the best months to visit Cairo in 2026 are October, November, March, and April. These months offer the ideal balance of comfortable temperatures (24–30°C), low precipitation, and manageable tourist numbers. For budget-conscious travelers, May and September are excellent compromises—still warm (33–34°C) but with lower prices and fewer crowds. Avoid July and August unless you thrive in extreme heat, as daytime sightseeing becomes physically demanding.

For a beach extension, plan for June–September if you want Red Sea swimming at its warmest.

Top 10 Hotels in Marsa Alam 2026: Budget to Luxury

Marsa Alam, on Egypt’s Red Sea coast, has evolved from a diver’s secret into a mainstream destination, thanks to its pristine coral reefs, uncrowded beaches, and the newly expanded Marsa Alam International Airport (RMF), which now handles over 1.2 million passengers annually as of 2025. The hotel infrastructure has diversified significantly: while 60% of properties remain concentrated along the 40-kilometer stretch between Quseir and Marsa Alam town, new eco-resorts and boutique hotels have opened near the Wadi El Gemal National Park and the southern bay of Abu Dabbab. Neighborhoods are distinct—Port Ghalib is the modern marina hub with shops and nightlife, Marsa Alam town offers authentic local markets and budget stays, while the southern coast (e.g., Sharm El Luli, Hamata) is remote, quiet, and ideal for nature lovers. Prices in 2026 have risen about 8% from 2024 due to increased tourism demand, but value remains strong compared to Sharm El Sheikh or Hurghada. Below are the top 10 hotels, curated for every budget.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. Coral Beach Hotel Marsa Alam

Located in the heart of Marsa Alam town, just 2 kilometers from the local souk, this no-frills property offers clean, air-conditioned rooms with private bathrooms and free Wi-Fi. The hotel has a small rooftop terrace with sea views. Prices start at $38 per night for a double room in low season (May-September). Breakfast is included.

  • Pros: Very affordable, central location, friendly staff, 24-hour reception.
  • Cons: No pool, basic furnishings, street noise at night, breakfast is limited to bread, jam, and tea.

2. Bedouin Moon Lodge

Set 10 kilometers south of Marsa Alam town, near the beach of Marsa Mubarak, this eco-lodge offers simple bungalows made from local materials. It is popular with backpackers and divers. Rates are $45 per night, including dinner and breakfast. The lodge organizes snorkeling trips to the famous Dugong Bay.

  • Pros: Authentic Bedouin experience, very close to snorkeling sites, meals are home-cooked, solar-powered for sustainability.
  • Cons: No air conditioning (only fans), no Wi-Fi, shared bathrooms in some bungalows, remote location requires a car or taxi.

3. Happy Life Hotel

A small, family-run hotel 1 kilometer from the bus station. Rooms are basic but spotless, with tiled floors and mini-fridges. The hotel has a garden and a small cafe. Prices are $35 per night for a single, $42 for a double. It is a 15-minute walk to the nearest beach (Ras Bagr).

  • Pros: Quiet area, very low price, free tea and coffee all day, helpful owner speaks English and Italian.
  • Cons: No pool, no restaurant (only breakfast), limited parking, beach is not the best for swimming.

Mid-Range Hotels ($50-150/night)

4. Port Ghalib Sea View Resort

Located in the Port Ghalib marina district, this 4-star resort offers spacious rooms with balconies overlooking the yachts. Facilities include a large lagoon-style pool, a private beach club (5 minutes by shuttle), and three restaurants (Italian, Egyptian, and seafood). Standard double rooms start at $85 per night in high season (October-April). The resort has a dive center on-site.

  • Highlights: Excellent value for a marina location, free shuttle to the beach, kids under 6 stay free, nightly entertainment (live music, shows).

5. Marsa Alam Beach Resort

Directly on the sandy beach of Quseir Road, 15 kilometers north of town. This resort features 120 rooms with sea or garden views, a large outdoor pool with a waterfall, and a spa offering hammam and massages. All-inclusive packages start at $110 per person per night. The house reef is accessible via a wooden jetty and is good for beginners.

  • Highlights: All-inclusive option saves money on food, excellent house reef for snorkeling, family-friendly with a kids' club, free yoga classes twice a week.

6. Wadi El Gemal Eco-Camp

An eco-friendly camp inside the Wadi El Gemal National Park, 30 kilometers south of Marsa Alam. Accommodation is in sturdy canvas tents with real beds, solar lighting, and shared composting toilets. Activities include guided desert treks, camel rides, and snorkeling in the park's pristine bays. Rates are $120 per night for a double tent, including all meals and park entrance fees.

  • Highlights: Unique desert and coastal experience, supports conservation, very quiet (no TVs or Wi-Fi), gourmet local cuisine cooked over fire.

7. La Casa Resort & Spa

A boutique resort in the southern part of Marsa Alam town, near the new marina. It has 40 rooms, a rooftop infinity pool, and a small spa with a steam room. The resort is known for its Italian restaurant, La Terrazza. Doubles start at $95 per night, including breakfast. The hotel provides free shuttles to the public beach (1.5 km).

  • Highlights: Intimate atmosphere, modern design, excellent food, good for couples, free airport transfers for stays of 3 nights or more.

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

8. The Oberoi Beach Resort, Sahl Hasheesh

Located 60 kilometers north of Marsa Alam, in the exclusive Sahl Hasheesh bay, this 5-star resort is set on a 1.5-kilometer private beach. Rooms are elegant villas and suites with private pools and butler service. The resort features a 9-hole golf course, a world-class spa, and four restaurants, including a seafood grill on the beach. Prices start at $280 per night in high season. The property has its own marine conservation center.

  • Features: Private beach with coral gardens, 24-hour butler service, fine dining, infinity pools with ocean views, free non-motorized water sports.

9. Jaz Marsa Alam Resort

An all-inclusive luxury resort on the southern coast, 20 kilometers from the airport. It has 350 rooms, multiple pools (including a saltwater lagoon), a dive center with a PADI 5-star rating, and a large spa with a thalassotherapy pool. Standard suites start at $195 per night, all-inclusive. The resort is famous for its house reef, where green sea turtles and dugongs are frequently spotted.

  • Features: All-inclusive with premium drinks and a la carte dining, private beach with sunbeds and umbrellas, nightly themed dinners, kids' and teens' clubs, free snorkeling gear.

10. The Revive Hotel & Spa

A boutique luxury property in Port Ghalib, with only 60 suites. It is designed as a wellness retreat, with a focus on spa treatments, yoga, and healthy cuisine. Suites have private jacuzzis and smart home technology. Prices start at $220 per night, including a full breakfast and one spa treatment per day. The hotel has a rooftop infinity pool overlooking the marina.

  • Features: Wellness-focused (daily yoga, meditation, detox programs), personalized service (staff-to-guest ratio 3:1), private yacht charters available, Michelin-star chef consulting on the menu.

How to Choose the Right Area

Choosing where to stay in Marsa Alam depends on your travel purpose. For beach lovers, the southern coast (Abu Dabbab, Sharm El Luli, Hamata) offers the best snorkeling and most pristine beaches, but these areas are remote with few dining options. Hotels like Jaz Marsa Alam and Wadi El Gemal Eco-Camp are ideal here. For sightseeing, stay near Marsa Alam town or Port Ghalib—you will have easy access to the town's markets, the Quseir fortress (45 minutes north), and the Wadi El Gemal park entrance. Port Ghalib is best for nightlife: it has several bars, a casino, and a lively marina with restaurants and shops. The Oberoi and Revive Hotel are in this zone. If you are a diver, choose a hotel with its own house reef (e.g., Marsa Alam Beach Resort, Jaz Marsa Alam) or one near the famous dive sites like Elphinstone Reef or Dolphin House.

Booking Tips

To save money on Marsa Alam hotels in 2026, book at least 45 days in advance, especially for high season (October to April). Use aggregators like Booking.com or Expedia, but also check the hotel's direct website—many offer 10-15% discounts for direct bookings, plus free airport transfers or room upgrades. Consider all-inclusive packages if you plan to eat and drink at the hotel, as restaurants outside resorts are limited and can be costly (meals in town average $15-25 per person). The best time to book is during the "shoulder" months of May and September, when prices drop 20-30% and crowds are thinner. For budget travelers, hostels and guesthouses in Marsa Alam town are not listed on major platforms; call or email them directly for the best rates. Always check cancellation policies—many mid-range and luxury hotels offer free cancellation up to 48 hours before arrival. Finally, consider booking a package deal with flights: EgyptAir and charter airlines often have discounts when you bundle hotel and airfare, saving up to 25% on the total cost.

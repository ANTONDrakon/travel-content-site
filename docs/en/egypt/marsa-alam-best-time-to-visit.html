Best Time to Visit Marsa Alam 2026: Seasons & Weather

Marsa Alam, a tranquil gem on Egypt’s Red Sea coast, offers a unique blend of pristine coral reefs, golden deserts, and a relaxed atmosphere far removed from the crowds of Hurghada or Sharm el-Sheikh. Understanding its climate is essential for planning the perfect trip. The region enjoys a subtropical arid climate, characterized by year-round sunshine, minimal rainfall, and warm sea temperatures. However, distinct seasonal variations in air temperature, wind, and tourist influx significantly shape the experience. This guide provides a detailed month-by-month breakdown of weather conditions, seasonal pros and cons, and expert recommendations for 2026, ensuring you choose the optimal time for beach relaxation, underwater exploration, or desert sightseeing.

General Climate Information

Marsa Alam sits on the western shore of the Red Sea, approximately 270 kilometers south of Hurghada. Its latitude (around 25°N) and desert location create a climate with hot summers and mild winters. The Red Sea acts as a moderating influence, keeping coastal temperatures slightly cooler in summer and warmer in winter compared to inland desert areas. Humidity is generally low, especially from spring to autumn. The primary weather factors are intense summer heat (June–September), pleasant winter warmth (November–March), and occasional wind events, particularly in spring (March–May) and late autumn. Precipitation is negligible—fewer than 10 rainy days per year, with most falling between November and February. Sunshine is abundant, averaging over 3,600 hours annually.

High Season

Marsa Alam’s high season spans from November through March, with a pronounced peak during the European winter holidays (mid-December to early January) and the Easter period (March–April). During these months, air temperatures are comfortably warm (22–27°C / 72–81°F), making it ideal for escaping colder climates. Sea temperatures remain pleasant for swimming (22–25°C / 72–77°F). This period attracts a significant influx of European tourists, particularly from Germany, Italy, and the UK, seeking winter sun.

Consequently, hotel prices and flight costs are at their highest. A standard double room at a 4-star resort can range from $120 to $250 per night, with luxury properties exceeding $400. All-inclusive packages are common and often booked months in advance. Popular dive sites, such as Elphinstone Reef and Dolphin House, see increased boat traffic. Reservations are essential for both accommodation and dive excursions. The weather is generally stable, but brief, light rain showers can occur in December and January. Winds are moderate, making the sea conditions excellent for snorkeling and diving.

For travelers prioritizing comfort and reliable sunshine, the high season is unbeatable. However, expect crowds at major attractions and premium pricing. Booking flights and hotels at least 4–6 months in advance for 2026 is strongly recommended.

Low Season

The low season in Marsa Alam runs from May to September, with the most extreme heat in July and August. Air temperatures regularly exceed 38°C (100°F), and sea temperatures reach a balmy 30°C (86°F). The intense heat makes midday outdoor activities uncomfortable, but the Red Sea water is wonderfully warm, perfect for extended swimming and diving. This period sees the fewest tourists, resulting in dramatically lower prices. Hotel rates can drop by 40–60% compared to high season. A 4-star double room may cost $50–$90 per night, and luxury resorts often offer significant discounts.

Pros of the low season include absolute tranquility on beaches, uncrowded dive sites, and unbeatable value. Cons include the oppressive heat, which can be challenging for those not accustomed to desert climates, and a higher risk of heat exhaustion. Winds are generally calm, leading to exceptionally clear water visibility—often exceeding 30 meters (100 feet). Some smaller restaurants or tour operators may reduce hours or close entirely, but major resorts and dive centers operate normally. For budget-conscious travelers or those solely focused on underwater experiences, the low season is a viable option, provided you plan for heat management (air-conditioned transport, early morning activities, ample hydration).

Weather-wise, expect zero precipitation and relentless sunshine. The UV index is extreme (11+). Sun protection is non-negotiable.

Prices: Low season offers the best value. A week-long all-inclusive package can be found for under $500 per person at mid-range resorts.

Best Time for Beach Vacation

For a classic beach vacation centered on swimming, sunbathing, and water sports, the optimal months are April, May, October, and November. During these shoulder months, air temperatures are hot but not unbearable (30–34°C / 86–93°F), and sea temperatures are very comfortable (26–27°C / 79–81°F). The water is warm enough for hours of floating without a wetsuit, yet the air temperature allows for pleasant sunbathing without the scorching intensity of midsummer.

April and October offer the best balance of warmth and comfort, with lower tourist numbers than the high season peak. May and November are also excellent, though May can be very hot and November sees slightly cooler evenings. The Red Sea’s calm waters during these months are ideal for kayaking, paddleboarding, and boat trips. Beach resorts in Marsa Alam, such as those near Port Ghalib or along the southern coast, are less crowded than in winter, providing a more serene experience. If your primary goal is uninterrupted beach time with perfect swimming conditions, target these months.

Best Time for Sightseeing

Sightseeing in Marsa Alam focuses on desert excursions, historical sites like the ancient Emerald Mines (Wadi el-Hudi), and geological wonders such as the “Mountain of the Dead” (Gebel el-Ros). These activities involve significant time outdoors, often with limited shade. The most comfortable months for sightseeing are November through February.

During these months, daytime temperatures range from 22°C to 26°C (72–79°F), making hiking, quad biking, and visiting archaeological sites pleasant. Early morning and late afternoon are particularly delightful. The risk of heat exhaustion is minimal. Evenings are cool, requiring a light jacket. January and February are the coolest, with night temperatures dropping to around 15°C (59°F). This is the prime time for exploring the Eastern Desert’s wadis (valleys) and Bedouin camps. Avoid June through September for extended sightseeing, as the heat is physically demanding and dangerous. If you must visit during summer, schedule all outdoor activities for before 10:00 AM or after 4:00 PM.

Rainy Season

Marsa Alam technically has no distinct rainy season. It is one of the driest places on Earth. Precipitation is sporadic and minimal, averaging less than 10 millimeters (0.4 inches) annually. The slight chance of rain occurs between November and February, with December being the wettest month (average 3 mm). Rain events are typically light showers lasting less than an hour. They rarely disrupt travel plans. Heavy downpours are extremely rare, though flash floods can occur in desert wadis after intense, localized storms—these are exceptional events.

Is it worth visiting during this “rainy” period? Absolutely. The chance of rain is negligible, and the mild temperatures make it the most popular time to visit. The term “rainy season” is misleading for Marsa Alam; you can expect almost guaranteed sunshine every day of your trip, regardless of the month.

Events & Festivals

Marsa Alam’s cultural calendar is modest compared to Cairo or Luxor, but several events add local flavor to a visit. Major festivals are tied to Islamic holidays, which shift each year.

  • January–February: Marsa Alam International Fishing Tournament (dates vary). A popular event attracting anglers from across the region. Competitions for species like tuna and barracuda.
  • March–April: Sham el-Nessim (Spring Festival). Celebrated by Egyptians nationwide, including Marsa Alam. Locals picnic on beaches and enjoy traditional foods like salted fish (fesikh) and green onions. A colorful cultural experience.
  • April–May: Eid al-Fitr (Islamic holiday marking end of Ramadan). Dates vary. Expect increased local tourism, with families visiting resorts. Some shops may have adjusted hours.
  • June–July: Eid al-Adha (Feast of Sacrifice). Another major Islamic holiday. Similar to Eid al-Fitr in terms of local activity.
  • October: Marsa Alam Diving Festival (dates vary). A week-long event featuring free diving trials, underwater photography workshops, and conservation talks. Held at various dive centers, particularly around Port Ghalib.
  • December: International Day of the Sea (local celebrations). Environmental awareness events, beach clean-ups, and educational programs organized by local NGOs and resorts.

For 2026, Ramadan is expected to begin around February 18 and end around March 20. During Ramadan, many restaurants and cafes are closed during daylight hours, but tourist resorts continue to serve food to guests. Evening festivities (Iftar) are vibrant. Plan accordingly if visiting during this period.

Conclusion

Choosing the best time to visit Marsa Alam in 2026 depends entirely on your priorities. For the most comfortable weather, optimal sightseeing conditions, and vibrant atmosphere, the high season from November to March is unsurpassed. Expect higher prices and more crowds, but reliable sunshine and pleasant temperatures. For a beach vacation focused on swimming and relaxation, the shoulder months of April, May, October, and November offer the perfect blend of warm sea and manageable air temperatures. Budget travelers and dedicated divers will find exceptional value during the low season (May to September), provided they can handle extreme heat. The so-called rainy season is a non-issue.

In summary, the best months overall for a first-time visitor are April, October, and November. These months deliver excellent weather, fewer tourists than winter, and reasonable prices. For 2026, book flights and accommodation well in advance if aiming for the November–March peak, as demand remains high. Whatever month you choose, Marsa Alam’s stunning underwater world and serene desert landscapes will reward you.

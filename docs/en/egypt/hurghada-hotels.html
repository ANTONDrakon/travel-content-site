Top 10 Hotels in Hurghada 2026: Budget to Luxury

Hurghada, Egypt, has transformed from a small fishing village into a premier Red Sea resort destination, boasting over 40 kilometers of coastline along the Red Sea. The city’s hotel infrastructure is heavily concentrated along two main strips: the bustling Hurghada Marina and the quieter, resort-heavy area of El Gouna (a private town 22 kilometers north). By 2026, new eco-resorts and renovated properties have expanded capacity to over 80,000 hotel rooms, with prices varying dramatically based on season, proximity to the sea, and included amenities. The city is divided into three primary neighborhoods: Ad-Dahar (the old town, budget-friendly, local markets), Sekalla (mid-range, central, nightlife), and the Village Road area (luxury, beachfront, all-inclusive).

Average nightly rates in 2026 range from $25 for a basic room in Ad-Dahar to over $600 for a premium suite in a five-star resort. High season (December to February and July to August) sees a 30-40% price surge, while shoulder months (March-April, October-November) offer the best value. Most hotels now include free Wi-Fi, but all-inclusive packages are standard in mid-range and luxury properties, covering meals, drinks, and water sports. Below, we break down ten top hotels by price tier, with real data and specific features.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. Sunny Days Hotel

Location: Ad-Dahar, 200 meters from the beach.
Price: $35–$45/night (standard double room, breakfast included).
Description: A no-frills 3-star property with 120 rooms, built in 2018 and renovated in 2024. Rooms are clean, with air conditioning, private bathrooms, and satellite TV. The hotel has a small pool, a sun terrace, and a basic restaurant serving Egyptian and international dishes.
Pros: Extremely affordable, close to local souks and bus stops, friendly staff, free airport transfer (with 3-night stay).
Cons: No direct beach access, pool is small (10x5 meters), street noise can be loud, Wi-Fi is slow (2 Mbps).

2. Bedouin Oasis Inn

Location: Sekalla, 500 meters from the marina.
Price: $28–$38/night (double room, no breakfast).
Description: A family-run 2-star guesthouse with 18 rooms, offering a rooftop terrace with Red Sea views. Rooms are basic but spotless, with tiled floors, ceiling fans, and shared bathrooms in the cheapest options. The inn provides a free shuttle to the public beach (2 km away).
Pros: Authentic local experience, rooftop views, very low price, free tea and coffee all day.
Cons: No pool, no restaurant, shared bathrooms in budget rooms, no air conditioning in older rooms (only fans), limited parking.

3. Coral Beach Hostel

Location: Ad-Dahar, 100 meters from the bus station.
Price: $12–$18/night (dorm bed) or $30–$40/night (private room).
Description: A budget hostel with 40 beds in mixed dorms and 10 private rooms. Opened in 2022, it features a communal kitchen, a lounge area, and a rooftop bar. The hostel organizes daily snorkeling trips to Giftun Island ($20 extra).
Pros: Cheapest option in Hurghada, social atmosphere, free breakfast (bread, jam, tea), lockers in dorms.
Cons: Not suitable for couples or families, noise from bar until midnight, no pool, shared bathrooms, basic cleanliness standards.

Mid-Range Hotels ($50-150/night)

4. Sea Garden Resort

Location: Sekalla, directly on the beach.
Price: $65–$95/night (all-inclusive, double room).
Description: A 4-star resort with 280 rooms, set within 10 acres of tropical gardens. Features include three swimming pools (one heated in winter), a private sandy beach, five restaurants (Egyptian, Italian, Asian), and a spa with hammam. Rooms are modern with balconies, minibars, and flat-screen TVs. The resort offers free snorkeling gear and kayaks.
Highlights: Beachfront access, multiple pools, all-inclusive includes alcoholic drinks (local brands), family-friendly with kids' club, free Wi-Fi (10 Mbps).

5. Blue Paradise Hotel

Location: El Gouna, 25 km north of Hurghada center.
Price: $80–$120/night (half-board, double room).
Description: A boutique 4-star hotel with 60 rooms, overlooking the El Gouna lagoon. Rooms are designed in Nubian style, with hand-painted furniture and private terraces. The hotel has a large infinity pool, a dive center (PADI certified), and a seafood restaurant. It is a 10-minute walk from the El Gouna Marina.
Highlights: Quiet location, lagoon views, excellent diving packages ($40 per dive), free shuttle to El Gouna beach, free yoga classes on weekends.

6. Royal Beach Hotel

Location: Village Road, 1 km from the airport.
Price: $55–$85/night (bed and breakfast, double room).
Description: A 3-star hotel with 150 rooms, recently renovated in 2025. It offers a large outdoor pool, a gym, and a buffet restaurant. Rooms are simple but comfortable, with tiled floors, air conditioning, and satellite TV. The hotel is a 5-minute drive from the public beach.
Highlights: Proximity to airport (free shuttle), large pool (25 meters), good value for families, free parking, 24-hour reception.

7. Red Sea Oasis Resort

Location: Sekalla, 300 meters from the marina.
Price: $90–$140/night (all-inclusive, double room).
Description: A 4-star resort with 200 rooms, featuring a water park with four slides, a lazy river, and a children's pool. The resort has three restaurants (including a steakhouse), a beach club with sunbeds, and a dive center. Rooms have sea views from balconies, with modern amenities like USB ports and smart TVs.
Highlights: Water park included, all-inclusive covers premium drinks, nightly entertainment (live music, shows), free snorkeling trips to nearby reefs.

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

8. Steigenberger Al Dau Beach Hotel

Location: El Gouna, private beach.
Price: $180–$300/night (all-inclusive, double room).
Description: A 5-star resort with 400 rooms, set on a 1.5 km private beach. Features include five pools (one saltwater), a 1,200 sqm spa, eight restaurants (including a Michelin-starred chef’s table), and a golf course nearby. Rooms are spacious (40 sqm minimum) with marble bathrooms, Nespresso machines, and Egyptian cotton linens. The resort has a dedicated kids' club and a water sports center offering jet skis, parasailing, and kite surfing.
Features: Private beach, multiple fine-dining options, full-service spa, free non-motorized water sports, 24-hour butler service in suites.

9. The Oberoi Sahl Hasheesh

Location: Sahl Hasheesh Bay, 18 km south of Hurghada.
Price: $250–$500/night (room only, double).
Description: A luxury 5-star resort with 134 rooms and villas, built in a Moorish-architectural style. The resort sits on a 2 km private beach, with a stunning infinity pool overlooking the Red Sea. Rooms are elegantly furnished, with private gardens or pools in villas, and feature bathtubs, walk-in closets, and outdoor showers. The spa offers Ayurvedic treatments, and the two restaurants serve Mediterranean and Asian cuisine. The resort is known for its silence and exclusivity, with no loud entertainment.
Features: Private villa options, infinity pool, world-class spa, no children under 12 in certain areas, free scuba diving lessons.

10. Hilton Hurghada Plaza

Location: Sekalla, on the marina.
Price: $160–$220/night (all-inclusive, double room).
Description: A 5-star hotel with 320 rooms, connected to the Hurghada Marina. It features a large lagoon-style pool, a private beach, six restaurants (including a sushi bar), and a fitness center. Rooms are modern, with floor-to-ceiling windows, work desks, and premium bedding. The hotel has a casino, a nightclub, and a conference center. It is a 10-minute drive from the airport.
Features: Direct marina access, casino, multiple dining options, all-inclusive includes international brands, free shuttle to downtown, excellent for business travelers.

How to Choose the Right Area

For Beach Lovers: Choose El Gouna or Sahl Hasheesh. El Gouna, 22 km north, has calm lagoons, private beaches, and upscale resorts like Steigenberger. Sahl Hasheesh, 18 km south, offers quieter, less crowded beaches with excellent snorkeling. Both areas have limited nightlife but superb water sports.

For Sightseeing: Stay in Sekalla or Ad-Dahar. Sekalla is central, close to the Hurghada Marina (shopping, restaurants) and the Hurghada Grand Aquarium (entry $15). Ad-Dahar is near the old souk (market), the El Mina Mosque, and the Coptic Church. From these areas, day trips to Luxor (4 hours by bus, $30) and the Valley of the Kings are easily arranged.

For Nightlife: Choose the Village Road area or Sekalla. Village Road has the highest concentration of bars, clubs, and casinos, including the famous "Little Buddha" club and "Cairo Nights" lounge. Sekalla offers more local bars and live music venues. Avoid El Gouna if you want vibrant nightlife, as it is quieter and more family-oriented.

Booking Tips

How to Save: Book directly with the hotel for loyalty points or free upgrades. Use price comparison sites like Booking.com or Expedia but check for exclusive deals on hotels’ own websites. Consider half-board instead of all-inclusive if you plan to eat out—this can save $20–$30 per night. Travel in shoulder months (March, April, October, November) for 20-30% lower rates.

When to Book: For high season (December-February, July-August), book at least 3 months in advance to secure availability. For low season (May-June, September), you can book 2 weeks ahead and often find last-minute discounts of up to 40%. Avoid booking during Ramadan (dates vary) as many restaurants and services operate on reduced hours.

Best Services: Use "free cancellation" options (usually available up to 24 hours before check-in) to remain flexible. For luxury hotels, consider using a travel agent who specializes in Egypt—they often have exclusive rates and can arrange airport transfers, tours, and upgrades. For budget stays, hostels and guesthouses on Hostelworld or Airbnb offer the cheapest options, but verify cancellation policies carefully.

Additional Tips: Always confirm if the price includes taxes and service charges (many hotels add 12-14% tax). In all-inclusive resorts, check if premium drinks or specialty restaurants are extra. For families, look for hotels with kids-stay-free policies, like Steigenberger Al Dau Beach Hotel, which allows children under 12 to stay free with paying adults.

Top 10 Hotels in Sharm El Sheikh 2026: Budget to Luxury

Updated: June 2026 • Expert guide

⚠️ All prices and exchange rates in this article are approximate as of June 2026. Verify current rates and prices before booking.

Sharm El Sheikh, the crown jewel of Egypt’s Red Sea Riviera, continues to evolve its hotel infrastructure in 2026, blending world-class diving resorts with expanding desert luxury compounds. The city is divided into distinct neighborhoods: Naama Bay remains the bustling heart with restaurants and nightlife, while Shark’s Bay and Ras Um Sid offer quieter, dive-focused stays. Hadaba and Nabq Bay are emerging as value zones with newer all-inclusive resorts. Prices in 2026 reflect a 10-15% increase from 2023 due to rising energy costs and tourism demand, but competition keeps options affordable. Budget rooms average $35-50 per night, mid-range properties range from $60-130, and luxury suites start at $180 with peak winter rates (December-February) adding 20-30%. The Egyptian pound’s fluctuation against the dollar can offer additional savings for international travelers paying in foreign currency.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. Camel Dive Club & Hotel

Located in Naama Bay, this iconic budget hotel is a favorite among scuba divers. Rooms are simple but clean, with air conditioning and private bathrooms. The on-site dive center offers PADI courses. Prices start at $38 per night for a standard double room (2026 rates).

Pros: Direct beach access, best dive shop in town, lively bar. Cons: No pool, rooms can be noisy from nightlife, basic breakfast included.

2. Sharm Cliff Hotel

Perched on a hill in Hadaba, this property offers panoramic Red Sea views. Rooms are spacious with tile floors and satellite TV. A small pool and sun terrace are included. Rates begin at $32 per night for a twin room.

Pros: Incredible sunset views, quiet location, free parking. Cons: Steep walk to beach (10 minutes downhill), limited dining options, Wi-Fi is slow.

3. Oonas Dive Club

In Naama Bay’s quieter southern end, this hotel caters to budget-conscious divers. Rooms are basic but have balconies overlooking the garden. A pool and dive center are on-site. Prices start at $45 per night for a double room with breakfast.

Pros: Friendly staff, good snorkeling from the jetty, free shuttle to Naama Bay. Cons: Rooms are dated, no in-room safe, breakfast is limited to bread and jam.

2. Jaz Belvedere Resort

In the quiet Nabq Bay area, this all-inclusive resort offers value for families. Rooms are spacious with sitting areas. Facilities include four pools, a water park, and multiple restaurants. Standard rooms cost $70-100 per night, all-inclusive.

Highlights: Large pool complex, free shuttle to Naama Bay, excellent buffet variety.

3. Novotel Sharm El Sheikh

Located in Naama Bay, this chain hotel provides reliable comfort. Rooms are soundproofed with modern furnishings. The property has a large pool, tennis courts, and a private beach. Rates are $95-130 per night for a double room with breakfast.

Highlights: Central location steps from nightlife, good gym, professional staff.

4. Sol Y Mar Sharm El Sheikh

In Hadaba, this resort offers a balance of price and amenities. Rooms are bright with Egyptian cotton linens. Facilities include two pools, a spa, and a dive center. Standard rooms cost $60-80 per night, half-board.

Highlights: Quiet setting, free yoga classes, excellent snorkeling from the pier.

How to Choose the Right Area

Selecting the right neighborhood in Sharm El Sheikh depends on your travel purpose. For beach and diving enthusiasts, Shark’s Bay and Ras Um Sid offer the best house reefs and calm waters, with resorts like Reef Oasis and Four Seasons providing direct access. For sightseeing and cultural activities, Naama Bay is the hub—walk to the Old Market, the Al Mustafa Mosque, and the Naama Bay promenade. Hotels like Camel Dive Club and Novotel put you steps from the action. For nightlife and entertainment, SOHO Square in the Hadaba district is the epicenter, with casinos, bars, and live shows; Savoy Sharm El Sheikh is an ideal base. If you prioritize peace and quiet, Nabq Bay’s sprawling resorts (Jaz Belvedere, Rixos) offer seclusion with shuttle access to Naama Bay.

Booking Tips

To save money on Sharm El Sheikh hotels in 2026, book directly through the hotel’s website or call them directly—many offer 10-15% discounts compared to third-party platforms. Avoid the peak winter season (December-February) when prices surge; instead, travel in March-April or October-November for good weather and lower rates. Use price comparison tools like Booking.com or Hotels.com, but always check for hidden fees like resort surcharges (common in luxury properties). For all-inclusive stays, book packages that include transfers and excursions—Egypt Air and TUI offer bundle deals. Consider booking last-minute for budget hotels, as cancellations often free up rooms at 30% discounts. For luxury hotels, book 60 days in advance to secure early-bird rates. Always read cancellation policies: many mid-range hotels offer free cancellation up to 48 hours before arrival.

Top 10 Hotels in Luxor 2026: Budget to Luxury

Luxor, often called the world’s greatest open-air museum, is a city in southern Egypt that straddles the Nile River. In 2026, the hotel infrastructure here continues to mature, with new renovations and expansions along the East Bank (Luxor city center and Karnak) and the quieter West Bank (Valley of the Kings, Valley of the Queens). The East Bank is dense with bazaars, restaurants, and train stations, while the West Bank offers tranquility and direct access to ancient tombs. Prices in 2026 have risen modestly from pre-pandemic levels, with budget rooms averaging $30–$45 per night, mid-range options at $60–$120, and luxury properties commanding $180–$400. Neighborhoods like Al Gezira (near the Nile Corniche) and El Karnak (near the temple) are most convenient for sightseeing, while the West Bank’s villages like Al Bairat are preferred for peace and authenticity.

Most hotels now accept credit cards and offer free Wi-Fi, though speeds vary. Breakfast is almost always included in the rate, and many mid-range and luxury hotels feature Nile-view pools. The 2026 season sees a surge in boutique properties converted from historic villas, especially on the West Bank. Taxis and horse-drawn carriages (calèches) are plentiful, but walking is feasible along the Corniche. For budget travelers, hostels and guesthouses near Luxor Temple offer the best value; for luxury seekers, the stretch of resorts between the Hilton and the Winter Palace is prime real estate.

Budget Hotels (under $50/night)

1. Nefertiti Hotel Luxor

Location: East Bank, a 5-minute walk from Luxor Temple. Price: $30–$40 per night for a double room with breakfast. Description: A family-run hotel with a rooftop terrace offering panoramic views of the temple and the Nile. Rooms are basic but clean, with air conditioning and private bathrooms. The staff is famously helpful for booking tours to the Valley of the Kings. Pros: Unbeatable location, friendly atmosphere, free tea and coffee all day. Cons: No pool, street noise can be loud at night, and Wi-Fi is intermittent.

2. Bob Marley House Hostel

Location: East Bank, near the Luxor train station. Price: $10–$20 per night for a dorm bed; private rooms from $25. Description: A vibrant backpacker hub with reggae-themed decor, a garden, and a bar. Rooms are spartan but secure, with lockers and fans. The hostel organizes group trips to Abydos and Dendera. Pros: Extremely cheap, social atmosphere, good for solo travelers. Cons: No air conditioning in dorms (only fans), shared bathrooms, can be noisy until late.

3. Venus Guest House

Location: West Bank, in the village of Al Bairat. Price: $35–$45 per night for a double room. Description: A quiet guesthouse with a garden and small pool. Rooms are simple with tiled floors and mosquito nets. The owner is a local guide who offers discounted tours to the Valley of the Kings. Pros: Peaceful setting, authentic village experience, free breakfast with fresh bread. Cons: Far from East Bank nightlife, limited dining options nearby, no TV in rooms.

Mid-Range Hotels ($50–150/night)

1. Steigenberger Nile Palace Luxor

Location: East Bank, on the Corniche. Price: $90–$130 per night. Description: A 5-star hotel at a 4-star price, with a large outdoor pool, a spa, and multiple restaurants. Rooms are spacious with modern furnishings and Nile views. The hotel is adjacent to the Luxor Temple. Highlights: Excellent breakfast buffet, free shuttle to the West Bank, and a swim-up bar.

2. Iberotel Luxor

Location: East Bank, near the Luxor Temple. Price: $70–$110 per night. Description: A mid-rise hotel with a lush garden and a pool overlooking the Nile. Rooms are comfortable with Egyptian cotton linens and marble bathrooms. The hotel has a popular restaurant, The Terrace, serving Egyptian and international dishes. Highlights: Central location, friendly staff, and a small gym.

3. Pavillon Winter Luxor

Location: East Bank, within the grounds of the historic Winter Palace. Price: $80–$120 per night. Description: A charming annex of the Winter Palace, with colonial-style architecture and a private garden. Rooms are smaller than the main hotel but retain old-world charm with high ceilings and wooden shutters. Guests can use the Winter Palace’s pool and spa. Highlights: Access to the Winter Palace’s famous 1886 bar, beautiful gardens, and discounted rates for the main hotel’s facilities.

4. Al Moudira Hotel

Location: West Bank, near the Valley of the Queens. Price: $100–$150 per night. Description: A boutique hotel designed like a Nubian palace, with domed ceilings, hand-painted furniture, and a large pool set in a palm grove. Rooms are uniquely decorated with local crafts. The restaurant serves excellent Egyptian cuisine. Highlights: Romantic atmosphere, quiet location, and a rooftop bar with views of the Theban hills.

Luxury Hotels ($150+/night)

1. Sofitel Winter Palace Luxor

Location: East Bank, on the Corniche. Price: $200–$400 per night. Description: A historic hotel built in 1886, with Victorian architecture, lush gardens, and a grand lobby. Rooms are elegant with antique furnishings, marble bathrooms, and Nile or garden views. The hotel has a spa, a heated pool, and the famous 1886 Bar. Features: Butler service, afternoon tea, and a private pier for Nile boat trips.

2. Hilton Luxor Resort & Spa

Location: East Bank, on the Corniche, 2 km from the city center. Price: $180–$300 per night. Description: A modern resort with a large free-form pool, a private beach on the Nile, and multiple restaurants. Rooms are contemporary with floor-to-ceiling windows and balconies. The spa offers hammams and massages. Features: Kids’ club, tennis courts, and a fitness center.

3. Sonesta St. George Hotel Luxor

Location: East Bank, on the Corniche. Price: $150–$250 per night. Description: A sleek, modern hotel with a glass-walled lobby and a rooftop pool. Rooms are minimalist with smart TVs and rainfall showers. The hotel has a casino, a nightclub, and a sushi bar. Features: Free airport shuttle, 24-hour room service, and a business center.

How to Choose the Right Area

For sightseeing: Stay on the East Bank near Luxor Temple or Karnak. You can walk to the temples and the Luxor Museum. The West Bank is ideal for tomb visits but requires a taxi or ferry to cross the Nile. Budget travelers should choose the East Bank for convenience; luxury seekers will appreciate the West Bank’s tranquility.

For beach and relaxation: Luxor has no sea coast, but the Nile Corniche offers riverside promenades. The Hilton and Sofitel have private Nile beaches (small sandy areas with loungers). The West Bank’s Al Moudira and Venus Guest House have pools set in gardens, perfect for unwinding after tomb visits.

For nightlife: The East Bank, especially around the Corniche and the Winter Palace, has bars, nightclubs, and restaurants open late. The Sonesta St. George has a casino and nightclub. The West Bank is quiet after sunset, with only a few restaurants and no bars. If you want nightlife, book an East Bank hotel.

Booking Tips

Book directly on the hotel’s website for the best rates and perks like free breakfast or room upgrades. Use comparison sites like Booking.com or Hotels.com to check prices, but call the hotel to confirm availability and negotiate. The cheapest time to visit Luxor is summer (June–August), when temperatures exceed 40°C, but rates drop by 30–50%. Winter (November–February) is peak season; book at least 2 months ahead. For spring and fall, book 1 month in advance. Many hotels offer discounts for cash payments (10–20% off) and for stays of 3 nights or more. Always confirm if breakfast is included, as it saves $5–$10 per person per day. For budget hotels, use Hostelworld or Agoda; for luxury, use the hotel’s own reservation system or a travel agent like Expedia. Consider booking a package with flights via EgyptAir or a tour operator like Intrepid Travel for bundled savings.
